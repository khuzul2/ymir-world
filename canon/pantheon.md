> peoples of the world worship them. Primary sources: **Player Guide** pp. 21–24

## The nature of worship

`[CANON]` *(Player Guide p.21; also wiki: Religione)* Ymir has a vast number of
cults and creeds, but most of the planet practices a kind of **monolatry**: no one
doubts that the many gods exist, but each person worships **one** in particular,
seen as the true representation of the Absolute that contains all the others. Few
on Ymir dare doubt the gods' power — clerics don't need to perform public miracles
to prove divine existence *(Player Guide p.10)*.

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Cosmologia)* The gods themselves are beings of **pure Essence
endowed with will**: formless, immortal, near-omnipotent, able to be many places
at once (within limits). A cross-cutting split labels any god **Luminous** (made
of uncorrupted Essence) or **Dark** (made almost wholly of the corrupt *Essenza
Oscura*), the two locked in perpetual war. In-world scholars stress that a god's
true nature is unknowable, so every category below is a guide, not doctrine. (For
Essence and the Dark Essence themselves → `cosmology.md`.)

## The generations of the gods (divine tiers)

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Cosmologia, Dei Esterni)* The wiki sorts divinity into four
**generations / tiers**:

- **Outer Gods (Dèi Esterni, also "the Ancients" / Antichi)** — a class of gods
  from a generation *before* the present one, ruling the cosmos long before the
  **Adepts** arrived. They long withdrew into a sleep-like stasis; some (disputed)
  sources say the Adepts **exiled** them to another dimension. They have lately
  begun to manifest again, to unfathomable ends. By far the mightiest of gods;
  they rarely take mortal champions but can empower their clerics anywhere in the
  universe. Detailed below.
- **Supernal Gods (Dèi Superni)** — pure incarnations of the Essence (clean or
  corrupt). Only two are known: **Mithra** and **Ahriman** (the Prime Duality,
  below). Many lesser gods are held to be merely aspects of these two under other
  names. They act anywhere in the Cosmos without hindrance.
- **Elemental Gods (Dèi Elementali)** — the six elemental Twins (below), named as
  "the current generation, trying to impose its dominion on the cosmos." They can
  act only in planes touching the elemental ones. `[CANON]` A heresy holds that
  they *are* the former **Adepts**, who seized divine power from the Ancients (or
  who shaped the cosmos after defeating and exiling them). → open question.
- **Minor Gods (Dèi Minori)** — beings of near-divine power: mighty servants of a
  greater god, or entities that reached semi-divine status somehow. Their reach is
  local, over specific regions of Ymir. The **Dragon-Gods (Dei-Drago)** are the
  type example. → `bestiary.md`, `cosmology.md`.

## The Outer Gods (Dèi Esterni)

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Dei Esterni)* The oldest known generation of gods:
**concept-entities** that persist in the universal spaces *outside* the planes and
worlds, bound by no natural law. Some hold them to be the makers of the universe;
others, agglomerations of pure Essence embodying every thought of the Cosmos — in
any case, beings as remote and alien as they are powerful. Their direct influence
on Ymir is slight, yet `[CANON]` **the Disaster** ("il Disastro") is said to have
been caused by the direct summoning of one of these Gods — or by a botched attempt
to complete such a summoning. `[INFERRED]` This plausibly names the failed summons
behind **La Catastrofe** and the birth of the Maelstrom → `cosmology.md`,
`history.md` (open question).

`[CANON]` Few worship them outside the **Xebechani**, whose own pantheon is in
fact *called* the Dei Esterni *(wiki: Xebechani)*; the rare outsiders are Sorcerers
and clerics who reach for these cosmic entities at the end of a hunt for
ever-greater power. Names are given in Xebechan, with a rough translation and the
usual epithet:

- **Tuulhak-Xanar** ("Gaze-upon-the-Void" / *Sguardo-sul-Nulla*), **the
  Observer** — the thought of those who look but do not understand (whether from
  the watcher's folly or the object's inconceivability). All that is dark,
  misunderstood, and alien. Worshipped by the less-initiated as a god of Wisdom,
  the Occult, and Madness.
- **Xuxhul'Xhathi** ("Trichotomy-of-Acts-and-Thoughts"), **the Judge** — the
  division of all action into three Ways: Good, Evil, and the Void (the
  "Trichotomy of Acting"). The least understood yet **most worshipped** Outer God,
  invoked as god of Good, of Evil, of Balance, of Quiet, or of Action.
- **Xatol-Nahuactl** ("Word-that-Falls-Silent"), **the Silent One** — the Cosmic
  Silence out of which Music and the Word are born. God of the Arts, Magic,
  Oblivion, and Destruction.
- **Tlahuiz'Halpantexuhtli** ("Essence-of-All-That-Was-Is-and-Will-Be"), **the
  First** — the Essence of Essence, the Primordial Essence. Worshipped by
  Arcanists.
- **Xotl-Nag'aaqe** ("Union-in-One"), **the Mother** — the all that merges into
  the one that is the all. God of Creation, Life, Death, Union, the Spirits, and
  Nature.
- **Xip-Tenalthe** ("River-of-Being"), **the Risen** — Time itself: past, present,
  and future, the flow of all that is into all that will be. God of Time, Death,
  Decay, Birth, and Destruction.

`[CONFLICT]` The Observer's alternate name **Sguardo-sul-Nulla** confirms it is a
true Outer God (matching the Cartosa timeline's intruding *divinità esterna* →
`history.md`). This revises the earlier guess that the Xebechani god **Xhulhu**
("He Who Sleeps and Dreams," of the Abysses) *was* Sguardo-sul-Nulla: they now read
as **distinct** beings that merely share a Lovecraftian register. → open question.

## The Prime Duality

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Cosmologia, Religione Mithraica)* In the tier scheme above these
two are the **Supernal Gods (Dèi Superni)** — the only two known — while **Ech** is
their prior, undivided unity. In Orthodox Mithraic theology many lesser gods are
only aspects of one of them.

| God | Title | Domains | Symbol |
|-----|-------|---------|--------|
| **Ech** | The One | The primordial unity; source of Spirits and magic; split to begin time | — |
| **Mithra** | The Creator, Lord of the Sun and Light | Justice, Good, light, life, creation, the Aether, peace, healing | Golden sun |
| **Ahriman** | The Corruptor, Supreme God of Evil | Injustice, Evil, darkness, destruction, death, the Nether, war, disease; bearer of **999 Plagues**; chief enemy of Mithra | Black sun |

## The Six Divine Twins (elemental gods)

`[CANON]` Born of the contrast between Mithra and Ahriman; each presides over one
element.

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Divinità Elementali, Cosmologia)* These same six are also called
the **Twin Gods (Gli Dei Gemelli)** or simply the **Elemental Deities (Dèi
Elementali)** — the "current generation" of gods. They are worshipped across all
of Ymir (under many local names), above all by mages and elementalists, and their
cult sits easily alongside Mithraic or local faith, since they are seen as allies
or complements of the other principal gods; in many creation myths they help the
greater gods make the world by channelling the elements for them. `[CANON]` A
faith most call heretical claims they are **false gods — or the Adepti
themselves** (see the tier scheme above and the open questions).

| God | Element | Title / domains | Symbol |
|-----|---------|-----------------|--------|
| **Diis** | Fire | The Lady of Flames; fire and passion, giver of life and destruction | Heart wreathed in flame |
| **Hudemia** | Water | The Just Mother of Waters; Law and justice | A spear holding the pans of a balance |
| **Varshnak** | Ice | The Beast; the dark side of nature, the fury of the elements, winter | An axe sprouting tree-roots from its haft |
| **Heis** | Air | The Mad God; wind, chance, fury, madness | A snarling monstrous face with a single horn |
| **Therìos** | Earth | The Hunter; forest and beasts, protector of travellers and hunters | A wolf's head |
| **Dingir** | Light | The Twofold; light and shadow, logic and Magic | A star |

## Other principal gods

`[CANON]` *(Player Guide pp.21–22; worship & geography notes from wiki: Religione
Mithraica)* These form the common "Mithraic pantheon" — the gods honoured
alongside Mithra across the human lands.

- **Ishtar** — the Mother Goddess; Moon, Love, and also War. Wife of Mithra.
  Symbol: silver moon. Temples stand in Letia (fewer than Mithra's) and the **Young
  Kingdoms**; she is also widely worshipped by the **Fairies** and the
  **Ophirites** *(wiki: Religione Mithraica)*.
- **Kur** — the Dark Lady; death and the underworld, giver of oracles and
  prophecy. Wife of Ahriman. Symbol: skull. Her cultists are few, but many seers
  invoke her for prophecies; some human cities of **Ophiur** keep an official
  church of Kur as guide of the afterlife *(wiki: Religione Mithraica)*.
- **Thurms** — Lord of Thunder; war and storms, who leads the just to victory.
  Symbol: a thunderbolt. Honoured as Mithra's helper in Letia and the Young
  Kingdoms, with a cult of his own in **Helgedad** and the **Belligerent
  Kingdoms** *(wiki: Religione Mithraica)*.
- **Gog** — the Serpent; shadowy God of the Damned, of Vengeance, Lies, and
  Corruption. Ancient enemy of the good, and especially of Thurms. Symbol: a
  self-coiled serpent. Only a handful of mad fanatics worship the god of Lies
  *(wiki: Religione Mithraica)*.
- **Aisen** — the Liberator; god of Freedom and Saviour of the Oppressed. Symbol:
  a broken chain. Widely worshipped by humans of the **Belligerent Kingdoms**; a
  secret cult to him is kept in **Qi-Long** *(wiki: Religione Mithraica)*.
- **Tifereth** — goddess of Love and Beauty. Symbol: a red rose. Worshipped
  chiefly by the **Fairies** *(wiki: Religione Mithraica)*.
- **Hokhmah** — goddess of Wisdom and Knowledge turned to Good. Symbol: a torch.
  Known across Ymir through the scholar-adepts of the **Brotherhood of the Torch
  (Confraternita della Torcia)**, though beyond them her followers are few *(wiki:
  Religione Mithraica)*.
- **Tuchulcha** — the Darkener; god of Eternal Night and of Knowledge turned to
  Evil. Symbol: a raven. Seekers of forbidden lore invoke him as protector; a
  hidden place in Letia reportedly keeps a secret cult to the **Dead King (Re
  Morto)**, held to be his avatar *(wiki: Religione Mithraica)*.
- **Orum** — god of the Sea, protector of sailors and fishermen. Symbol: a shell.
  *(Chief cult of Cartosa — `factions.md`/`geography.md`; wiki: Religione
  Mithraica.)*
- **Ranin** — the Merchant; transactions, artisans, commerce. Symbol: a hammer.
  The Principality of **Cartosa** holds his largest following *(wiki: Religione
  Mithraica)*.
- **Yogh** — the Spider-God; poison and assassination. Symbol: a spider. The
  wiki's Mithraic god-list places him among the common gods, worshipped by the
  priest-sorcerers of **Ulan-Tang** *(wiki: Religione Mithraica)*; he also heads
  the darker aspects of the Xebechani pantheon (below).
- **Diis / Heis / Hudemia / Therìos / Dingir / Varshnak** — the six elementals,
  listed in the table above.
- **Niethel** — the Source; a place (or state of existence) where the elves dwelt
  before coming to Ymir. Elven religion centres on this divine place (detailed
  under *Religions by people* below). → `peoples.md`.

## The Xebechani pantheon

`[CANON]` *(Player Guide p.22)* The Xebechani keep a pantheon of their own; some
gods mirror the common ones, others are wholly distinct.

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Xebechani)* The Xebechani call their pantheon the **Dei Esterni**
and centre their faith on reverence of their ancestors ("the Ancients") and on
rites to earn **Rebirth** into a higher Caste. → `peoples.md`. Note the Player
Guide's Xebechani god-names below (Quetzalcoatl, Azatzotech, …) run parallel to —
and are distinct from — the six Outer Gods proper listed earlier.

- **Quetzalcoatl** — the Feathered Serpent; Life, Birth, Good; "the Great
  Benefactor." *(= Mithra.)* Symbol: feathered serpent.
- **Omotecuthli** — Lord of Duality; Abundance and Wisdom. Symbol: a star.
- **Tezcatlipoca** — Lord of the Night Sky, protector of warriors (Lawful Good).
  Symbol: a jaguar.
- **Ehecatl** — the Inconstant *(= Heis)*. Symbol: chameleon.
- **Mictlantecuhtli** — Lord of the Underworld; Earth and beasts *(= Therìos)*.
  Symbol: a bat.
- **Xiuhtecuhtli** — Lord of the Land of Ash; Fire *(= Diis)*. Symbol: a baboon.
- **Tamoanchan** — the Incorruptible *(= Hudemia)*. Symbol: a gull.
- **Heichpyel** — the Observer; scribe of the gods, keeper of secrets that must
  not be revealed. Symbol: an owl. `[CONFLICT]` Shares the epithet "the Observer"
  with the Outer God **Tuulhak-Xanar / Sguardo-sul-Nulla** (above) — coincidence,
  or a Xebechani mask of that Outer God? → open question.
- **Azatzotech** — the Great Putrefier; Putrefaction, Death, the Undead; "the
  Putrescent Horror" *(= Ahriman)*. Symbol: a condor.
- **Yogh** — the Spider-God; Poison and Assassination; "the Creeping Death."
  Symbol: a spider. Also worshipped beyond the Xebechani by the priest-sorcerers
  of **Ulan-Tang** *(wiki: Religione Mithraica)* — see "Other principal gods."
- **Xhulhu** — He Who Sleeps and Dreams; god of the Abysses, the Wrecker and
  Storm-Bringer; "the Madness from the Deep." Symbol: a tentacle.
  `[CONFLICT]` A Lovecraftian/Cthulhu register distinct to the Xebechani. The
  earlier guess that Xhulhu *is* the outer god **Sguardo-sul-Nulla**
  ("Gaze-upon-the-Void") is now weakened: the wiki fixes Sguardo-sul-Nulla as the
  Outer God **Tuulhak-Xanar, the Observer** (see above), so the two read as
  distinct beings sharing a register. → `history.md`, open question.

## The Sovereign Demons

See `cosmology.md` (their nature, "Beyond the Circles") and the full ranked roster
in `history.md`/`bestiary.md`. In brief: the mightiest demons, near minor-deity in
power, most trusted vassals of Ahriman (the Corrupt Essence), whose sole aim is
Ymir's destruction. Two are recorded **dead**: **Ea** (Lord of the Abysses,
destroyed 1570 d.M.) and **Saxul** (Lord of Fevers and Pestilence, destroyed 2563
d.M.).

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Entità)* The wiki now supplies the full ranked roster —
**nineteen named Sovereign Demons**, from **Asmodeus** (Lord of Tyranny) down to
Saxul — each with a sigil. `[CONFLICT]` It marks **six** as *Defunto* (destroyed):
**Abbadon** (Torment), **Abigor** (Battlefields), **Adramelek** (Lies),
**Netunshiel** (Crypts), **Ea** (Abysses), and **Saxul** (All Fevers and
Pestilences) — more than the two the existing canon dates. The full roster with
sigils belongs in → `bestiary.md`; the destruction dates in → `history.md`.

## Religions by people

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Religione)* Ymir carries more cults than anyone could list, but
the dominant pattern is the **monolatry** described at the top of this file. The
major people-specific faiths:

### Mithraism (chiefly human)

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Religione Mithraica)* One of the most widespread creeds on Ymir,
with believers throughout **Letia**, the **Young Kingdoms**, **Helgedad**,
**Cartosa**, and the **Belligerent Kingdoms**. Mostly human, but members of other
races adhere. It centres on **Mithra** as supreme creator and bringer of justice
(its creation myth is the standard cosmogony → `cosmology.md`). Two main currents:

- **Inclusivist / Orthodox Mithraism** — the doctrine preached by the Church of
  Letia: every god is essentially a *manifestation* of Mithra (if benign) or of
  Ahriman (if dark). This is the Supernal-Gods view of the tier scheme above.
- **Popular / Schismatic Mithraism** — worships Mithra as one's own god but
  accepts the other divinities as **separate** beings.

The Church of Mithra as an institution (state faith of the Letio Empire) is in →
`factions.md`.

### Elven religion — the Source

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Religione Elfi)* The elves say they came from **Niethel ("the
Source")**, a paradise where they lived among their gods, deathless and free of
hunger and thirst, fed on the gods' nectar. For a grave, disputed **fault** they
were cast out — the **Exile (Shulma)** — and severed from the gods' life-giving
presence. Each elven faction blames a different cause:

- **Light Elves (Elfi Chiari):** **Fearin**, an early king of the Dark Elves, who
  first forged a weapon and raised it against a fellow elf — inventing war and
  drawing the gods' wrath.
- **Dark Elves (Elfi Oscuri):** an ambitious ancestor of **Queen Inanna**, who
  seduced the people into revolt against the gods.
- **Enlightened Elves (Elfi Illuminati):** discord arose among the elves, fouling
  their spirit until it was unworthy of the Source; they imposed the *Shulma* on
  themselves, coming to Ymir to seek spiritual balance through a mortal life.

`[CANON]` Their sacred books hold a **prophecy** given just before the exile: a
**Sacred Realm** where the elves must await a **Messenger** who will announce the
gods' return and the blessed age. Its rival readings are the root of the three
elven "factions" (**Nhos**, "Groups") → `peoples.md`.

`[CANON]` The **Source** itself is the nearest thing to a "god" in elven faith.
The beings they call **Firìn** ("Gods," more literally "the Departed") are more
like patron-saints or guardian spirits — the immortalised souls of great elves of
the past, each watching over an aspect of life. Among elves one can *become* a god,
though only the worthiest do. (**Niethel** also appears under the principal gods
above; → `cosmology.md`, `peoples.md`.)

### Dwarven religion

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Religione Nani)* The dwarves worship a divine couple:

- **Durkaam**, the **Great Smith** — chief dwarven god, revered as father of the
  dwarves and maker of the world. Symbol: an **anvil**.
- **Damkianna**, the **Lady of Frost** — Durkaam's wife and mother of the dwarves.
  Symbol: a **snowflake**.

### Sohleugir (Lizardmen) religion

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Religione Sohleugir)* The **Sohleugir** (Lizardmen) worship
**Manàt**, the manifold **Goddess of Fate**, shown as a **wheel of eight
sections**. Her eight manifestations are conceived as opposed pairs of minor
divinities `[INFERRED — the wiki lists the eight in this opposed order but does not
bracket the pairs explicitly]`:

- **Shessa**, the Benevolent Providence ↔ **Yl'hass**, the Doom of Misfortune
- **Tsoh-k'sha**, the Uncertain Future ↔ **Zehlash**, the Ineluctable Fate
- **Hsone'ss**, the Hope of Life ↔ **Neglassh**, the Certainty of Death
- **Akenaz**, the Hand of Glory ↔ **Nemezaz**, the Eye of Oblivion

`[CANON]` Together they are **Manàt, the Eightfold Univocal — She Who Is One, the
Wheel of Fate**. → `peoples.md`/`bestiary.md` for the Sohleugir themselves.

## Known institutional religions (pointer)
