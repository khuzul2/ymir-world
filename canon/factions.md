# Factions & Powers

> Nations, orders, guilds, cults, and fighting schools. Sources: **Player Guide**,
> **Cartosa Guida** (rich on southern factions), timeline events, and the **World of
> Ymir wiki** (world-spanning societies; armed-combat and martial-arts traditions).
> Nations-as-territories are in `geography.md`; this file is about organisations, who
> wields power, and the martial traditions they carry.

## World-spanning bodies `[CANON]`

- **Il Concilio delle Genti (the Council of Peoples)** — the great alliance of the
  free peoples against the Sovereign Demons, born of the **Coalizione delle Genti**
  (1735 d.M.) and formalised at the First (~1700) and Second (2590–2595) Councils.
  Its world seat is the **Seggio di Cristallo (Crystal Seat)** in **Elendor**, in the
  Folletti's Silver Isles. The nearest thing Ymir has to a united government.
- **The Church of Mithra** — the dominant organised religion; state faith of the
  **Letio Empire**, which it came to fully command (2610 d.M.), led by the
  **Teocrate**. Missionary and expansionist (e.g. the resented Mithran mission near
  Cartosa). The lich-cultist **Kramer** was a *former* Mithran inquisitor. → `pantheon.md`

## Magical orders `[CANON]`

- **Ordine Ermetico dei Manti Azzurri (Order of the Blue Mantles)** — the official
  mages' guild of the Principality of Cartosa, founded around the **Accademia Arcana**
  (2508 d.M.); allied to Cartosa's Navy (2615). Every user of **Essence, Mentalism,
  or Arcane** magic in the Principality must register and pay dues or be outlawed.
  Three rival factions within: the **Navigatori** (navigation-magic, richest), the
  **Occhio Ceruleo** (communication & divination — rumoured to be the Prince's secret
  police), and the **Affogatori** (martial/combat & protection magic). → `magic.md`
- **Ordine dei Dragoni del Mare (Order of the Sea Dragons)** — an elite martial order
  founded at Santa Hermana (2684 d.M.); overlaps the high command of Cartosa's Navy.

## Sacred & militant orders (world-spanning) `[CANON — wiki: Società e Corporazioni]`
These orders answer to no single realm, operating across Ymir.

- **Confraternita della Torcia (the Brotherhood of the Torch)** — a sacred order
  dedicated to **Hokhmah**, goddess of wisdom and knowledge (whose own symbol is a
  torch → `pantheon.md`); it admits both lay members and priests. Its charge is to
  protect and spread knowledge bent to good, and to destroy any knowledge deemed
  "dangerous" or evil. Its seat is at **Armora** in the **Regni Giovani (Young
  Kingdoms)**, home of its grand master, **Colui-che-Cammina-nella-Luce (He-Who-
  Walks-in-the-Light)**. Symbol: a torch. → `geography.md`, `characters.md`
- **La Fratellanza (the Brotherhood)** — a militant order founded by the mage-hunter
  (**Cacciamaghi**) **Tholaer il Prode (Tholaer the Brave)**, dedicated to hunting and
  rooting out evil **Usufruitori** (magic-users) and exterminating malign magical
  creatures. Its cadets endure brutal military discipline alongside the study of magic,
  to temper both mind and spirit; the training is so hard that few last to the end, but
  those who do are reckoned among the finest combatants alive. Symbol: a falcon.
  → `magic.md`, `characters.md`

## Powers of the Cartosan south `[CANON — Cartosa Guida]`

- **La Guardia d'Oro (the Gold Guard)** — Cartosa's land army and, in peacetime,
  city police; a 13-rank hierarchy (Soldato → Generale). Low-prestige among nobles;
  some high ranks are tied to the Shadow Society.
- **La Marina (the Navy)** — Cartosa's pride and true power; supreme authority at sea
  (piracy, smuggling); the **Admiral** is among the realm's greatest offices. Contains
  the elite **Dragoni del Mare** (4 squads of 50 under a Gran Dragone; led by the
  **Dragone Anziano**, by tradition also the Admiral).
- **La Compagnia degli Scudi (the Shield Company)** — mercenaries the Gold Guard
  subcontracts.
- **La Società delle Ombre (the Shadow Society)** — Cartosa's criminal underworld,
  with hooks into the Gold Guard and the Escolar house.
- **L'Alba Nera (the Black Dawn)** — the faction behind the failed coup of the **Anno
  del Sole Nero** (3017 d.M.); very likely a local cell of the world-spanning demon
  cult of the same name — the coup's very name echoes the cult's **Sole Nero (Black
  Sun)** emblem `[INFERRED]`. See its full entry under **Hostile powers** below.
- **Gilda dei Mercanti / Gilda dei Cantori** — merchant and bard guilds (the latter at
  Meridian licenses bardic magic).

### The Nine Noble Houses of Cartosa `[CANON]`
**Carrillo** (the princely house) · **Escorsa** (always heads the Blue Mantles) ·
**Cadenas** (rich; a family Archmage) · **Contreras** (military/Navy; the Admiralty) ·
**Vallerini** (Meridian-origin turncoats; Merchant Guild; hold Meridian's governorship) ·

rumoured necromancy and demon-summoning).

## Hostile powers `[CANON]`

- **The Sovereign Demons** and their cults — e.g. the **Culto dell'Ostrica Blu**
  (Cult of the Blue Oyster), through which the demon **Ea** nearly seized Ymir
  (thwarted 1570). → `pantheon.md`, `bestiary.md`, `history.md`
- **The Orc Hordes / Regni Belligeranti (Belligerent States)** — born of the Great
  Horde of the Supreme **Uglush** (2015–2030); a standing threat under a **Grande
  Khan** (Ugrumuulsh One-Eye in 3026).
- **L'Esercito dei Lupi (the Army of the Wolves)** — the terror that seized much of
  Helgedad (3015–3024); its generals vanish mysteriously.
- **Dark Elf demon-binders** of the Gaerwath. → `peoples.md`
- **L'Alba Nera (the Black Dawn)** — worshippers of **Arhiman** and cultists of the
  Sovereign Demons, and by repute the most malign and deranged organisation on all of
  Ymir. It works through independent local cells in every corner of the world; the
  cells seem to answer to a central core, yet almost no one — not even most members —
  knows the movement's true structure. Its ultimate aim is to summon the Sovereign
  Demons in person, and at least once it is said to have come very close before being
  stopped in time. Symbol: the **Sole Nero (Black Sun)** — likely the same hand behind
  the Cartosan coup of the **Anno del Sole Nero** (3017 d.M.) listed above `[INFERRED]`.
  *(wiki: Società e Corporazioni)* → `pantheon.md`, `history.md`
- **Azar-Kalish (il Conclave delle Ombre / the Conclave of Shadows)** — a dark sect of
  sorcerer-assassins out of **Ulang-Tang**. Nothing is known of its members or where to
  find them; it is they who reach out to a would-be client, never the reverse. Ruthless
  and reputedly infallible, once they have signed a contract to kill they stop at
  nothing, and no contract can ever be recalled. Symbol: a spider.
  *(wiki: Società e Corporazioni)* → `geography.md`

## Martial traditions & fighting schools `[CANON — wiki: Stili di Combattimento Armato, Stili Arti Marziali]`
Ymir's warrior cultures preserve named fighting traditions — schools handed from
master to student, each with a home region, an ethos, and a signature reputation.
*(Presented as living traditions, not game rules.)*

### Blade & fencing schools
- **Danza delle Foglie Autunnali (the Dance of the Autumn Leaves)** — a traditional
  Elven two-sword tradition, developed to be at its deadliest when facing many enemies
  at once. The swordsman fights with a long sword in each hand, weaving a constant
  mobile guard. Its signature figure, the **Turbine d'Autunno (the Autumn Whirl)**,
  lets a master cut down two foes to either side in a single pass — at the price of
  leaving his own defence briefly open. Kept alive among the elves. → `peoples.md`
- **Scherma Cartosano Classico (Classical Cartosan Fencing)** — the most traditional
  fencing school of Cartosa: all reach and lunge, subtle footwork, and the fencer's
  lightning reflexes, fought with a rapier (*stocco*) backed by a main-gauche. The
  refined art of the Principality's duellists. → `geography.md`
- **Scherma Cartosano Torquez (Torquez Cartosan Fencing)** — a rival Cartosan school
  founded expressly to defeat the Classical style, which its trained duellists reliably
  out-time. More meditative and patient, it relies on the **Guanto d'Arme**, a special
  plated steel gauntlet, to trap, parry, and wrench aside the opponent's blade; its
  masters are feared for disarming their foes and snapping swords outright. → `geography.md`
- **Scherma Armorita (Armoran Fencing)** — the fencing of **Armora** in the Young
  Kingdoms; plainer and more static than the Cartosan schools, but far less tiring to
  fight with, making it more a soldier's craft than a duellist's art. → `geography.md`

### Unarmed & martial-arts traditions
- **Waghrashilat** — a fighting art of the **Regni Belligeranti (Belligerent States)**,
  kept by the **Monks and Warrior-Monks** who worship **Hudemia**; an advanced grappling
  tradition of locks, holds, and sweeping throws. → `pantheon.md` (and the Belligerent
  States under **Hostile powers** above).
- **Danza dei Tre Venti Divini (the Dance of the Three Divine Winds)** — an unarmed
  tradition of the **Qi-Long**, a branch of the school of the **Quattro Venti Divini
  (the Four Divine Winds)** handed down for generations by masters of the **triade
  dell'Airone Grigio (the Grey Heron triad)**. This particular form was shaped by
  **Han Hsian-Tz'**, pupil of **Pai Yuchan**, to suit his own martial inclinations.
  Drawing on the movements and postures of Qi-Long opera-dance, it fights with fluid,
  unpredictable motion — sudden leaps and constant changes of guard to bewilder a foe
  before striking, great defensive agility answered by abrupt blows meant to stun or
  unbalance. It is deadliest against humanoid opponents, and its three families of
  blows are named for the celestial beasts: the **White Tiger of the West** (strikes),
  the **Red Phoenix of the South** (sweeps), and the **Azure Dragon of the East**
  (nerve-strikes). → `geography.md`, `characters.md`
- **Shiuyiaa** — an alien tradition said to come from **Umbra**, the otherworld of the
  alien travellers (whose light-drinking material shares the name). It incorporates the
  natural weapons — claws and fangs — of its practitioners, and is unlike anything
  native to Ymir. → `cosmology.md`

  needs a source doc — **Storia Letio** is a brief timeline, not an institutional
  description.

  are only known by name so far; their guilds/orders await the regional docs.
- **Maraviglie** and **Edizione Straordinaria** (not yet mined) may hold additional
  organisations, wonders, or setting-wide institutions.

  Cartosan coup faction of 3017? The shared **Sole Nero (Black Sun)** emblem strongly
  implies it, but no source states it outright. → `NOTES.md`
- **Ulang-Tang** (home of Azar-Kalish) and **Umbra** (origin of the Shiuyiaa style;
  also a light-drinking material in **Materiali**, tied to alien travellers) are not
  yet placed in `geography.md` / `cosmology.md`.
- The **school of the Quattro Venti Divini** and the **triade dell'Airone Grigio (Grey
  Heron triad)** in the Qi-Long — a master-lineage (Pai Yuchan → Han Hsian-Tz') that
  awaits a fuller Qi-Long source.
- Do the **Monks / Warrior-Monks of Hudemia** in the Belligerent States form a named
  monastic order? The Waghrashilat tradition implies an institution not yet documented.
