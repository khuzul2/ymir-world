# Magic

> How magic works in Ymir, its realms, and how society treats it. Sources so far:
> **Player Guide** (Maelstrom effects, magic in society, realms of power) and
> **Cartosa Guida** (regulation of magic). The dedicated deep source —
> **LIBRO ROSSO DEL CONTROLLO MAGICO** ("Red Book of Magic Control," 52 pp.) — is
> **not yet fully mined**; this entry is a first pass to be expanded from it.
>
> System note: Ymir was played in **Rolemaster (RMSS/RMFRPG)**, whose realms
> (Essence, Channeling, Mentalism, Arcane) map onto the in-world terms below. The
> manual will present magic **system-agnostically** — concepts, not rules.

## The source of magic `[CANON]`
Magic flows ultimately from **Ech, the One**, through the **Spirits** that emanate
from it (→ `cosmology.md`). The raw stuff of magic is variously called **Essenza
(Essence)** and **mana**; **Arvo** is pulverised, physical Essence, gathered where
the Maelstrom's **Risonanza** (Resonance) causes it to sediment (prized by the Dark
Elves). → `peoples.md`

## The four realms of magic `[CANON — LIBRO ROSSO, pp.9+]`
Practitioners are called **Usufruitori** (channelers/users). The Red Book of Magic
Control classifies the Art into **four realms**:
- **Mentalismo (Mentalism)** — magic channelled *through the mind* (the mind is a
  catalyst, not the source). Subtle and stealthy: cast without gesture or voice (only
  a faint reddening of the eyes betrays it); strong in illusion, mind-attacks,
  emotion-control, self-enhancement, movement (levitation/teleport). Weakness: most
  effects are self-only and single-target; poor at elemental force.
- **Flusso (Flow)** — the **divine** realm; the power of priests/clerics, granted by
  the gods (Rolemaster's "Channeling"). Answers to no mage-guild — "no one dares ask
  the priests to register."
- **Essenza (Essence)** — the **arcane/elemental** realm, drawn from the world's
  essence; the bread and butter of the **Manti Azzurri** (esp. **Elementalisti** of
  Water & Wind). Tied to the six **Elementi** and their gods (→ `pantheon.md`).
- **Usufruitori Corrotti (Corrupted Channelers)** — the fourth, dark category:
  necromancers, demon-binders, and those who wield corrupted power. The chief target
  of the anti-magic corps below.

`[INFERRED]` "**Arcano (Arcane)**" (referenced at Cartosa as a registrable realm) is
best read as the unified/highest realm in the Rolemaster sense, above the three.

The **Adepti** were the supreme masters of magic — beside them "the best mage alive
is a raw apprentice." Their portal-craft still scars the world. → `history.md`

## The Maelstrom's grip on magic `[CANON — Player Guide p.20]`
The Maelstrom warps all magic near the Central Ocean:
- **Deflusso (Outflow):** long-range **teleportation** fails ever more with distance
  (doubling failure per 50 km past 100 km) — so no one moves armies or goods by
  teleport.
- **Offuscamento (Obscuring):** **divination** is clouded; no divination penetrates
  the Maelstrom, and visions of far past/future grow unreliable.
- **Tempeste Arcane (Arcane Storms):** periodic magical storms channel energies from
  other dimensions across the Central Ocean.
- **Il Marchio (the Mark):** magic in the womb mutates some newborns (**Marchiati**).
→ `cosmology.md`

## Magic in society `[CANON — Cartosa Guida pp.9–10]`
Magic is practised **openly** in the civilised south, but regarded with reverent fear
and suspicion by non-users. It is **tightly regulated**: in Cartosa every Essence /
Mentalism / Arcane user must register with the **Manti Azzurri** and pay dues, or be
outlawed; priests (Flow) and nobles are exempt. Common licit uses: **protection**
(golems, wards, bodyguard-mages), **travel** (navigation magic for ships), **healing**
(temple clerics, lay healers — costly and rationed), and **entertainment** (bards,
licensed). Forbidden arts — **necromancy** and **demon-summoning** — are capital
matters (though whispered of in houses like Gibraleon and mastered by the Dark Elves).
→ `factions.md`

## Anti-magic & the magitech of the Young Kingdoms `[CANON — LIBRO ROSSO, Maraviglie]`
- **Sezione di Controllo Magico (S.C.M.)** — Armora's military **anti-magic corps**,
  which trains ordinary (non-caster) soldiers to counter each realm of magic. Its
  field manual is the **Libro Rosso del Controllo Magico** ("Vademecum of Practical
  Anti-Magic"), produced with the **Collegio del Settimo Circolo** (a magic college).
  This is a defining institution of Armora's technocratic, magic-regulating culture.
- **Magic permits (Livelli A–D)** — the Young Kingdoms license casting by permit: A
  (legal practice without guild membership), B (temporary access to normally-forbidden
  lists, or bringing a summoned entity into a city), C (longer/broader), D (near-total
  licence — only a Young-Kingdom prince may grant it). → `factions.md`
- **Magitech devices** — Armora sells the **Essenziometro** (Essence-meter: detects
  active magic, wired to alarms), the **Oniroscopio** (dream-machine into the **Mondo
  dei Sogni**), **Giare Leyden** (Leyden jars powering **Automata**), enchanted
  firearms (the **Chimera/Arco Aeterico**, electric and steam lances), and vehicles
  (steam trams, airships, ornithopters, the **Nautilo** submarine). → `geography.md`,
  `factions.md`

## Dating note `[CANON]`
The magic system book is dated **Anno 3027 d.M. / Era della Marea 2** — the latest
date attested in any source, and the anchor for the **campaign present**. → `history.md`

## Artefacts, portals & relics `[CANON]`
- **The Vere Vie (True Ways)** — the Xebechani portal network (built ~1400–1500 d.M.).
- **The Adepts' portals** — dormant behind the Maelstrom's seal until the Era della
  Marea reactivates them all. → `history.md`
- **The six Reliquie Divine (Divine Relics)** and the **Rune del Potere (Runes of
  Power)** — the artefacts whose misuse ends the age and looses the Bewitched Tide.
- **Djinn** — Spirits given fixed form with **mana** to prevent their corruption
  (bound to place, object, or task). → `cosmology.md`
