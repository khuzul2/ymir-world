## Named weapons beyond magic.md `[CANON — wiki: Armi]`

- **The demon-forged triad — La Mannaia, La Schiantatrice, La Rovinatrice** — three
  sister-weapons of the **Demon Wars**, each said to have been borne by one of the **three
  lieutenants of the demon host**, each powered by a **demon imprisoned inside it** and
  forged of **Acciaio Demoniaco** (→ `magic.md`, materials): the Cleaver (a slayer of
  humans and folletti), the Shatterer (a flail, bane of stone, metal, and dwarves), the
  Ruiner (an axe, bane of animate plants, tanoth, and elves). `[INFERRED]` The three banes
  imply the three lieutenants warred on different fronts — **none of the three is named
  anywhere**; an open bestiary/history hook.
- **Tessionde** — the **lost twin blade of Frangionde** (the Wave-Breaker, → `magic.md`).
  Both are **Bulusture** work ("metal corrupted by elemental Water") of the **Guerra delle
  Onde**; where Tessionde lies is a ready-made quest hook. `[CONFLICT → HARV-9]` Whether
  that elemental War of the Waves is the mythologised Cartosa–Meridian naval war
  (2320–2350) or a third, older war is logged, not resolved.
- **Sentient & cursed blades** *(one-liners; full prose in the digest)*: **Strappacarne**
  (a telepathic shape-choosing blade that seizes its bearers with berserker Fury, then
  abandons them), **Mieticorpi** (black-metal longsword tempered in its forger's own
  blood), **La Dominatrice** (a bladed whip, grip bound in human skin, that enslaves by
  embedded metal and agony), **Forgiavedove** (a scimitar whose cuts never stop bleeding),
  the **Frecce Sanguinarie** (arrows that bleed a victim to death).
- **Relics of avarice & blasphemy**: **Morgenstern** (mithril morning star of the dwarf
  **Kellar**, whose lingering greed infects its wielders) and the **Pugnale del Rituale
  Blasfemo** (a sacrificial gold dagger, kept in a carved bone casket, that raises its
  victims as undead while a ruby devours their souls).
- **Weapons of the good & elder-crafted**: **Piccolo Artiglio** (an Adepti-era rapier of
  Alto Acciaio, inscribed in the Adepts' tongue — *"Forged, with the Blessing of Mithra,
  by Urkhramest the Devout"* — it glows blue near demons), the **Ascia di Droan** (the
  golden-bladed axe of the dwarven hero **Droan**), the **Pugni di Hexor** (mithril
  gauntlets that absorbed a Vorskgen woman's grief and now shield their wearers from
  sorrow's spread), and **Elethar, il Cacciatore** (an elven longbow whose price was the
  planting of a hundred trees — the birth of the **forest of Caelebawn**, → `geography.md`
  Helgedad).

### Oblivion — forging note `[CANON — wiki: Armi, merged under decision HIS-13]`
`magic.md`/`characters.md` hold the ruling: **Oblivion is Tofango's demon-axe** — the
trapped demon's will drove him to madness, and he ended his days a hermit (HIS-13). The
wiki adds the **forging history**, which merges cleanly *under* that ruling: a two-handed
axe of purest Acciaio Demoniaco, forged at **Hagelbarn in Letia** by the alchemist **Hans
Schlecht**, originally commissioned by the inquisitor **Heinrich Kramer** as a lure to
corrupt the paladin **Urikal**; vowed to Ahriman, its dormant demon waking as its bearer's
corruption deepens. The axe's *making* touches Kramer; its canonical *bearer and victim*
is **Tofango**.

## The in-world library — Scritti e Pergamene `[CANON — wiki-worldofymir: Scritti e Pergamene]`
Roughly **one hundred named books, tomes, and treatises** circulate on Ymir — the full
catalogue (titles, authors, RM-mechanical effects) is the source page,
`sources/extracted/wiki-worldofymir/Scritti e Pergamene.md`; **do not re-transcribe it**.
Canonised standouts (existence + lore; mechanics dropped):
- **Sacred scriptures** (→ `pantheon.md`): *Il Libro degli Abissi* (holy book of
  **Hudemia**) · *Parole al Vento* (of **Heis** — reading it erodes sanity, fitting the
  Mad God) · *Il Libro dei Precetti* (of **Thurms**) · *La Ruota del Fato* (**Sohleugir**
  scripture — names the Wheel-creed's book).
- **Forbidden & outer**: *Litanie per il Grande Agothu* (demon-pacts; independently
  attested in batch2, sold by the necromancer Korak Garth) · **Il Libro degli Estranei**
  (a rite to summon a **Natharl'Nacna** → `bestiary.md`) · *Oltre la Soglia* · *Il Tomo
  della Magia Nera* (many copies, each a different evil discipline) · *Lo Specchio Nero* ·
  *Osservazioni sui Corpi Confinanti* ("Dark Entities"; batch2's Corpi Confinati motif) ·
  *La Chiave d'Argento* (Dreamlands lore — a Lovecraft nod; → COS-16).
- **Adepti & magitech**: *Le Macchine degli Adepti* · *Apparati Leggendari* ·
  *Annotazioni a proposito degli Adepti* (by **Dreyfus il Sadhirita**) · the two-volume
  *Manuale di Meta-Taumaturgia Meccanica* (Armora's magitech curriculum in-world) ·
  *Inventare l'Impossibile* (Automata) · *Motori a Vapore* · *Guida alla vita
  Artificiale* · *Sulla Costruzione di Golem Servitori* · *La Magna Opera ovvero i
  Segreti della Vita* (simulacra).
- **Histories & ethnographies** — proof the canon topics have in-world literature:
  *Declino e Caduta dell'Impero Letio* · *Breve Storia di Cartosa* · *La Guerra dei
  Principi* · *La Battaglia della Terra Infranta* · *La Leggenda dell'Occhio di Kzorr* ·
  *I Regni Neri* (Ophiur) · *Il Paese delle Sabbie* (Grande Erg) · *L'Isola delle Nebbie*
  (Kyria) · *Le Guerre dei Goblin* (dwarf history) · **Ztoria di Krande Imperum** ("ti
  **Mugraash lo skrifano**" — an orc-accented history of the Regni Belligeranti, by an orc
  chronicler!) · *Elfi: il popolo della Shulma* (ties to the Exile/Messaggero prophecy) ·
  *Le Origini degli N'Kishi* · *Il Cargo: alle Origini del Mito* (scholarship on the
  M'Bantha cargo-belief) · *Comprendere gli Xebechani* · *I Figli della Notte* (two
  volumes: Licantropi, Vampiri) · **Vita di San Dwayne, l'Edificatore** (hagiography of
  the canonised PC → `characters.md`) · the **two rival biographies of the pirate
  Rodrigo** (*La Leggenda* vs *La Vera Storia*, the latter "a cura di **Diego Alehandro de
  la Corona**" — the PC bard; its lore is *monster*-knowledge, a wink that Rodrigo was a
  monster; feeds the "Roberto Rodrigo l'Armorita" legend).
- **The sanity-eroding motif**: several volumes (*Parole al Vento*, *Manuale Pratico di
  Chirurgia Composita* — by the PC **Ruben Montoya** — *Tomo della Stolidità Vermiglia*,
  *Il Libro degli Estranei*) cost the reader their mind — "forbidden knowledge costs
  sanity," pairing with the Outer-God axis (→ `secret-the-great-lie.md`, `magic.md`
  Follia).
`[NOTE]` The fuller Fandom page *drops* the earlier stub's two items — the *Tomo della
Falsa Sapienza* and the **Pergamene Dimenticate** survive via *Oggetti Vari* / `magic.md`
(Named artifacts).
