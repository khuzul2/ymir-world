# Items, Materials & Equipment

> The wondrous things of Ymir: legendary weapons, armour, wands and staves,
> magical tomes, enchanted metals and stones, herbs, and sundry relics. Sources:
> the **World of Ymir** Fandom wiki — *Armi*, *Armature e Scudi*, *Staffe,
> bacchette e bastoni*, *Scritti e Pergamene*, *Materiali*, *Metalli e Pietre*,
> *Erbe*, and *Oggetti Vari*.
>
> System note: these pages were written for **Rolemaster** and give hard numbers
> (bonuses, criticals, resistance rolls). This entry keeps only what matters to the
> *world* — origins, ownership, and the qualitative magic (corruption, magic-immunity,
> Essence-storage, sentience) — and drops the mechanics. Names are kept exactly as
> spelled, glossed in English on first use.
>
> Tags: `[CANON]` from sources · `[INFERRED]` implied · `[INVENTED]` gap-fill ·
> `[CONFLICT]` sources disagree. See root `README.md`.

## Legendary & named weapons `[CANON — wiki: Armi]`

### The demon-forged triad — La Mannaia, La Schiantatrice, La Rovinatrice

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Armi)* Three sister-weapons dating to the **Demon Wars** (→
`history.md`, Era I), each said to have been borne by one of the **three
lieutenants of the demon host**. Each draws its power from a **demon imprisoned
inside it** by its maker, and each is forged of **Acciaio Demoniaco** (Demonic
Steel; see below) — so massive and cursed that they cannot be raised to parry and
sit ill in a mortal grip.

- **La Mannaia** ("the Cleaver") — a great two-handed blade; a slayer of humans and
  folletti (sprites), it strikes with a crushing after-blow.
- **La Schiantatrice** ("the Shatterer") — a flail; bane of creatures of stone and
  metal, and of dwarves.
- **La Rovinatrice** ("the Ruiner") — a battle-axe; bane of Animate Plants, **tanoth**,
  and elves.

Each can once a day deliver a "**Devastating Blow**" so ruinous it ignores all
armour — but the effort leaves the wielder half-crippled and slow for a long while
after. `[INFERRED]` The three banes (against different peoples) suggest the three
lieutenants warred on different fronts of the Demon Wars.

### The elemental blades (lame elementali)

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Armi)* A category of swords keyed to the six elements; several
survive, some corrupted from their original nature.

- **Vendicatrice Oscura** ("Dark Avenger") — the **major elemental blade of Earth**,
  wrought entirely of a mysterious black, **indestructible** metal, its pommel
  shaped as a skull. An exterminator of the undead; it can shatter the weapon that
  parries it.
- **Hellion** — the **major elemental blade of Fire**, forged in **Acciaio Demoniaco**,
  forever shedding a pale reddish light. On striking it can "unleash Hell upon the
  earth" in a burst of flame that spares not even its own wielder.
- **Mornlosse** — a longsword of exquisite make, gold hilt set with lapis and
  sapphires. **Once one of the elemental blades**, it was **tainted by evil** and
  its power half-corrupted: its mithril like now throws dark reflections. It carries
  killing cold and slays fire-bound beings, yet is feeble against cold; grimmest of
  all, it can raise any humanoid it kills as a **Frost Skeleton** in the bearer's
  service.
- **Frangionde** ("Wave-Breaker") — a bastard sword of **Bulusture**, a **minor**
  elemental blade used during the **War of the Waves** (Guerra delle Onde). It is
  the twin of the **lost blade Tessionde**, and lets its wielder fight unhindered
  underwater. `[INFERRED]` Bulusture is described as "metal corrupted by elemental
  Water" (→ Armour, below), fixing this whole set in a mythic, elemental War of the
  Waves — possibly distinct from the mundane Cartosa–Meridian naval war of the same
  name (→ `history.md`, 2320–2350; see Open questions).

### Sentient, cursed & blood-thirsty blades

- **Strappacarne** ("Flesh-Render") `[CANON]` *(wiki: Armi)* — an **intelligent
  blade** that speaks by telepathy and chooses whatever form (falchion, scimitar,
  or broadsword) best pleases its master. Its one desire is to fight and drink the
  blood of enemies. It has passed through many warriors, all seized by **berserker
  Fury**, leading them to victory and then abandoning them without warning.
- **Mieticorpi** ("Body-Reaper") `[CANON]` — a black-metal longsword tempered in the
  blood of its own forger, so keen it is said to sever a leaf that merely alights on
  it; a cruel, blood-thirsty thing that throbs with lust for battle.
- **Mietianime** ("Soul-Reaper") `[CANON]` — "**one of the most evil objects ever
  made in all Ymir**," said in legend to have been forged **by the god of death
  himself**. Its blade is a strange, wholly black crystal that swallows light; its
  black-metal hilt is a skeletal dragon breathing flame. Many warriors have tried
  to wield it and none survived its touch, for the blade **hates life** — every
  living hand that grips it is drained. It is presently borne by **Morannagul**, lord
  of the Dark Elves, the only known living being able to wield it, thanks to his
  **metal arm**. → `characters.md`, `peoples.md`.
- **La Dominatrice** `[CANON]` — a long metal whip of bladed, spiked links with a
  grip **bound in human skin**. When it strikes it can leave a fragment of metal in
  the victim's flesh (removable only by lifting a curse); thereafter any disobedience
  to the whip's owner brings the victim searing agony until they obey, die, or the
  fragment is cut out.
- **Forgiavedove** ("Widow-Forger") `[CANON]` — a scimitar of burnished steel with
  its name written along the blade in **blood-red runes**; its cuts will not stop
  bleeding.
- **Frecce Sanguinarie** ("Sanguine Arrows") `[CANON]` — red arrows said to bleed a
  victim to death from the slightest wound.

### Cursed relics of avarice, blasphemy & Ahriman

- **Morgenstern** `[CANON]` *(wiki: Armi)* — a finely wrought mithril morning star,
  the maker's name cut into the haft in **dwarven runes**. Built by the dwarf **Kellar**,
  a skilled armourer and noted alchemist, and reckoned one of his finest works — but
  Kellar was as famous for **greed and avarice** as for skill, and a shadow of his
  spirit lingers in the weapon: it is a slayer of thieves, and whoever wields it
  grows steadily greedy and jealous of their wealth.
- **Pugnale del Rituale Blasfemo** ("Dagger of the Blasphemous Ritual") `[CANON]` —
  a fine gold dagger with a great ruby in the hilt, kept in a **bone casket** carved
  with scenes of dark priests at human sacrifice. Any humanoid slain by the sacrificial
  rite carved on the casket rises as an **undead** in the wielder's service while its
  soul is **devoured by the ruby**; carried more than fifty paces from its casket, the
  dagger teleports straight back inside it.
- **Oblivion** `[CANON]` — a two-handed battle-axe of purest **Acciaio Demoniaco**,
  forged at **Hagelbarn in Letia** by the alchemist **Hans Schlecht**, originally at
  the behest of the former inquisitor **Heinrich Kramer**, and given to the paladin
  **Urikal** in a bid to corrupt him. Oblivion is **vowed to Ahriman**, a Corruptor of
  Faith and a slayer of the good; it holds a **dormant demon** that wakes once its
  bearer is deeply corrupted, whereupon the axe becomes sentient and speaks to the
  wicked. Every non-evil soul it reaps deepens the bearer's corruption and floods him
  with sublime pleasure. `[INFERRED]` **Heinrich Kramer** is almost certainly the same
  **Kramer** — the fallen Mithran inquisitor turned mad lich-cultist — who activates
  the Ulan-Tang portal in 3026 d.M. → `history.md`, `characters.md`.

### Weapons of the good, the heroic & the elder-crafted

- **La Spada di Valdis** ("the Sword of Valdis") `[CANON]` *(wiki: Armi)* — famously
  borne by the great paladin **Valdis Nemertine** in the **War of the Broken Land**.
  Forged from a **metal fallen from the sky** and worked by the finest dwarven smiths
  of **Naugrith** into an intelligent, telepathic blade of **Galvorn**; it slays
  undead and demons, wards its bearer, and burns any who wield it with an unclean or
  evil heart. → `characters.md` (Valdis Nemertine), `history.md` (Battle of the Broken
  Land, 2563 d.M.).
- **Piccolo Artiglio** ("Little Claw") `[CANON]` — a rapier of *Alto Acciaio* (High
  Steel) dating to the **Era of the Adepti**, forged by forgotten techniques. Its blade
  is inscribed in the **Adepts' tongue**: *"Little Claw. Forged, with the Blessing of
  Mithra, by Urkhramest the Devout."* It has served many good warriors and **detects
  demons**, glowing blue when they are near. → `history.md` (the Adepti).
- **Ascia di Droan** ("Droan's Axe") `[CANON]` — a dwarven battle-axe with a golden
  blade, wielded by the great dwarven hero **Droan** to defeat countless foes; it
  wraps its bearer in a protective aura and can shatter an enemy's limbs.
- **Pugni di Hexor** ("Hexor's Fists") `[CANON]` — mithril plate **gauntlets**, once
  part of a paladin's armour. Dying, he gave them as a pledge to his companion, a
  **Vorskgen** woman, to guard her in battle; when he fell, her grief so filled the
  gauntlets that they **absorbed her sorrow**, and ever since they protect any who
  wear them, to keep more grief from spreading through the world.
- **Elethar, il Cacciatore** ("Elethar, the Hunter") `[CANON]` — a silvered longbow of
  clear **elven make**, its string woven of elf-hair that will never break. A human king
  commissioned it; seeing the elf's work he said no wealth in his realm could pay for
  such a thing, and the elf asked only to plant a tree to repay nature for what had been
  taken. The king granted a hundred — and so the **forest of Caelebawn** was born.

## Armour & shields `[CANON — wiki: Armature e Scudi]`

- **Corazza a Piastre del Difensore** ("the Defender's Plate") — worn by paladins and
  good warriors against evil; steadies its bearer against **Fear**.
- **Scudo del Drago** ("the Dragon Shield") — a mithril shield legend says was forged
  in the heat of a **dragon's fire**; near-**indestructible** and proof against
  elemental attacks.
- **Elmo del Lupo** ("the Wolf Helm") — a helm shaped as a wolf's head that shields
  without dulling the senses, and grants **Fear** or **Wolf-Senses** once a day.
- **Pelle di Lupo** ("Wolf-Skin") — a mail shirt hung with a wolf-hide cloak; it eases
  hiding in the open — but a failed attempt risks the wearer being **mistaken for an
  actual wolf**.
- **Corazza dell'Immortale** ("the Immortal's Cuirass") — finely wrought plate, its
  pauldrons like talons and its breastplate a dragon's head; **once a year** it lets its
  wearer shrug off a mortal wound.
- **Veste d'Ossa** ("Robe of Bones") — a strange armour of bone and black leather, said
  to have belonged to the **Re Morto** (Dead King), high priest of **Tuchulcha** (→
  `pantheon.md`). It doubles the reach of the wearer's undead-mastery magic and grants
  **immunity to the undead's energy-drain**.
- **Armatura del Destino Oscuro** ("Armour of Dark Fate") — field armour of a metal so
  black it **drinks light**, shaped like a demon; wards strongly against **Light and
  Electricity**.
- **Armatura delle Profondità** ("Armour of the Depths") — full plate of **Bulusture**,
  "metal corrupted by elemental Water," worn by great warriors during the **War of the
  Waves**; the wearer moves freely underwater and **breathes water like air**.

## Wands, staves & rods `[CANON — wiki: Staffe, bacchette e bastoni]`

- **Scettro dei Sospiri** ("Sceptre of Sighs") — a rod of black iron topped with a
  crowned skull whose mouth gapes in an endless silent scream; an **aura of despair**
  surrounds any who hold it, and it magnifies the power of **evil channelers of the
  Flow**.
- **Staffa di Tshot** ("Staff of Tshot") — a staff of blackened bone crowned with a
  horse's skull, rubies set in its eye-sockets. Made by the wicked sorcerer **Tshot**
  for his blasphemous rites, and later borne by **Radamantis, the first known Nosferatu
  on Ymir**. On command it takes its **True Form: a Demonic Steed** at its master's
  command.
- **Scettro d'Osso** ("Bone Sceptre") — a plain sceptre carved from a single **dragon's
  femur**, covered in evil runes; a potent multiplier for the **evil Flow**.
- **Staffa del Dio-Ragno** ("Staff of the Spider-God") — a black-metal staff wielded by
  the high priests of the god **Yogh** (→ `pantheon.md`); it grants command of spiders,
  spider-venom, and the power to conjure a swarm.
- **Staffa dell'Autarca** ("Staff of the Autarch") — an elaborate staff said to have
  belonged to some great mage of old; it aids **Arcane** channelers and can **store a
  spell** to be loosed later without preparation.
- **Scettro di Lorith** ("Sceptre of Lorith") — a golden sceptre once owned by **King
  Lorith**, said to grant great power over **lesser demons**: continuous demon-speech,
  choice of exactly which demon-kind one summons, and pacification of nearby fiends.
- **Inferno, la Staffa delle Fiamme** ("Inferno, the Staff of Flames") — a long golden
  staff shaped as a dragon clutching an **amber globe** in its jaws; a charged rod of
  escalating fire-magic.

`[INFERRED]` Several rods (Scettro dei Sospiri, Scettro d'Osso, Staffa del Dio-Ragno)
are explicitly tools of the **evil Flow** — priestly, not arcane, magic — tying them to
the demon-cults and the god Yogh rather than to the mage-guilds. → `magic.md`, `factions.md`.

## Scrolls & writings `[CANON — wiki: Scritti e Pergamene]`

### Le Pergamene Dimenticate — the Forgotten Scrolls

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Oggetti Vari)* Eight scrolls sheathed in a magical **Krypton**,
said to be the **only documented knowledge to survive the great disaster** (→ `history.md`,
La Catastrofe). Written in a lofty variant of the **Language of the Ancients** and hard
to read, they comprise five works:

- ***Il Risveglio degli Dèi*** ("The Awakening of the Gods," 2 scrolls) — a **forbidden
  ritual** (the one that reopens the Gate of Despair; see below).
- ***La Filosofia degli Immortali*** ("The Philosophy of the Immortals," 3 scrolls) —
  the secrets of **High Alchemy**.
- ***Demonologia Superiore*** and ***Necromanzia Superiore*** — higher demonology and
  necromancy.
- ***La Serpe Unificatrice*** ("The Unifying Serpent," the most coveted) — how to learn
  the **language of the Other Gods** (Altri Dèi). `[INFERRED]` The "Other Gods" point to
  the outer-god axis (Sguardo-sul-Nulla / Xhulhu). → `pantheon.md`, `cosmology.md`.

### The wider library

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Scritti e Pergamene)* A catalogue of roughly **a hundred** named
books, tomes, and treatises circulates on Ymir — mostly reference works granting lore of
a subject. The lore-bearing standouts:

| Title | What it is |
|-------|-----------|
| *De Daemonolatrya* · *Litanie per il Grande Agothu* · *Disquisizioni sulle Evocazioni Perigliose* | Demonology and demon-pacts |
| *Oltre la Soglia* · *Necromanzia, una bibliografia ragionata* | Necromancy |
| *Il Tomo della Magia Nera* (many copies, each a random evil discipline) · *Lo Specchio Nero* · *Il Libro dei Segreti Rivelati* | Dark and forbidden magic |
| *Le Macchine degli Adepti* · *Apparati Leggendari* · *Annotazioni sugli Adepti* (by **Dreyfus il Sadhirita**) | The Adepti and their artefacts/machines |
| *La Magna Opera, ovvero i Segreti della Vita* · *Guida alla vita Artificiale* · *Sulla Costruzione di Golem Servitori* | Creating artificial life, golems, simulacra |
| *La Chiave d'Argento* ("The Silver Key") | Lore of the **Dreamlands** (Mondo dei Sogni) |
| *Il Libro degli Estranei* ("The Book of Strangers") | Teaches a rite to summon a **Natharl'Nacna** — an outer/eldritch being |
| *Osservazioni sui Corpi Confinanti* | Grants access to the "Dark Entities" arts |
| *Il Libro degli Arcani Trascendenti* | Contains abstruse **prophecies** |
| *Il Libro degli Abissi* (sacred book of **Hudemia**) · *Parole al Vento* (of **Heis**) · *Il Libro dei Precetti* (of **Thurms**) · *La Ruota del Fato* (Sohleugir scripture) | Holy scriptures of the gods → `pantheon.md` |

`[INFERRED]` Several of the darkest tomes (*La Serpe Unificatrice*, *Parole al Vento*,
*Manuale Pratico di Chirurgia Composita*, *Il Libro degli Estranei*, *Tomo della
Stolidità Vermiglia*) are noted to **erode the reader's sanity** — a recurring
"forbidden-knowledge costs the mind" motif that pairs with the outer-god material.

## Materials `[CANON — wiki: Materiali]`

- **Umbra** — a rare and peculiar substance **not native to Ymir**: it comes from
  **another planet**, brought here in small quantities before the **magical collapse**
  by the **Notturni** (Nocturnals), who once travelled between their world and Ymir.
  Worked Umbra takes whatever form is desired — rigid, soft, even shifting from liquid
  to adamant hardness — and **drinks light**, so it is a deep, "living" black that seems
  to flame with dark tendrils. It makes superb weapons, cloaks, and armour (peerless for
  stealth), but it is **richly elemental** and **corrupts** those long in contact with it
  (the corruption of **Darkness**, the absence of Light) — less pervasively than Demonic
  Steel, but dangerous still. A single kilogram fetches some **10,000 gold** on Ymir's
  magical market. `[INFERRED]` The **Notturni** and their "magical collapse" are otherwise
  unattested in canon — likely another people/event lost to La Catastrofe. → `peoples.md`,
  Open questions.
- **Guscio di Yarhim** ("Yarhim shell") — the bluish carapace of the strange **Yarhim**
  (colossal Maelstrom-mutated crabs; → `bestiary.md`), prized because it **absorbs magic**.
  It makes fine shields that ward against spells, and ground to powder is sought as an
  alchemical component. `[INFERRED]` This is really a **creature-product**; the Yarhim
  themselves live in `bestiary.md`.

## Metals & stones `[CANON — wiki: Metalli e Pietre]`

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Metalli e Pietre)* Ymir's enchanted metals and gems are as storied as
its weapons — several are the reason certain weapons matter at all.

- **Shald** — a **violet magical glass**, born when common volcanic glass is bathed in
  strong magical energies. Strong, but prized above all as a **receptacle of Essence**: a
  fragment the size of a thumbnail holds a charge of Essence a channeler can draw into
  themselves, and the crystal **recharges itself** by drinking Essence from its
  surroundings (faster where magic runs strong). Effectively **priceless** and coveted by
  every mage. → `magic.md` (Essenza/Essence).
- **Acciaio Demoniaco** ("Demonic Steel") — a metal the colour of **clotted blood**,
  monstrously heavy (~250% of iron) and all but **indestructible**. Scholars judge it an
  **alloy whose every component is from outside our dimension** — reckoned literally
  "forged in Hell" — and it radiates a faint but constant **evil aura**. Demons wield it
  with ease; mortals find its weapons unwieldy, and prolonged contact with its armour
  breeds **mutation, madness, or outright demonic possession**. The material of the
  demon-forged triad, Hellion, and Oblivion (above). → `bestiary.md` (demons).
- **Acciaio Onirico** ("Dream-Steel") — a metal of shifting colours mined **only in the
  depths beneath the Dreamlands** (Terra dei Sogni). Fiendishly hard to work, its
  properties vary with where it was dug — but one is constant: an object of Dream-Steel is
  as strong or as weak as its wielder's **conviction**. Believe wholly in the deed and it
  serves magnificently; doubt, and it fails you. It alloys with **Shald, Eog, Mithril, and
  common steel**. `[INFERRED]` Together with the *Silver Key*, the *Green Lotus*, and the
  Dreamlands themselves, this points to a **plane of dreams** not yet mapped in `cosmology.md`.
- **Lagorak** — an **electric-blue** metal from an outer dimension built on elements
  unlike Ymir's. Some, misreading its origin, deem it as ill-fated as Demonic Steel; in
  truth it is merely **alien**. Pure Lagorak shears iron and steel like cardboard, yet
  struggles against organic matter such as leather (and, oddly, offers little advantage
  against living skin). Alchemists have made a greenish **Mithril–Lagorak alloy** that
  keeps Mithril's virtues but loses Lagorak's — and is **savagely poisonous** to living
  things.
- **Orihalcum** — a rare, copper-like metal, harder than copper and strangely **cold to
  the touch**, with an astonishing gift: it is **wholly immune to magic** and **nullifies
  any spell it meets**. No divination sees into an Orihalcum box; no spell scratches it.
  Weapons of Orihalcum ignore an enemy's magical defences (force-walls, elementals);
  armour of it can render its wearer effectively **untouchable by magic**.
- **Thalaras** — a bluish, iridescent **stone** (its sheen like the inside of a shell)
  found only in deep coastal or undersea seams, which makes it hard to mine. A superb
  **conductor of magic** that, in quantity, seems to **amplify** the power passed through
  it: wands of Thalaras strengthen direct spells, objects of it enchant far faster than
  normal, builders set it into **automata and constructs**, and priests carve Thalaras
  sigils to channel divine force. → `magic.md`.
- **Actorios** — a dark-red gem with a faintly glowing, pulsing core. It **does not occur
  in nature**: it is a **refined form of Shald**, sought by summoners and conjurers of
  every stripe because it powerfully **aids the rites of Summoning** (more gems help more).
- **Vetro Abissale** ("Abyssal Glass") — a very strong grey-blue **magical resin from
  another plane**, used to arm and armour some **demons and extradimensional creatures**.
  Unlike Demonic Steel it is **manageable even for common mortals**, and armour made of it
  is **impervious to elemental attacks of every kind**.

`[CANON]` Named in passing elsewhere are further exotic metals: **Eog** (from which the
sealed containers of elemental blood are forged), **Galvorn** (the Sword of Valdis),
**Adamantio**, **Ebony**, **Alto Acciaio** / magical **Acciaio Nanesco** (dwarven steel),
**Bulusture** (metal corrupted by elemental Water), **Mithril**, and the magical **Krypton**
that protects the Forgotten Scrolls.

## Herbs & substances `[CANON — wiki: Erbe]`

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Erbe)* Ymir's south and its deserts yield potent drugs and poisons; the
**Loto** (Lotus) family is the most storied.

| Substance | Origin | Effect / lore |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| **Loto Nero** ("Black Lotus") | Desert wastes; among the rarest drugs of Ymir | Its powdered petals grant enormous **sensitivity to magic** — but each dose permanently costs the user Reasoning and Self-Control. Fiercely addictive. |
| **Loto Verde** ("Green Lotus") | The **Grande Erg** (Great Erg) | Opens the mind to **visions of past and future**, sending the soul into the **Realm of Dreams**; officially banned in many realms, for it can end in irreversible coma or death. |
| **Loto Rosso** ("Red Lotus") | (flowers like the Black Lotus but red) | A powerful **poison** — tremors and hallucinations, permanent blindness, or coma. |
| **Aljira** | Distilled from **Black Lotus nectar** | A bluish liquid, rare and coveted by mages, that instantly **restores magical power** — but repeated use in a day risks **draining** it instead. Addictive. |
| **Lacrime di Davedars** ("Tears of Davedars") | White sap of the aloe-like *Davedars* | A **poison used chiefly by the Cacciamaghi** (Mage-Hunters) that **saps a target's magic**. → `factions.md`. |
| **Radice di Qarando** ("Qarando Root") | Fern-like plant of the deep jungle | Chewed, it brings **euphoria** and resistance to being stunned, but makes the user reckless and rash. Addictive. |
| **Distillato di Utombe** ("Utombe Distillate") | A jungle plant | A powerful **analgesic** that erases all pain for about an hour (so the numbed user may worsen unnoticed wounds); in large doses a paralysing, killing **nerve-poison**. |
| **Essenza di Ashala** ("Essence of Ashala") | A perfume of the **Qi-Long** | A sought-after scent that aids **seduction**. |

`[INFERRED]` Qarando and Utombe are the same Cartosan jungle drugs flagged in
`bestiary.md`'s open questions; the herb roster belongs partly to an **ecology/appendix**
alongside the southern flora.

## Miscellaneous wonders `[CANON — wiki: Oggetti Vari]`

### The Gate of Despair and its ritual apparatus

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Oggetti Vari)* The **Porta della Disperazione** ("Gate of Despair") is
the **one ancient portal whose function might still be restored** — at ruinous cost and
peril. To wake it, a forgotten ritual (described in *Il Risveglio degli Dèi*, two of the
Forgotten Scrolls) must be worked, drawing on a set of relics that together read as a
**cosmological plot device**:

- **Pietre-Chiave** ("Key-Stones") — seven river-smoothed pebbles of indefinite colour,
  ancient **dwarven artefacts** with runes floating inside them; slotted into seven of the
  Gate's thousand niches, they lift the **first of three Seals**.
- **Sangue Elementale** ("Elemental Blood") — **six seamless containers of Eog**, each
  holding a few centilitres of the blood of one of the **Six Elemental Lords** —
  **Hudemia** (Water), **Heis** (Air), **Diis** (Fire), **Therìos** (Earth), **Dingir**
  (Light), **Varshnak** (Ice) — **drawn from the days when they were still mortal**, and
  said to have been used in the **ritual that caused the Cataclysm**. A circle traced in
  all six bloods lifts the second Seal. `[CONFLICT]` This states the Six Elemental Lords
  were **once mortal**; `pantheon.md`/`cosmology.md` hold the Six Divine Twins were **born
  of the contrast between Mithra and Ahriman**. → `NOTES.md`.
- **Sangue di Topramesk** ("Blood of Topramesk") — a like Eog vessel of the blood of
  **Topramesk the Yellow**, likewise tied to the Cataclysm ritual. → `characters.md`,
  `history.md`.
- **Chiave del Nulla** ("Key of Nothing") — a white cube of gold-and-silver joinery whose
  use as a key demands absurd feats of astronomy, mathematics, and planar lore; it opens
  the **third and final Seal**.

`[INFERRED]` This apparatus — Forgotten Scrolls, the mortal-era blood of the Six Lords and
of Topramesk, the dwarven Key-Stones — is really a set of **cosmology/history relics**:
the physical residue of **La Catastrofe** and a live doomsday device. It belongs as much to
`cosmology.md` and `history.md` as to any item list.

### Relics of the dark

- **Corona della Stregoneria** ("Crown of Sorcery") `[CANON]` — an artefact of the Evil
  made with the power of **three malign deities**, of **legendary intelligence and will**,
  wholly bent on **dragging the whole world into darkness**. Legend warns that should it
  reappear, "the cities will tremble and perennial night will fall." It grants
  overwhelming command over the living and the dead. The single most dangerous relic on
  these pages.
- **Sudario della Pestilenza** ("Shroud of Pestilence") `[CANON]` — a tattered burial
  shroud stained with clotted blood, its clasp a golden skull; its wearer becomes a
  **healthy carrier of a terrible contagious plague** and can loose Pestilence at will.
- **Elisir della Servitù Oscura** ("Elixir of Dark Servitude") `[CANON]` — a night-black
  liquid kept in obsidian; whoever drinks it may be bound to **serve and obey evil
  creatures**, gaining darkvision but shunning the sun.
- **Anello della Sete Eterna** ("Ring of Eternal Thirst") `[CANON]` — a golden bat-shaped
  ring with a glittering ruby in its jaws; a boon for evil channelers, but under a curse
  that **turns any wearer into a Vampire within a month**.
- **Anello dei Cattivi Pensieri** ("Ring of Evil Thoughts") `[CANON]` — worn by the evil
  inquisitors of the **Mano Nera** (Black Hand); it fuels evil Mentalism. → `factions.md`.
- **Borsa delle Ossa** ("Bag of Bones") `[CANON]` — a bag of frayed black cloth of small
  bones that, cast on the ground, raise **fifty lesser skeletons** at the caster's command.
- **Corona d'Ossa** ("Crown of Bones") `[CANON]` — a circlet cut from an **elf's skull**,
  lending a spectral aspect and extending control over the undead.
- **Teschio Cornuto** ("Horned Skull") · **Stendardo Grottesco** ("Grotesque Standard") ·
  **Monile dell'Occhio di Tenebra** ("Necklace of the Dark Eye") `[CANON]` — a fear-radiating
  goblin skull, a battle-standard of human bone dipped in **elf-blood** that heartens the
  Legions of Evil and cows the good, and a black eye-stone pendant for evil mentalists.

### Curiosities, charms & wonders

- **Forgia Speculare** ("Mirror Forge") `[CANON]` — an iron cube with one face pierced by
  a hole of impenetrable darkness; reach in and draw out a **magical weapon of the type you
  wield best**, which grows with its bearer. Break the box (no easy feat) and it is found
  empty and powerless.
- **Ragno di Ferro** ("Iron Spider") `[CANON]` — an iron spider-figurine that, thrown down
  with the right words, becomes a **Giant Spider** servant (with a small chance of turning
  on its owner).
- **Amuleto Ottico** ("Optic Amulet") `[CANON]` — an iridescent eye-sized stone on a gold
  chain that wards against gaze-attacks; set into an **empty eye-socket** it serves as a
  true eye, granting darksight and magical sight.
- **Mantonero** ("Blackmantle") `[CANON]` — not truly a cloak but a **living, semi-intelligent
  symbiote** that instinctively protects and warms its bearer. `[INFERRED]` A living garment —
  arguably a creature (→ `bestiary.md`).
- **Corno da Guerra** ("War Horn") `[CANON]` — carved from a **dragon's horn**, it rallies
  troops and steadies allies against fear. `[INFERRED]` A dragon-product (→ `bestiary.md`).
- **Luna Cacciatrice** ("Huntress Moon") `[CANON]` — a silver pendant of a wolf howling at
  the moon; on moonless nights it summons **Night Hounds** to complete one task.
- **Anello della Medicina** ("Ring of Medicine") · **Possente Amuleto ... di Taros** ·
  **Orecchini del Masochismo** ("Earrings of Masochism") `[CANON]` — a healers' ring, a
  golden amulet of beauty and persuasion once worn by great charmers (attributed to
  **Taros**), and self-burrowing earrings that trade a slice of the wearer's vitality for
  **immunity to pain**.
- **The luck-charms of Gwynder** `[CANON]` — **Gwynder** was a mighty archmage cursed with
  relentless **bad luck**, who forged a suite of talismans from lucky materials and ancient
  formulae to end his misfortune. It worked — until he **drowned in a bathing-tub**, caught
  without them. His gaudy, tasteless creations survive: the **Giacchetta Fortunata** (Lucky
  Jacket), the **Ciondolo Portafortuna** (Lucky Charm), the **Sciarpa della Buonasorte**
  (Scarf of Good Fortune), the **Stivaletti della Fortuna** (Boots of Luck), and the **Guanti
  del Buon Augurio** (Gloves of Good Omen) — each bending fortune the bearer's way.
- **Portentoso Anello del Baluginio** ("Wondrous Ring of the Glimmer") `[CANON]` — a plain
  gold ring attributed to the supreme archmage **Topramesk** ("the Yellow," "the Mad"), who
  ended the Demon War. A stone once sat in it but is now gone; the ring **glimmers with its
  own light even in the deepest magical darkness**, and its true powers are **unknown**. →
  `characters.md`, `history.md`.

### Open questions
- **[CONFLICT] The mortality of the Six Elemental Lords.** *Sangue Elementale* says the six
  (Hudemia, Heis, Diis, Therìos, Dingir, Varshnak) **were once mortal** and their blood fed
  the Cataclysm ritual; `pantheon.md`/`cosmology.md` say the **Six Divine Twins** were *born
  of the contrast between Mithra and Ahriman*. Were the elemental gods **apotheosised mortals**?
  → `NOTES.md`.
- **Two "Wars of the Waves"?** Frangionde, Bulusture, and the Armour of the Depths belong to a
  mythic, **elemental** War of the Waves (elemental blades, water-corrupted metal, great
  warriors). `history.md` records a mundane naval **War of the Waves** (Cartosa vs Meridian,
  2320–2350 d.M.). Same war, or two? Where is Frangionde's lost twin **Tessionde**?
- **The Dreamlands are unmapped.** Acciaio Onirico (mined beneath the Dreamlands), the *Green
  Lotus* (soul sent to the Realm of Dreams), and *La Chiave d'Argento* all imply a **plane of
  dreams** absent from `cosmology.md`'s taxonomy. Add it.
- **The Notturni and the "magical collapse."** Who were the **Notturni** who ferried Umbra from
  another world before the collapse — and is that collapse La Catastrofe or an earlier event? →
  `peoples.md`.
- **The Other Gods / outer-god axis.** *La Serpe Unificatrice* teaches the language of the
  **Altri Dèi**; *Il Libro degli Estranei* summons a **Natharl'Nacna**. These tie to
  Sguardo-sul-Nulla / Xhulhu (→ `pantheon.md` open question) — a possible third cosmological
  axis outside the Ech duality.
- **Unregistered makers & owners.** Many named artificers/figures appear only here: **Kellar**
  (dwarf armourer), **Tshot** (sorcerer), **Radamantis** (the first Nosferatu), **Droan**
  (dwarven hero), **Urkhramest the Devout**, **King Lorith**, **Hans Schlecht**, **Urikal**,
  **Gwynder**, **Taros**, the **Re Morto** (Dead King). Candidates for `characters.md`.
- **The three demon lieutenants.** The demon-forged triad implies three named lieutenants of
  the Demon-War host, each wielding one sister-weapon — none is named. → `bestiary.md`/`history.md`.
