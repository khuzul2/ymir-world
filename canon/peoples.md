> pp.3–19 (races); **Cartosa Guida** pp.6–7 (regional peoples); **elfi oscuri.doc**;
> **Gaerwath.doc** (not yet fully mined); and the **World of Ymir wiki**

## The "Pure Races" (Razze Pure) `[CANON]`

### Xebechani
Ancient, mysterious humanoids with **pale skin and some reptilian traits**, of
undoubtedly magical origin; arguably Ymir's most technologically advanced people.
Their true origins are lost — some hold them kin to the **Dragons or the Great
Serpents of antiquity**, but no one knows for certain *(wiki: Xebechani)*. Members
of other races often find them strangely **hypnotic**, likely an effect of the
enchanted aura about them. Key traits: **thermovision** (they always "see" ambient
heat, letting them move in the dark, but sudden temperature swings dazzle and stun
them); unusual **elasticity** (double-jointed, supple); a **passive/cold** affect —
even the liveliest Xebechano seems to watch the world from a great distance; and
they are **asexual** — they lack functioning sexual organs and do not breed naturally
*(wiki: Xebechani)*.

New Xebechani are grown by an ancient magical process in colossal organic machines
called **le Pozze** (the Pools) — one kept in the main temple of each great city,
built millennia ago with potent alchemical formulae. When a Xebechano dies, its
body is fed to a Pool, which absorbs its organic and spiritual particles to grow a
newborn; this rite is **la Rinascita** (the Rebirth), their only means of
reproduction. But the science behind the Pools is now half-forgotten — they can
neither fully understand nor repair the machines — so malfunctions grow common,
some Xebechani are stillborn or deformed, and the **shadow of extinction** hangs
over the whole race *(wiki: Xebechani)*.

Xebechani society is **rigidly hierarchical**. From birth every Xebechano bears
thin coloured scales on the back forming **cobra-spectacle patterns**; by their
colour and complexity, the race reads not only the newborn's **Caste** but its
social role and future occupation — most of a Xebechano's life is decided at birth.
Caste is fixed for life (though one may hope for Rebirth into a higher Caste). They
tend to treat other races **like children**, holding themselves intellectually
superior. They dress in light silks that leave shoulders and back bare, wear gold
jewellery set with feathers and gems, and don long hooded tunics when abroad
*(wiki: Xebechani)*. They live in great cities, usually deep in jungle or atop
inaccessible peaks, isolated from the world.

Each of the four **Castes** is effectively its own sub-race, differing in body and
mind; at the top sits the least-numerous Xattva, at the bottom the most-numerous
Xudra:
- **Xudra** — the **warrior/labourer** caste; soldiers and heavy labour. Massive
  and heavily scaled, the least human-looking Xebechani, resembling humanoid
  anacondas with small, elongated reptilian heads. Over **half** of all Xebechani;
  brown or dark-green scales (yellow belly), yellow vertical-pupil eyes; roughly
  190 cm, ~100 kg; lifespan 80–100 years. Their scales form a **natural armour**,
  and despite their bulk they are startlingly quick, often catching foes off guard.
  Deeply bound to their tasks and homeland, they travel rarely — usually only as
  escorts for a higher caste *(wiki: Xudra)*.
- **Xidhe** — the **merchant/diplomat** caste and the Xebechani most often met
  abroad; the very image most people hold of a Xebechano. Slender, milk-pale skin
  (coloured scales only on the back), long straight black hair, silver/green/violet
  vertical-pupil eyes; ~180 cm, ~65 kg; lifespan 250–300 years. Cultured and
  multilingual, the **least passive** of their kind, able to form real friendships
  across races — some even emigrate, chafing at Xebechani rigidity. Their gaze is
  **magnetic, near-hypnotic**, unnerving or entrancing those it falls on
  *(wiki: Xidhe)*.
- **Xion** — the **priestly/sage** caste; tend the gods and rites and serve the
  Xattva as scribes and counsellors. In principle they maintain the Pools, but with
  the science lost they merely guard them and assist Xattva experiments. Fragile,
  50–60 kg, 160–170 cm, white-yellowish skin, black hair, yellow/violet eyes;
  lifespan 250–300 years. They **sense ambient magical energies** and cast with
  unusual ease even when armoured *(wiki: Xion)*.
- **Xattva** — the ethereal **noble/mage** caste, "purest and noblest"; the
  Xebechani *par excellence* and barely 10% of the population. To them fall
  government, law and administration, though Xion scribes do most of the work and
  the Xattva reserve themselves for the gravest matters — mostly they pursue magical
  study and alchemy, seeking a cure for the reproduction crisis. Tall and slender,
  ~190 cm, 65–70 kg, near-white skin with soft scales (cobra-spectacle patterns on
  the back), black hair; lifespan 250–300 years. They sense magic like the Xion and
  can **channel and store magical energy** with great ease. They almost never leave
  their lands — those met abroad are political or religious **exiles**, and bear it
  with deep sorrow *(wiki: Xattva)*.

They worship their own pantheon — the **"Dei Esterni" (Outer Gods)**, chief among
them **Quetzalcoatl** — but their cult centres above all on **reverence for their
ancestors** ("gli Antichi," the Ancients, held to be keepers of great knowledge)
and on rites to secure Rebirth into a higher Caste. → `pantheon.md`

### Tanoth
A **bestial, feline-looking** humanoid race. The wiki recognises **two** sub-races,
the Ur-Tanoth and the Tal-Tanoth *(wiki: Tanoth)*:
- **Ur-Tanoth** — like a cross between a large predator cat and a man; some resemble
  panthers, others tigers, others lions, and a tribe's members generally look alike.
  Despite the fearsome look they are a **peaceful, shy** people, little given to
  violence: tall and massive (males ~200 cm/100 kg, females ~185 cm/80 kg), thick
  fur of any colour, yellow or green (rarely red) eyes; lifespan 60–70 years; thick
  hide and claws; hardy and cold-resistant. Above all they **dread captivity** — an
  imprisoned Tanoth sickens and dies — and shun enclosed places and cities, which
  they find noisy, stinking and claustrophobic. Their faith is **totemic/animistic**;
  they hate Orcs and Trolls, and make good warriors, barbarians, rangers and even
  thieves, but struggle with Essence and Mentalism magic *(wiki: Ur-Tanoth)*. They
  are nomad hunter communities who shun humans and civilisation; peerless
  woodworkers and gatherers of natural materials (healing herbs, furs, rarities).
- **Tal-Tanoth** — smaller and slighter, more like a cat-man crossbreed (males
  ~120 cm/45 kg, females ~115 cm/40 kg); fur any colour, blue/green/yellow eyes;
  lifespan 60–80 years; thick hide, resistant to poison, disease and cold, but must
  sleep at least eight hours a day. Unlike the Ur-Tanoth they **do not fear man** and
  often live in the cities *(wiki: Tal-Tanoth)*. Small folk who live cheerfully off
  humans in the cities — "a plague to some, a nuisance to most"; fickle and prankish,
  so the affection is not always returned. They think men big oafs and hate the
  orcs, but otherwise get on with most races; superb thieves and wanderers, they
  take up whatever cult they live among. They are the only ones able to deal with
  the shy Ur-Tanoth.
- **Tanoth (proper)** `[CONFLICT]` — a Player Guide branch, "of the semi-arid inland
  plains," listed here in the first pass. The wiki, however, recognises **only two**
  Tanoth sub-races (Ur- and Tal-Tanoth) and no distinct plains "Tanoth." Preserved
  pending reconciliation; see Open questions. → `NOTES.md`

### Nani (Dwarves)
Widespread across Ymir (especially **Letia**, the **Young Kingdoms/Regni Giovani**,
**Helgedad**, and the **Principato di Cartosa**), on broadly peaceful terms with most
Pure Races. After the Xebechani, the most technologically advanced race — masters of
mechanisms and steam engines and, above all, sole keepers of the secret of **Polvere
Nera** (Black Powder), which no other race has cracked; their smiths and engineers
are sought everywhere and their inventions worth their weight in gold *(wiki: Nani;
Player Guide: "identical to those in R&C")*. Lifespan 100–120 years. They bear a
special hatred for **dragons, goblins and all degenerate races**, are very suspicious
of the **Tal-Tanoth**, and cannot fathom the Ur-Tanoth or the Folletti though they
bear them no real ill will *(wiki: Nani)*. Branches/related peoples:
- **Nano Comune (Common Dwarf)** — the typical stock; main tongue **Nanico**.
- **Nani di Pianura (Plains Dwarves)** — not truly a separate race but descendants of
  dwarves who emigrated to human cities for work; purist dwarves eye them askance,
  and the Nanico word *Laak-nama-Khazalim* ("Plains-Dweller") is a mild insult
  *(wiki: Nani)*.
- **Arawak** — a dwarf people of the **Cartosan jungles**, with **dark skin and
  violet eyes**, who work **stone and bone, never metal**; they adorn their hair with
  gems to count their deeds and status; proud warriors who prefer death to capture.
  Their mountains hold **Shald**, a prized magical glass — expeditions after it
  rarely return. Their culture is Silvan-Barbarian; main tongue **Arawak**
  *(Cartosa; wiki: Nani)*.
- **Barbarian dwarf tribes** of the interior highlands — very territorial, hostile.

### Umani (Humans)
Found in every climate and region. By legend, humanity's ancestors were shaped from
**Fire, one of the two elemental energies** — the source, some say, of the frantic
pace and impulsiveness that mark human beings *(wiki: Umani - Primigeni)*.
→ `cosmology.md`, `pantheon.md`. Divided into:

- **Primigeni (Primeval)** `[CANON]` — the ancient progenitors of humankind
  *(resolves prior gap)*. Because Fire also holds the **principle of Destruction**,
  an evil god was able to corrupt the Primigeni, and over time most degenerated into
  servants of darkness, pale shadows of what they were; today only a **few
  true-blooded Primigeni** survive, descendants of those who kept their spirit pure.
  Lifespan 90–100 years. They **hate the degenerate races**, orcs above all, and
  openly **admire the mystic wisdom of the Xebechani** (this confirms the first-pass
  hint that the Primigeni are linked to Xebechani mysticism) *(wiki: Umani -
  Primigeni)*. Primigeno sub-peoples:
  - **Vorskgen** — barbarian tribes of frozen **Helgedad** and Letia's northern
    marches who keep the ancient spirit and Primigeno blood; main tongue Vorskgen.
  - **Ophiriti** — the human inhabitants of **Ophiur**, tall and massive with very
    black skin; most, and especially the aristocracy, are Primigeni; main tongue
    Ophirita. → `geography.md`
- **Uomini (Men)** — the descendants of Primigeni who were partly corrupted by Evil:
  weaker in body and spirit and more easily seduced by darkness, though far from all
  are corrupt and some keep a trace of the old Primigeno pride *(wiki: Umani -
  Uomini)*. Lifespan 70–90 years. Two grades:
  - **Uomini Misti (Mixed Men)** — the minority who still carry a **trace of
    Primigeno blood**; the stock most adventurers spring from. `[CONFLICT]` The first
    pass took "Mixed Men" for the cosmopolitan *majority* (e.g. most of Cartosa); the
    wiki instead makes them a minority, with the **Uomini Comuni (Common Men)** — the
    vast bulk of the population — as the true majority *(wiki: Umani - Uomini)*.
  - **Uomini Comuni (Common Men)** — the ordinary majority. Their prejudices vary
    entirely by culture and temperament.

### Sohleugir (Lizardmen)
True lords of the southern seas; an undersea kingdom millennia old, capital
**Ssas'Kher**, whose coral walls have never been breached. Equally at home on land
and in the water (their element of origin), they usually dwell on the coasts in
splendid **stone-and-coral cities built on many levels, above and below the
waterline**. They are **open-minded**, rarely swayed by prejudice, and renowned as
**exceptional merchants and formidable warriors**. Many believe their terrifying
appearance stems from an **ancient curse**; aware that it frightens other races, they
take care to cloak themselves in veils and wide hoods. They come to the surface (e.g.
to Cartosa) to trade pearls and coral, rarely for diplomacy. Lifespan 80–90 years;
they especially detest the **Gratar** and hate all corrupt races
*(Cartosa/Player Guide; wiki: Sohleugir)*.

### Folletti (the "Little People" / Piccolo Popolo)
The smallest Pure Race. Branches:
- **Fate (Fairies)** — who call their own kind the **Tylweth Teg**. Contrary to
  common belief the race has both sexes (males only slightly rarer than females);
  they look like miniature humans or elves and rarely top 90 cm, dwelling in tiny
  woodland towns away from the "Gente Alta" (Tall Folk). They are **not immortal**,
  living on average 150 years. They distrust the Tall Folk, struggle to understand
  dwarves, and hate all degenerate races — the **Coboldi (Kobolds)** most of all
  *(wiki: Fate)*.
- **Gnomi (Gnomes)** — a **separate race**, not (as often wrongly assumed) the males
  of the Fairies — a confusion that irritates both peoples. They live around 150
  years, hold no special prejudices, and prefer to be left in peace, even by their
  own kind *(wiki: Gnomi)*.
Their kingdom is in the **Isole d'Argento (Silver Isles)**, capital **Elendor** —
seat of the world **Council of Peoples** (the Seggio di Cristallo). → `factions.md`

### Elfi (Elves)
`[CANON]` The elves are **not native** to Ymir: they arrived during the Demon Wars,
at the free peoples' hour of greatest need, and sided with them. Their origin is a
secret they deflect all questions about; the divine **Niethel** was named in the
first pass, while the wiki's own account points to the dying world **Tchyo** (see
below) *(wiki: Elfi; Tchyo)*. Unlike the R&C manual's elves they are **not immortal**
here — whatever granted them immortality on their home plane does not reach them on
Ymir — though they are very long-lived (about **200 years** on average). They are
suspicious of all other races and hate the degenerate races *(wiki: Elfi)*.

About **800 years after the Catastrophe** that made the Maelstrom (~2200 years ago),
a long spell of infighting the historians call the **Anni della Discordia** (Years of
Discord) split them into **three factions** *(wiki: Elfi)*:
1. **Elfi Chiari (Light Elves)** `[CANON]` *(resolves prior "name pending" gap)* —
   the first faction; they held that the elven destiny was to withdraw into the
   newly-founded Sacred Kingdom (present-day **Alfheim**) and there await the
   **Ultimo Messaggero (Last Messenger)**, who will one day lead them back to their
   homeland.
2. **Elfi Oscuri (Dark Elves)** — held that all Ymir was the Sacred Kingdom promised
   the elves in prophecy and that every other race existed only to serve them,
   preaching a holy crusade of conquest. → detail below.
3. **Elfi Illuminati (Enlightened Elves)** — the moderates, called the **Rinuncianti
   ("Renouncers")** by the other two but "Illuminati" by themselves. They withdrew
   from the struggle to live among the other races in contemplation, convinced the
   Sacred Kingdom is no physical place but lies **within each elf**. Most elves met
   outside the elven realms belong to this faction *(wiki: Razze)*.

There is **no racial difference between the factions**. Cutting across them are
**three sub-races** of unknown origin, spread more or less evenly among all three
*(wiki: Elfi)*:
- **Elfi Alti (High Elves)** — the noblest and purest, versed in the arts and magic
  yet also fine warriors; the elite of elven society.
- **Elfi Silvani (Sylvan Elves)** — the great majority of Ymir's elves.
- **Elfi Grigi (Grey Elves)** — the **sea elves**: slightly rarer than the Sylvan,
  they love the sea and settle the coasts, and have the most dealings with other
  races.

`[CONFLICT]` The first pass listed "Elfi Grigi" as the third (moderate) *faction*.
The wiki corrects this: the moderate faction is the **Elfi Illuminati/Rinuncianti**,
while **Elfi Grigi is a sub-race** (the sea elves), cutting across every faction.
`[CONFLICT]` The Player Guide Glossary also seemed to distinguish "sea elves" from
"Grey Elves" (warning that R&C's elves match the sea elves, *not* the Grey Elves),
whereas the wiki equates the two ("gli elfi marini o Grigi"). See Open questions. →
`NOTES.md`

The High and Sylvan elves are the "light elves" of **Alfheim** (capital
**Ost-en-Galad**, "the Shining City"), ruled by a queen and consort with a Council.
*(Player Guide Glossary.)* → `geography.md`, `factions.md`

#### Elfi Oscuri (Dark Elves) — detail `[CANON: elfi oscuri.doc + Gaerwath.doc]`
Homeland the **Gaerwath**. They sort all beings into three categories:
- **Elar** — elves recognised as true elves.
- **Nemyel** — elves *not* recognised; pariahs/outcasts, often deformed or mad.
- **Kelvar** — "everything else that lives": animals, monsters (a dragon, a
  manticore), and every non-elf humanoid race.
Children (a special class, the **Hyrul**) undergo a **trial** testing spirit and
magical affinity; passing makes an Elar (and may raise one's **Nath**, a rank/caste),
failing makes a Nemyel — often with terrible bodily/mental cost. Especially after
**la Marea** and the **Risonanza Magica** (Magic Resonance) cut them off further,
the Dark Elves specialised in summoning and binding extraplanar entities called
**Valrin** ("Forces" — mostly demons), used even as currency. They prize **Arvo**,
pulverised magical **Essence**, gathered by their demon-servants or by forced Nemyel.

## Mixed, hybrid & "impure" peoples `[CANON]`

### Mezze Razze (Half-Races)
Not true races but the offspring of crosses between different peoples *(wiki: Mezze
Razze)*. Each is listed with its technical name, its common (often derogatory) name
in parentheses, and whether the line is **[S] sterile** or **[F] fertile**:
- Human + Elf → **Yafysh** (Mezzelfo / half-elf) `[S]`
- Human + Orc → **Ghzaak** (Mezzorco / half-orc) `[F]`
- Human + Dwarf → **Gurbad** (Mezzo-Nano / half-dwarf) `[S]`
- Human + Rahab → **Uomo-Pesce** (Fish-Man) `[S]`
- Human + Tanoth → **An-Tanoth** (half-Tanoth) `[S]`
- Human + Fata → **Changeling** `[S]`
- Tanoth + Rahab → **Sibert** `[S]`
- Orc + Elf → **Ghynr** ("l'Immondo," the Foul) `[S]`
- Orc + Tanoth → **Gha-Tanoth** (Orc-Tanoth) `[F]`
- Goblin + Dwarf → **Dwergar** (Nano Immondo / Foul Dwarf) `[F]`
- Goblin + Orc → **Hobgoblin** `[F]`
- Goblin + Tal-Tanoth → **Lash-Tanoth** `[S]`
- Coboldo + Fata → **Gremlin** `[S]`
- Coboldo + Gnomo → **Pishacha** (Gnomo Famelico / Ravenous Gnome) `[S]`
- Troll + Tanoth → **Ugra-Tanoth** (Tanoth Oscuro / Dark Tanoth) `[F]`
- Sohleugir + Gratar → **Kappa** `[F]`

How obvious a half-blood's ancestry is varies by individual; not everyone is
broad-minded enough to accept mixed blood without prejudice *(wiki: Razze)*.

### Ibridi (Hybrids) — the peoples aspect
Creatures of the Maelstrom, mutated by magic; the category spans part-human,
part-animal beings and creatures carrying the **seed of an element** (as some giants
do). By tradition the **centaurs** (of every kind) are counted Ibridi too, though they
are no product of magical mutation. As *peoples*, players may take a Hybrid race with
the Game Master's blessing, mindful that their unusual look causes friction in social
dealings *(wiki: Ibridi)*. Recognised Hybrid peoples:
- **Centauri (Centaurs)**
- **Satiri (Satyrs)**
- **Rahab** — the fish-folk (their blood also yields the "Uomo-Pesce" and "Sibert"
  half-races above).
- **Tritoni (Tritons)**
- **Giganti (Giants)** — including the element-seeded kind.
The Ibridi also have a monstrous/creature aspect. → `bestiary.md`, `cosmology.md`

### Razze Degenerate (Degenerate Races)
All the races generally deemed malign or **servants of the Demoni Sovrani (Sovereign
Demons)** *(wiki: Razze Degenerate)*. They are feared and hated by every other race —
often killed on sight, and few think it a crime to slay an orc and take its goods
*(wiki: Razze)*. Not advisable as player peoples save in an outright evil campaign.
The category comprises:
- **Orchetti / Orchi (Orcs)**
- **Goblin**
- **Coboldi (Kobolds)**
- **Troll**
- **Vulfen**
- **Gratar**
→ `cosmology.md`, `bestiary.md`, `pantheon.md`

### Maelstrom-touched individuals
- **Marchiati (the Marked)** — individuals mutated in the womb by the Maelstrom;
  bear deformities but sometimes rare magical gifts; usually killed young. →
  `cosmology.md`

## Regional peoples of the Cartosan south `[CANON: Cartosa Guida pp.6–7]`

- **Nkishi (Wild Orcs)** — ferociously brave; scar themselves from childhood by
  letting beasts bite them. Shamans (**Bokkor**) serve ancestor-spirits and the dark
  forest gods (**Orisha**), the fiercest of which are the **Mayombe**.
- **M'Bantha (Goblins)** — jungle-edge raiders; believe humans hold magical power
  ("**Cargo**") in their everyday objects, so they steal objects to steal the power;
  deck themselves in trinkets made from human goods. Cunning ambushers, *not* stupid.
- Orcs, half-orcs, and goblins also crew the worst pirate ships; they war constantly
  with the inland dwarves. (These are specific instances of the **Razze Degenerate**
  above.)

## Peoples of other lands & worlds `[CANON]`

### Popoli Neri (Black Peoples) of Ophiur
The human peoples of the **Ophiur** continent — a mostly wild, unexplored land of
dense jungle (population ~400 million). They live divided into **tribes** in jungle
villages, some peaceful, some warlike, with widely varying customs; the coastal and
border towns (e.g. **Zabhula**, **Gazal**) are fixed stops for merchants and
adventurers. All the tribes hold the **deep interior taboo**, where legend places
fantastic cities of gold and jewels — and vampires and foul demons *(wiki: Ophiur)*.
Their aristocracy is largely of **Primigeno** stock (see the **Ophiriti** above). →
`geography.md`

### Zohàriti
Adherents of the cult of the **Zohàr** (*Splendour / Perfection*), also called
**Zohàrismo**. It is guided by the **Four Great Blessed Trees** — **Atzilùt, Beriàh,
Yetziràh** and **Asiyàh** — held to be the cult's four great prophets, incarnate as
luminescent trees that speak the Zohàr's will to the high priests. **"Lo Zohàr"** is
a staged path toward a higher spiritual state ("Perfection"), triggered by contact
with the Trees' **sacred spores** and advanced by graded draughts of their **sap**,
administered to a worthy believer at a sacred rite of passage *(wiki: Zohàriti)*.
More a faith than a race; → `pantheon.md`

### Vlatch `[CANON — contested]`
Not clearly a people at all: **Vlatch** is a term some arcanists use for mysterious
**entities**, an **etheric phenomenon**, *and* an artefact (or artefacts). Three
accounts survive *(wiki: Vlatch)*:
- Some ancient texts say the Vlatch will be **made in the far future by the
  Xebechani** to repel one last great Demonic Invasion — beings infused with
  **Cronomanzia (Chronomancy)**, the supreme art of manipulating time, who in
  pursuing their purpose will spawn **temporal paradoxes that rewrite Ymir's
  history**.
- Other scholars call the Vlatch a rare, inexplicable **parasitic energy** that arises
  amid very high concentrations of Essence, feeding on ambient mana and supplanting
  it — most arcanists dismiss this as fantasy.
- Heretical manuscripts in the deep crypts of the **Alto Clericato di Solanthya**
  describe the Vlatch as one or more **machines** — of the age of the **Adepti** or
  from a possible far future — able to show past and future events; none has ever
  been found.
→ `cosmology.md`, `magic.md`

### Tchyo — the Dying World
Another world entirely, and the elves' **apparent homeworld** *(wiki: Tchyo)*. On
Tchyo the elves were **created** and rose as "the Ancients"; later the Ancients
opened the Gates to the **Adepti** (who reigned some **50,000 years**, as on Ymir),
and through the portals came the **Razze Esterne (Outer Races)** — Dwarves, Men and
the **Mawgrin** (a people otherwise unknown on Ymir). In an age called **La Caccia
(The Hunt)** the elves were hunted by the other races and **fled to Ymir** shortly
before **La Scomparsa (The Disappearance)**, when the High Lord left Tchyo forever
and the portals died. The ensuing **Guerre Millenarie (Millennial Wars)** burned for
some three thousand years, until they guttered out for want of survivors; today Tchyo
is a ruin of battlefields and **ninety-two isolated city-states** amid vast, hostile
wastes. This is the "homeland" the **Elfi Chiari** await the Last Messenger to lead
them back to. → `cosmology.md`, `history.md`, `geography.md`
