# Bestiary

> Creatures of Ymir, and the roster of the Sovereign Demons. Sources: **Player
> Guide** (demons, dragons, extraplanar entities), **Cartosa Guida** (southern
> fauna), and the **World of Ymir wiki** pages *Creature*, *Abominii*, *Draghi*,
> *Ibridi*, *Entità* (creatures & monsters). Extraplanar *categories* (Spirits,
> Elementals, Djinn, Angels, Demons and their six Circles) are defined in
> `cosmology.md`; this file catalogues actual creatures and monsters.
>
> Rolemaster stats in the sources (levels, criticals, hit points, attack numbers)
> are deliberately stripped here — entries describe what a creature *is* and *does*,
> its danger and its lore, not the numbers.

## The Sovereign Demons (Demoni Sovrani) `[CANON — Player Guide pp.24–25; wiki: Entità]`

The mightiest demons, near minor-deity in power, "**Beyond the Circles**," most
trusted vassals of Ahriman; their one aim is Ymir's destruction (the wiki frames it
as "the corruption of Creation"). Like all demons they are immortal and cannot
manifest unless summoned by ruinously complex ritual; they are destroyed only by
finding and unmaking their true essence (not merely killing the body). "Do not be
deceived — even the least of the Sovereign Demons wields power beyond a mortal's
imagining." *(wiki: Entità)* Listed in descending hierarchy of power:

| Sovereign Demon | Title | Symbol |
|-----------------|-------|--------|
| **Asmodeus** | Lord of Tyranny | black dragon |
| **Astharoth** | Lord of Massacre | inverted pentacle |
| **Xardas** (the Puppeteer) | Lord of Plots | spiderweb |
| **Belial** | Lord of Perfidy | white two-headed serpent |
| **Behemoth** | Lord of Perverse Nature | satyr |
| **Azazel** | Lord of Damnation | seven-fingered hand |
| **Abraxas** | Lord of Forbidden Magic | black key |
| **Moloch** | Lord of Sacrifice | bull's skull |
| **Arioch** | Lord of Darkness | eight-pointed star |
| **Masak** | Lord of Illusions | mask |
| **Alastor** | Lord of Twilight | black crown |
| **Abigor** | Lord of Battlefields | trident |
| **Ismael** | Lord of Avarice | coin |
| **Adramelek** | Lord of Lies | peacock |
| **Nergall** | Lord of Assassination | dagger |
| **Netunshiel** | Lord of Crypts | jackal |
| **Gaziel** | Lord of Wrath | bear |
| **Ea** † | Lord of the Abysses | kraken | — **DEAD** (destroyed 1570 d.M.) |
| **Saxul** † | Lord of All Fevers & Pestilence | (none) | — **DEAD** (destroyed 2563 d.M.) |

`[INFERRED]` These names draw on real-world demonology (Asmodeus, Belial, Moloch,
Azazel…) — a deliberate familiar-but-reordered infernal hierarchy.

**Reconciliation with the wiki roster** *(wiki: Entità)* — the wiki lists the same
Sovereigns but diverges from the table above on several points; treat each as
`[CONFLICT]` pending confirmation:

- **Abbadon, Lord of Torment** (symbol: **inverted trident**; marked *deceased*) is a
  Sovereign Demon on the wiki that is **absent** from the table above. Its
  inverted-trident symbol is the very one the table assigns to **Abigor**, which
  strongly suggests the two were **conflated** when this roster was first extracted.
  `[INFERRED]`
- **Abigor**'s wiki symbol is an **axe** (not a trident), and the wiki marks him
  **deceased**. `[CONFLICT]`
- **Ea**'s wiki symbol is an **oyster** (*Ostrica*), not a kraken — corroborated by
  the *Ombra dell'Ostrica Blu* (Blue Oyster Shadow) below, the ghosts of Ea's failed
  summoners. `[CONFLICT]`
- The wiki additionally marks **Adramelek** and **Netunshiel** as **deceased**; the
  table above leaves both living. `[CONFLICT]`
- **Gaziel, Lord of Wrath** (bear) appears in the table above but **not** on the wiki
  roster. `[CONFLICT]`
- Belial's serpent is left uncoloured on the wiki (the table's "white" is an extra
  detail from the Player Guide). Minor.

Net effect: both sources describe **19 living-or-recently-slain** Sovereigns, but the
sets differ (the Player Guide has Gaziel and not Abbadon; the wiki the reverse), and
the wiki counts more of them dead. See `NOTES.md`.

## Dragons & Dragon-Gods `[CANON]`

- **Grandi Vermi (Great Worms) / Draghi (Dragons)** — appear at the world's dawn,
  when Ymir is first "peopled with life." Also named the mightiest of Hybrid
  **Elementals** (→ `cosmology.md`). The ancient dragon **Gwalgamaur** wages the
  Great War of 3025 (he is the golden Fire Dragon of Wald, below).
- **Dei-Drago (Dragon-Gods)** — appeared in the Qi-Long when the Mah-Dhol empire fell
  (1900–1920 d.M.). `[INFERRED]` These are almost certainly the six elemental Qi-Long
  members of the Twenty True Dragons below (Fire, Water, Earth, Ice, Spectral, Light),
  which the *Draghi* page singles out as the clearest example of Dragons that turned
  from their appointed mission. Fuller nature still to be mined from **Qi-Long.docx**.

### The Twenty True Dragons (Veri Draghi) `[CANON — wiki: Draghi]`

Ymir holds a fixed number of **"True Dragons"** — major dragons whose nature is bound
to the elements. There are exactly **twenty**. They were **created by the Adepti as
the ultimate biological weapon**: mighty living fortresses able to overcome almost any
foe. Before the Adepti mysteriously vanished, they set their Dragons to **guard the
secrets of Ymir**, with one charge — to keep the planet's inhabitants from drawing too
near the **Truth**, or evolving far enough to rival the Adepti's own techno-magical
level. Over the millennia few Dragons stayed faithful; most began pursuing agendas of
their own (the Dei-Drago of the Qi-Long are the plainest example). *(wiki: Draghi)*

The two framings can perhaps be reconciled (the Adepti binding elemental power into
living weapons), but the origin and dating of dragons remain genuinely tangled — see
Open questions and `NOTES.md`.

## Demons & infernal horrors made flesh `[CANON — wiki: Creature]`

When demons manifest, their corrupt essence forces a horrible, distorted shape (→
`cosmology.md`). A few recur as fieldable monsters:

- **Phlegetauro** — a rare kind of **major demon** from dark outer planes of fiery
  atmosphere; also met on Ymir underground or near volcanoes. Coal-black humanoids
  over three-and-a-half metres tall, with **three horned heads** on serpentine necks,
  wreathed in undying flames that scorch anything close. Each of the three mouths can
  breathe cones of fire, and they command the magic of Fire Law. Slain, a Phlegetauro
  **detonates in a great fireball**. Immune to fire and heat, but water and cold wound
  them doubly — cold is outright lethal to them. *(wiki: Creature)*
- **Ombra dell'Ostrica Blu (Blue Oyster Shadow)** — the spectral remains of the mad
  cultists who tried to summon the Sovereign Demon **Ea** onto Ymir and perished when
  the ritual failed catastrophically. `[INFERRED]` This ties Ea's true symbol to the
  **oyster**, not the kraken (see the roster reconciliation above). Two kinds:
  - *Minor* — evanescent skulls giving off a faint bluish glow, floating in mid-air;
    they cannot be touched by non-magical weapons and drain the life and vitality of
    every living thing nearby.
  - *Major* — the ghosts of the more powerful cultists: faceless, hooded figures in
    long blue robes wrapped in a pale azure aura. Beyond draining more life and
    constitution, they still wield the sorcerous **Dark Essence** spell lists they knew
    in life. Being ethereal, only magic weapons harm them. *(wiki: Creature)*

## Abominii & Chaos-spawn `[CANON — wiki: Abominii, Creature]`

**Abominii (Abominations)** are creations of magic — races built in the laboratory by
powerful, unscrupulous mages — or beings thought to hail from other planets or
dimensions who have settled on Ymir (some scholars go so far as to call the Elves
Abominii). A player may only take an Abominio character with the Game Master's
consent. *(wiki: Abominii)*

- **Notturni** — a mysterious, furtive race few have even heard of, seeming made
  wholly of **solidified shadow**: ill-defined outlines and luminous green eyes. They
  act mostly by night or out of direct sun (which bothers but does not harm them) and
  can **control the shadows** at will. Their purpose and origin are unknown; the few
  who have communed with them say they come from a place they call simply "**Night**"
  or "**Shadow**" (whether a physical place, a plane, or a kind of mother-entity is
  unclear) and are on Ymir to perform a "**Work**" or "**Task**" they will not — or
  cannot — describe. They appear **hive-minded**: individuals seem to struggle to tell
  themselves apart from one another, and even from "Night" itself, when they speak.
  *(wiki: Abominii)*
- **Omungus** — worm-like humanoids: two short tentacles for arms, no legs (they slither
  on their tail), a large drooling mouth, and no eyes — but preternaturally sharp other
  senses. Highly intelligent and strongly drawn to **Mentalism** (most know several
  spell lists). Two traits set them apart: they **perceive the Essence** and its every
  flow, "seeing" the world around them — living from inert, active or latent magic,
  even invisible beings — and they **feed on others' arcane energy**, draining a
  target's magical power and absorbing part of it. Extremely rare, dwelling only in
  deep underground complexes; they claim to come from the planet **Omalgat**, which is
  why they are classed as Abominii. *(wiki: Abominii)*
- **Zortos** — Abominii made by the **Manti Azzurri (Blue Mantles)**, the mages' guild
  of the Principality of Cartosa, by crossing goblins with other creatures and
  corrupting the result with elemental **water and air** through a secret alchemical
  rite. Small fox-headed humanoids (~150 cm) in blue-silver fur that always looks wet;
  small claws and sharp fangs inject a circulatory venom, they are keen scent-trackers,
  see by day and night with infravision in total darkness, and are tireless swimmers
  who breathe underwater as easily as in air. As **hybrid elemental** creatures they
  shrug off air and water as mere physical impact and ignore earth and ice, but take
  double, slaying harm from **fire and light**. Silent movers and superb hunters — the
  Blue Mantles bred them to hunt dangerous criminals and renegade mages; a trained few
  know a spell list or two (usually Lesser Air/Water Mastery). → `peoples.md` (Cartosa,
  Manti Azzurri), `cosmology.md` (Hybrid Elementals). *(wiki: Abominii)*
- **Magres** — an **abomination of Chaos**: a towering (~7 m) mass of grey, hairy,
  scaly flesh, with a hugely oversized mouth of thousands of small sharp teeth, tiny
  piggy eyes, two pathetically small useless hands flapping at mid-body, and dozens of
  little feet along its underside for locomotion. Scholars theorise Magres are
  **kobolds radically mutated by prolonged exposure to Chaos** (unverified). Mercifully
  few, they are stupid and all but oblivious to the world, and above all **cowardly** —
  they flee at the first wound or the sight of anything human-sized or larger, and in
  fleeing **trample everything in their path**, sowing terror and destruction. *(wiki:
  Creature)* `[INFERRED]` "Chaos" here reads as the Maelstrom's mutating energy (→
  `cosmology.md`), aligning Magres with the Marchiati and other mutant life.

## Constructs & the restless dead `[CANON — wiki: Creature]`

- **Relitto (Wreck)** — possessed constructs: rusted, oil-bleeding hollow shells of
  ancient war machines that wealthier empires had master alchemists forge into
  tireless iron-and-steel warriors, then abandoned on old battlefields among the dead.
  Built only to kill, they leave an **echo of death** even after their bodies are
  downed and their animating spells dispelled — an echo that draws the spirits of
  warriors slain in battle, who possess the shell to wage war again, this time on all
  the living. Each Relitto looks unique (mirroring its original build; some are welded
  from several constructs by necromantic energy) and wields varied weapons, but all
  share the **cold aura of the undead**: their blows bite with added frost, and every
  living thing nearby loses vitality round by round — a soul drained to nothing is
  **sucked into the Wreck**, strengthening it. Those near a Relitto hear whispers,
  curses and stifled laments — the trapped souls mourning — and may be gripped by
  crippling despair. *(wiki: Creature)*

## Beasts & predators `[CANON — wiki: Creature]`

- **Fungalidi** — bizarre **fungoid** beings, vaguely like giant mouthless toads,
  sheathed in a strange spongy greyish substance. In place of legs they have two long
  tentacles ending in sharp bony spurs, and they bear sturdy **dragonfly wings**. Their
  fungous skin secretes a potent **acid** (so their strikes burn), their claws inject a
  powerful **paralytic venom**, they **regenerate** their wounds continuously until
  slain, and they shrug off harm from void, nether, cutting and acid — hard creatures
  to put down. *(wiki: Creature)*
- **Slashquark** — big **green pigs** with wrinkled necks and sucker-like mouths set
  with two rows of fangs. They strike by snapping the neck out (up to three metres) to
  seize prey; once the fangs have hold, the second row snaps backward, **stripping the
  flesh from the bone**. They are said to be named for the sound their victims make
  during this. *(wiki: Creature)*
- **Zaar'Qrak** — "**Death Render**" in the tongue of the Erg; the most feared
  predators of the desert. Vaguely humanoid, over three-and-a-half metres tall, covered
  in bristly black fur and very hard bony plates, and **nocturnal** (they see perfectly
  in any darkness). They have **six limbs** — two used only as legs, four usable as legs
  or arms — all ending in dexterous, near-handlike digits and dagger-long claws, so they
  can strike up to **four different targets at once**, falling back on long fangs only
  when starving or cornered. Cunning ambush hunters, they do not fear man, know weapons
  (though they never use them), are all but unmatched at stalking and hiding in their
  own terrain, and are prodigious leapers — dropping into a caravan, mauling a few
  victims, and bounding off with someone as a snack. Pressed, they turn extraordinarily
  ferocious and blindingly fast. Mercifully **solitary** — the true nightmare is
  stumbling on a mother with cubs. *(wiki: Creature)*

## Hybrids (Ibridi) `[CANON — wiki: Ibridi]`

**Ibridi (Hybrids)** are creatures of the **Maelstrom, mutated by magic** — beings part
human and part animal, and creatures bearing the **seed of an element** (as some giants
do). Centaurs of every kind are counted Hybrids even though they are *not* the product
of any magical mutation. A player may take a Hybrid race with the Master's consent,
bearing in mind that their unusual look can cause them trouble in social dealings.
*(wiki: Ibridi)* The creature aspect is catalogued here; the *peoples* aspect (playable
lineages, culture) lives in `peoples.md`. Types named by the wiki:

- **Centauri (Centaurs)** — of every type; Hybrids by classification despite no magical
  mutation.
- **Satiri (Satyrs)** — (the satyr is also the symbol of the Sovereign Demon Behemoth,
  Lord of Perverse Nature — presumably unrelated.)
- **Rahab** — named only; nature not yet described. `[INFERRED]` likely a serpentine or
  sea-monster lineage, from the biblical Rahab.
- **Tritoni (Tritons)** — merfolk-like water Hybrids `[INFERRED]`.
- **Giganti (Giants)** — those "bearing the seed of an element," per the definition
  above.

→ See `peoples.md` for playable Hybrid lineages and the **Marchiati (the Marked)**, and
`cosmology.md` for the Maelstrom's mutating **Mark**.

## Southern fauna `[CANON — Cartosa Guida p.5]`
- **Daretas** — six-legged, ox-sized armadillo-like beasts of the highlands; pack
  animals, prized for tender meat and very tough hide (shields, armour).
- **Reptoidi** — horse-sized quadruped reptiles, farmed for hide and as mounts;
  source of most Cartosan leather.
- **Yarhim** — colossal bluish crabs (1.5 m to 9–10 m across), **mutated by the
  Maelstrom's** magic; rare but found along the coasts. → `cosmology.md` (Hybrids)
- **Mayombe** — the oldest, fiercest ancestral/forest spirits the Nkishi Bokkor
  shamans summon. → `peoples.md`, `pantheon.md`

### Open questions
- **What are dragons, really — and when did they arise?** Three framings now collide:
  primordial "Grandi Vermi" appearing at the world's dawn; the top tier of Hybrid
  Elementals (`cosmology.md`); and **Adept-engineered bioweapons** set to guard Ymir's
  secrets (*Draghi* page). Are the twenty True Dragons all of these at once? And when
  did the **Adepti** make them, relative to the "world's dawn"? → `cosmology.md`,
  `history.md`.
- **Sovereign Demons roster mismatch:** the Player Guide lists **Gaziel** (not on the
  wiki) and gives Abigor a trident and no death-mark; the wiki lists **Abbadon** (not
  in the Player Guide) with an inverted trident, gives Abigor an **axe**, gives Ea an
  **oyster** (not a kraken), and marks Adramelek, Netunshiel and Abigor **dead**. Which
  roster is authoritative? Was Abbadon conflated into Abigor? → `NOTES.md`.
- **Ea's symbol** — the *Ombra dell'Ostrica Blu* corroborates the **oyster**; the
  existing "kraken" appears to be an extraction error.
- **Rahab** (a Hybrid type) is named but undescribed; the Qi-Long Dragon-Gods still
  await **Qi-Long.docx**; the fuller Ibridi descriptions (Centauri, Satiri, Tritoni,
  Giganti) are stubs on the wiki.
- A dedicated stat-block "monster manual" clearly exists behind these wiki pages
  (Rolemaster levels, criticals, breath frequencies); if the underlying doc surfaces,
  it should be mined for the remaining creatures and the numbers translated to prose.
- Native flora with mechanical/narrative use (Cartosa's **Utombe, Qarando, Subotan,
  Rud-Tekma** drugs/herbs) — capture in the manual's appendix or an ecology section.
