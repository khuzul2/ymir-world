## The Creation Myth — Cosmogony

`[CANON]` *(Player Guide p.21, "COSMOGONIA")* — In the beginning, per the most
widespread myth of Ymir (with local variants), there existed a single god:
**Ech, the One**, from whom flows the multitude of **Spirits** that give rise to
magic. For reasons none know, Ech divided into two distinct entities, beginning
time itself:

- **Mithra, the Creator** *(Xebechani: Quetzalcoatl)* — the creating energy that
  gave rise to life. Bound to light, good, creation, life, the **Aether**, peace,
  and healing.
- **Ahriman, the Corruptor** *(Xebechani: Azatzotech)* — the destroying energy
  that gave rise to death and decay. Bound to darkness, evil, destruction, death,
  the **Nether**, war, and disease.

The two are locked in perpetual struggle, yet neither can exist without the
other — as there is no light without dark, good without evil, life without death.

From the **contrast between these two** were born the **Six Divine Twins**, gods
who preside over the six elements: **Fire, Water, Ice, Air, Earth, Light**. With
their help, Mithra created the world and the races that people it. Ahriman,
loathing all things, tried to destroy Mithra's creation but only partly succeeded
in corrupting it. He then withdrew into **the Void beyond creation**, where he
gave rise to the race of **Demons** by corrupting the Spirits that dwelt there.

`[INFERRED]` The pantheon's dualism (a good creator + an evil corruptor + six
elemental children) has clear Zoroastrian naming (Mithra/Ahriman) and a
Mesoamerican mirror in the Xebechani names — a deliberate "many peoples, one
cosmology" design. See `pantheon.md`.

## Essenza and Essenza Oscura — the Fabric of Reality

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Cosmologia)* — Underlying all of the above is the **Essenza**
(Essence): an impersonal, invisible force that permeates the whole Multiverse.
Without it nothing would exist, and some scholars hold that all of creation is
merely one of its emanations. It is the wellspring every practitioner of magic
draws upon to alter reality — the different traditions simply tap it in different
ways: users of **Essenza** draw magical power from the surrounding environment
(since the Essenza is everywhere); users of the **Flusso** (Channelling) use
power sent to them by the gods; **Mentalismo** (Mentalism) users draw on the
Essenza sealed within their own bodies; and users of the **Arcano** (Arcane
realm) manipulate raw Essenza directly, blending their personal reserve with the
ambient supply. (The traditions themselves are detailed in → `magic.md`.)

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Cosmologia)* **Essenza Oscura** (Dark Essence) is a stain
*within* the Essenza — a kind of disease that seeks to spread and corrupt its
nature. Most inhabitants of Ymir's cosmos equate it outright with **Evil**;
others regard it more neutrally as simply a second kind of Essenza. It is the
principle to which the Demons are wholly bound, and of which Ahriman is the
purest incarnation (below).

`[INFERRED]` This gives the setting a single physical substrate: gods, Spirits,
souls, magic, and matter are all configurations of one force, and "good vs. evil"
is at bottom the contest between clean Essenza and its corrupting stain — the
metaphysical layer beneath the Mithra/Ahriman myth above.

### The orders of divinity

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Cosmologia)* The gods are beings of **pure Essenza endowed with
will**. Having no true form, they are immortal, all but omnipotent, and able to be
in many places at once (within the limits noted below). They are broadly split
into **Divinità Oscure** (Dark Divinities, made almost wholly of Essenza Oscura)
and **Divinità Luminose** (Luminous Divinities, of uncorrupted Essenza), locked in
perpetual war. The wiki cautions that a god's true nature and aims are unknowable
to mortals, so these categories are a guide, not an absolute. Four orders are
distinguished (named members belong to → `pantheon.md`):

- **Dèi Esterni / Antichi (Outer or Ancient Gods)** — a generation of divinity
  *prior* to the present one, of unknown origin, who ruled the cosmos long before
  the **Adepti** arrived. They long ago withdrew into a sleep-like stasis; some
  (disputed, partisan) sources claim it was the Adepti who exiled them into another
  dimension. They have lately begun to manifest again, to unfathomable ends. By far
  the mightiest of the gods, they rarely choose mortal champions, but can empower
  Flusso-users anywhere in the universe. They dwell in the **Spazio Esterno** beyond
  the planes, and are the gods worshipped by the **Xebechani** and the pantheon of
  the Terra dei Sogni (below). → `pantheon.md`, `peoples.md`.
- **Déi Superni (Supernal Gods)** — pure incarnations of the Essenza, normal or
  corrupted. Only two are known: **Mithra** and **Ahriman**; several named deities
  are thought to be merely aspects of one or the other under local names. They can
  exert their influence anywhere in the Cosmos without difficulty.
- **Dèi Elementali (Elemental Gods)** — the six elemental divinities, the *current*
  generation striving to impose its dominion on the cosmos. `[CONFLICT]` Their
  origin is disputed: the Player Guide cosmogony (above) makes them the **Six Divine
  Twins**, born of the contrast between Mithra and Ahriman, who helped Mithra shape
  the world; the wiki instead reports that they may be the former **Adepti**, who
  seized divine power *from* the Ancient Gods — either usurping it, or shaping the
  cosmos after defeating and exiling the Ancients into oblivion. They can act only
  in the planes in contact with the elemental planes.
- **Dèi Minori (Minor Gods)** — beings with god-like power: mighty servants of a
  greater deity, or entities that have reached semi-divine status by some means.
  The **Dei-Drago** (Dragon-Gods) are an example. Their reach is narrow — usually
  only specific regions of Ymir. → `pantheon.md`, `bestiary.md`.

## The Maelstrom

`[CANON]` *(Player Guide p.20; origin in `history.md`)* — The single most
extraordinary and mysterious feature of Ymir: an enormous vortex of bluish energy
like a colossal tornado, tens of kilometres in radius, reaching from the deepest
sea-abyss to the edge of the sky, at the centre of Ymir's great **Central Ocean**.
The sea around it is perpetually storm-tossed, yet the air within is dead still.
It was born of **La Catastrofe** (see `history.md`) and time is counted from that
event: years are numbered **d.M.** — *dopo il Maelstrom* ("after the Maelstrom").

Its effects on the world:
- **Tempeste Arcane (Arcane Storms):** the Central Ocean is periodically wracked
  by storms of magical energy that convulse air, water, and the very essence of
  creation, channelling arcane energies from other dimensions.
- **Magia Selvaggia (Wild Magic):** *(wiki: Maelstrom)* in the Maelstrom's
  immediate vicinity a vast reservoir of magical energy lies condensed. Users of
  Essenza, Mentalismo, and Arcano nearby recover their power far faster than
  normal, and every spell is amplified — the closer the caster, the stronger the
  effect — but this readily produces wild, unintended reactions and dangerous
  eruptions of power for anyone caught in them. → `magic.md`.
- **Marchio (the Mark):** the Maelstrom's energy sometimes mutates newborns,
  giving them deformities and handicaps but also strange magical gifts. These
  **Marchiati** ("Marked") are mostly killed at birth or in infancy.
- **Aberrazioni (Aberrations):** *(wiki: Maelstrom)* kin to the Marchio, these
  are the strange, deformed creatures that infest the Central Ocean, thickest in
  the Maelstrom's vicinity. → `bestiary.md`.
- **Deflusso (Outflow):** long-range teleportation is deflected by the
  Maelstrom's chaos — failure chance doubles per 50 km beyond 100 km of range —
  which is why no one on Ymir relies on teleport for large-scale transport.
- **Offuscamento (Obscuring):** divination is likewise clouded and distorted; no
  such spell can penetrate the Maelstrom, and visions grow less reliable the
  further into past or future they reach.
- **Materiali Incantati (Enchanted Materials):** *(wiki: Maelstrom)* the
  Maelstrom's magical emanations seed rare enchanted materials, prized across Ymir
  and — almost uniquely — able to persist between Ymir and the **Terra dei Sogni**
  (below). → `magic.md`.

`[CANON]` The central open mystery, stated in-world *(Player Guide p.2)*: **what
is the Maelstrom's true purpose?** Is it a seal placed by the Adepts to keep the
demons out — and what happens if it fails? (In the Cartosa timeline, it does: the
"campana di isolamento"/isolation bell shatters at the dawn of the Era della
Marea — see `history.md`.)

## Structure of Reality — the Planes

`[CANON]` *(Player Guide pp.23–24)* The material plane is surrounded and
enveloped by other planes of existence:

- **The Material Plane** — Ymir and the physical cosmos.
- **The Spirit World / Ethereal Plane (Mondo Spirituale / Piano Etereo)** —
  home of the **Spirits**, immaterial entities. Poorly understood, because
  Spirits tend to corrupt into **Spiriti Furiosi** (Raging Spirits: uncontrollable
  and malevolent) if they linger too long in contact with the material world.
- **The Terra dei Sogni (Dreamlands)** — a world unto itself, bound to Ymir (and
  perhaps other worlds), where the souls of dreamers go; see its own section below.
- **The Elemental Planes** — sources of the Elementals.
- **The Void beyond creation** — where Ahriman withdrew and the Demons arose.
- **The Aether / Nether** — the polar essences of Mithra and Ahriman respectively.

### The Elemental Walls (Mura Elementali)

`[CANON]` *(wiki: I Piani e il Multiverso)* The six Elemental Planes are not
scattered but form a single vast **sphere** that encloses the universe holding
Ymir. Picture a sphere made of six restless "materials" — one wholly Fire, one
Water, one Ice, one Air, one Earth, and one Light (with Dark as its negative pole)
— each an entire dimension of a single pure element. The six flow ceaselessly into
one another in two opposite directions at once: every plane *generates* the next
and every plane *devours* another. Because this shell walls the universe off from
the **Vuoto Esterno** (Outer Void) and other dimensions, the Elemental Planes are
also called the **Mura Elementali** (Elemental Walls); the **Universo** proper is
simply the space they enclose.

`[CANON]` *(wiki: I Piani e il Multiverso)* **Aether** and **Nether** have no
plane of their own: they are the "positive" and "negative" combination of all six
elements taken together, and it is their flow that lets reality hold together as a
coherent whole. Beyond the Walls lies the **Spazio Esterno** (Outer Space), the
empty region where the **Dèi Antichi** (Ancient Gods) reside.

`[INFERRED]` The Player Guide's "Void beyond creation" (where Ahriman withdrew and
raised the Demons) and the wiki's **Vuoto Esterno / Spazio Esterno** (home of the
exiled Ancient Gods) read as the same outer darkness beyond the Elemental Walls —
the place for everything creation shut out. The Nether would then be its
essence-signature within the system. Treated as one open frontier until the
sources say otherwise.

### The many worlds and the portal network

`[CANON]` *(wiki: I Piani e il Multiverso)* Ymir's universe is not one world but
many. Several inhabited planets share it, historically knitted together by a
**network of dimensional portals** — the elves and other peoples are said to have
come "from another world through dimensional portals," and the artificial world
**X-01** exists expressly to control that portal network. At least one world,
**Zend**, was placed under **quarantine by the Adepti**. The individual worlds and
the peoples that spring from them (Ymir itself, Tchyo, Omalgat, Naxtis, Zend, X-01,
Ouronos, Gwart, Seelie) are catalogued in → `peoples.md`; what matters
cosmologically is that Ymir is one node in a plural, portal-linked universe walled
in by the Mura Elementali.

### Extraplanar entities (the great taxonomy)

`[CANON]` *(Player Guide pp.23–24)*

- **Spiriti (Spirits)** — immaterial dwellers of the Ethereal Plane; the wellspring
  of magic; corrupt into Raging Spirits over time in the material world. `[CONFLICT]`
  *(wiki: Entità)* The wiki names their home the **"Mondo Spirituale o Mondo dei
  Sogni"** (Spirit World *or* World of Dreams), conflating it with the **Terra dei
  Sogni** — which the Dreamlands page instead paints as a distinct realm of
  dreamer-souls ruled by the Outer Gods. Whether the Spirit plane and the Terra dei
  Sogni are one realm or two is unresolved.
- **Elementali (Elementals)** — from the elemental planes or artificially made,
  ranked: Wispling → Ibridi → Servitori → Cavalcature → Guardiani → Giganti
  Elementali → Veri Elementali → Elementali Superiori → **Draghi** (the mightiest
  Hybrid elementals) → **Signori Elementali** (one per element; semi-divine).
  `[CANON]` *(wiki: Entità)* By tier: **Wispling** are the feeblest (no greater in
  power than an insect or rodent); **Ibridi** are half-elemental, at least half
  corrupted by an element; **Servitori, Cavalcature, Guardiani** and the mighty,
  barely-controllable **Giganti Elementali** are the ascending grades of
  *artificially made* elementals; the **Veri Elementali** (Weak to Strong) and the
  **Elementali Superiori** above them are the natural kind; the **Draghi** are the
  greatest of the Hybrids; and each **Signore Elementale** is a semi-divine ruler of
  one element.
- **Djinn** — semi-artificial entities: mana given to a Spirit to fix its form and
  prevent corruption, usually bound to a place, object, or single mission. Ranked:
  **Jann → Jinn → Shaitan → Ifrit → Marid**. *(wiki: Entità confirms.)*
- **Angeli (Angels)** — the opposite of demons; Spirit-servants of the god of Good,
  made purely of beneficent energy. Three Spheres: Third (Angels, Archangels,
  Principalities) · Second (Dominions, Powers, Virtues) · First (Seraphim,
  Cherubim, Thrones).
- **Demoni (Demons)** — Spirits wholly corrupted and enslaved to the god of Evil.
  On the material plane their true, distorted form is nearly impossible to hide
  (the mightier the demon, the harder to conceal). Ranked in **six Cerchie
  (Circles)**, weakest (1st) to strongest (6th). Beings too powerful to classify
  — the **Demoni Sovrani** and some lieutenants — are "**Oltre le Cerchie**"
  (Beyond the Circles). See `pantheon.md` for the Sovereign Demons and `bestiary.md`.

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Entità)* On the demons' nature the wiki is more cautious than the
Player Guide cosmogony: all agree the demons are wholly bound to the Essenza Oscura
and dwell in planes outside the material, but scholars offer three accounts of
their *origin* — (a) the souls of mortals who yielded to Evil in life, reincarnated
in demonic form after death; (b) especially malevolent, corrupted Spirits (the
Player Guide's account); or (c) true incarnations of the Dark Essence itself, each
with its own will. Whatever their source, a demon corrupts everything it touches:
however noble the summoner's intent, the demon inevitably taints the soul of
whoever calls it. Mightier demons are also rarer — only high magic can summon them
— and the higher Circles are the hardest to control, which deters most mages from
attempting them.

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Entità)* The **Demoni Sovrani** (Sovereign Demons) are the
greatest of all, approaching the might of Minor Gods; they are the most trusted
vassals of the Corrupted Essence, and their one apparent purpose is the corruption
of Creation. For all their power they are bound by the same law as every demon:
they cannot appear on Ymir unless **summoned**, by rituals of staggering
complexity. Though immortal, they *can* be annihilated — but killing the manifest
body is not enough; one must find and destroy the demon's **true essence**, far
easier said than done. The named Sovereigns, their domains and sigils (and which
are reckoned slain) are catalogued in → `pantheon.md`.

## The Terra dei Sogni (Dreamlands)

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Terra dei Sogni)* The **Terra dei Sogni** is a world unto itself,
bound to Ymir and perhaps to other worlds as well. The souls of *all* of Ymir's
dreamers pass into it while they sleep, yet almost none can truly interact with it
— most who even grasp that it exists are only passive spectators. A rare few,
called **Sognatori** (Dreamers), **Viaggiatori** (Travellers) or other names, move
through it consciously and by will.

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Terra dei Sogni)* Conscious entry is reached through special
skills or through magic, and the hard part is entering while keeping command of
one's faculties. A traveller who manages it can use their full abilities and magic,
which work much as they do on physical Ymir — but *failure* there twists into
strange effects, typically far more dangerous to the dreamer than the same misfire
would be on Ymir. Passage of matter between the worlds is possible but vanishingly
rare: objects able to exist in both are almost always forged from the enchanted
materials born of the **Maelstrom** (above). Dream-native *creatures* crossing to
Ymir is rarer still, because the gods themselves forbid it — lest the **Dèi
Esterni** exploit the crossing to gain more direct sway over Ymir.

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Terra dei Sogni)* The Dreamlands' own pantheon **is** the **Dèi
Esterni** (Outer Gods) — the same ancients the Xebechani worship. Mightier in raw
power than the gods of Ymir, they can touch the waking world only indirectly, but
the dream world is far easier for them to shape; this is precisely why conscious
travellers run grave *mental* risks there. → `pantheon.md`.

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Terra dei Sogni)* Its inhabitants fall into distinct kinds
(creatures detailed in → `bestiary.md`):
- **Sogni (Dreams)** — the benevolent natives, the opposite of Nightmares (with
  notable exceptions).
- **Incubi (Nightmares)** — native dream-entities, *not* the demonic incubus (mate
  of the succubus); their nature runs from playful to utterly evil, and they are
  graded in classes like the Demons. A demon-summoning spell cast inside the Terra
  dei Sogni instead calls an Incubo of matching power. They are normally penned
  within their own territories by unseen forces.
- **Orrori (Horrors)** — monstrous, malignant or idiot-minded things, including the
  formless **Esseri Striscianti** (Crawling Things) of its darkest places and the
  silent, faceless, bat-winged **Magri Notturni** (Night Gaunts).
- Certain Ymir natives commune with it — notably the **Fauni** (Fauns), whose fae
  songs are said to enchant their guests and bleed the dream world into the waking
  one as daydreams.

`[CANON]` *(wiki: Terra dei Sogni)* By contrast, the Demons that can be summoned
onto Ymir can reach the Terra dei Sogni by no means at all — the two categories of
malign entity are sealed apart.

### Open questions
- The **Grandi Vermi / Draghi** (Great Worms / Dragons) appear "when the
  planet is peopled with life" *(Player Guide chronology, "Tempi Dimenticati")* —
  are dragons primordial native life, or the elemental "Draghi" above, or both?
  (Later "Dei-Drago"/Dragon-Gods appear in Qi-Long ~1900 d.M.) → `bestiary.md`.
- What exactly are the **Adepti**, and where did they go? (The Player Guide flags
  this as an explicit in-world mystery — "quello degli Adepti".) The wiki deepens
  it: the **Elemental Gods may *be* the Adepti**, risen by stealing power from the
  Ancient Gods, and the Adepti quarantined the world Zend and (per some) exiled the
  Ancients. → `history.md`.
- Relationship between **Niethel** (the elves' divine place of origin) and this
  cosmology — the elves came "from another world through dimensional portals,"
  which now fits the wiki's plural, portal-linked universe (control node **X-01**).
  → `peoples.md`.
- **Is the Spirit plane the same realm as the Terra dei Sogni?** The *Entità* page
  calls the Spirits' home the "Mondo Spirituale o Mondo dei Sogni," yet the
  Dreamlands page describes a separate world of dreamer-souls ruled by the Outer
  Gods. One realm, or two overlapping ones? `[CONFLICT]`
- **Origin of the Elemental Gods:** children of Mithra and Ahriman (Player Guide)
  or the **Adepti** who usurped the Ancient Gods (wiki)? `[CONFLICT]` →
  `pantheon.md`, `history.md`.
- Is the Player Guide's "**Void beyond creation**" (Ahriman + Demons) the same
  place as the wiki's **Spazio Esterno / Vuoto Esterno** (the exiled Ancient Gods)?
  If so, Ahriman, his Demons, and the Ancients all share the same outer dark.
- The **Dèi Esterni** have "lately begun to manifest again" — when, and to what
  end? (Possible tie to the Maelstrom's failure / Era della Marea.) → `history.md`.
