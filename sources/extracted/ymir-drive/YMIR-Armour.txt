Armour Penalties

Penalties in brackets are used for social actions, if armour is overtly worn in an inappropriate environment.

Other penalties are applied to all character’s physical manoeuvres involving the location covered by the armor.

Cloth Armour

A character wearing only plain clothing can opt to reduce by 1d4 the damage dealt by a single attack against him. If he does, his clothing is torn and ragged in the process, and will offer no further protection.

Stacking Armour

If a character is wearing different layers of armour over a location, only the best one will be taken into account when computing damage.

Damage Resistance coming from natural armour, on the other hand, is always added to that of worn armour.

Special Materials

Armour made of special materials can have a DR higher (or lower\!) than the listed one. For example, a Mithril Chainmail could have a DR of 8, or a Bronze Plate could have a DR of 6.
