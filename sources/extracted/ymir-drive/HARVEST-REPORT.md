## 1. NEW LORE not in our canon — by target file

### → cosmology.md

- **The Atlas cosmogony (an entire alternate/deep creation myth).** [Atlas §"The
  Elements, The First Clash, The Old Gods"] In the beginning only the two **Original
  Elements** — **il Vuoto (the Void)** and **il Mana** — in still Balance. Mana pushed
  into the Void (wanting to fill everything) and the Void pushed into Mana (wanting to
  swallow everything): **the First Clash**, which birthed the four **Lesser Elements**
  (Fire, Earth, Air, Water) and **six Old Gods** who could control the Elements. None
  of this stratum exists anywhere in our canon. *Integration note: with our COS-9/COS-19
  ("Ancient/Outer Gods = a third cosmic axis, sealed away"), the Atlas reads naturally as
  the mythic memory of our secret layer — see the mapping opportunity below.*
- **The six Old Gods, named by epithet.** [Atlas] **The Scion of the Beginning**
  (strongest; kin of Mana, chaotic, loved and hated); **the Gazer in the Dark** (kin of
  the Void; most gifted and cunning, shunned as repulsive, "hid in the shadows at the
  very corner of the Universe"); **the Lady of Sorrow**, **She who Sees**, **the
  Gatherer of Circles**, **the Daughter of Nowhere** (the four who share the Lesser
  Elements, "doomed to an eternal conflict"). Deeds: the Scion + She who Sees raised the
  **eight Elemental Planes**; the Scion created **Life**, the **Dreamlands and Astral
  planes** (for souls in sleep and death), and the **Adepts** as stewards; the **Gazer**
  enviously modelled the Adepts and made the **Demons** plus their hidden **plane of the
  Seven Demonic Circles**; the **Gatherer of Circles** made **Men** ("a race that would
  have everything in it… able to adapt, evolve, create and survive").
- **✨ Mapping opportunity (high value, GM-secret layer):** the Atlas's **six Old Gods,
  banished** by the New Gods ↔ our **six named Outer/Ancient Gods, sealed** by the
  Adepti (`secret-the-great-lie.md`). Six and six, with resonant epithets:
  Gazer in the Dark ↔ **Tuulhak-Xanar the Observer**; Scion of the Beginning ↔
  **Tlahuiz'Halpantexuhtli the First**; Gatherer of Circles ↔ **Xotl-Nag'aaqe the
  Mother**; She who Sees ↔ **Xuxhul'Xhathi the Judge**; Lady of Sorrow ↔
  **Xatol-Nahuactl the Silent**; Daughter of Nowhere ↔ **Xip-Tenalthe the Risen**
  (pairings are ours to decide — the counts and the banishment/sealing arc line up).
  Adopting this would turn the Atlas "conflict" into corroborating deep-lore.
- **Mithra & Ahriman as *made* "New Gods."** [Atlas §"The Fall of the Old Gods"] The
  four sibling Old Gods crafted two children **to kill and replace** the Scion and the
  Gazer: bodies of the Lesser Elements, **Mithra** ensouled with **Essence + Aether**,
  **Ahriman** with **Void + Nether**. The two then **banished the Old Gods** and ruled
  together before falling out over the living; each made **lesser gods** ("Ahriman
  created Yogh and other gods"; "Mithra created XXX and other gods" — placeholder left
  in the source).
- **Il Mana Oscuro (Dark Mana) — the "ultimate Evil" as cosmic cancer.** [Atlas, design
  notes in Italian] Dark Mana is a third force that wants not merely to replace the Mana
  but **to devour the Void as well**: "Mana e Void… insieme formano un corpo sano
  (l'universo), il Dark Mana è una sorta di virus o di tumore che lo infetta e lo
  divora." Hence sacrificing to a dark god (Yog/Yogh) merely feeds that god's influence,
  but **sacrificing to a Demon infects part of the cosmos** (the offered soul/body) with
  Dark Mana and spreads it — the in-world reason demon-cults are feared categorically
  more than dark gods. *Interlocks with our **Essenza Corrotta** (Corrupted Essence);
  "Dark Mana" could canonise as its older/mythic name.*
- **The Ahriman-heresy.** [Atlas, design note] "e se Ahriman non fosse altro che uno
  degli dèi antichi che è sfuggito al banishment corrompendo la sua stessa essenza e
  quindi diventando esso stesso il dark mana?" — Ahriman as a **corrupted Old God who
  escaped the Banishment by betraying his kin** and *became* the Dark Mana, "which
  explains why he is seen as alien and universally evil… potentially dangerous even to
  the Ancient Gods." A ready-made in-world heresy/theory box for the manual, dovetailing
  with our third-axis Ancient Gods.
- **The eight Elemental Planes (Atlas plane-set).** [Atlas] Essence, Void, Aether,
  Nether + Fire, Earth, Air, Water — raised to confine the pure elements "away from the
  Universe and maintain balance." *Conflicts with COS-15 (Aether/Nether are
  Essence-states, not planes) — see §2; but "planes as confinement of the raw elements"
  matches our secret's "elemental planes as shield/energy-source walls."*
- **Void-beings drive mortals mad.** [M&S §"Paying the Price"; Summ] In the summoning
  price-rules, Corruption gained from demons is **Dark**, from elementals **Elemental**,
  and from "**esseri del Vuoto**" (beings of the Void) it is **Follia (Madness)** —
  i.e. the corpus systematically equates **Void-contact with madness**. [BR-1.0.7
  §Mental Corruption] "creatures that alter reality by their very presence and feed on
  the mental stability of the unfortunates who meet them." *Strong flavour support for
  our Outer-Gods/Spazio-Esterno axis: exposure to the Beyond = Punti Follia.*

### → geography.md

  geography's map section reconciles only the older hand-drawn `world-map-ymir.png`
  with labels Solanthya/Hibernia/etc.). [MAP-Y (4429×2953 px, **2000 km scale bar**),
  MAP-E] West→east layout:
  - **Letia** — the large **western** continent; **Regni Belligeranti** labelled as its
    **northern region**, **Regni Giovani** as its **south-western arm** (both read as
    sub-regions of the Letia landmass — matching their history as Letio breakaways).
  - **Cartosa & Meridian** — paired small islands **in the sea between Letia and Erg**.
  - **Helgedad** — a **north-central peninsula/isle just south of the Cold Maelstrom**
    (fits our "coldest place on Ymir").
  - **Erg** — the large **central** continent, with **Sadhir labelled as its southern
    region** (Grande Erg north + Sadhir desert south = one landmass).
  - **Gaerwath** — a **ring/rose-shaped archipelago** in the central ocean east of Erg.
  - **Qi-Long** — the large **north-eastern/eastern** landmass.
  - **Ophiur** — an **east/south-east** landmass between Qi-Long and Xebech.
  - **Xebech** — appears **twice**: a small island off the far-west coast AND the large
    south-eastern landmass (a genuine map mystery worth keeping: colony? error? two
    Xebechs?).
- **The climatic frame: Frozen Lands and Obsidian Lands.** [MAP-Y, MAP-E] The map's
  north margin is banded "**Frozen Lands**" and the south margin "**Obsidian Lands**"
  (each appearing twice, as off-map climatic zones) — the world runs **cold (north) →
  hot (south)**. Two brand-new named world-edge regions for the gazetteer.
- **The Cold Maelstrom and the Hot Maelstrom.** [MAP-Y, MAP-E] Two great ocean
  vortices — a **Cold Maelstrom** (ring of islets, north-central sea) and a **Hot
  Maelstrom** (matching ring, south-central sea). **No central Maelstrom is drawn.**
  → CONFLICT with our single central Maelstrom (see §2), but even under our canon the
  two polar rings are usable (e.g. as secondary vortices, Tide-era phenomena, or
  cartographic heresy).
- **The Gaerwath archipelago in full detail** (our canon says only "an archipelago…
  cities of dark beauty"). [MAP-G, map dated **"c.a. 3500 p.M."** — i.e. essentially
  **our manual-present of ~3518 d.M.**; for us this is a *present-day* nation map, not
  a far-future oddity]:
  - **Ten named islands**: **Cn'ethyr** (north — holds the capital **G'nethyr**),
    **Kh'myr**, **Yr'thyr**, **Wag'myr**, **Daarwyr**, **Moloch**, **N'ysyr**,
    **Gh'yrst**, **Dr'hakyr**, and the bare, settlement-less **Isola del Caos**;
    plus a small unnamed isle holding **Gh'anuss / Selva Ignota / L'ykry**.
  - **Thirteen stepped Ziggurat temples** (Hywon, Golog, Thelog, Ghom'a, Hr'yth, Wyaya,
    Agar, Mooloch, **A'rwo**, Gh'yrst, T'alan, Wag'myr, Ah'rkan) — a temple-building,
    death-worship register; note the **A'rwo Ziggurat** (island N'ysyr) apparently named
    for **Arvo**, the Dark Elves' Essence-dust currency.
  - **Soul/dream/death toponymy** confirming Dark-Elf culture: *Fiume dei Pensieri*
    (River of Thoughts), *Percorso/Fiume di Anime* (Path/River of Souls), *Lago dei
    Sensi*, *Lago degli Infanti* (Lake of the Infants), *Bosco dei Perduti*, *Foresta
    dei Sedici Pianti* (Forest of the Sixteen Weepings), *Canale di Sangue* (Channel of
    Blood), **Palazzo delle Aule dei Sogni** (Palace of the Halls of Dreams — a
    Dreamlands hook!), *Catenaccio degli Antichi Saperi*, *Selva della Perdizione*,
    *Corso dei Tempi*, *Bastione Malevolo*, *Flusso Inverso* (Reverse Flow), *Forte
    della Forgia*, *Foresta di Caccia dei Nobili Wag'myr* (a Wag'myr noble house!),
    *Picchi senza Nome*, *Picchi delle molte Pire* (Peaks of the Many Pyres).
  - **Map furniture**: a **Medusa/Gorgon-head compass medallion** as the national
    emblem; a winged-female scale plaque (100–500 units); settlements typed as Torre /
    Bastione / Forte / Presidio / Castello / Palazzo. Dozens more settlement names in
    [MAP-G] (Wy'lid, Thelmar, Yannis, Themys, G'yrsh, My'sha, W'ardyr, Thykra, Eskra,
    D'wyss, N'Athor, Z'daar, Castello Krynn, Torre E'og…) — ready place-name stock.
- **Al'Sawgdam — the Salt of the Deserto Scintillante.** [Rules-1.0.0 §Substances] "il
  Sale che ricopre larga parte del Deserto Scintillante, una bizzarra mistura di
  **Essenza, Vera Terra e Vuoto**" — the Shimmering Desert's covering is a blend of
  Essence, True Earth and **Void**. **I Sadhiriti are immune to it but must stay in
  constant contact with it or they waste away**; to everyone else the pure Salt is

- **Arvo deposit geography.** [Rules-1.0.0 §Substances] Arvo is "relatively common near

  where it occurs.

### → peoples.md

- **The Vanyr and the Asar — the two orders the Adepts split into** (wholly absent from
  our canon). [Atlas §"The Birth of Life"] After **the Banishment** (of the Old Gods),
  the abandoned Adepts split: the **Vanyr** "wanted to stay pure, apart from other
  races… practiced incest to preserve their pure bloodline, but they got corrupted after
  discovering the Demons, by interacting with them to control them. In little time, they
  were Adepts no more, just Vanyr." The **Asar** "started living among Men to try to
  watch over them closely and influence their erratic development but, in time, their
  blood was diluted by crosses with Men until they were Adepts no more, just Asar."
  Their **First War** "fought without quarter on all the worlds… Many of the worlds were
  destroyed" — a striking corroboration of our secret's **multi-world Adepti empire**.
  *Vanyr = ready-made deep origin for demon-binding bloodlines (Dark-Elf mentors? house
  Gibraleon? the Stregoni Neri?); Asar = a diluted Adepti-blood human strain.*
- **"Asar = the Primigeni" (their resolution).** [their canon/peoples.md, from Atlas +
  Races] They settled the Player Guide's Primigeni question as: Primigeni = the Asar,
  ex-Adepti diluted among Men. → CONFLICTS with our RAC-12/Additions Primigeni-as-
  **Iperborei** ("created with Fire"; Vorskgen/Ophiriti/Leti Sanguepuro lines) — see §2.
  Even if we keep the Hyperborean reading, **Asar** survives as a distinct playable
  near-human strain [Races: +5 ST +5 CH, Mental & Physical resistance 15].
- **N'Kishi = "The Corrupted Sons" — a deep origin for ALL monstrous humanoids.**
  [Atlas] "The war generated N'Kishi, which means 'The Corrupted Sons'. They were **Men
  that devoted themselves to Ahriman in order to survive the First War**. They are
  creatures with leathery skin in the shades of green and grey **of all sizes, from
  child-like to giants**." [Atlas §Races] "N'Kishi (non giocabili, includono tutti gli
  umanoidi 'mostruosi')" — i.e. one umbrella covering our orcs, goblins (M'Bantha),
  Nkishi Wild Orcs, hobgoblins, giants-of-a-kind. Our canon has no unified origin for
  the orcoid/goblinoid peoples; this supplies one (and chimes with our secret's "orcs =
  degenerated bio-soldiers from Gwart" as the *other* layer of the truth).
- **A Tanoth origin story.** [Atlas] "The Maelstrom started spreading its corruption
  over the whole Ymir. Soon some Men were **Marked** by its influx. Marked men soon
  started giving birth to strange children, **hybrids between humans and animals**.
  Those were cast from all societies and have been called the **Tanoths**, whose
  children are always Tanoths themselves." [Atlas §Races] proposes the split "**figli
  di Tanoth = Ur-Tanoth; figli di umani mutati = Tal-Tanoth**." Our canon defines the
  four Tanoth branches but gives **no origin**; this links Tanoth ↔ Marchiati ↔
  Maelstrom — and inverts interestingly against our COS-23 keystone ("Humans are the
  native Tanoth of Ymir") — see §2.
- **Dwergar origin (Atlas variant).** [Atlas] "The Dwergar were **Men who took refuge
  underground**, and lived there from then, digging deeper and deeper and adapting
  during the centuries." [Races: +ST +CO −DE +SD, Infravision, +25 RR vs poison &
  disease.] → double CONFLICT (with Nani-as-own-race and with our mezze-razze list
  where "Dwergar" is one of the 16 half-races) — see §2.
- **Aeryan — the system's umbrella name for the elves** ("ex-elfi"). [Atlas §Races,
  Races] One playable elf race (+5 DE +5 IN +2 CH, Mental resistance 20, "start with
  1d10 CPs" — elves begin corruption-touched!). Useful as an archaic/foreign exonym for
  elves even under our full taxonomy. The "1d10 starting Corruption Points" is a
  flavourful racial hook (elves as beings already marked by Essence-exposure).
- **Xebechani made by the Adepts, in three functional types.** [Atlas] "Some Adepts…
  used their powers to **make the Xebechans**: tall and thin ones that were fond of
  using the Essence [= Xattva], big and strong ones that were good for construction
  work and fighting [= Xudra] and devoted ones that would have some sort of **direct
  contact with the gods** [= Xion]." Hard confirmation of our RAC-10/RAC-1 secret
  (Pozze = Adepti bioscience); note the Races sheet **lacks the Xidhe caste** (Player
  Guide's fourth, everyday caste) — flag as caste-history nugget (Xidhe as a later,
  post-creation caste?). Stats: Xudra heaviest Natural Armor + double-roll initiative;
  Xion Magesense; Xattva +1 PP/level, Magical resistance 20.
- **The seven human ethnic groups (playable-culture roster with knacks).** [Races]
  A Common Man belongs to an ethnic group granting a cultural knack: **Cartosa** (+10 a
  Social skill) · **Letia** (+10 Magical Resistance) · **Warring States** [= Regni
  Belligeranti] (melee die results of "1" count as "2") · **Young Realms** (+10 Alchemy
  / Mechanisms / Streetwise — the magitech culture!) · **Great Erg** (+15 RR vs heat &
  illusions) · **Helgedad** (+10 Adventuring or +5 Vitality) · **Qilong** (+10
  Perception or +2 Initiative). Mechanics aside, this is a canonical **list of the
  seven "core" human cultures** and one-line cultural signatures — manual-ready.
  Common Men also "start with 10 more Luck points" — humans as the *lucky* race.
- **Sadhiriti salt-symbiosis.** [Rules-1.0.0, see Al'Sawgdam above] The desert people
  are physiologically **bound to the Salt** — immune, but deteriorating without
  constant contact. A striking racial-culture trait our Sadhir writeup lacks.

### → magic.md

- **The Domain taxonomy of magic (the homebrew's in-world "spheres of Magic").**
  [M&S §Domains] Four families of **Domains**: **Elemental** (Fire, Ice, Air, Earth,
  Water, Light, **Darkness**, **Æther**, **Nether** — nine, not six!), **Natural**
  (Plant, Animal, Poison, **Time**, Life, Death), **Planar** (**Demonic, Spiritual,
  Astral**), **Humane** (Mental, Physical). Casting styles: **Standard / Force /
  Directed**; spells learned via **Sorcery (Domain)** and **Arcane Arts (Spell)**
  talents. Our magic.md has only the three-realm (Essenza/Mentalismo/Flusso) frame;
  the Domain grid is an alternative in-world scholastic taxonomy worth a sidebar
  (note it treats Aether/Nether/Darkness as castable elements).
- **Spells ranked in 9 Ordini (Orders).** [`YMIR-1.0.3-Magia.txt`] "Gli incantesimi
  sono stati divisi **dagli studiosi** in 9 Ordini, a seconda della loro potenza e
  difficoltà… Solamente coloro che sono stati iniziati ai segreti di un ordine hanno le
  conoscenze" — i.e. in-world, magic is organised in **nine Orders of initiation**.
- **The summoning framework — the best new magic-lore block in the corpus.**
  [Summ; Skills §Summoning; M&S §Entity summoning]:
  - **Four kinds of summonable entity**: **Demon, Elemental, Spirit, Outsider** —
    "Outsider" being a category our Player-Guide taxonomy lacks (the Void/Beyond
    bucket; Outsider-vesselling causes **Mental Corruption/Follia**, demons Dark,
    elementals Elemental, spirits none).
  - **Five effects**: **Evoke** (bring it here), **Contact** (speak without moving
    it), **Bind** (into a ritually prepared object), **Vessel** (imbue it in the
    caster's own body), **Possess** (send it into a target's body).
  - Entities dwell in ranked **Pales** (First → Sixth); summoners advance **Acolyte →
    Cabalist → Conjurer → Evoker → Summoner → Master Summoner** [T&F §Path of
    Summoning], then specialise as **Demonologist / Elementalist / Animist (spirits) /
    Mad Summoner (Outsiders!)** — plus the **Speaker of Names** talent-line.
  - **The Price of Service ladder** [Summ]: First Pale = **Offering** (an item of
    worth) · Second = **Mark** ("the summoner is marked by the entity… 1d10 CPs"; the
    indelible mark "tends to show itself at the least opportune moments" [M&S]) ·
    Third = **Service** (a task — e.g. build a shrine, or **kill a sorcerer who knows
    the entity's True Name** [M&S]) · Fourth = **Submission** (yield part of yourself:
    5d10 CPs *or a body part destroyed*) · Fifth = **Soul** (your own, a volunteer's,
    or "una vittima inerme e ritualmente preparata") · Sixth = **Ecatombe / Hecatomb**
    (ten [M&S] / a hundred [Summ] souls). If Corruption passes 100, "l'entità potrà
    divorare la sua anima a termine del servigio."
  - **True Names**: knowing an entity's True Name lets you **summon it instantly,
    without ritual** [T&F Demonologist/Elementalist Lord]; names are won by luck, by
    the Speaker-of-Names discipline, or by "Perform quests and Roleplay! :D" [Summ].
  - **Summoning circles**: **Confinement** (keep in/out) and **Weakening** (sap the
    entity's will in negotiation) [Summ]; **sacrifices** sweeten the ritual ("1 common
    soul/+10, 1 infant soul/+20" for demons and Outsiders — grimdark price-list) [Summ].
  - **What summoned entities can do, by rank** [M&S §Leagues and Services]: fight,
    guard a place (days → **decades** as rank rises), carry things/people, scry and
    answer yes/no questions, teleport people (Lords: up to 10 km/level), and at Epic
    rank **carry passengers across the planes, enchant objects, destroy enchanted
    objects "e molto altro."** A complete in-world economy of what demon-binding buys —
    the mechanical skeleton behind our Dark-Elf **Valrin/Arvo** trade.
  - **Arvo as summoning currency** [Rules-1.0.0]: summoners "possono consegnare alle
    entità evocate **l'Arvo al posto della loro Essenza**" — pay the entity in Arvo
    instead of your own Essence. Directly upgrades our Arvo lore.
- **The three-track Corruption system (in-world stakes of forbidden power).**
  [BR-1.0.7 §Corruption; Rules-1.0.0]:
  - **Dark Corruption** — from Evil spell-lists, demon-pacts, Evil artifacts, and
    **wandering Evil Lands** (1/day unless resisted — i.e. *cursed geography is
    canon*). At 25+ "you have become evil… for all game purposes"; at 100 "you are
    damned." Its effects table is pure setting flavour: blind pale eyes opening on the
    body, a tongue become a worm-tentacle, needing sentient blood to recover power,
    an indelible **mark of the Darkness-follower**, "un servitore di una divinità
    nemica ad Ahriman… inizia a dargli la caccia" (Ahriman-opposed god-hunters!),
    demonic visitors offering dubious pacts, visions of a corrupted relic's location.
  - **Elemental Corruption** — from True-Element damage, botched elemental casting,
    and **days spent on the elemental planes**; at 100 "you become a pure elemental
    entity." Effects: elemental symbiosis (must sleep immersed in your element),
    caustic dragon-like blood, the body usable **once a day as a mystic portal to the
    elemental plane**, attunement-sense for True-Element sources.
  - **Mental Corruption / Punti Follia (Madness Points)** — from reality-warping
    creatures and "rituali talmente assurdi e blasfemi"; stages **Turbato → Scosso →
    Disturbato → Al Limite → "Mwahahaha!"** (fully mad, becomes an NPC). Our canon has
    the CEO corruption + elemental axis (wiki) but **no madness track** — this is the
    Lovecraft-axis mechanic matching our Outer-Gods lore.
- **Schools attested in the spell corpus.** [Necro] **Schools of Necromancy**
  ("generally considered an evil and very dangerous branch of Magic"), **School of
  Soul Manipulation** — "generally considered dangerous and delicate and **strictly
  regulated in civilized areas**" (dovetails with Cartosa's Manti-Azzurri regulation
  and the Young Kingdoms' permit system) — and **four Schools of Elementalism** (Fire,
  Ice, Earth, Air). [`YMIR-Incantesimi.txt`] **Dominio Elementale**: "è necessario
  imparare una versione diversa di questa scuola **per ogni elemento**."
  Named soul-craft spells worth keeping as in-world formula-names: *Soul Extirpation,
  Soul Transfer, Soul Exchange, Soul Trap, Soul Banishment, Soul Destruction, Soul
  Preservation, Soul Restore/Restoration, Soul Merge, Soul Healing, Create Soul
  Vessel; Control/Turn/Order/Enslave Undead*. Also the named spell **Dark Sleep**
  [`YMIR-1.0.0-Rules.txt` combat example] and **Unseen** (the ring on PC Kharim).
- **Blood Magic as a named tradition.** [T&F §Magician Specialties] **Blood-letter**
  (wound yourself to power the casting) and **Torturer** ("you must **ritually
  torture** your victims… If your victim dies in the process, the bonus is lost") —
  a codified, chillingly specific dark tradition; our magic.md never defines blood
  magic.
- **The Path of Corruption / Dark Mage tradition.** [T&F] Casters who cultivate **Dark
  Power Points**: *Dark Mage, Dark Rage, Dark Eloquence* (cast silenced and bound),
  *Dark Quickening, Dark Power* — the named talent-arc of the Usufruitori Corrotti.
- **New substances & poisons (for the herbs/drugs list).** [Rules-1.0.0 §Substances]:
  - **Vino di Luna (Moon Wine)** — "un pregiato liquore **distillato dagli elfi del

## 2. CONFLICTS with our canon (flag, don't resolve)

1. **Two cosmogonies.** Our canon (Player Guide, keystone + COS-24): **Ech, the One**
   (mythologised L'Essenza) splits into **Mithra + Ahriman**; the **Six Divine Twins**
   born of their contrast. **[Atlas]**: **Void + Mana → First Clash → six Old Gods**,
   who *make* Mithra (Essence+Aether) & Ahriman (Void+Nether) as **New Gods**; the four
   sibling Old Gods share the Lesser Elements (no Six Twins). *Their canon kept both
   side-by-side, PG primary. For us the Atlas can additionally be read against our
   SECRET layer (Old Gods ↔ sealed Ancient Gods — see the §1 mapping), which their
   session never had.*
2. **Origin of the Maelstrom.** Our canon (+ COS-2 "one event seen three ways"): the
   Adepti's **theft-ritual/Catastrofe at Ragash**. **[Atlas]**: the **Vanyr–Asar First
   War** "created the eternal Maelstrom." Both blame the Adepts; events differ. (COS-2's
   machinery could absorb this as a *fourth* seen-way — the war among the Adepti's
   successor-orders — but that's a decision, not a given.)
3. **Demonic Circles: six or seven — and whose.** Our canon (MAG-2 = A, single
   hierarchy): **six Circles**, demons made by **Ahriman** corrupting Spirits in the
   Plane of Chaos. **[Atlas]**: the **Gazer in the Dark** made the Demons and "the
   plane of the **Seven Demonic Circles**."
4. **Aether/Nether: planes or states.** Our COS-15: **Essence-states, not planes**
   (and Nether ≠ Corrupted Essence). **[Atlas]**: **eight Elemental Planes** including
   planes *of* Essence, Void, **Aether**, **Nether**. Also [M&S] lists **Æther** and
   **Nether** as castable Elemental *Domains*.
5. **The Dreamlands' place.** Our COS-16: a **region of the Spirit World**, ruled by
   the Outer Gods. **[Atlas]**: the **Scion of the Beginning created the Dreamlands
   and the Astral planes** for souls in sleep and death (distinct planes, divine-made).
6. **Who made Men — and the Tanoth arrow.** Our COS-23 keystone (secret): **Humans are
   the native Tanoth of Ymir — NOT engineered** (the one un-made people). **[Atlas]**:
   Men were **created by the Gatherer of Circles**; and the **Tanoth are descended from
   Maelstrom-Marked Men** (Ur-Tanoth = children of Tanoth; Tal-Tanoth = children of
   mutated humans). Same pair, opposite derivation arrows (humans→Tanoth vs
   Tanoth→humans' kin) and made-vs-native clash. Also our Tal-Tanoth are *feline*
   humanoids by Additions.md — the Atlas's "hybrids between humans and animals" is
   compatible in texture but not in genealogy.
7. **Primigeni identity.** Their canon (from Atlas): **Primigeni = Asar** (diluted
   ex-Adepti). Our canon (RAC-12/RAC-13 + Additions): **Primigeni = Iperborei**,
   created **with Fire**, surviving in the **Vorskgen / Ophiriti / Leti Sanguepuro**
   lines. Two incompatible resolutions of the same Player-Guide mystery.
8. **Dwarves / "Dwergar."** Our canon: **Nani** are their own race — AND "Dwergar" is
   one of the **16 mezze-razze crossbreeds** (races-index.md). **[Atlas/Races]**:
   **Dwergar = the dwarves themselves** ("ex-nani"), derived from **Men gone
   underground**. Name-collision + origin conflict, twice over.
9. **Elf taxonomy.** Our canon: full faction × sub-race grid (Chiari/Illuminati/Oscuri
   × Alti/Silvani/Grigi), elves from Tchyo/Niethel. **[Atlas/Races]**: one playable
   umbrella race **Aeryan** ("ex-elfi"). (Also [Atlas] has *no* elves-from-elsewhere —
   they simply don't appear in its creation account.)
10. **One Maelstrom or two.** Our canon (+ GEO-13 globe, Ragash beneath the central
    Maelstrom): **one central Maelstrom**. **[MAP-Y/MAP-E]**: a **Cold Maelstrom**
    (north) and a **Hot Maelstrom** (south), ring-shaped island scatters, **no central
    vortex drawn**.
11. **Where Cartosa & Meridian sit.** Our geography: "two islands in the **warm
    southern seas**." **[MAP-Y]**: a pair of small islands **between Letia and Erg**
    in the central-western sea.
12. **Where the Sadhir is.** Our geography (GEO-4 partial resolution): the Sadhir on
    the **western reach of the great southern desert-continent** that carries Ophiur
    eastward. **[MAP-Y]**: **Sadhir = the southern region of Erg**, the *central*
    continent (with Ophiur a separate E/SE landmass). ([MAP-E] instead renders Sadhir
    as its own big south-central continent — the two Drive maps don't fully agree with
    each other either.)
13. **Where the elf-lands sit.** Our GEO-12: Alfheim anchored **south-east**, near the
    Gaerwath and the Isole d'Argento. **[MAP-Y/MAP-E]**: the Gaerwath is a
    **central-ocean archipelago east of Erg**; Alfheim and the Isole d'Argento do not
    appear on the Drive maps at all.
14. **Xebech twice.** [MAP-Y] labels **Xebech** both as a small far-western island and
    as the large south-eastern landmass — internally anomalous; our canon has one
    Xebech (SE).
15. **"Sadhiriti" and the Salt.** Our GEO-1 layering: **Sadhiriti = the pre-Drying
    human island-kingdom people** (the ruins-beneath), the desert now ruled by the
    **Sarradhi** lizardfolk. **[Rules-1.0.0]** describes living "**Sadhiriti**" who
    are immune to — and dependent on — the Deserto Scintillante's Salt (Al'Sawgdam),
    i.e. present-day desert-dwellers. Which people does the Salt-symbiosis belong to
    (surviving human Sadhiriti? the Sarradhi under an older name?) — needs a ruling.
16. **Hecatomb size.** Internal to the corpus: [M&S] "Ecatombe: l'evocatore offre
    **10 o più anime**" vs [Summ] "Hecatomb: summoner must offer **a hundred souls**."
    Trivial, but pick one if the term is canonised.
17. **(Inherited, already logged by them)** "Ymir" vs "Yimir" spelling → standardised
    **Ymir**; and their "la Marea vs il Maelstrom" Dark-Elf-doc ambiguity (still open
    in our NOTES too).
