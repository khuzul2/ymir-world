## The Elements, The First Clash, The Old Gods

At the beginning of time there were only the Original Elements, the Void and the Mana, and the Universe was still, and the Balance was kept. But at last the Mana pushed into the Void, wanting to fill everything, and the Void pushed into the Mana as it wanted to swallow everything. This is known as the First Clash. The immense powers involved and the chaos generated many things. Among all, four new elements were born: Fire, Earth, Air and Water, which are known as the Lesser Elements. The Clash also gave birth to six powerful beings, who are the Old Gods, who could control the Elements themselves and restore the balance of the Universe.

The Scion of the Beginning was the strongest of them all, but also the most conflicting. He was both loved and hated by all if his brothers, because he had control over the powerful Mana, and like the Mana he was chaotic and wanted to pervade everything.

The Gazer in the Dark is kin of the Void. He was the most gifted and cunning among the Old Gods, but he was also very different from them, so lonely and quiet that he made them uncomfortable. So they found him repulsive and hideous, and he hid in the shadows at the very corner of the Universe.

The Lady of Sorrow, She who Sees, The Gatherer of Circles and The Daughter of Nowhere were doomed to an eternal conflict within and between themselves, as they could control all the Lesser Elements at once, but not completely.

## The age of the Creation

The Old Gods couldn’t reach an agreement about how to rule the Universe, and about what to create with their immense power over all the Elements.

The Planes (Elemental)

Planets

So every one of them tried to push together and mold the Elements in different ways, to create what they wanted. The Scion of the Beginning wanted to create the stars and the worlds, to host the Life. So he created four new elements to build them: Fire, Earth, Air and Water, which are known as the Lesser Elements. But he saw that there were too many elements in the universe now, and that they would destroy all of his creations by continuous conflicts. So he reached for She who Sees, and the two created the Elemental Planes to confine the pure elements away from the Universe and maintain balance. So there are eight Elemental Planes, one for every element, and those are the plane of Essence, the plane of Void, the plane of Aether, the plane of Nether and the planes of Fire, of Earth, of Air and of Water.

## The Birth of Life

While his brothers and sisters were creating the New Gods, the Scion of the Beginning created Life by himself.

Plants and Animals were the first to be created by the Scion of the Beginning, to populate the worlds that were at the right distance from a star.

With life came the souls, and also death. So the Scion of the Beginning created the Dreamlands and the Astral planes for souls to rest during sleep and after death.

As Plants and Animals were too many, the Scion of the Beginning created the Adepts, so that they could watch over their father’s creations.

In the meanwhile, the Gazer in the Dark was observing, and growing envious. As none of his brothers and sisters wanted anything to do with him, he modeled the Adepts and created the Demons along with a hidden plane for them to hide from the other gods, and that is the plane of the Seven Demonic Circles.

The Adepts were few, and still they couldn’t agree on how to rule the worlds. Some Adepts soon felt the need of devoted servants who would grant the balance of Life on the worlds themselves. So they used their powers to make the Xebechans, tall and thin ones that were fond of using the Essence, big and strong ones that were good for construction work and fighting and devoted ones that would have some sort of direct contact with the gods.

In the meanwhile, the Gatherer of Circles thought about creating a race that would have everything in it. She thought that such a race, able to adapt, evolve, create and survive would have been the true warrant of the perpetration of Life. So she created the Men, who most of all shaped the fate of Life in the universe.

After The Banishment, the Adepts felt abandoned and clueless, and two factions arose. The Vanyr wanted to stay pure, apart from other races. So they practiced incest to preserve their pure bloodline, but they got corrupted after discovering the Demons, by interacting with them to control them. In little time, they were Adepts no more, just Vanyr.

On the other side, the Asar started living amon Men to try to watch over them closely and influence their erratic development but, in time, their blood was diluted by crosses with Men until they were Adepts no more, just Asar.

The Vanyr and the Asar eventually came to war. They fought without quarter on all the worlds, using their powerful sorcery against each other. Many of the worlds were destroyed, many became unsuitable for life, all the others were forever changed. This war is known as the First War.

Ymir was one of the worlds that survived the First War, even if it was forever changed by it, by the creation of the eternal Maelstrom. The war generated N’Kishi, which means “The Corrupted Sons”. They were Men that devoted themselves to Ahriman in order to survive the First War. They are creatures with leathery skin in the shades of green and grey of all sizes, from child-like to giants.

The Dwergar were Men who took refuge underground, and lived there from then, digging deeper and deeper and adapting during the centuries to their new homes.

The Maelstrom started spreading its corruption over the whole Ymir. Soon some Men were Marked by its influx. Marked men soon started giving birth to strange children, hybrids between humans and animals. Those were cast from all societies and have been called the Tanoths, whose children are always Tanoths themselves.
