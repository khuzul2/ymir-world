=== DESCRIPTION / VISUAL READING ===

This is a hand-drawn, antique-style fantasy nation map of an ARCHIPELAGO titled "Gaerwath",
dated "c.a. 3500 p.M" (circa 3500 post-something; likely an in-world dating era). It is rendered
in sepia/greyscale on aged parchment with torn/burned edges over a slate-grey sea.

Cartographic furniture:
- Top-left: a large capital letter "N" (north indicator) above an ornate circular compass-rose
  medallion depicting a MEDUSA / GORGON female head with flowing hair and blank white eyes.
  Below the medallion is the map title "Gaerwath" and subtitle "c.a. 3500 p.M".
- Top-right: a decorative distance SCALE BAR shaped like a stone plaque flanked by a winged
  female (angel/harpy) figure, marked 100 / 200 / 300 / 400 / 500 (units unlabeled).

The land is a scatter of many separate ISLANDS across the grey ocean. Faint large watermark
letters spell each island's name across its body. Islands identified (watermark names):
- Isola Cn'ethyr (north; the island of the capital G'nethyr) — watermark reads roughly
  "Isola C...ethyr"
- Isola Kh'myr (large western island)
- Isola Yr'thyr (large central island)
- Isola Wag'myr (large south-central island)
- Isola Daarwyr (small island of D'wyss / Daarwyr)
- Isola Moloch (eastern-central island of the Mooloch Ziggurat)
- Isola del Caos (bare central island, no settlements labeled)
- Isola N'ysyr (eastern island of N'yisyr / F'alass)
- Isola Gh'yrst (far-eastern elongated island)
- Isola Dr'hakyr (south-eastern island)
- one small unnamed-watermark island holds Gh'anuss / Selva Ignota / L'ykry

Settlement style: named towns are drawn as little walled towers/keeps; larger cities as
castle/temple clusters; many sites are stepped pyramid "Ziggurat" temples; watchtowers are all
prefixed "Torre"; strongholds are "Bastione" / "Forte" / "Presidio" / "Castello" / "Palazzo".
Physical features use Italian generic terms: Monti/Picchi/Catena/Denti/Punte (mountains/peaks/
range/teeth/points), Foresta/Selva/Bosco/Riserva (forest/wood/reserve), Lago (lake), Fiume/Corso/
Percorso/Flusso/Canale (river/course/path/flow/channel). Place-names are mostly apostrophe-laden
Welsh/Elvish-style constructions (Kh'myr, Yr'thyr, Wag'myr, W'ardyr, G'nethyr, N'yisyr, etc.).

The naming reveals a THEME of dreams, souls, the dead and lost things: "Fiume dei Pensieri"
(River of Thoughts), "Percorso delle Anime" / "Fiume di Anime" (Path/River of Souls), "Lago dei
Sensi" (Lake of the Senses), "Lago degli Infanti" (Lake of the Infants), "Bosco dei Perduti"
(Wood of the Lost), "Foresta dei Sedici Pianti" (Forest of the Sixteen Weepings), "Canale di
Sangue" (Channel of Blood), "Palazzo delle Aule dei Sogni" (Palace of the Halls of Dreams),
"Catenaccio degli Antichi Saperi" (Bolt/Bar of the Ancient Knowledges), "Selva della Perdizione"
(Wood of Perdition), "Corso dei Tempi" (Course of the Ages), "Bastione Malevolo" (Malevolent
Bastion), "Selva Ignota" (Unknown Wood), "Flusso Inverso" (Reverse Flow), "Forte della Forgia"
(Fort of the Forge), "Foresta di Caccia dei Nobili Wag'myr" (Hunting Forest of the Wag'myr
Nobles), "Picchi senza Nome" (Peaks Without Name), "Picchi delle molte Pire" (Peaks of the Many
Pyres). No player-characters, gods, spells or weapons are named on the map itself; the recurring
"Ziggurat" pyramid-temples (Hywon, Golog, Thelog, Ghom'a, Hr'yth, Wyaya, Agar, Mooloch, A'rwo,
Gh'yrst, T'alan, Wag'myr, Ah'rkan) imply a temple-building / possibly death-worship culture, in
keeping with the Medusa emblem and soul/dream toponymy.
