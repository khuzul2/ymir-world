## Letia / Impero Letio `[CANON]` `[DEEPENING]`
*The central/northern human theocracy — thin in canon, so captured fully.*

- **Demography:** ~**300 million**, predominantly **Humans**.
- **Political history:** Once a great human empire; lost much territory to two
  shocks — (1) **orc invasions** that carved out the **Regni Belligeranti**
  (Belligerent Kingdoms), and (2) the **succession crisis of the imperial dynasty**
  that spawned the **Regni Giovani** (Young Kingdoms).
- **Government now:** ruled by the **Clericato di Mithra** (Clergy/Church of Mithra),
  seated in the city of **Solanthya**. Government is headed by the **sommo sacerdote
  di Mithra** (High Priest of Mithra). `[DEEPENING]` Canon calls this office the
  *Teocrate/Alto Teocrate*; the raw wiki uses "sommo sacerdote" — same office,
  different label. Solanthya as the seat is **confirmed**.
- **Terrain/climate:** still a vast land, **temperate climate**, subdivided into
  **many small marches and baronies** (marche e baronati) that answer directly to
  the central government.
- **Peoples:** predominantly Human, but the **Monti Bianchi** (White Mountains)
  `[NEW]` hold a large dwarf population **and one of the greatest dwarven strongholds
  of all Ymir: Naughrit** `[NEW — HIGH VALUE]`. Most other races are welcome
  **except orcs, dark elves, and followers of the Tenebre (the Darkness/Shadow)**.
- **Tone/law:** travellers quickly learn **not to meddle in Church affairs** and not
  to openly profess any cult other than **Mithra**. Sacred, intolerant theocracy.
- **NEW named places:** **Naughrit** (great dwarven fortress), **Monti Bianchi**
  (White Mountains). These are the two most valuable new geographic facts here.

## Helgedad — the Frozen Hell (grimdark far North) `[CANON]` `[DEEPENING]`
- **Demography:** ~**9 million**; High/Mixed/Common Men, Dwarves, Silvan Elves,
  Orcs, Goblins, Trolls.
- **Location/climate:** one of the northernmost inhabited lands and **the coldest
  place on Ymir** — nicknamed **"Inferno Gelato"** (Frozen Hell).
- **Elves:** rare; live almost only in the **Bosco di Caelebawn** (Caelebawn Wood),
  a genia of **Elfi Silvani** who withdrew here millennia ago to escape the fratricidal
  war between the Alfheim elves and the Gaerwath Dark Elves. `[DEEPENING]` The wood is
  now a realm of the **"Elfi Illuminati"** (Enlightened Elves) faction, a few thousand
  strong, ringed by powerful **misdirection enchantments**. (Origin legend: planted by
  a single elf craftsman to "repay nature" for a bow.)

**The six Marche** (dating to when the Letio emperor ruled here):
1. **Kelmark** (SW) — House **Rudstar** (head **Jormund Rudstar**), city **Kelmar**
   (largest city in Helgedad). Renowned horse-knights; worship **Thurms**; traditional
   enemies of the Nortmaar clans (hostilities ended <50 yrs ago). Founded by legend by
   **Aslund il Senzaspada** (the Swordless), centurion of the **Decima Legione**, who
   built the **Vallo di Aslund** — a 400 km fortified wall from the Monti Aestyr to the
   sea — and beat the northern barbarians 10-to-1. Order of chivalry: **Ordine della
   Montagna Bianca** (dedicated to Thurms; open to non-nobles by merit). Common tongue:
   a bastardised **Letio**.
2. **Tallvar** (SE) — House **Talvain** (head **Eyleiff Talvain**), city **Tallvar**
   (first Imperial city founded on Helgedad; 2nd-largest after Kelmar). Rumour of elf
   blood in the Talvain line. Cults of **Thurms** and **Heis** (gambling). Port city
   with three loose zones: **Zona del Porto/Città Bassa**, **Zona del Mercato/Città di
   Mezzo**, **Zona del Castello/Città Alta**. Powerful **Gilda Mercantile**; a **Gilda
   dei Guerrieri** runs the militia, prisons, and bounty payments; a notorious **Gilda
   dei Ladri**. `[CONFLICT — internal source error]` The detailed Tallvar section says
   the castle belongs to **"famiglia Geidmund,"** but the marches list and canon assign
   Tallvar to **Talvain** and Geidmund to Nortislong. Treat "Geidmund castle at Tallvar"
   as a source typo.
3. **Nortislong** (west coast) — House **Geidmund** (head **Branan Geidmund**), city
   **Nortislong**. Large, sparsely populated; the **Foresta d'Inverno** (Winter Forest)
   blocks big towns. Inhabitants the **Norsi** (sing. **Norsk**), descended from an
   indigenous island people now heavily mixed with mainland Leti; totem-spirit tradition,
   shamans still claim communion with forest spirits.
4. **Grudgemark** (center-west) — House **Tannermar** (head **Ailan Tannermar**), city
   **Grudgeburg**. Newest march after Nortmaar (union of two small barbarian kingdoms);
   allied with the Aule Dorate dwarves; **best road-builders in Helgedad**. Famous **Case
   di Guarigione** (Healing Houses) by the temple of **Hudemia** — treat rich and poor
   alike on a sliding scale; train Helgedad's best healers (and biggest charlatans).
5. **Nortmaar** (free land, hill country bordering the Landa Gelida) — warrior
   **Vorskgen** clans (native to Helgedad), united ~50 yrs ago under the **Ulding** (head
   **Fjorek Ulding**), seat **Nortcastle** (the only sizeable town). Ancestor cult: each
   clan venerates its founder as a god and guards a **sacred weapon** the founder
   "invented"; clan priests are warrior-specialists in that weapon. Clan chiefs form a
   **Consiglio** meeting yearly/in crisis.
6. **Seamark** (small but among the richest) — House **Ognir** (head **Eben Ognir**),
   port **Harbor**. Master sailors trading with Letia, the Regni Belligeranti, the Isole
   d'Argento, even Sadhir. Worship the triple female goddess set **Hudemia, Diis, and
   Kur** `[CONFLICT]` — canon lists the third as **Thal'Khal**; raw wiki says **Kur**.
   Temple: the **Torre Splendente** (Shining Tower).

**Other places/peoples:**
- **Dwarves:** mostly in the **Aule Dorate** (Golden Halls, Monti Aestyr — largest/most
  populous) and ancient **Stonebridge** (Monti di Bruma — oldest, partly abandoned).
  Legend: all dwarf holds linked by old tunnels; below the Aule Dorate mines lie
  **older, non-dwarf passages** from a mysterious past.
- **Monti di Bruma:** nesting site of the **Grandi Aquile** (Great Eagles) `[NEW]`;
  goblins in the roots.
- **Monti Aestyr:** once home of the sorcerer **Signore dell'Oblio** (Lord of Oblivion),
  whose labyrinthine prison-fortress is hidden there.
- **La Landa Gelida** (Frozen Waste, far north): giants, frost trolls, beasts; ancient
  ruins in the eternal ice; the **Castello di Ghiaccio** (Ice Castle), seat of the
  **Criomanti** (Cryomancers) — a powerful sect of frost-mages `[NEW]`.
- **L'Esercito dei Lupi** (Army of the Wolves) `[DEEPENING]`: a new horde of orcs,
  trolls, and demonic creatures vomited from deep underground, led by **5 dark generals**,
  based on the **Monti Aestyr**, attacking the Aule Dorate and the valleys below.
  (Corresponds to canon's "Wars of the Wolves 3015–3024.")

## Alfheim — the Light Elves `[CANON]`
- **Demography:** ~**6.5 million**, mostly **Elfi** (high + sylvan).
- Homeland of the **elfi chiari** (light elves); a vast forested realm with many elven
  settlements, the greatest being the capital **Ost-en-Galad** (High Elvish: "the Shining
  City" / *Città Splendente*).
- Elves are cordial to other races and travel to human/dwarf realms, but outsiders
  (mostly merchants) rarely reach inside Alfheim: cities are hidden and roads guarded by
  **royal rangers** (ranger reali) and less conventional means.
- **Government:** a **Queen** and her **royal consort** preside over the **Concilio**
  (Council of the leading elven aristocracy). The Council recommends policy; the Queen
  approves or overrides it. (Canon: line of **Inanna the Splendent**.)

## Gaerwath — the Dark Elves / "Sea of Sorcery" `[CANON]` `[DEEPENING — MAJOR]`
- **Demography:** ~**50 million (3000 d.M.)** falling to **35 million (3500 d.M.)**,
  predominantly **Elfi**.
- **Geography:** *Gaerwath* = Elvish **"Mare della Stregoneria"** (Sea of Sorcery). Not
  always an archipelago — **before the Catastrofe it was one landmass with the continent
  and the Isole d'Argento.** Surrounding seas are storm-torn and treacherous; crossable
  only by expert or magic-guided ships, and never without an elven guide. Each island is
  nearly wholly occupied by an elf city ("a masterpiece of dark beauty"); cities (and,
  some say, the islands themselves) are linked by **underground roads and magic bridges**.
  Magic is foundational — not knowing it means being an outcast or a slave.
- **Society (caste order):** rigidly racist — **Elar** (Elves) vs **Nemyel** (Non-Elves,
  rightless serfs). Elves are grouped into **Lyar** (lit. "Names," i.e. clans), split
  across three social castes called **Nath**:
  - **Galadlyan** ("Shining Name") — top caste; the **imperial family** alone, headed by
    the Emperor.
  - **Celeblyar** — high aristocracy.
  - **Mallyar** — lesser/poorer families.
  `[CONFLICT]` Canon renders the order as "**Lyar/Nath/Gash**"; the raw wiki gives the
  three Nath as **Galadlyan / Celeblyar / Mallyar** (no "Gash"). Reconcile — the wiki
  names look authoritative. Some mobility between the two lower castes via arranged
  marriage. Patriarchal; arranged lifelong marriages (divorce allowed); each spouse may
  keep up to **five concubines** (any race/sex).
- **Religion:** elves say they come from **Niethel** ("The Source" / *La Fonte*), a
  paradise where they were immortal and fed on the gods' nectar, until a great sin
  (attributed to an ambitious ancestor of **Queen Inanna** who led a revolt against the
  gods) got them exiled. A prophecy promises a **Sacro Regno** (Sacred Realm) where the
  elves must await a **Messaggero** heralding the gods' return; Dark Elves believe **Ymir
  IS that Sacred Realm** and it is their right/duty to conquer it. Informal worship + a
  formal clergy answering to the **Emperor (also supreme pontiff)**. Chief gods: **Valrin**
  (warrior god of holy war) and **Myethel** (goddess of magic/oracles).
- **Morannagul l'Eterno:** king of Gaerwath **2198–3131 d.M.** (suspected still alive in
  3518). `[CONFLICT]` Canon says the Dark Elves were "unified under Morannagul (2550)";
  the raw wiki dates his reign 2198–3131 — reconcile the unification date vs reign span.
- **The Five Factions (emerged ~3000–3500 d.M.)** `[NEW — HIGH VALUE]` — philosophical
  schools disputing the correct path to **Niethel** (now mostly held to be a *moment in
  time* reachable only via physical + spiritual **immortality**), listed by popularity:
  1. **Meccanisti** (Mechanists) — ~3/10 of elves; oldest theorised faction, built on the
     writings of **Morannagul l'Eterno**. Their art, **Meccanomagia**, drew (secretly) on
     the work of the **Humans of Armora**; focused not on building objects but on
     replacing mortal body parts with durable **meccanomagic artificial organs**
     (cybernetics). *Ties Gaerwath directly to Armora.*
  2. **Demonologi** (Demonologists) — ~3/10; largest by number, 2nd by political power.
     Rose as a faction only after **3026 d.M.**, when demon contact became physical due to
     **the Invasion** and the birth of the **Isola dei Demoni** (Isle of Demons) in Ymir's
     **southern hemisphere** `[NEW place]`. In **3131** their overreach caused the **Caduta
     di Morannagul**: a ritual summoned one of the **Nuovi Signori dei Demoni** onto
     Gaerwath, which took Morannagul's soul and confined it to the plane of Chaos.
  3. **Biomanti** (Biomancers) — ~2/10; grew from the **"Circolo dei Puristi"** (from 3030)
     opposing Meccanomagia/Demonology. Invented **Genomagia** from studies of an **Armorite
     criminal mage** (acquired pre-3026 by corrupting the **"Veri Veggenti"** organisation);
     magic/alchemy to boost racial **Purity** and physical/mental potential.
  4. **Sognatori** (Dreamers) — ~15%; the **oldest** faction philosophically, codified as a
     political body only after the **Decreto delle Fazioni (3096)**. Believe reaching
     **Niethel** requires finding a precise spacetime point **inside the Mondo dei Sogni**
     (Dream World) — not merely becoming immortal. *Ties Gaerwath to the Ymir Dreamlands.*
  5. **Necromanti** (Necromancers) — ~1/20; founded by **Wehyrr il Maledetto**, a powerful
     Demonologist turned **Lich** (by a demon's curse), expelled from the Demonologist
     Circle. Imported necromancy from distant lands, **especially the desert of Khorish**.
     Denied Council access and political power; tolerated but shunned. *Ties Gaerwath to
     Khorish.*
  - **I Non Schierati** (the Unaligned): interdisciplinary researchers who refuse a faction;
    hunted in the **Caccia agli Indipendenti (3096)** ordered by Morannagul.
  - **Il Consiglio** (the Council) has elected members from the factions (Necromanti
    excluded).

## Xebech — reptilian jungle homeland `[CANON]`
- **Demography:** ~**50 million**, predominantly **Xebechani**.
- Lush jungles; homeland of **the oldest civilisation on Ymir**. Millennial **city-temples**
  of masterwork architecture seem to grow from the foliage or mountainsides.
- **Vere Vie** (True Ways): every Xebechani city has at least one — a **magic portal** for
  near-instant travel. Legend says many more Vere Vie lie hidden across Ymir, once allowing
  world-spanning travel.
- **Loquitchatzotech** (Xebechano: **Isle of Death**): a vast abandoned territory, avoided
  by all; covered by a **fetid sulfurous swamp**, with a small **volcanic chain** at its
  centre hiding the ruins of the **Città Senza Nome** (City Without a Name). The whole isle
  is cursed, full of monsters and undead. (Canon: lost in a 75-yr undead war 2020–2095.)

## Sadhir — the salt desert `[CANON]` `[CONFLICT — MAJOR]`
- **Demography:** ~**18 million**.
- A relatively **young land** created by environmental upheaval from **La Catastrofe**: once
  a small archipelago, now that the sea withdrew it became a **desert**.
- Two largest cities, **Al-Jasul** and **Al-Sidath** — the only ones predating the Catastrofe.
  Elsewhere few big cities; small settlements and temporary nomad camps.
- **Il Deserto Scintillante** (the Shining Desert): a hostile central zone where salt-mixed
  sand creates strange optical **illusions**; locals say it **expands a few cm/year** with no
  known cause — if true, it endangers all the desert's peoples.
- `[CONFLICT — MAJOR]` This thin raw wiki page **disagrees with the much richer Sadhir in
  canon** (drawn from a separate "Sadhir gazetteer"). Canon has a **matriarchal lizardfolk
  Sultanate of the Sarradhi**, hermit-crab folk, and cities **Zarash-Sur / Kalzharan /
  Moluth-Har / Qarsaan / Tuleph / Nashifet**, plus the **Blue Oyster Cult of the demon Ea**,
  drying ~2400 d.M. The wiki page mentions **none** of that and instead names **Al-Jasul** and
  **Al-Sidath** as the principal (pre-Catastrophe) cities, with no lizardfolk. The two accounts
  need reconciling; the wiki page may be an older/thinner draft. Only shared element: the
  **Deserto Scintillante** and the sea-to-desert cataclysm origin. (Note: **Al-Sidah** appears
  as an unplaced world-map label; likely = wiki **Al-Sidath**.)

## Il Grande Erg & Khorish/Korish `[CANON]` `[DEEPENING — HIGH VALUE]`
- **Demography:** ~**20 million**.
- The **largest desert of Ymir**, containing three zones: **Ulan-Tang**, the **Distesa Arida**,
  and the **Regno di Korish**. Home to nomad raiders and traders seeking perfumes, spices,
  exotic woods and oils — above all the **Loto Nero** (Black Lotus) `[NEW]`, a very rare flower
  yielding drugs that "open the doors of the mind" and boost magical power.

**ULAN-TANG** `[DEEPENING — HIGH VALUE]` (canon had only "ancient portal where Sguardo-sul-Nulla
entered Ymir, 3026"):
- A **city-state** perched on a rocky hill amid the desert, beside the **river Tang** (its
  namesake); a major trade hub where merchants from the neighbouring **Regni Giovani** and the
  desert meet. Once a free city dedicated to the goddess **Ishtar**, for centuries fallen under
  the worshippers of **Yogh, the dark spider-god**, becoming a place of perversion and corruption.
- A great **tendopoli** (tent-city) of nomads and the poor rings the walls; the city climbs the
  hill, crowned by the **great temple of Yogh**. **No real law** — everyone fends for themselves;
  the great families rule via private guards, assassins, healers, mages.
- **Gran Sacerdote di Yogh: Sazar-Gadash lo Stregone** (the Sorcerer) — supreme authority; said
  to be a **demon's son** (some say son of **Asmodeus** himself). He assassinated his predecessor
  **Azim-Mulath**.
- **Noble families** (color-coded robes):
  - **Ishra** (head **Bal-Ishra**) — great mages/summoners; feared; **Tuniche Rosse** (red).
  - **Tyarreth** (head **Yugal-Tyarreth**) — alchemists & drug-sellers; **Vesti Verde Oliva**
    (olive green).
  - **Miraz** (head **Bozisha-Miraz**) — Seers & spies; little escapes them; even the High Priest
    consults them; **Mantelli Azzurri** (blue hooded cloaks). (The predecessor's murder is rumoured
    to have followed a slight by Azim-Mulath against a Miraz.)
  - **Gaib** (head **Baras-Gaib**) — shadow-manipulators & illusionists; rumoured close ties to the
    **Azar-Kalish** `[NEW term]`; dress in **Nero** (black).
  - **Gadash** (head **Sazar-Gadash**) — now hold the Temple offices; **Tuniche Violacee** (purple).

**DISTESA ARIDA** (Arid Expanse): the vast majority of the Grande Erg — desolate sand and dunes
broken by oases; almost uninhabited except nomad camps of **Ur-Tanoth, Men, or Orcs** (usually
around oases).

**REGNO DI KORISH** `[DEEPENING]` (canon spells it **Khorish**; the raw wiki uses both **Khorish**
and **Korish**):
- A relatively small kingdom along part of the Erg's coast, but ancient — founded **~1000 years
  ago, around the end of the 23rd century** per official chronicles. `[CONFLICT — minor]` Canon
  dates Khorish's founding to **2160–2180 d.M.** (22nd century); the raw wiki says "end of the
  23rd century" — a ~100–150 yr discrepancy to reconcile.
- Ruled by the **two Faraoni Eterni** (Eternal Pharaohs): **Athmokethepek I** and his wife **Nefthi**
  `[NEW: Nefthi]`, who claim to be immortal gods-on-earth demanding unconditional worship. (Canon
  already names Athmokethepek I as Khorish's founder.)
- **Necromancy** is widespread: all the dead are embalmed and reanimated by **priest-functionaries**
  to serve the pharaohs forever as laborers. *(Ties to Gaerwath's Necromanti, who imported necromancy
  from Khorish.)*

## Isole d'Argento — the Silver Isles `[CANON]` `[DEEPENING]`
- **Demography:** ~**1.2 million**; races **Faeries (Folletti), Elves, Sohleguir**.
- Archipelago **south-east of Letia**, mild/temperate climate. Home of the **greatest Folletti
  (faerie) realm on Ymir**, capital **Elendor**, which also hosts the **Seggio di Cristallo**

- `[DEEPENING]` The **southernmost isles** host a **small but flourishing ducato di Uomini Lucertola**
  (Duchy of Lizard Men) `[NEW]`, on good terms with the Folletti.

  other realms as **neutral territory**.

## Cartosa e Meridian — swashbuckling South `[CANON]` `[DEEPENING — HIGH VALUE]`
- **Demography:** ~**35 million**; High/Mixed/Common Men, Dwarves, Tanoth, Orcs.
- **Cartosa** is the capital of a large southern territory (the **Principato**). A people of fishing
  and trade; Cartosan ships prized worldwide for maneuverability. Notorious as the **"città dei ladri"**
  (city of thieves) — pirates, buccaneers; some pirates are secretly **crown-funded** to kill off
  competition. Warm most of the year with violent squalls/**tifoni** (typhoons), esp. summer. The
  **entroterra** (interior) is far less civilised — jungles, semi-arid/near-desert patches near the
  big mountain ranges, sparse small settlements, **barbarian tribes of wild dwarves** and hostile
  creatures. Only the great city of **Meridian** stands out inland (with river access via the fiume).
  `[CONFLICT — soft]` Canon (from a fuller sourcebook) treats **Meridian** as a rival **Repubblica**
  on a second island; this raw wiki treats Meridian merely as a large city within/near the Principato.
  Reconcile.
- **Government:** **Principe Alberto Carrillo di Cartosa V**; each city answers to a **Governatore**
  appointed by the Prince. (Confirms canon's "Carrillo.")
- **Guilds/orgs** `[NEW]`:
  - **La Guardia d'Oro** — the land army; peacetime city police.
  - **La Marina** — the navy; supreme in all sea matters (piracy, smuggling); the **Ammiraglio** is
    among the realm's most powerful figures, able to act without consulting the Prince.
  - **Ordine Ermetico dei Manti Azzurri** — the official mage guild (allied with the Marina); mostly

  - **Le Vele Rosse** (Red Sails) — crown-authorised **privateers** (red sail = their mark); may prey

  - **La Società delle Ombre** (Society of Shadows) — the illegal thieves' guild (theft, robbery,

    the non-living."
  - **Gilda dei Mercanti** — a private merchant association open to all nationalities.
  - **Compagnia degli Scudi** (Company of Shields) — mercenary/adventurer hiring hall; sometimes backs
    the Guardia d'Oro in remote interior towns.
- **Cults:** **Orum** (sea god, patron of sailors — Cartosa's biggest temple), **Ranin** (patron of
  merchants/transactions), **Heis** (chance/luck). Interior tribes worship many gods. A **Mithra
  mission** of clerics from Letia sits just outside Cartosa (to convert wild tribes) — resented, since
  Cartosa–Letia relations are unfriendly; the Prince tolerates it to avoid an economically ruinous war.
- **Peoples:** mostly **Umani Misti**; **Nani** (dwarves) prized artisans and **sole keepers of the
  Polvere Nera / Black Powder formula**; **Tal-Tanoth** live cheerfully among humans in cities while
  larger **Ur-Tanoth** shun contact in the forests; **Elfi** uncommon; **Xebechani** essentially none
  (only passers-through); **Orchetti/mezz'orchetti/goblin** in pirate crews — e.g. **Bagrat il mezz'orco's**
  feared crew including **sea trolls**.
- **Le Tribù Selvagge** (wild interior tribes) `[NEW — voodoo/Afro-Caribbean flavor]`:
  - **Nkishi** (Orcs) — ferociously brave; ritual **scarification**; shamans **Bokkor** invoke ancestor
    spirits and dark forest gods (**Orisha**); the most feared summon the ancient **Mayombe** spirits.
  - **Arawak** (Dwarves) — master workers of **stone and bone** who **reject metal**; adorn hair with
    gems to mark deeds/status; prefer death to capture. Their mountains are rich in **Shald** `[NEW]` — a
    special **magic glass** prized for many properties; expeditions to seize it die so often the Prince
    banned them.
  - **Ur-Tanoth** — nomad hunters; unrivalled woodland lore; masters of woodcraft; reachable only via
    the Tal-Tanoth.
  - **M'Bantha** (Goblins) — jungle-edge raiders; believe humans' everyday objects hold magical **"Cargo"**

    trinkets/talismans.
- **Magic & medicine:** magic is public and tightly controlled through the Manti Azzurri (protection,
  travel, healing, entertainment; bards may use magic if registered). Medicine relatively advanced
  (simple surgery, some cured diseases) but no vaccines/penicillin; healing herbs rare and costly;
  magical/divine healing exists but is expensive and hard to access.

## Kyria — the Misty Isle `[CANON]` `[DEEPENING — HIGH VALUE]`
*(Canon had only "wrapped in the Mists ~1710; nature unknown." This is the full description.)*
- **Demography:** ~**500,000**; predominantly **Ur-Tanoth**.
- A large, wild island in the **north-west of Ymir**, **perennially wrapped in fog**, dominated by high
  mountains and tangled forests. Lies nearly as far north as Helgedad but is **not as cold**.
- The **Tanoth** — a strange people of **beast-humanoids** — claim Kyria as their homeland. They build
  neither cities nor villages, so the **only settlement on the whole island is Thonis** `[NEW]`, founded
  by a group of **human and elf refugees**.
- **Untouched by the Catastrofe**; isolated from the world since the **Guerre Demoniache** (Demonic Wars).
  Thonis's people just farm and want to be left alone; the reclusive, independent Tanoth oblige. Most
  Ymirians **don't even know Kyria exists.**

## Ymir Dreamlands — La Terra dei Sogni (the Dream World) `[NEW REGION / PLANE — HIGH VALUE]`
*(Canon cosmology mentions the Dream World reached via Armora's Oniroscopio; this is its full write-up.
It is a **plane**, not a physical region, but is the "new region" flagged for this batch.)*
- A **world unto itself, bound to Ymir** (and perhaps to other worlds). **All souls of Ymir's dreamers
  pass into it.** Most participate only as **passive spectators**; a few — called **Sognatori**,
  **Viaggiatori** (Travellers), or other names — move through it **consciously**. *(Directly connects to
  the Gaerwath **Sognatori** faction seeking Niethel here.)*
- **Interacting:** two means — special **abilities/skills** or **magic**. The hard part is entering while
  keeping control of one's faculties. A conscious dreamer can use all skills/spells normally, but **failed
  actions/spells produce strange, far more dangerous effects** than the equivalent failure on Ymir.
  *(Source flags ToDo tables for dream-world maneuver/spell failures — not yet written.)*
- **Materialisation:** objects can rarely cross between worlds; cross-world objects, if forged on Ymir, are
  almost always made from **magical materials originating in the Maelstrom** *(ties to cosmology)*.
  Materialising **creatures** onto Ymir is far rarer — **the gods themselves oppose it**, to stop the
  **outer gods** (worshipped by the **Xebechani**) from gaining more direct influence over Ymir.
- **Divinity:** the Dream World's pantheon **is the outer gods** (**dèi esterni**) — superior to Ymir's
  gods in raw power but able to touch the real world only indirectly. The dream world is far easier for
  them to influence — hence the grave mental risk to conscious travellers. *(Ties Xebechani religion,
  cosmology's outer gods, and the Sognatori together.)*
- **Creatures:**
  - **Fauni** (Fauns) — Ymir creatures known for interacting with the Dreamlands; enchant guests with
    **fairy songs**, fusing the oneiric and real worlds into waking dreams.
  - **Orrori** (Horrors) — generally malign/idiot-minded, monstrous:
    - **Esseri Striscianti** (Crawling Things) — formless horrors creeping the darkest dream-places.
    - **Magri Notturni** (Night-Gaunts) — rubbery, cold, damp, faceless humanoids with **bat wings**;
      move and fly in total silence.
  - **Incubi** — **distinct from demonic incubi**; native dream creatures ranging from playful to wholly
    evil, **classed like demons**. Demonic-summoning spells cast in the Dreamlands summon Incubi of power
    proportional to the demon that would be summoned on Ymir; normally confined to dream territories by
    invisible forces.
  - **Dèmoni** — demons summonable on Ymir **cannot reach the Dream World at all**.
  - **Sogni** (Dreams) — the entities **opposite to Incubi**; almost always benevolent (with notable
    exceptions).

## Cross-region connective tissue (worth noting for the world bible)
- **Armora ↔ Gaerwath:** Gaerwath's **Meccanomagia** (Meccanisti) and **Genomagia** (Biomanti, via a
  criminal Armorite mage and the "Veri Veggenti") both derive from **Armorite human research** — a
  concrete link between the steampunk Young Kingdoms and the Dark Elves.
- **Khorish ↔ Gaerwath:** Gaerwath's **Necromanti** (founded by the Lich Wehyrr) imported necromancy from
  **Khorish**, whose Faraoni Eterni reanimate all their dead as labor.
- **Dream World ↔ Gaerwath / Xebech:** the **Sognatori** seek Niethel inside the Dream World; the Dream
  World's **outer gods** are the gods the **Xebechani** worship.
- **Isola dei Demoni** (southern hemisphere, born of "the Invasion" ~3026) `[NEW place]` underpins the
  rise of Gaerwath's Demonologi and connects to Ulan-Tang's **Sguardo-sul-Nulla** entry (3026) in canon.
