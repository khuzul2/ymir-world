# Ymir World Bible — Creatures, Dragons & Monstrous Beings

Digest extracted from the fan wiki source files:
- `sources/raw/creature.txt` (general bestiary — 7 creatures)
- `sources/raw/draghi.txt` (dragons — the 20 True Dragons, taxonomy, stat blocks)

Original Italian creature/proper names are preserved. English glosses follow in
parentheses. Tags: **[CANON]** = stated in source; **[INFERRED]** = reconstructed
from garbled text / context; **[CONFLICT]** = tension with existing world-bible canon;
**[DEEPENING]** = source adds detail to an existing canon entry.

Note on source: text is Italian with HTML markup (stripped here) and frequent `?`
substitutions for accented letters/apostrophes, reconstructed below.

## Stat-line glossary (Rolemaster-derived ruleset)

The bestiary uses a game stat block. Abbreviations, for reference:
- **Lvl** = Level (the trailing `?` is a source artifact, not a real value — e.g. "Lvl: 15?")
- **Tag / Taglia** = Size category (Large, Super Large, etc.); `#`/`@` are crit-size markers
- **PF** = Punti Ferita (Hit Points)
- **TA(BD)** = Armor Type (Defensive Bonus) — Rolemaster AT(DB)
- **Attacchi/Attachi** = Attacks (OB values); **Iniziativa** = Initiative
- **TR** = Tiro Resistenza (Resistance Roll / RR); **PM** = Punti Magia (Power Points)
- **Co** = Costituzione (Constitution); **BD/BO** = Bonus Difesa / Offesa
- Attack codes: L=Large, Bi=Bite, Cl=Claw, Ba=Bash, Ho=Horn; criticals graded A/B/C… down to slaying

# PART 1 — CREATURES (`creature.txt`)

All 7 entries below are **NEW** relative to the supplied canon fauna list
(Daretas, Reptoidi, Yarhim, Sadhir creatures, Helgedad troll, Great Eagles,
Ibridi/Marchiati, Automata).

## Fungalidi
**[CANON]** Lvl 15 · Large · PF 180 · TA(BD) 1(60) · Init +12
Attacks: 140 Large Claw [Acid] / Poison.

- **Description:** Bizarre fungoid beings vaguely resembling giant **mouthless toads**,
  covered in a strange grayish spongy substance. In place of legs they have two long
  tentacles ending in sharp bony protrusions; they also bear robust **dragonfly wings**.
- **Abilities/Danger:**
  - Ignore all critical hits from **Void, Nether, Cutting (Taglio), and Acid**.
  - **Regenerate 3 PF per round** until killed.
  - Fungous skin secretes potent acid → deliver an **additional Acid critical** on any hit.
  - Claws inject a **paralyzing poison** (RR, or paralysis for 1 round per 10% of failure).
- **Habitat/Origin:** Not stated. **[INFERRED]** fungal ecology suggests damp/subterranean.

## Magres
**[CANON]** Lvl 35 · Super Large · PF 500 · TA(BD) 9(35) · Init +4
Attacks: 220 Huge Trample / 215 Huge Bite.

- **Description:** A chaos-born abomination. A towering mass of grayish, hairy-to-scaly
  flesh, roughly **7 meters tall**. Grotesquely oversized mouth lined with thousands of
  small sharp teeth; tiny piggish eyes; two pathetically small hands flailing uselessly
  at mid-body. Moves on dozens of small feet sprouting from its underside.
- **Origin (lore):** Scholars claim Magres are **coboldi (kobolds) radically mutated by
  prolonged exposure to the Caos (Chaos)**. Truth of this is uncertain.
  → **[DEEPENING]** Connects to the canon theme of Maelstrom/Chaos-mutated beings
  (Ibridi/Marchiati). A base race (kobolds) warped into a monster by Chaos exposure.
- **Behavior/Danger:** Stupid, wandering with no real awareness of their surroundings.
  **Incredibly cowardly** — flee the moment they are wounded or perceive any threat
  (basically anything human-sized or larger). The danger is that in fleeing they
  **trample everything in their path**, sowing terror and destruction. Fortunately few
  in number, described as a "living scourge."

## Ombra dell'Ostrica Blu (Blue Oyster Shadow)
Two grades: **Minore (Minor)** and **Maggiore (Major)**.
- **Minor:** Lvl 8 · Tag II# · PF 80 · TA(BD) 1(40) · Init +12 · Attacks: Special
- **Major:** Lvl 20 · Large# · PF 150 · TA(BD) 9(40) · Init +12 · Attacks: Special

- **Origin (lore):** These are the **spectral remains of the mad cultists who tried to
  summon the Demon Lord Ea onto Ymir**, and who died in the catastrophic failure of the
  ritual meant to call their lord.
  → **[DEEPENING] of canon Ea.** Existing canon: Ea is a Sovereign Demon (Demone Sovrano),
  now **dead**. This source establishes that a cult attempted to summon Ea to Ymir, the
  ritual failed catastrophically and killed the cultists, and their spirits linger as
  these shadows. Ties the "dead Sovereign Demon Ea" to a named failed-summoning event.
- **Minor Shadows:** Appear as **evanescent skulls** emitting a faint bluish glow,
  floating in mid-air. No physical attacks; **cannot be struck by non-magical weapons**.
  Sole attack: drain **1d10+10 PF and 1 point of Constitution** (RR-15 to resist) from
  all living beings within 3m.
- **Major Shadows:** Spirits of the more powerful cultists; **hooded, faceless figures in
  long blue tunics** wreathed in a faint azure aura. Drain **1d10+20 PF and 1d4
  Constitution** (RR at -20). They know the lists of the **Essenza Oscura (Dark Essence,
  a.k.a. the Stregoni/Sorcerers)** up to their level, with **6 PM per level**. Being
  ethereal, they can only be hit by magical weapons.

## Phlegetauro (pl. Phlegetauri)
**[CANON]** Lvl 25 (VI tipo / **Type VI demon**) · Large# · PF 300 · TA(BD) 12(60) · Init +8
Attacks: 120 Large Claw / 120 Large Bite (x3) / 60 Fire Breath.

- **Description:** A rare kind of **greater demon (demone maggiore)** dwelling in dark outer
  planes with a fiery atmosphere; also found on Ymir underground or near volcanoes. A dark,
  coal-black humanoid **over 3.5 m tall**, with **three horned heads on three serpentine
  necks**.
- **Abilities/Danger:**
  - Wreathed in **everlasting flames** that inflict a "C" heat critical to anyone within 2m.
  - Breathes cones of fire from each mouth — each of the three heads can breathe once every
    6 rounds.
  - Knows the **"Fire Law"** list up to its level and casts one spell per round at **no PM cost**.
  - Attacks with three breaths, claws, or bite.
  - **On death, explodes in a Fireball +60** centered on itself.
  - **Immune to fire/heat**, but takes **double damage from water and cold** (cold criticals
    count as "Slaying").
- **Classification note:** **[INFERRED/CONFLICT-watch]** Labeled "VI tipo" (Type VI). Existing
  canon organizes Demons into **six Circles**. Whether "Type VI" maps to the sixth Circle
  (greatest?) or is a separate classification axis is unclear — flag for reconciliation. The
  name derives from **Phlegethon**, the river of fire of the underworld.

## Relitto (Wreck)
**[CANON]** Lvl 12 · Grande/Grande# (Large/Large#) · PF 250 · TA(BD) 20(30) · Init +0
Attacks: 100 Weapon [Cold/Gelo] (x2) / Special.

- **Description:** Hollow, rust-corroded armor with creaking mechanical joints that drip oil
  like blood from a dying soldier's wounds, shrouded in a grim aura of rage and pain.
- **Origin (lore):** **Possessed constructs** — the remains of ancient war-machines abandoned
  on battlefields among the corpses of the fallen. The wealthiest, most advanced empires hire
  **master alchemists** to forge tireless, unstoppable iron-and-steel warriors to crush their
  enemies. Even after such a construct's metal body is destroyed and its animating spells are
  dispersed, an "echo of death" lingers, which sometimes draws in the **spirits of warriors
  killed in battle**; these possess the wreck to make war again — this time against all the
  living.
  → **[DEEPENING]** Bridges the canon **Automata/golems** entry with necromancy: alchemically
  forged war-constructs that become undead-possessed after destruction. Some Relitti are
  "welded together" from several different constructs by necromantic energies (so each looks
  unique and wields different weapons).
- **Abilities/Danger:**
  - Undead icy aura → attacks inflict an **additional Cold critical one grade lower** than the
    main hit (an "A" becomes "A-20").
  - Every living creature within 3m must make a **Constitution RR each round or lose 1–5 Co**;
    a victim reduced to 0 Co has their **soul drawn into the Relitto**, which then gains 3 PF
    per level of the victim.
  - Anyone nearby hears **whispers, curses, and muffled laments** (the trapped souls) and must
    make a **Fear RR or be seized by despair** (-10 to actions per 10% failure; at -100 or worse
    the victim collapses helpless).

## Slashquark
**[CANON]** Lvl 15 · Large · PF 150 · TA(BD) 11(40) · Init +16
Attacks: 120 Huge Bite / Special.

- **Description:** Large **green pigs** with wrinkled necks and a sucker-like mouth bearing
  **two rows of fangs**.
- **Abilities/Danger:** Attack by **suddenly extending the neck up to 3 m** to bite the prey.
  Once the fangs have seized the victim, the **second row of fangs snaps backward, stripping the
  flesh from the bones** — resolved as a second bite attack that counts only magical defenses.
  Said to be named for the sound their victims make during this process.

## Zaar'Qrak
**[CANON]** Lvl 20 · Large@ · PF 250 · TA(BD) 12(40) · Init +16
Attacks: 100 Huge Claw (x4) / 160 Large Bite.

- **Name:** In the **language of the Erg**, roughly "Morte Squartatrice" (**Ripping/Rending Death**).
- **Habitat:** The **Erg** (the Grande Erg / Great Erg desert). The most feared desert predators.
  → **[DEEPENING]** Same region as the Earth Dragon **Selebkher delle Sabbie** (see Dragons).
- **Description:** Vaguely humanoid beasts **over 3.5 m tall**, covered in bristly black fur and
  very hard bony plates. Nocturnal, with **perfect vision in any darkness**. Six-limbed: two
  stouter limbs used only as legs, and four others usable as either legs or arms; all six end in
  **dexterous, monkey-like paws** with dagger-long, razor-sharp claws.
- **Abilities/Danger:**
  - In combat mainly use the upper limbs, **attacking up to 4 different targets in one round**;
    if starving or cornered they also use their long fangs.
  - Cunning ambush hunters — do not fear man, know weapons (but never use them), prefer swift
    ambushes over frontal assault. In their native terrain they have the equivalent of **+100
    Stalking and +100 Hiding**.
  - **Incredible leaps** (as the **Leap** spell) — used to land amid caravans, mangle people, and
    flee carrying off a victim as a snack.
  - If pressed they become extraordinarily ferocious and lightning-fast (as if with **120 Adrenal
    Speed**).
  - Fortunately **solitary** — the worst-case encounter is a **mother with cubs**.

# PART 2 — DRAGONS (`draghi.txt`)

## Origin & nature of the "Veri Draghi" (True Dragons)
**[CANON]** Ymir hosts a **limited number of "Veri Draghi" (True Dragons)** — major dragons whose
nature is **bound to the elements**. There are exactly **twenty (20)**.

- **Creation:** The True Dragons were **created by the Adepti (the Adepts)** as the **ultimate
  biological weapon** — mighty living fortresses able to overcome almost any foe.
  → **[NEW LORE]** The **Adepti** are a precursor/ancestral civilization not named in the supplied
  canon. They possessed a high **techno-magical** level, then **mysteriously vanished**. Before
  disappearing they set the Dragons as **guardians of the secrets of Ymir**, with a specific
  mission: **prevent the planet's inhabitants from getting too close to "the Truth"** or evolving
  enough to risk reaching the Adepts' own techno-magical level.
- **Present day:** Over the millennia **few Dragons stayed faithful** to that charge — the **Dei-Drago
  (Dragon-Gods) of the Qi-Long** being the most glaring example of the faithful — while each of the
  rest began pursuing its own **personal agenda**.
  → **[DEEPENING] of canon Qi-Long "Dei-Drago."** Confirms the Dragon-Gods of the Qi-Long as a
  subset of the True Dragons and identifies them by name (see #2–#7 below).

**[CONFLICT — flag for reconciliation]:** Supplied canon states dragons include **"Grandi Vermi"
(Great Worms) that appear at the world's dawn** and **the mightiest Hybrid Elementals**. This source
instead frames the 20 True Dragons as **artificial biological weapons engineered by the Adepti**, not
primordial dawn-of-the-world beings. Possible resolutions: (a) the "Grandi Vermi / dawn dragons" are a
distinct, older category separate from these 20 engineered True Dragons; or (b) the Adepti bred the
True Dragons *from* primordial dragon stock. Note also that the Qi-Long Earth Dragon
**Weichong-Shanxuan** is titled **"il Possente Verme"** ("the Mighty Worm") and the Eastern/Storm
Dragon is explicitly a **mixed elemental hybrid** — echoing the canon "Great Worms" and "mightiest
Hybrid Elementals" phrasings. Unresolved; needs canon adjudication.

## Minor dragons
**[CANON]** Beyond the twenty True Dragons there is an **unspecified number of minor dragons** —
**drakes, winged beasts, viverne (wyverns), serpenti (serpents), etc.** — of varied size and nature,
scattered across the planet.

## Dragon taxonomy — variant types (deviations from the "C&M" manual)
The listed dragons carry some variations relative to those presented in the **C&M** (the base
"Creature & Mostri" / Creatures & Monsters ruleset). Documented variant types:

### Drago del Gas / dell'Aria (Gas / Air Dragon)
Two varieties:
1. The manual's standard **poisonous breath**.
2. An **ultrasonic scream** as its breath weapon — resolved on the **Force Bolt** table with BO by age:
   **80 / 100 / 120** (young / mature / old).

### Drago Orientale (Eastern Dragon) — a.k.a. "Drago delle Tempeste" (Storm Dragon)
- A **mixed elemental hybrid of Ice, Light, and Water**; its **blood corrupts** the victim with one
  of these three elements at random.
  → **[DEEPENING]** Matches the canon note that dragons include "the mightiest **Hybrid Elementals**."
- Casts spells from the **Storm Mage** lists; its **breath is High Element (Light)**.

### Drago della Terra (Earth Dragon)
- A **pure elemental Earth dragon**; has all traits of normal Great Dragons plus the stats below.
- **Breath:** a cone of **magnetic/gravitational force** (resolve on the **Force Bolt** table).
- **No Earth Dragon has wings.**
- **Habitat:** deserts, mountains, and underground complexes.
- **[INFERRED]** the True Dragon **Selebkher delle Sabbie** (#15, Great Erg) and **Dwmballa il
  Cangiante** (#17, Ophiur) are of this type.

# SUMMARY OF FINDINGS

## Notable NEW creatures (not in supplied canon)
- **Fungalidi** — regenerating fungoid flyers; acid + paralytic poison; immune to Void/Nether/Cut/Acid crits.
- **Magres** — 7 m chaos-mutated *kobolds*; cowardly but catastrophically destructive when they flee (ties to Chaos/Maelstrom mutation theme).
- **Ombra dell'Ostrica Blu (Blue Oyster Shadow)** — Minor & Major undead cultist-shades from the **failed summoning of the demon Ea** (see below).
- **Phlegetauro** — three-headed, thrice-breathing **"Type VI" greater fire-demon**; explodes into a +60 Fireball on death.
- **Relitto (Wreck)** — undead-possessed alchemical war-construct; soul-draining icy aura (bridges Automata/golems + necromancy).
- **Slashquark** — extensible-neck green "pigs" with double fang-rows that flense flesh from bone.
- **Zaar'Qrak** — six-limbed, four-attack apex desert predators of the **Erg** ("Rending Death").

## Dragon taxonomy (concise)
- **20 "Veri Draghi" (True Dragons)**, elemental in nature, **engineered by the vanished Adepti** as
  living superweapons and set to guard Ymir's secrets. Only the **6 Qi-Long Dei-Drago (Dragon-Gods)**
  remain loyal; the rest pursue personal agendas.
- Elemental types observed among the 20: **Fire, Water, Earth, Ice, Air, Storm/Tempeste, Light,
  Nether, Spectral, Air(Force), Leviathan**.
- Plus an **unspecified number of minor dragons** (drakes, wyverns, serpents, winged beasts).
- **Variant types detailed:** Gas/Air Dragon (poison-breath OR ultrasonic-scream variety), Eastern/Storm
  Dragon (Ice+Light+Water elemental hybrid, Storm Mage caster, corrupting blood), Earth Dragon
  (wingless, magnetic/gravitational Force-cone breath; full age-scaled stat blocks provided).

## Contradictions / items to reconcile with canon
1. **[CONFLICT] Dragon origin:** Canon "Grandi Vermi appear at the world's dawn" vs. source "20 True
   Dragons **created by the Adepti** as biological weapons." Are the dawn Great Worms a separate, older
   class, or the primordial stock the Adepti bred from? (Note: #4 Weichong-Shanxuan is titled "the Mighty
   Worm"; the Eastern Dragon is a hybrid elemental — both echo canon phrasing.)
2. **[CLARIFY] Phlegetauro "Type VI":** Reconcile the demon's "VI tipo" label with the canon **six Circles
   of Demons** — same axis or different?
3. **[NEW LORE for canon] The Adepti:** A vanished, high techno-magical precursor civilization who created
   the Dragons and guard "the Truth" of Ymir — not present in the supplied canon; likely warrants its own
   canon entry.

## Deepenings of existing canon
- **Ea** (dead Sovereign Demon) → named **failed-summoning cult** whose ghosts are the Blue Oyster Shadows.
- **Gwalgamaur** → #8, "il Dorato," Fire Dragon of Wald (Young Kingdoms).
- **Qi-Long Dei-Drago** → six named Dragon-Gods (#2–#7), the only loyal True Dragons.
- **Helgedad** (troll & Great Eagles) → also home to the Ice Dragon **Nihoggr la Solitaria**.
- **Ibridi/Marchiati (Chaos-mutated)** → **Magres** = kobolds mutated by the Caos.
- **Automata/golems** → **Relitto** = destroyed war-construct re-animated/possessed by battlefield spirits.
