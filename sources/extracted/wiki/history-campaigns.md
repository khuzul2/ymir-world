## A1. L'Era della Marea — full detail (`lera-della-marea.txt`) `[CANON]`

This is the single richest chronology source in the batch. Canon already has the
opening beat (Year 0: six relics unseal the Runes, Sguardo wakes, the isolation bell
shatters, portals reactivate, the **Marea Stregata** sweeps out). Everything below is
**new / deeper than canon.**

### Cardinal events of the new era `[CANON]`
- The six Reliquie Divine strip the seals from the ancient **Rune del Potere**.
- **Sguardo-sul-Nulla awakens.**
- The Maelstrom's **"campana di isolamento"** (isolation bell) shatters — it had kept
  demons out, portals dead, and people trapped in.
- All the Adepts' portals reactivate **simultaneously**.
- The **Marea Stregata** (Bewitched Tide), a chaotic magical storm, pours out of the
  Maelstrom and wraps the whole world.
- **NEW:** The force-wall the Xebechani placed around the **Isola della Morte** (Isle
  of Death / Loquitchatzotech) **disintegrates**; its undead are loosed.
- **NEW — the Sovereign Demons' prison cracks.** A rift opens in the dimensional
  prison of the **Demoni Sovrani**; the demons fight each other to get out and **very
  many die.** Of the survivors, some reach Ymir **dragging a slice of the demonic
  world with them, which rises as a NEW CONTINENT on Ymir.** → `geography.md`, `cosmology.md`
- **NEW — only three Sovereign Demons reach Ymir:** `[CANON]`
  - **Asmodeus** — Signore della Tirannia (Lord of Tyranny)
  - **Xardas** — Signore dei Complotti (Lord of Plots/Conspiracies)
  - **Masak** — Signore delle Illusioni (Lord of Illusions)
  The rest die in the struggle or exhaust their strength trying to escape and stay
  **permanently trapped.** → `bestiary.md` (adds three named demons to the roster)
- **NEW — the "Vero Dio Drago" (True Dragon God) awakens.** (Identified below as
  **Ouroboros**.)

### Risonanza Eterea (Ethereal Resonance) `[CANON]` → `magic.md`
A phenomenon spawned by the Marea Stregata, concentrated around the **Gaerwath** and
the **Isole d'Argento**, where the Maelstrom's magical energy flooded already
enchantment-saturated lands. Effects:
- Magic is so dense it is **visible to the naked eye** in places (strange light-play,
  paranormal phenomena).
- Magic senses (e.g. *Detect Essence*) are **blinded** — like staring at the sun.
- Conversely such senses are nearly superfluous: every caster mid-spell is haloed
  with light; active spells glow.
- Spells are **invigorated** and far harder to dispel: **+50 to all RR vs Dispel and
  Cancel**; casters **cannot** automatically cancel their own spells (must wait them
  out).
- Magical energy regenerates faster: **spells cost 75% PM**; casters recover **double
  PM** resting.
- Spells are sometimes stronger than normal, or have bizarre side-effects.
- The zone draws crowds of magic-eating creatures and enchanted beings.

### Il Giorno della Liberazione (Day of Liberation) `[CANON]`
The Marea broke the seals of **control-magics** worldwide: golems, constructs and
other bound entities were suddenly **freed and gained individuality.** In the chaos
some killed their ex-masters, some were quickly re-bound, some fled, and some stayed
on with their masters under a **reasonable employment agreement.** → `bestiary.md`, `magic.md`

### Il Fato dei Nani (Fate of the Dwarves) `[CANON]` → `peoples.md`, `geography.md`
- Before the Tide the dwarves had **three kingdoms**: **Helgedad**, the **Monti
  Bianchi** (White Mountains, in Letia), and the **Wald** (in the Young Kingdoms).
- **Helgedad's** dwarf realm **disintegrated** after the **Esercito dei Lupi** (Army
  of the Wolves — "a vast host led by a coven of dark sorcerers and mad cultists")
  sacked it; the magnificent capital **Aule Dorate** (Golden Halls) fell to ruin. Few
  who stayed submitted; most chose exile or death; some withdrew into Helgedad's wilds
  and fell into barbarism.
- The **Wald's** dwarves: the awakening of the great dragon **Gwalgamaur il Dorato**
  devastated the region; they withdrew deep underground, sealed the entrances, and —
  aided by the Marea — **adapted to subterranean life, altering their racial traits
  and becoming something different.** The outer world lost all news of them. `[CANON]`
  → connects directly to Campaign IV (below).
- Only the **Monti Bianchi** kingdom survived intact, becoming the dwarves' reference
  point — and **seceded** from the crumbling Letio Empire to become **fully
  independent.**

### Il Fato dei Tanoth (Fate of the Tanoth) `[CANON]` → `peoples.md`
- Previously subjugated, confined to small wild zones. The Tide gave them the chance
  to reclaim territory: **Ur-Tanoth** tribes coalesced and conquered lands; forests
  sprang up magically over human villages/towns overnight. An entire **county in
  eastern Letia** fell to Ur-Tanoth rule.
- **Tal-Tanoth** joined the uprising: the notorious city of **Hagelbarn** (Northern
  Letia) was taken **in a single night** by an ex-bandit group called **"I Gatti"
  (The Cats)**, which declared it **independent.**
- The Tanoth often claimed to act **on behalf of the forgotten gods**, to "restore the
  order of things" — a claim suspected to be tied to the **new moon** that recently
  appeared in Ymir's sky. `[INFERRED link]` This is likely the same red/new moon that
  cronologia ties to Sguardo-sul-Nulla's arrival (3026).

### Il Fato degli Xebechani (Fate of the Xebechani) `[CANON]` → `peoples.md`, `races-index.md`
- The barrier around the **Isola della Morte** collapsed; undead flooded toward the
  rest of **Xebech**, drawn by living signals. To the Xebechani's horror the undead
  had **evolved** over the centuries: no longer slow and stupid but **fast, cunning,
  lethal and near-immortal.**
- Salvation came from the **mass reactivation of portals**, which **re-opened
  communication with the artificial planet X-01** — where for centuries an army of
  Xebechani had preserved the Adepti's techno-magical knowledge, awaiting their lords'
  return. X-01 sent reinforcements: **Xudra** squadrons (ancient techno-magic
  weapons), **Xion** scientists (to repair the **Pozze** / Pools and damaged
  fortress-cities), **Xidhe** embassies, and powerful **Xattva**. The undead hordes
  were exterminated quickly. Open hook: **"is it truly time for a new intergalactic
  empire?"** → confirms the four Xebechani sub-castes (Xudra/Xion/Xidhe/Xattva) and the
  off-world planet **X-01**.

### Il Fato degli Uomini (Fate of Men) `[CANON]` → `factions.md`, `geography.md`
- The **Letio Empire** is on the brink. Fighting **east** against the Young Kingdoms
  it met Armora's war-machines: **aerial raids razed cities**; cavalry charges were
  annihilated by Armora's **armoured war-chariots**. Only internal Young-Kingdoms
  disorder stopped Armora conquering eastern Letia.
- Meanwhile from the **west** an orc horde under **Gran Khan Ugrumuulsh Occhiosolo**
  (One-Eye) poured over the Letio border; several cities fell.
- **Turning point — Vespertina VonGriffen.** `[CANON]` `[CONFLICT — see below]`
  **Vespertina VonGriffen, having succeeded to Armora's throne after her husband's
  death**, immediately **ceased hostilities** on the Letio border, declaring it vital
  for humankind to unite against the orc threat and to save the sacred city of
  **Solanthya**, symbol of human culture. The **Alto Teocrate of Letia** accepted the
  collaboration → the long-thought-impossible **reconciliation between the Young
  Kingdoms and the Letio Empire.** United, they halted and pushed back the orcs. Open
  hooks: how long will the peace hold; is the orc threat really over?
- Other fronts named:
  - **Ulan-Tang** opened the **first Portal** and was the **first city to make contact
    with other worlds.** `[CANON]` (Direct tie-in to "The Gate to Unknown," §B7.)
  - **Khorish** invaded by undead hordes from the Isle of Death — new slaves for the
    Pharaohs, or the end of a millennial kingdom?
  - **Sadhir** and **Ophiur** front the emergence of **Hell/Inferno on Ymir** (the new
    demonic continent / demonic armies).
  - The **Principato di Cartosa**, just out of civil war, was hit by a **particularly
    intense wave** of the Marea — long-term effects and mutations pending. `[CANON]`
    (Cartosa as the mutation frontier.)

### Il Fato degli Elfi (Fate of the Elves) `[CANON]` → `peoples.md`, `bestiary.md`
- Soon after the Tide, **Alfheim** was struck by the **Vero Dio Drago = Ouroboros**,
  which fell on the elven kingdom **from the sea** "like a storm of fire and sorcery,"
  devastating everything for kilometres. Elven seers had had visions of doom. The
  **queen of Alfheim** mustered her army — thousands of the bravest warriors and
  mightiest casters hurled themselves at **Ouroboros**.
- **It was a massacre.** Not one attacker survived; the entire elven army was
  annihilated **without inflicting a single meaningful wound** on the True Dragon God.
  Survivors (those who hadn't fought) fled into **exile to the Isole d'Argento** or
  **submitted to Ouroboros, swearing fealty.**
- **KEY LORE:** Ouroboros then settled in the ex-elven kingdom, gathering strength and
  **plotting revenge on his six brothers of the Qi-Long** — i.e. the **Dei-Drago**
  (Dragon-Gods) of the Qi-Long are Ouroboros's **six brothers**, seven great dragons
  in all. `[CANON]` → major deepening of the Dragon-God lore (`bestiary.md`, and links
  to Campaign IV & the Qi-Long sourcebook).
- The **Gaerwath** dark elves were **cut off** from the rest of Ymir by the Marea and
  too busy with their own troubles to gloat over Alfheim's ruin.
- **The Prophecy:** many elves are hopeful — their sacred books foretell **an era of
  disorders immediately preceding the fulfilment of the Prophecy**: the **end of the
  Shulma** and the arrival of the **Messaggero** (Messenger) seems near. `[CANON]`
  → `peoples.md`, `pantheon.md` (elven eschatology).

### Il Fato dei Sohleugir (Fate of the Sohleugir / Lizard-Men) `[CANON]` → `peoples.md`
- In their undersea cities, the **Sohleugir** (Uomini Lucertola) were the **first of

- Open hooks: **is the Maelstrom exhausting itself?** What causes the boom in

## A2. The Legend of the Magic Runes (`parte-2-la-leggenda-delle-rune-magiche.txt`) `[CANON]`

This file is **Part 2 of the campaign "A Steamclad Sky"** (Campaign IV, §B4). It is the
**in-play discovery of the very mechanism that opens the Era della Marea** — the
**Rune Magiche** and the **six Reliquie del Potere.** This is the most important lore
document in the batch after `lera-della-marea.txt`.

### The core lore (transcribed in-world from stone steles) `[CANON]`
> *To counter the assault of the Sovereign Demons the **Adepti** created the mighty
> **Rune Magiche** (Magic Runes), instruments of immense power. In the last days of
> the Demon Wars, the great fortress-city that housed them was assaulted so ferociously
> by the demon hordes that the Runes themselves risked being corrupted by the dark
> power. For this reason the Adepti **sealed** them using 6 objects, the six **Reliquie
> del Potere**: **Ignus, lo Scettro delle Braci** (Scepter of Embers); **Saralis,
> l'Anello dell'Inverno** (Ring of Winter); **Meletskar, la Lama di Luce e Ombra**
> (Blade of Light and Shadow); **Tassalung, il Gioiello degli Abissi** (Jewel of the
> Abysses); **Natharien, il Diadema Celeste** (Celestial Diadem); and **Oedring, il
> Martello del Mondo Sotterraneo** (Hammer of the Underworld). Only the possessor of
> **all six** Relics can fully activate the Magic Runes and command their immense
> power.*

This **names, for the first time, the six relics** that `lera-della-marea.txt` /
`cronologia.txt` reference only as "the six Reliquie Divine." → `magic.md`, `bestiary.md`,
`history.md`. **Design note:** the relics are elementally/thematically flavoured
(Embers/fire, Winter/ice, Light-&-Shadow, Abysses/water, Celestial/air, Underworld/earth)
— i.e. **six**, matching the recurring "six" motif across the batch (six relics; six
"Signori"/elemental lords; the six-planet/god schema at Obnubia; Topramesk's "six
companions"; see §B7). `[INFERRED]`

### Story so far (chapters actually written)
- **Intro — La Festa Danzante.** To lift morale during the war, the **Granduca di
  Armora** throws a grand Spring festival around the **Padiglione delle Scienze**.
  PCs Drusilde and Isidoro (members of the new medical order **OIAMR**) meet **Yonker**
  (arrived as envoy of **Ennin-Lar**, the Wald's last fortress) and **"Doc"** (a Xidhe
  colleague). Summoned by **Setorn** (head of the OIAMR).
- **Le Rovine.** Setorn shows them a specimen that is **part-undead, part-machine** —
  built with the **machine that was meant to resurrect Samoth** (see Campaign IV) and
  sharing traits of the vampire **Leonard.** The party flies to ruins in the **Bosco
  di Rhems** held by **Caeldirite** forces and mechanical clones; general **Leon
  Artois** briefs them. The ruins are an **ancient Xebechan temple.**
- **Le Reliquie del Potere.** Deep in the temple they find the steles (above). They
  rush to the **Santuario dell'Inverno** but arrive too late: **Leonard**, a hooded
  sorcerer, and a mechanical knight named **Calavark** steal the relic **Saralis**
  (the Ring of Winter) and escape; **Yonker** bombs the clones.
- **Nanauatl.** A talking image of **Nanauatl** — a long-dead **Xion**, high theurge
  of the sanctuary — reveals that the trinket Isidoro carries (a gift from the dwarves
  of **Tor Varn**) is the **key to the Santuario delle Braci**, making its bearer
  **"Sacro Ierofante delle Braci."** The locations of the other sanctuaries are in the
  **great library of Xuthal, capital of the Xebechan empire.** `[CANON]`
- **Orchi! / Processo.** Orcs overrun the ruins; trapped, **Drusilde summons the demon
  Anung Un Rama** (a demon of rare power) to escape, barely mastering it, and teleports
  the party to Armora's main square. They are tried for demonology (a fine).
- **Nuovi Intrighi.** **Marzio** (black-clad agent of the mysterious entity Drusilde is
  bound to) tells her the entity now demands its payment: **find the Relics and use
  them for its purposes**, and gives her the location of a **Vera Via** (forgotten
  portal) to **Xuthal.**

## B2. Campaign II — *A Black Sun Rising* (`campagna-ii…`) `[CANON — actual-play]`
**Setting:** Principato di Cartosa. **Ties to:** the **Anno del Sole Nero (3017)** and
the failed **Alba Nera** coup already in canon. Prologue + Chapters I–III written;
IV–VI are empty headings.

**Story.** Sole survivors of the wreck of the merchant ship **"Fortuna"** carry a
**gold fragment** inscribed in **Ragashano** (the Adepti's tongue) — part of a
**demon-summoning ritual.** The murders around it trace to the **"Alba Nera" (Black
Dawn)**, an offshoot of the **"Incappucciati" (Hooded Ones)** crime syndicate, which
hunts **Adepti artefacts and forbidden books "about the ruin of the Adepti"** and runs
an **iron-smuggling** ring. The trail leads through the pirate free-port **Ombra del
Mar** to the lost city **Cumbre de Oro** — actually **Taremorn**, a city cursed
millennia ago by a **Principe Demone (Demon Prince)**: a curse of **greed** turns the

**Tobias** is unmasked as the **Alba Nera's leader**; he seizes the malign **Scettro di
Lorith** and flees, waking the king's furious ghost (**Raphael loses a hand** covering
the escape). At **El Paso**, Tobias enslaves townsfolk to mine iron and build
**demon-powered constructs.**

**Locations/ships:** Cartosa (Città Vecchia), Ombra del Mar, Cumbre de Oro/**Taremorn**,
**Nevarrez** (fortress-city), El Paso, Coste Gemelle; ships **Fortuna**, **Indomabile**

**Named characters.**

- **NPCs:** **Tobias** (revered old Blue-Mantle tutor → corrupt archmage, **Alba Nera

## B4. Campaign IV — *A Steamclad Sky* (`campagna-iv…`) `[CANON — actual-play]`
**Setting:** Regni Giovani (Young Kingdoms), centred on the technocratic **Granducato
di Armora**. This is the **fullest log in the batch** (13 chapters), and its **Part 2**
is §A2 above. It is **contemporaneous with the Player-Guide "present"** — the
three-sided Young-Kingdoms war and **Gwalgamaur's** conquest of the **Wald** (from
**3025 d.M.** `[INFERRED date]`). Ends with the party swearing to reunite at Armora "in
a year."

**Arc summary.**
1. Intruder + a theft at Armora's **Padiglione delle Scienze** lead the PCs into
   **rovine sotterranee** beneath the city and a **shadow-being**; they are framed,
   then cleared by **Belengard.**
2–3. **L'Omicidio del Nano / Il Salvataggio.** The Artificer **Nairin** is murdered
   over plans for a **machine that drains life to reanimate the dead.** The trail
   (via the brigand **Sharky** and a **replicant** of **Telesius**) reaches **Keswig**
   (on the river **Telendrium**) and the **Tomba di Samoth** — a necromancer executed
   ~35 years earlier for a failed coup. The vampire **Leonard** is trying to
   **resurrect Samoth** with the machine; the party stop him and burn his body.

## B7. "The Gate to Unknown" (`riassunto-sessioni-prima-di-luned-04-06-2007.txt`) `[CANON — actual-play]`
Internal title **"The Gate to Unknown."** A dense session-recap (master **danny**;
players **lucy, simo, gunz, khuz, tarter** + others). **This is the actual-play origin
of the canonical 3026 event** — **Kramer**, the **Ulan-Tang portal**, and the coming of
**Sguardo-sul-Nulla** — and it deepens **Topramesk** and the Adepti "six" motif. Very
important; set in **Letia ~3025–3026 d.M.** `[INFERRED date]`, sliding into the Tide.

**Arc summary (in order).**
- **Hunt for Kramer.** In the ruined northern-Letia city **Hagelbarn** ("La città
  Perversa" / "La Perduta"), refuge of heretics, the party pursues the corrupt
  **Tredicesima Legione Iscariota** (Thirteenth Iscariot Legion — legionaries who are
  **not fully human**, "guerrieri cosa") to rescue the inquisitor **Heinrich Kramer.**
  Kramer is found imprisoned and is **tortured to death by a guard**; dying, he tells
  **Tofango** to find **Hans Schlecht.** `[CANON]` — Kramer's **death** (his later
  return as a **lich** and role at Ulan-Tang is the canonical 3026 beat).
- **Hagelbarn — the inquisitor's legacy.** The alchemist **Hans Schlecht** conveys
  Kramer's last will, forges the axe **Oblivion** for Tofango, gives **Cipriano** the
  **Chiave del Nulla** (Key of the Void), and returns **six stones** to **Vemeer.**
  `[CANON]` — introduces the **Chiave del Nulla** + the **six pietre**, the McGuffins
  that will open the Ulan-Tang portal.
- **Toward Stanthea / stealing a Portal.** At **Norgen** (between Hagelbarn and the port
  **Stanthea**) the party **steals an actual Adepti Portal** being carted to a heretic
  Count's fortress, and hauls it away — after which the seven stones and the Portal
  travel together. `[CANON]` (a mobile Vera Via as loot).
- **The Astro Armorita.** A storm strands them; a legion warship and an **Armora
  flying/submarine spy-ship, the "Astro Armorita,"** rescue them; the fanatical
  paladin **Martius/Marzius Magnus** demands the "sorcerers" be handed to the
  Inquisition. → confirms Armora's aeronaval espionage over Letia. `[CANON]`
- **Dreamlands — Ragash & Topramesk.** `[CANON]` **MAJOR DEEPENING.** Drugged/dreaming,
  **Tofango** and **Vemeer** wander **"Ragash la Splendente, capitale del Mondo dei
  Sogni"** — the **Palazzo delle Feste** of **Topramesk il Giallo** (**Topramesk the
  Yellow**), ruled from a tower by the madman **Ragamesh.** Topramesk hosts a party,
  gives Tofango the **"sangue di Diis"** (blood of **Diis**, elemental **Lord of
  Fire**), then **gradually forgets his own name, reverts to Xebechan form, relives the
  past, and departs to "take part in a ritual with his six companions"** — i.e. **La
  Catastrofe.** → confirms the Catastrophe ritual had **seven Adepts (Topramesk + six
  companions)** and that **Topramesk persists inside the Dream World** as a demented
  host. → `history.md`, `cosmology.md`, `characters.md`.
- **Obnubia — the six-god schema.** `[CANON]` Shipwrecked on the island **Obnubia**,
  the party finds a vast **underground temple of an ancient advanced civilisation**
  where "savages" worship. Its wall-schema depicts **six planets/gods**, one decoded as
  **Sguardo-sul-Nulla** — read as **Xebechan deities.** A **shaman** uses a black cube =
  **"sangue di Heis"** (blood of **Heis**, Lord of **Air**); Tofango's cube =
  **sangue di Diis** (Fire). → these cubes are **"sangue dei Signori"** (blood of the
  Elemental Lords), and Sguardo-sul-Nulla is grouped among **six** cosmic entities.
  Strongly parallels the **six Reliquie** (§A2) and **six companions** (above).
  `[INFERRED]` the "six stones/planets/relics/companions" are one unified Adept motif.
- **Letia interlude — slavery, demons, corruption.** At the slave-port **Hayatula**,
  **Cipriano** (secretly a **Void** sorcerer who repeatedly pacts with demons /
  **Ahriman**) sells **Vemeer**, who is re-bought by **Arcade Nemo Manlio** (ex-Armorite
  ambassador) and becomes **"Nero" / "Vemeer the Black."** The **Inquisition/Torcia**
  hunts everyone who saw the axe-maddened Tofango; several PCs die and are variously
  resurrected via demon-pacts (Gundar; corruption points soar). New arrivals: **Fel'Fenor**,
  **Rekon**, **War-Ra**, the child **Romarzio**, **Akarnaset** (a Dark Elf who tries to
  corrupt Oblivion with the Light element and is turned into a zombie, then killed).
- **Endgame — Ulan-Tang & Sguardo-sul-Nulla.** `[CANON]` `[INFERRED = the 3026 event]`
  Travelling via the **Erg** to **Ulan-Tang**: the **search for Kramer** (now a
  **lich**), the arrival of **War-Ra** and **Zooriam**, a **failed portal opening**,
  and the **comparsa di Sguardo-sul-Nulla** ("Gaze-into-the-Void"). The group **passes
  through the portal**, has adventures on the worlds **"La Notte"** and **Tchyo**, and —
  after **Zimmermann's** magical disaster — is **transported bodily inside
  Sguardo-Nel-Nulla**, escaping only via the **Dream World.** The log closes amid the
  **Letio–Young-Kingdoms war** (aeronaval bombardment; a stolen Armora flyer; a run to
  **Alfheim**), a final clash with **Martius Magnus**, and confrontations with a
  **Figlio del Nulla** (Son of the Void) and **Kramer.** → this is the in-play
  machinery behind canon's **"Kramer activates the Ulan-Tang portal, drawing
  Sguardo-sul-Nulla into Ymir (3026)."** `[CANON DEEPENING]`
