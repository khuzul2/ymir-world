Source: fan-wiki raw files (Italian, HTML-stripped; broken export replaces accented vowels and apostrophes with "?"). Proper nouns preserved in Italian. Game-mechanics values are Rolemaster (RM) / Races & Cultures (R&C) references.

## 0. Overarching Race Taxonomy (from `razze.txt`, `razze-degenerate.txt`, `ibridi.txt`)

Ymir's peoples are grouped into three broad legal/moral categories:

- **Razze Pure** ("Pure Races") — Umani, Xebechani, Ur-Tanoth, Tal-Tanoth, Nani, Sohleugir, Folletti, Elfi. Playable without special notes in most campaigns. [CANON]
- **Mezze Razze** ("Half-races") — cross-breeds; sometimes visibly mixed, sometimes not. Not everyone tolerates mixed blood. [CANON]
- **Razze Degenerate** ("Degenerate Races") — generally malign or servants of the **Demoni Sovrani** (Sovereign Demons). Feared/hated by all; frequently attacked on sight, and killing an "orchetto" and looting it is rarely considered a crime. [CANON]
- **Ibridi e Abominii** ("Hybrids & Abominations") — Maelstrom-mutated; require Master approval to play. [CANON]

### Playability notes (`razze.txt`) [CANON]
- **Umani:** no problem in most campaigns.
- **Xebechani:** outside the Xebech, a PC is most likely an **Xidhe** (the caste that travels most) or an exiled **Xattva**.
- **Ur-Tanoth:** rare/absent in many urban settings, often treated as second-class citizens; in undeveloped areas locals may never have seen one and react to their "monstrous" look. Warn the Master before playing one "before the guards mistake you for a troll."
- **Tal-Tanoth:** welcomed about as much as "a party of festive children in a museum."
- **Nani:** very attached to origins — decide the dwarf's homeland and clan relations up front.
- **Sohleugir:** highly evolved and civilized, but their appearance causes social problems; many humans and dwarves react with instinctive fear at first meeting an Uomo Lucertola. They often hide behind veils and hoods.
- **Folletti:** rarely leave their communities; seen with curiosity/sympathy; sometimes mistaken for elf or dwarf children.
- **Elfi:** outside an elven-kingdom campaign, a PC elf most likely belongs to the **Elfi Illuminati** faction or has been exiled. Elves are often viewed with suspicion — **in Letia theologians still debate whether elves even have a soul.** [CANON — new detail]
- **Mezz'elfi / Mezzi Nani / etc.:** mixed blood may or may not be obvious; not all cultures accept it.
- **Orchi / Mezz'orchi / Goblin / etc.:** feared/hated by all other races, often attacked on sight.
- **Ibridi e Abominii:** clear with the Master.

## 1. UMANI (Humans)

**Source:** `umani.txt` (stub intro), `uomini.txt`, `primigeni.txt`.

Humans are spread across all of Ymir, in every environment and climate. On Ymir they are divided into **two sub-races**: an older one, the **Primigeni** (Primeval), and a younger one, the **Uomini** (Men). [CANON]

### 1a. Primigeni (Primeval) [CANON]
- Legend: the Primigeni, ancient progenitors of humankind, were **created from Fuoco (Fire), one of the two elemental energies.** This may explain humanity's frenetic pace of life (from other races' viewpoint) and impulsiveness. **[CANON — new cosmological hook: "the two elemental energies," Fire being one.]**
- Because **Fire contains within itself the principle of Destruction**, a malevolent god was able to manipulate and corrupt the Primigeni; over time they became servants of darkness, "pale shadows" of their former selves. Today only very few pure Primigeni survive — descendants of those who kept their spirit pure. [CANON]
- Lifespan: **90–100 years.** Race description: use R&C "High Men."
- **Prejudices:** hate all degenerate races, especially orchetti; **admire the mystical wisdom of the Xebechani.** [CANON]

**Primigeni regional groups:**
- **Vorskgen:** barbarian tribes of frozen **Helgedad** or the northern borders of **Letia** who keep the ancient primeval spirit and blood. Cultures: Artica (any sub-culture), Altopiano-Barbarica, or Altopiano-Militaristica. **Main language: Vorskgen.** [CANON — Vorskgen framed here as *Primeval-blooded human tribes*, not a separate race.]
- **Ophiriti:** human inhabitants of **l'Ophiur** — tall and massive with **very black skin**; most, especially the aristocracy, are Primigeni. Culture: Pianura-Urbana. **Main language: Ophirita.** **[CANON — NEW: Ophiur / Ophiriti not in the supplied canon list.]**

### 1b. Uomini (Men) [CANON]
- Descendants of the Primigeni who were partly corrupted by Evil. Weaker in body and spirit than the Primigeni, and more easily seduced by Evil — though not all are corrupt; many fight for good and some retain part of the ancient fierceness of Primeval blood.
- Lifespan: **70–90 years.**
- **Special (playable):** a human PC is normally built with the **Umano Misto** (Mixed Man) sub-race, since PCs are assumed to belong to the minority still carrying a trace of Primeval blood. **The vast majority of the population are Uomini Comuni (Common Men).** [CANON — confirms "Uomini Misti + Uomini Comuni" split.]
- **Prejudices:** none inherent; vary by culture and personal inclination.

> Regional human cultures (Alti/Comuni/Boschi/Colline, Norsi) named in the supplied canon are **not** enumerated in these particular files; only Vorskgen and Ophiriti appear here. [INFERRED — those live in other source files.]

## 2. NANI (Dwarves)

**Source:** `nani.txt`.

- Relatively widespread across Ymir, **especially in Letia, the Regni Giovani (Young Kingdoms), Helgedad, and the Principato di Cartosa.** Mostly peaceful relations with the other Pure Races. Not very different from base-Rolemaster dwarves. [CANON]
- **Technology:** with mechanical devices, **steam machines**, and the invention of **black powder / gunpowder (la polvere nera) — a secret no other race has managed to steal** — the Nani are probably the most technologically advanced race on Ymir, **excepting the Xebechani.** Dwarven smiths and engineers are sought everywhere; their inventions are paid for their weight in gold. **[CANON — new detail: dwarven gunpowder monopoly & steam-tech.]**
- Lifespan: **100–120 years.**
- **Prejudices:** special hatred for **dragons, goblins, and all degenerate races**; very suspicious of **Tal-Tanoth**; don't understand Ur-Tanoth or Folletti but bear them no particular ill will. [CANON]
- **Rules option "Nano Empatico":** may swap the racial Em/In bonus (and swap Essence↔Flusso RR bonus and PP progression) to allow Dwarf Alchemists or Earth Elementalists. [CANON — mechanics]

**Dwarf sub-groups:**
- **Nano Comune (Common Dwarf):** the typical dwarf, identical to the base manual. Language: **Nanico.** [CANON]
- **Nano di Pianura (Plains Dwarf):** not truly a separate race — descendants of dwarves who emigrated seeking work in human cities. Viewed with distrust by purist dwarves; in Nanico the word **"Laak-nama-Khazalim"** (= "Plains-Dweller") is an insult. Language: Nanico. **[CANON — new lore/term.]**
- **Arawak** (singular and plural): a race of dwarves with **dark skin and violet eyes, indigenous to the Cartosan jungles.** Use normal dwarf stats but **Silvana-Barbarica** culture. Language: **Arawak.** [CANON] — *Note:* this file does **not** mention the canon detail that Arawak "work stone/bone"; it only gives appearance, homeland, and culture. [INFERRED — the stone/bone trait comes from another source.]

## 3. ELFI (Elves)

**Source:** `elfi.txt` (see also `elfi oscuri.txt` and `Gaerwath.txt` in same folder — Dark Elf detail lives there).

- Elves are **alien to Ymir**; they arrived during the **Guerre Demoniache (Demonic Wars)**, at the moment of greatest need for Ymir's free peoples. **No one knows exactly where they came from, nor why they left their homeland** — asked about it, elves change the subject. **[CANON — note tension with supplied canon "Elves came from Niethel": this file states the homeland is unknown/evaded. The Niethel identification must come from another source.] [CONFLICT — mild]**
- **The Schism ("Anni della Discordia," Years of Discord):** ~**800 years after the Catastrofe that created the Maelstrom (~2200 years ago)**, the elven kingdoms were torn by internal wars, splitting into **three factions**: [CANON]
  1. **Elfi Chiari (Light Elves):** the elven destiny is to withdraw into the newly founded Sacred Realm (present-day **Alfheim**) and prepare for the coming of the **Ultimo Messaggero (Last Messenger)**, who will lead them back to their homeland.
  2. **Elfi Oscuri (Dark Elves):** all of Ymir *is* the Sacred Realm promised to elves in prophecy, and all other races exist solely to serve elves — so they proposed a **holy crusade to conquer the Realm.**
  3. **Elfi Illuminati (Enlightened Elves)** — the most moderate, called **"Rinuncianti" (Renouncers)** by the other two — withdrew from the fight, left elven territory to live among the other races in contemplation, believing the Sacred Realm is not a physical place but **within each elf.**
- **Crucial structural point:** there is **no racial difference between the factions.** Separately, there exist **three sub-races** (origin unknown) that are **distributed *equally* across all three factions.** [CANON]

> **[CONFLICT] vs supplied canon.** The supplied canon frames "Elfi Grigi/Grey" as *the moderate faction*, and "Light = High+Sylvan of Alfheim." This file instead defines:
> - the **moderate faction** as the **Illuminati / Rinuncianti** (NOT the Grey), and
> - **Elfi Grigi** as a **sub-race** (sea/marine elves), distributed across all factions like the other sub-races.
> So the three *factions* (Chiari / Illuminati / Oscuri) are orthogonal to the three *sub-races* (Alti / Silvani / Grigi). Any High or Sylvan or Grey elf can belong to any faction. Reconcile carefully.

**Elf sub-races (racial, faction-independent):** [CANON]
- **Elfi Alti (High Elves):** noblest and purest; versed in arts and magic, also excellent fighters; the elite of elven society.
- **Elfi Silvani (Sylvan Elves):** the majority of Ymir's elves.
- **Elfi Grigi (Grey / Sea Elves):** slightly rarer than Sylvan; love the sea and often settle on coasts; **the elves who most often deal with other races.** Use R&C Grey Elf stats.

- **Special — Mortality:** unlike R&C, **Ymir's elves are NOT immortal.** Whatever the source of their immortality on their home plane, it doesn't reach them here, forcing mortal (if very long) lives — **~200 years average.** **[CANON — significant: elves are mortal on Ymir.]**
- **Prejudices:** suspicious of all other races; hate the degenerate races. [CANON]

## 4. FOLLETTI (Little People / "il Piccolo Popolo")

**Source:** `folletti.txt` (stub intro), `fate.txt`, `gnomi.txt`.

- The Folletti, a.k.a. **"il Piccolo Popolo,"** are the **smallest of the Pure Races and probably the rarest.** Commonly divided into two sub-races — **Fate** and **Gnomi** — **though it is not yet fully proven the two are related.** [CANON]

### 4a. Fate (Fairies) [CANON]
- Contrary to the belief that only females exist, **both sexes exist** (males only slightly rarer). Like miniature humans or elves; **rarely reach 90 cm tall.** They call their own kind **"Tylweth Teg."** Love living in woods, where they build their own **small cities**, far from the dwellings of the **"Gente Alta" (Tall Folk).**
- **Special:** contrary to R&C, **Fate are NOT immortal — ~150 years average.** [CANON]
- **Prejudices:** distrust the "Gente Alta"; struggle to understand the Nani; hate all degenerate races, **especially Coboldi.**
- Race description: use **Tylweth Teg** in R&C.

### 4b. Gnomi (Gnomes) [CANON]
- Another false belief is that **Gnomi are the males of the Fate** (irritating to both races) — in reality a **separate and substantially different race.**
- Lifespan: **150 years.**
- **Prejudices:** nothing in particular, but they **prefer to be left alone — even by other Gnomi.**
- Race description: use **Gnomes in *Underground Races*** (NOT R&C).

## 5. SOHLEUGIR (Lizard-Men / "Uomini Lucertola")

**Source:** `sohleugir.txt`.

- Comfortable both on land and in water — **water being their element of origin.** Usually live on coasts in **splendid cities of stone and coral built on multiple levels, above and below sea level.** [CANON]
- **Very open-minded**, rarely prejudiced; renowned as **exceptional merchants** and **fearsome warriors.** Many believe their terrifying appearance stems from an **ancient curse**; aware their look frightens others, they wear concealing clothing. [CANON]
- Lifespan: **80–90 years.**
- **Prejudices:** especially detest the **Gratar**; hate all corrupt races. [CANON]

## 6. TANOTH (Beast-folk)

**Source:** `tanoth.txt` (stub intro), `tal-tanoth.txt`, `ur-tanoth.txt`.

- The Tanoth are a **bestial humanoid race** split into two sub-races: **Ur-Tanoth** and **Tal-Tanoth.** [CANON]

### 6a. Ur-Tanoth (large feline nomads) [CANON]
- Look like a cross between a **large predatory feline and a man.** Not all alike: some panther-like, some tiger-like, some lion-like; **members of the same tribe resemble each other.** Despite the unsettling look, normally **peaceful, shy, disinclined to violence.**
- **Physiology:** Tall and massive — males ~**100 kg / 200 cm**, females ~**80 kg / 185 cm.** Body fully covered in thick fur of any color; eyes **yellow or green, rarely red.** Stamina: **Incredible (+20 Fatigue Points).** Lifespan **60–70 years.**
- **Resistances:** +15 vs Poison, +10 vs Disease, **+20 vs Fear**, +10 RR & DB vs Cold. **Special abilities: Tough Skin (AT4); Medium Claws.**
- **Fears & Disabilities:** above all else they fear **imprisonment and loss of freedom** — an imprisoned Tanoth will **sicken and die quickly.** Most shun enclosed spaces (find them suffocating). [CANON — strong flavor hook]
- **Religion:** totemic / animistic.
- **Prejudices:** hate Orchetti and Troll; keep away from cities (noisy, smelly, claustrophobic).
- **Preferred professions:** good Warriors, Barbarians, Rangers; also excellent Thieves. **Trouble with the Essence and Mentalism realms.**
- **Cultures (by climate):** Artica Nomadica; Altopiano Barbarica; Pianura Nomadica; Silvana Nomadica. Everyman: Claws, Survival (home climate). Occupational: Hunting.
- **Stat block (RM):** AG +6, CO +2, ME +0, RA +0, AC +0, EM −2, IN +0, PR −4, RF +4, FO +4, **AS −25** (very socially penalized/monstrous). RR: Essence +5, Mentalism +5, Arcane +5, Channeling +0... Poison +15, Disease +10. Fear +20. Soul departure 12 rnds. Healing ×0.5. Race Type 3. Languages 2. Background options 4 (40).

### 6b. Tal-Tanoth (small cat-folk; the "Halfling slot") [CANON]
- Similar to Ur-Tanoth but **much smaller — like a cross between a cat and a man.** Unlike Ur-Tanoth they **do not fear man and often live in cities.**
- **Physiology:** small and slender — males **45 kg / 120 cm**, females **40 kg / 115 cm.** Fur any color; eyes **blue, green, or yellow.** Normal stamina; **must sleep at least 8 hours/day.** Lifespan **60–80 years.**
- **Resistances:** +15 vs Poison, +10 vs Disease, +10 RR & DB vs Cold. **Special: Tough Skin (AT3).**
- **Prejudices:** hate orchetti; regard men as "big dolts"; otherwise get along with most races — **but** their fickle, prank-loving nature isn't always reciprocated, and many consider Tal-Tanoth "at least a nuisance."
- **Religion:** not much concerned with it — superstition or the main cult of whatever people they live among.
- **Preferred professions:** **excellent Thieves**, good **Zingari (Gypsies)**; the most wandering become **Ranger or Esploratore (Scout)**; rarely anything else. Everyman: Move Silently.
- **Cultures:** whatever people they live among, usually **Urban (lower class).**
- **Stat block (RM):** AG +6, CO +2, ME +0, RA +0, AC −4, EM +0, IN +0, PR −4, RF +5, FO −6, AS +0. RR: Essence +5, Mentalism +5, Poison +15, Disease +10. Fear +0. Soul departure 12 rnds. Healing ×0.5. Race Type 3. Languages 5. Background options 5 (50).

> **[CANON]** Confirms supplied canon: Tal-Tanoth fill the Halfling slot; Ur-Tanoth are the nomad hunters. Deepens both with full physiology, psychology, and stats.

## 7. XEBECHANI (reptilian caste-race)

**Source:** `xebechani.txt` (overview), plus caste files `xudra.txt`, `xidhe.txt`, `xion.txt`, `xattva.txt`.

### Overview [CANON]
- An **ancient, mysterious race of humanoids** with pale skin and some reptilian traits, of **undoubted magical origin.** Some hold they are linked to the **Draghi (Dragons) or the Grandi Serpenti (Great Serpents) of antiquity**, but their origins are shrouded in mystery. **[CANON — Dragon/Great-Serpent origin theory is new detail.]**
- Their bizarre look makes them oddly **"hypnotic"** to other races, probably from the **enchanted aura around them.** Always calm, cold, detached — they seem to observe the world from a great distance, uninterested in surrounding events. Not emotionless, but very good at hiding emotions, which makes them more enigmatic.
- **Four Castes, each a true sub-race** (differing in physical and intellectual capacity): **Xudra** (warriors/laborers), **Xidhe** (merchants/diplomats), **Xion** (sages/priests), **Xattva** (nobles/mages). [CANON]

### Traits common to all Xebechani ("CARATTERISTICHE COMUNI") [CANON]
- **Termovisione (Thermal vision):** limited heat-sight. +5 Detect Lies, +10 Tracking (if tracks <30 min old); move in darkness up to **20 m.** Always on (can't be disabled) alongside normal sight; makes them **vulnerable to sudden temperature swings** (e.g. Fireball, Coldball) which can confuse/stun momentarily.
- **Elasticità:** especially elastic, articulated joints. +10 to all **Contortion** maneuvers; +5 to all **Martial Arts: Locking Holds** attacks.
- **Passivi:** even the liveliest Xebechani seems cold/detached — situational **−10 to −20 penalty to Influence** skills (Master's discretion).
- **Asessuati (Asexual):** **no sexual organs; do not reproduce normally.** Seduction skill restricted. [CANON — confirms canon]

### Society & the Pozze [CANON]
- Society is **rigidly hierarchical**: **Xattva** at the top (also least numerous); **Xudra** at the bottom (most numerous). **Caste is decided at birth and cannot be changed** — though one may hope for a **"Rinascita" (Rebirth) into a better caste.**
- From birth every Xebechani bears **thin colored scales on the back forming patterns like a cobra's "eyeglasses."** The **color and complexity of these patterns determine caste, social role, and future occupation** — most of a Xebechani's life is decided at birth.
- **Reproduction — "le Pozze" (the Pools):** Xebechani are created by a secret magical process. In every large Xebechani city, inside the main temple, sits a **colossal organic machine built thousands of years ago with powerful alchemical formulas**, called simply **"le Pozze."** When a Xebechani dies, its body is **fed into the machine, which absorbs it and uses its organic and spiritual particles to create a newborn Xebechani** — this is the **Rinascita**, the *only* way they reproduce. **[CANON — confirms & greatly deepens canon "grown in le Pozze."]**
- **Extinction threat:** the machines are extremely ancient; the Xebechani have **forgotten the science** to understand or even repair them (if they ever knew it). Malfunctions are increasingly common — some are **born dead or deformed.** Priest-alchemists grope in the dark; **the shadow of extinction looms over the whole race.** [CANON — strong plot hook]
- **Clothing:** light garments, often silk, leaving shoulders and back bare; gold jewelry adorned with feathers and gems. Abroad, long hooded tunics.
- **Prejudices:** treat other races as children, considering themselves generally intellectually superior.
- **Religion:** their own pantheon of gods called **"Dei Esterni" (Outer Gods)**, but their cult centers mainly on **reverence for their ancestors ("gli Antichi," the Ancients, held to be keepers of great knowledge)** and on rituals to secure Rebirth into a better caste. **[CANON — "Dei Esterni" / "gli Antichi" are new named concepts.]**
- **Homelands:** large cities, usually in the **middle of the jungle** (culture: Silvana) or on **inaccessible mountain peaks** (culture: Aerea), isolated from the world.

### 7a. Xidhe (merchant/diplomat caste) [CANON]
- The image most people have of a Xebechani: slender pale humanoids, **long black hair, magnetic gaze.** Their caste handles **commerce and external diplomacy**, so they are the Xebechani most often met **outside** Xebechani lands. Cultured, multilingual, adaptable; the **least "passive"** of Xebechani — can form deep friendships even with other races. Some emigrate abroad, feeling oppressed by the rigidity of Xebechani society.
- **Physiology:** slender build ~**65 kg**, ~**180 cm.** Skin **milk-white** except colored scales on the back; eyes **silver, green, or violet, vertical pupil**; hair black, usually straight. **Lifespan 250–300 years.** Decent poison/disease resistance.
- **Special ability — Sguardo Intenso (Intense Gaze):** magnetic, near-hypnotic stare — **+15 to Influence skills when the eyes are visible**; targets of a **Mentalism ("m"-type) spell** cast by an Xidhe suffer **−10 RR if his eyes are visible.**
- **Skills:** Everyman — Bargaining/Haggle, Persuasion, Storytelling; Occupational — **Hypnosis.**
- **Stat block:** AG +0, CO −2, ME +0, RA +0, AC +2, EM +4, IN +0, PR +6, RF +0, FO −2, **AS +10.** RR: Essence −5, Mentalism −5, Arcane −5, Channeling +0... RF/Poison +20, Disease +20. Soul departure 9 rnds. Healing ×1. Race Type 3. Languages 5. Background 2 (20).

### 7b. Xudra (warrior/laborer caste) [CANON]
- The **warrior caste** — soldiers and heavy laborers. **Massive**, with robust scales; **least human-looking** of the Xebechani, vaguely like **humanoid anacondas**; their head is the most reptilian (small and elongated). **More than half of all Xebechani are Xudra.** Hard to meet outside their cities — they are deeply bound to their duties and homeland and travel very rarely, one exception being as **escort to a higher-caste member.**
- **Physiology:** tall and robust ~**100 kg**, ~**190 cm.** **Scales instead of skin** — usually brown or dark green on the back, yellow on the belly; eyes usually **yellow, vertical pupil.** Very resistant (**+20 Fatigue Points**). **Lifespan 80–100 years** (the *shortest*-lived caste). Very resistant to poison and disease.
- **Special abilities:** natural armor from scales — **Pelle Resistente (Tough Skin): AT3 natural**; and, despite their bulk, remarkably fast — **Riflessi Fulminei (Lightning Reflexes): roll 3d10 instead of 2d10 for initiative, keep the best two.**
- **Skills:** Everyman — **Power Striking.**
- **Stat block:** AG +3, CO +2, ME +0, RA +0, AC +0, EM −3, IN +0, PR −3, RF +4, FO +3, **AS −30.** RR: Essence +20, Mentalism +20, Arcane +50, Channeling +0... RF +50, Disease +25. Fear +10. Soul departure 9 rnds. Healing ×0.5. Race Type 3. Languages 2. Background 3 (30).

### 7c. Xion (priest/scribe caste) [CANON]
- The **priestly caste** — tend the cult of the gods, officiate the main rites, and serve the **Xattva as scribes and advisors.** In theory responsible for **maintaining the Pozze**, but since the necessary science is lost they merely **guard them** and assist Xattva experiments. An Xion may leave his city seeking new knowledge, or to accompany an **Xidhe on a diplomatic mission as guide and supervisor.**
- **Physiology:** **fragile**, ~**50–60 kg**, ~**160–170 cm** (the *shortest* caste). Skin **white-yellowish** (except back scales); black hair; eyes **yellow or violet, vertical pupil.** **Lifespan 250–300 years.** Decent poison/disease resistance.
- **Special abilities:** **Senso Arcano (Arcane Sense):** by concentrating, sense active spells within **10 m** — direction and distance, but **not the Realm.** **Trascendenza:** innate magical affinity — **+25 to Transcend Armor** (less hampered by armor when casting).
- **Skills:** Everyman — **Engineering, Biology.**
- **Stat block:** AG +0, CO +0, ME +0, RA +2, AC +3, EM +2, IN +6, PR +0, RF +0, FO −2, **AS +0.** RR: Essence +0, Mentalism +0, Arcane +0, Channeling +0, RF/Poison +20, Disease +20. Soul departure 9 rnds. Healing ×1. Race Type 3. Languages 2. Background 3 (30).

### 7d. Xattva (noble/mage caste) [CANON]
- The **ethereal Xattva** are considered the Xebechani par excellence — the purest and noblest caste — and the **least numerous (barely ~10% of the population).** They hold **government, lawmaking, and administration**, though most actual work is done by Xion scribes; the Xattva handle only the gravest, most important matters. They mainly spend their time on **magical studies and alchemical experiments, hoping to solve the reproduction problem.** They almost never leave their lands; those met abroad have generally been **punished with exile for political or religious reasons** and endure it with great sadness.
- **Physiology:** tall and slender ~**65–70 kg**, ~**190 cm.** Skin very pale, near-white; parts of the body replaced by **small soft scales** that on the back form the cobra-eyeglass patterns. Black hair; eyes **yellow, grey, green, or violet, vertical pupil.** **Lifespan 250–300 years.** Very resistant to poison and disease.
- **Special abilities:** like the Xion, **Senso Arcano** (sense active spells within 10 m — direction/distance, not Realm); plus **Aura** — channel and store magical energy very easily (already figured into their PP progression).
- **Skills:** Everyman — none.
- **Stat block:** AG +0, CO −3, ME +0, RA +0, AC +2, EM +6, IN +6, PR +6, RF +0, FO −2, **AS +20.** RR: Essence −5, Mentalism −5, Arcane −5, Channeling −5, RF +50, Disease +25. Soul departure 9 rnds. Healing ×1. **Race Type 2** (uniquely, not 3, among the castes). Languages 5. Background 2 (25).

> **Caste summary:** Xudra (most numerous, ~50%+, warriors, shortest-lived 80–100 yr, AS −30 most monstrous) → Xion (priests/scribes, fragile) → Xidhe (diplomats, most sociable, only outward-facing caste) → Xattva (~10%, rulers/mages, Race Type 2). The three magical/upper castes all live 250–300 years; only the Xudra are short-lived.
