## 1. Magic & Character Creation

### 1.1 Corruption — *Corruzione da Essenza Oscura* (CEO) `[CANON — corruzione.txt]` `[DEEPENING of the "Usufruitori Corrotti" realm]`
The definitive rules for the fourth/"corrupted" realm hinted at in `canon/magic.md`.
- **Dark Essence Corruption (CEO)** is an explicit game mechanic (measured in **Punti
  Corruzione / PC**), representing anything that "stains" a character's Essence and
  makes them a tool of **Ahriman** — demon pacts, evil magic items, casting **Flussi
  Oscuri** (Dark Flow), knowing "Evil" spell lists. It is explicitly **not** a morality
  meter; it also balances "evil" channelers, who may learn **4 extra base lists** (more
  powerful than normal).
- **Effects, by threshold:** 1+ PC → detected as "Evil"/"of Darkness". 25+ PC →
  treated as an "enemy" by all benign **Flusso** (divine) spells (can take Holy crits).
  100+ PC → the character **has sold their soul to Ahriman**; Soul Departure becomes 0
  and at death the soul is absorbed by Ahriman (as a "Dark Absolution"). Corruption also
  cancels/penalises divine Utility spells cast across the pure/corrupt line, blocks entry
  into deity-blessed areas, and penalises casting in corrupted zones (e.g. an evil god's
  temple).
- **Gaining PC:** +1 per evil list known; +1 more per evil list carried past 10th level;
  optionally +1 per cast of dark magic; certain items; pacts with demons/Ahriman.
- **A SECOND, distinct corruption type — Elemental Corruption** `[DEEPENING]`: some
  materials and bloodlines corrupt not by Darkness/Ahriman but by an **Element** (e.g.
  Umbra gives "punti corruzione da elemento (Buio)"; the *Sangue Elementale* talent
  starts a PG with "20 punti corruzione e 4 punti **fattore** in un elemento"). Being
  element-corrupted grants **fattore** (factor) points that buy element-typical traits —
  i.e. corruption can mutate you *into* an elemental being. This ties directly to the
  **Fattore/Essenziometro** concept in canon Armora magitech.

### 1.2 Character creation `[CANON — creazione-personaggi.txt]`
Generic, system-agnostic **advice** only (a translated 7-point guide: make the PG fun,
playable, group-compatible, adventure-motivated, long-term-motivated, campaign-appropriate,
and interested in others). Only setting colour: cautionary examples — don't play a **Light
Elf (Elfo Chiaro)** in a **Gaerwath**-set campaign; don't bring a necromancer into an
undead-hunter party. No Ymir-specific mechanics here.

### 1.3 Played characters & professions `[CANON — pg-di-ymir.txt]`
A roster of ~40 PCs. Not classes per se, but it reveals the **professions/archetypes in
play** and seeds lore. Ymir-flavoured or notable professions attested: **elementalista
arcano**, **elementalista della luce** (Accademia di Studi Elementali, Armora),
**magent** (an evil human "magent" of Ulan-Tang — likely a Rolemaster Magent profession),
**biomante** and **necromante** (a Gaerwath elf "ex-biomante ex-necromante"), **stregone
demonologo** esperto di golem, **arcimaga/arcimago**, **inquisitore/inquisitrice**,
**paladino**, **chierico**, **bardo**, **mistico**, **monaco danzatore**, **alchimista**,
**spadaccino**, **rogue/assassino**, **veggente (Fata)**, **barbaro**.
- Lore nuggets worth keeping: **Drusilde (Thulilachtle)** — exiled **Xattva** archmage
  who founds her own private school of magic (matches canon's Arcimaga Sir Drusilde of
  Armora's Collegio del Settimo Circolo → `[DEEPENING]`, gives her a race and backstory).
  **Kamal "il Folle"** — a dwarf mutated into a deformity after swallowing a **seed of
  the Albero del Caos (Tree of Chaos)**. **Raggot-Tod** — Cartosan smuggler with
  **Tanoth** blood, captain of a cursed ship sailing "half between Ymir and the World of

  **Masamune** from the Dragon-Gods. **Varg** — Vorskgen who transferred his soul into a
  **metal body** (losing his will) to perfect his combat art. **Han Hsian-Tz'** — dancing
  monk of the secret **Aironi Grigi (Grey Herons)** triad fighting the Dragon-God realm.
  Races/peoples in play: **Umli, Tanoth/Ur-Tanoth, Vorskgen, Xattva, Fata, Notturni,
  Mezzelfi, Nani, Orchi, Elfi (Gaerwath/oscuri)**.

### 1.4 Ymir-specific talents `[CANON — talenti.txt, "NEW/YMIR TALENTS"]`
`talenti.txt` is overwhelmingly a **standard Rolemaster (RMSS) Talents & Flaws catalogue**
(~2,600 lines; English talent names — Arcane Discovery, Inner Reserve, Power L/Mi/Ma/Gr,
Skilled, Racial Training, Special Familiar, etc. — with an Italian "Note" column). **No
setting flavour** in the main table. The **only Ymir-original content** is a short
"NEW/YMIR TALENTS" section at the end, all lore-bearing:
- **Sangue di Drago (Dragon Blood, 50 pt):** a dragon has mingled its blood with the PG's
  via ritual, in exchange for eternal obedience/loyalty; grants a slightly "draconic" look
  and options: Dragon's Might (stat bonuses), Dragon's Breath, Tough Hide (scales), or
  **Wings (flight)**.
- **Sangue Elementale (Elemental Blood, 15 pt):** an ancestor was element-corrupted and
  passed it down; PG starts with **20 corruption points and 4 element "fattore" points**
  (see §1.1).
- **Affinità Elementale (25 pt):** +10 DB/RR vs a chosen element, +5 to cast lists of that
  element.
- **Leggenda Vivente (Living Legend) Major/Greater:** unlocks Heroic / Fantastic **Chi
  Powers** (Rolemaster "Ki" mysticism) beyond normal limits.
- **Alleato Elementale (25 pt):** pact with an elemental — 25% chance a Lesser Elemental
  aids the PG in danger.
- **Inarrestabile / Luminare:** halve HP-loss penalties; make 5 Lore skills Occupational.

### 1.5 The six Elemental Lords `[CANON — oggetti-vari.txt]` `[DEEPENING of the "six Elementi"]`
The *Sangue Elementale* artefact set names **all six elemental lords** and pairs each to
its element — confirming and naming the canon "six Elementi":
**Hudemia (Acqua/Water) · Heis (Aria/Air) · Diis (Fuoco/Fire) · Theréos (Terra/Earth) ·
Dingir (Luce/Light) · Varshnak (Ghiaccio/Ice).** They were **once mortal**; their blood
was used in "il rituale che portò al **Cataclisma**." A **seventh, separate** figure —
**Topramesk "il Giallo"/"il Folle"** — is tied to **Mana/Void** (his blood grants *Mana
Law, Void Law*) and is elsewhere named "l'arcimago supremo... colui che pose termine alla
**Guerra Demoniaca**" (the ring "Portentoso Anello del Baluginio" is attributed to him).
`[INFERRED]` Topramesk sits apart from the elemental sixfold, associated with raw
Mana/Void (cf. canon's Essenza/Arvo). Cross-links: **Heis (Air)** is worshipped in
**Helgedad** (Kamal is "Alto Sacerdote di Heis in Helgedad").

## 2. Weapons & Armor (setting-flavour / named items)

Nearly all entries are **legendary named items** with rich lore; treat as an artefact
catalogue. Recurring lore threads: **Guerre Demoniache**, **Guerra delle Onde** (War of
the Waves), **Guerra della Terra Infranta**, **Era degli Adepti**, elemental blades, and
elemental-corrupted metals.

- **La Mannaia / La Schiantatrice / La Rovinatrice** — a **triad of twin weapons** from
  the Guerre Demoniache, each holding a bound demon, once wielded by the **three
  lieutenants** of the demon forces (greatsword / flail / battleaxe; Acciaio Demoniaco).
- **Strappacarne** — an intelligent, telepathic berserker blade (appears as falchion,
  scimitar or broadsword by its own whim); thirsts to fight and drink blood.
- **Mornlosse** — formerly one of the **lame elementali (elemental blades)**, since
  **corrupted by evil**; mithril longsword with cold power, animates the slain as Frost
  Skeletons.
- **Vendicatrice Oscura** — a **major Earth elemental blade** of an indestructible black
  metal (Ebony).
- **Hellion** — a **major Fire elemental blade** of Acciaio Demoniaco.
- **Frangionde** — bastard sword in **Bulusture**, used in the **Guerra delle Onde**; a
  **minor elemental blade**, twin of the lost **Tessionde**.
- **Mietianime (Soul-Reaper)** — "one of the most evil objects ever made in all Ymir,"
  legendarily forged **by the god of death** himself; black crystal blade, Adamantium;
  currently wielded by **Morannagul, lord of the dark elves**, the only living being able
  to hold it (thanks to his **metal arm**). Companion piece **Mieticorpi (Body-Reaper)**,
  a black-metal longsword tempered in its own smith's blood.
- **Elethar, il Cacciatore** — silvered elvish longbow; its origin story births the

- **La Spada di Valdis** — wielded by paladin **Valdis Nemertine** in the **Guerra della

- **Piccolo Artiglio** — from the **Era degli Adepti**, forged with **Mithra's blessing
  by Urkhramest il Devoto**; detects demons (glows blue).
- **Oblivion** — battleaxe forged at **Hagelbarn in Letia by the alchemist Hans Schlecht**,
  commissioned by the **ex-inquisitor Heinrich Kramer** and gifted to the paladin **Urikal**
  to corrupt him; purest Acciaio Demoniaco, votive to **Ahriman**, holds a dormant demon
  that wakes at 25+ corruption. `[DEEPENING]` — ties to canon's lich-cultist Kramer.
- **La Dominatrice** — spiked metal whip with a human-skin grip; enslaves victims via an
  embedded metal fragment.
- **Pugnale del Rituale Blasfemo** — golden ritual sacrifice dagger; sacrificed victims
  become Class II undead, souls consumed by its ruby.
- **Morgenstern** — mithril morning star by the greedy dwarf alchemist-smith **Kellar**;
  "Slayer of Thieves," slowly makes its wielder avaricious.
- **Ascia di Droan** — golden dwarven battleaxe of the hero **Droan** (mithril + magico
  Acciaio Nanesco).
- **Pugni di Hexor** — mithril plate gauntlets; a dead paladin's grief-imbued gift to his
  Vorskgen consort.
- Also: **Frecce Sanguinarie** (bleeding arrows), **Forgiavedove** (Widow-Forger,
  bleeding scimitar).

## 3. Martial & Armed Combat Styles `[CANON]`

### 3.1 Unarmed / martial-arts styles `[stili-arti-marziali.txt]`
- **Waghrashilat** (60 pt) — style of the **Regni Belligeranti**, of the **Monaci &
  Monaci Guerrieri worshippers of Hudemia** (Water).
- **Danza dei Tre Venti Divini** (Dance of the Three Divine Winds, 60 pt) — Qi-Long style
  of the **school of the 4 Divine Winds**, handed down by masters of the **Airone Grigio
  (Grey Heron)** triad; an adaptation by **Han Hsian-Tz', pupil of Pai Yuchan**. Strikes
  named for the cardinal beasts (White Tiger of the West, Red Phoenix of the South, Azure
  Dragon of the East); evokes Qi-Long opera-theatre dance.
- **Shiuyiaa** (60 pt) — an **alien style native to Umbra** (the shadow-plane material's
  home world); can incorporate natural weapons.

### 3.2 Armed combat styles `[stili-di-combattimento-armato.txt]`
- **Danza delle Foglie Autunnali** (Dance of Autumn Leaves, 60 pt) — traditional **elvish
  two-sword** style for fighting many foes at once; special move **Turbine d'Autunno**.
- **Scherma Cartosano Classico** (60 pt) — Cartosa's classic rapier fencing (reach,
  footwork, reflexes).
- **Scherma Cartosano Torquez** (60 pt) — a **counter-style** designed to beat the Classic
  (uses the **Guanto d'Arme** plate gauntlet to parry/grab/break the enemy's blade).
- **Scherma Armorita** (30 pt) — Armora's simpler, more static, less tiring fencing.

## 4. Materials & Metals `[CANON — materiali.txt, metalli.txt]`

Key magical substances (capture-all). Note the recurring **elemental-corruption** motif:
several metals are "corrupted by" an element and can taint their user.

- **Shald** `[CANON — canon material]` `[DEEPENING]` — violet **magic glass**, made when
  volcanic glass is exposed to intense magic. Resistant (weapon bonus +15/+25); prized
  because it is an **Essence reservoir** — a thumbnail-sized shard holds ~15 PP, which an
  Essence/Arcane user can absorb (1/round) and which **self-regenerates** from ambient
  magic (~1 PP/hr, faster where magic is rich). "Priceless, sought by every mage." (Adds
  hard numbers to the canon "magic glass.")
- **Umbra** — extremely rare; **from another planet**, brought to Ymir **before the magic
  collapse** by the **Notturni** who travelled between worlds. Shape-shifting, **light-
  absorbing** (deep black, "living"/faintly flaming look). Superb for weapons (+35,
  Tearing crits + cold), cloaks/armor (+35 DB, +50 stealth). **High elemental content →
  corrupts** the bearer (Dark/absence-of-Light corruption: 1 PC per 24h worn). ~10,000 gp
  per kilo. (Also the home world of the Shiuyiaa martial style.)
- **Acciaio Demoniaco (Demon Steel)** — blood-clot-coloured, ~250% iron's weight, virtually
  indestructible, faint evil aura; an alloy whose elements are **not of this dimension**
  ("forged in Hell"). Weapons +35 + evil abilities but −3 initiative, ≤50% BO to parry.
  Wearing full demon-steel armor grants **1 corruption point/day** (mutations, madness,
  possession).
- **Acciaio Onirico (Dream Steel)** — mined only deep beneath the **Terra dei Sogni (Dream
  World)**; colour-shifting, near-impossible to work. Universal property: bonus **from −50
  to +50 based on the user's belief/conviction** in the action. Alloys with Shald, Eog,
  Mithril, ordinary steel. `[DEEPENING]` — ties to canon's Oniroscopio / Mondo dei Sogni.
- **Lagorak** — electric-blue metal **from an outer dimension**; cuts iron/steel like
  cardboard but is baffled by organic armour (**inverts armour types** — treats heavy
  armour as soft leather and vice-versa). A **Mithril-Lagorak alloy** (greenish) keeps
  Mithril's +25 but is **extremely poisonous** to living Ymirians.
- **Orihalcum** — copper-like, very hard, cold to the touch; **completely immune to and
  nullifies all magic** it contacts. Weapons ignore magical defences; armour grants
  anti-magic DB/RR proportional to amount worn (a full Orihalcum harness ≈ near-total
  spell immunity).
- **Thalaras** — bluish, iridescent **arcane stone** from deep coastal/undersea deposits;
  an excellent **conductor/amplifier of magic**. Wands of Thalaras give +10/+15 to
  directed-spell BO; Thalaras objects enchant ~50% faster; used in **automata/constructs**
  and by priests as channeling foci.
- **Actorios** — dark-red pulsing gem; **not natural — a refined form of Shald**. Prized by
  **summoners**: +15/+25 to Summoning manoeuvres (stackable).
- **Vetro Abissale (Abyssal Glass)** — resistant (+20) grey-blue **magical resin from
  another plane**; used by some **demons/extradimensional creatures** for gear. Unlike
  Demon Steel it is wieldable by mortals; armour of it is **impervious to all elemental
  attacks**.
- **Guscio di Yarhim (Yarhim shell)** — the bluish carapace of the "Yarhim" creatures;
  **absorbs magic**; makes good shields (+10, +5 anti-spell) and, powdered, is a potion/
  alchemy component (150–800 gp). → ecology cross-link.
- **Metals named only in item entries** (not in the material files, worth a glossary):
  **Mithril** (the standard "strikes as Mithril" benchmark), **Ebony** (indestructible
  black metal), **Galvorn** ("fallen from the sky"), **Adamantio (Adamantium)**, **Alto
  Acciaio (High Steel)**, **Acciaio Nanesco (Dwarven Steel)**, **Bulusture** (metal
  **corrupted by elemental Water**, of the Guerra delle Onde), **Eog** (used for the six
  sealed **Sangue Elementale** vessels).

## 5. Ecology — Herbs & Diseases

### 5.1 Herbs, drugs & poisons `[CANON — erbe.txt]`
- **Radice di Qarando** — deep-jungle fern root; ingested → ~15–30 min euphoria (stun
  resistance) but reckless, headlong behaviour; addictive.
- **Distillato di Utombe** — jungle plant; potent **analgesic** (ignore pain/HP penalties
  for ~1 hr), but risks unnoticed self-injury; in heavy doses a paralytic/lethal nerve
  poison.
- **Essenza di Ashala** — sought perfume from the **Qi-Long**; +10 Seduction for 2 hrs.
- **Loto Verde (Green Lotus)** — powerful drug from the **Grande Erg**; sends the soul to
  the **Regno dei Sogni (Dream Realm)** for ~2 hrs of guided visions (as a "Dream" spell);
  officially **banned in many realms**; risk of irreversible coma/death. → Dream World lore.
- **Aljira** — bluish liquid from **Loto Nero nectar**; instantly restores 1d10 PM, but
  repeat doses risk **subtracting** PM instead; addictive.
- **Lacrime di Davedars (Davedars' Tears)** — poison favoured by **Cacciamaghi (mage-
  hunters)**; drains a caster's PM. → ties to §6 La Fratellanza.
- **Loto Rosso (Red Lotus)** — potent poison (tremors/blindness/coma by severity).
- **Loto Nero (Black Lotus)** — one of Ymir's rarest drugs, grows only in deserts; its
  powder grants huge magic sensitivity (+20 casting, +30 Spell Mastery, ×1.5 spell
  duration) but permanently drains Reasoning & Self-Discipline; highly addictive.

### 5.2 Diseases `[CANON — malattie.txt]`
Mostly **real-world diseases** transposed (given clinical detail, useful as period plague
flavour): **Vaiolo (Smallpox)** — Variola major/minor, ~50%/1% mortality; **Colera
(Cholera)** — Vibrio cholerae, oro-faecal, 30–50% untreated; **Peste (Plague)** — Yersinia
pestis, three forms: **Bubbonica** (60%), **Polmonare** (95%, airborne), **Setticemica**
(99%).
- **Magositi** `[Ymir-specific]` — **not a true disease**: tiny red louse-like **parasites
  that feed on the magic energy gathering around casters**. Cause itching and, worse, make
  **spellcasting costlier** (+1 to +5 PP per spell by infestation size). Cured by **not
  casting for a couple of weeks.**

## 6. Notable Items, Artifacts & Written Works `[CANON — oggetti-vari.txt, oggetti-ed-artefatti (stub), scritti-e-pergamene.txt]`

### 6.1 The Door of Despair complex (major set-piece lore)
- **Porta della Disperazione (Door of Despair)** — the only ancient portal potentially
  **re-activatable**. Opening it needs a forgotten ritual and: two of the **Pergamene
  Dimenticate**, the **seven Pietre-Chiave**, the **Chiave del Nulla**, removal of **three
  Sigilli** (the second needs the **elemental blood of all Six Lords**, *or* the blood of
  **Topramesk il Giallo**), then casting **Unholy Gate** for 6 weeks — with a high chance
  of catastrophe/madness.
- **Pergamene Dimenticate (Forgotten Scrolls)** — **eight scrolls** protected by a magic
  Krypton, "the only documented knowledge to survive the great disaster." They form:
  *Il Risveglio degli Dèi* (forbidden ritual, 2 scrolls), *La Filosofia degli Immortali*
  (**Alta Alchimia / High Alchemy**, 3 scrolls), *Demonologia Superiore*, *Necromanzia
  Superiore*, and the most coveted, *La Serpe Unificatrice* (teaches the **language of the
  Other Gods / Altri Dèi**). Written in an archaic **Linguaggio degli Antichi**.
- **Chiave del Nulla** (interlocking white cube; portal key), **Pietre-Chiave** (seven
  rune-bearing river-glass pebbles), **Sangue Elementale** (six seamless **Eog** vessels of
  the six lords' mortal-era blood; each grants that element's spell mastery lists) and
  **Sangue di Topramesk** (grants Mana/Void lists) — all used in the **Cataclysm ritual**.
  → see §1.5 for the elemental lords.

### 6.2 Legendary/named artefacts
- **Corona della Stregoneria (Crown of Sorcery)** — evil artefact **made by the power of
  three malign deities**; a legendary-intelligence item (Will 345) devoted to dragging the
  world into darkness; grants ×9 realm multiplier, all evil lists to 50th, mass Domination
  and undead control. "If it reappears, cities will tremble and endless dark will fall."
- **Portentoso Anello del Baluginio** — attributed to **Topramesk "il Giallo"/"il Folle,"
  the supreme archmage who ended the Guerra Demoniaca**; true powers unknown; glows even in
  the deepest magical darkness.
- **Gwynder's lucky set** — the **giacchetta/ciondolo/sciarpa/stivaletti/guanti fortunati**
  of **Gwynder**, an archmage cursed with bad luck who crafted talismans to beat his fate
  (then drowned in a tub without them). All manipulate open-roll/luck mechanics.
- Evil/undead-themed gear: **Borsa delle Ossa** (bag summoning 50 skeletons), **Corona
  d'Ossa** (elf-skull crown), **Sudario della Pestilenza** (plague-carrier shroud, casts
  Pestilence), **Elisir della Servitù Oscura**, **Teschio Cornuto**, **Stendardo
  Grottesco** (evil war-banner), **Anello dei Cattivi Pensieri** (worn by the **malvagi
  inquisitori della Mano Nera / Black Hand**), **Anello della Sete Eterna** (turns wearer
  into a **Vampire** in a month), **Monile dell'Occhio di Tenebra**.
- Utility/benign: **Anello della Medicina**, **Forgia Speculare** (Mirror Forge — pull an
  evolving weapon from a lightless box), **Possente Amuleto di Taros**, **Corno da Guerra**
  (dragon-horn war horn), **Mantonero** (a semi-intelligent symbiotic living cloak), **Luna
  Cacciatrice** (summons Night Hounds), **Ragno di Ferro** (iron figurine → giant spider),
  **Amuleto Ottico** (replacement magic eye), **Orecchini del Masochismo**.
- Written works: **Tomo della Falsa Sapienza** (lets a bluffer be believed on academic
  matters) plus the **Pergamene Dimenticate** (duplicated in scritti-e-pergamene.txt).

## 7. Societies & Corporations `[CANON — societ-e-corporazioni.txt]`

Global organisations (each with a symbol):
- **Alba Nera (Black Dawn)** — "probably the most malign and insane organisation in all
  Ymir." Worshippers of **Ahriman** and cultists of the **Demoni Sovrani (Sovereign
  Demons)**; act through independent local cells referring to an unknown central nucleus;
  ultimate goal is to **summon the Sovereign Demons in person** (nearly succeeded once).
  **Symbol: Sole Nero (Black Sun).** `[DEEPENING]` — canon `factions.md` lists Alba Nera
  as the faction behind the **Anno del Sole Nero coup (3017 d.M.)**; this gives its creed,
  cell structure, goal, and symbol (which explains the coup's name). Also appears in play:
  Raphael and Ruben Montoya died fighting the Alba Nera.
- **Azar-Kalish — the Conclave delle Ombre (Conclave of Shadows)** `[NEW]` — a secretive
  sect of **sorcerer-assassins of Ulan-Tang**; members and location unknown — *they* find
  you; a signed kill-contract can never be withdrawn or stopped. **Symbol: Ragno (Spider).**
- **Confraternita della Torcia (Brotherhood of the Torch)** `[NEW]` — sacred order of the
  goddess **Hokhmah** (laity + priests); preserves/spreads benevolent knowledge and
  destroys dangerous/evil lore. Based at **Armora**, led by **Colui-che-Cammina-nella-Luce
  (He-Who-Walks-in-Light).** **Symbol: a Torch.**
