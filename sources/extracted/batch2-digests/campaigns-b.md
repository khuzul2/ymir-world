# Campaigns-B Digest (Ymir actual-play session logs)

Source: `/home/user/ymir-world/sources/extracted/batch2/campaigns-b/` (25 `.txt` files, mixed Italian/English GM prep notes + session write-ups). Rolemaster-style stat blocks (Lvl/Tag/PF/TA/BO/Iniz) are omitted here except where a name or fact is load-bearing.

## Overview of arcs

These files belong to **three (possibly four) distinct campaigns**, all set in the world of Ymir, chiefly in the **Regni Giovani (Young Kingdoms)**:

1. **The Armora War campaign** (the bulk: ~18 files). A war-torn **Granducato di Armora** under **Granduca Leonius VonGryffen I**, fighting **Vallanya**, **Caeldir**, and orc/goblin hordes while the dwarf kingdom of the **Wald** collapses. The overarching threat is the golden dragon **Gwalgamaur** and his four dragon-lords (Vorak, Maara, Durga, Shura), plus a hidden techno-necromantic villain, **il Tecnocrate**, and his vampire agent **Leonard**. Recurring PCs: **Isidoro, Yonker, Baudolino, Surya, Han, Drusilde** (with allied NPCs Uther, Edain, Valentine).
2. **"A Black Sun Rising"** (`riassunto.txt`). A separate party in the **Principato di Vallanya** (Cartosa, Ombra del Mar, El Paso) hunting the **Alba Nera** cult and the corrupt mage **Tobias**. PCs: **Alfonso, Don Pablo, Raphael, Kiki, Ruben, Valerio Luiz**.
3. **"Evil Never Dies"** (`New1.txt`). A sci-fantasy shipwreck arc on an alien planet (the old **Valesian Empire** invaded by the **Xatos**). PCs unnamed. Distinct enough that it may be a planar/other-world excursion rather than mainland Ymir.
4. A **demon-cult sub-arc** (`Toyman.txt`, `Una Tenebrosa Adunanza.txt`) tied to the Sovereign Demon **Abigor**; connects back to the Armora campaign via Belengard, Valentine, Isidoro, and the reborn vampire Leonard.

### CRITICAL DATING NOTE (bears on "present ≈ 3518 d.M.")
Every explicit in-world date in these logs places the action at **~3025–3026 d.M.**, roughly **490 years BEFORE** the 3518 present the task assumes:
- `Intro.txt`: "**3 Grifone dell'Anno 3026**".
- `Prologo 01 - il Complotto dei 5.txt`: "**Anno 2990 d.M. (35 anni fa)**" — internally consistent with a ~3025 present. (The same file also writes "il 15 della Viverna del **3290**"; that "3290" is almost certainly a typo for 2990, since the surrounding text repeatedly calls it "35 years ago.")

The reigning ruler here is **Leonius VonGryffen I** (spelled *VonGryffen*, cf. the conflict's *VonGriffen*), NOT Vespertina. See "Conflict flags" at the end.

## Per-file digests

### The Armora War campaign

**Intro.txt — "La Festa Danzante / Il Messaggero / La Torre"**
On the evening of **3 Grifone 3026**, Armora throws a grand ball at the **Padiglione delle Scienze** to boost morale during the war; the PCs are invited (lower-tier guests). A war briefing lists the fronts: orcs pushed back near Bosco di Rhems, stalemate in the mountains beyond Forragelida, chaos in the North-West (Armora vs Tydriell vs orcs), Armora + Zaalesha winning at sea to the East, stalemate against Vallanya and Caeldir on the other front. A messenger (**Iulius**) arrives by airship from General **Loren (Leon) Artois**, whose scouts found an ancient dug-out **xebechani tower** on the NW border, defended by supposed "Caeldirites" using **mechanical ghouls (ghoul meccanici)**. PCs are sent to infiltrate the tower.
- Dates/locations: 3 Grifone 3026; Armora; NW border; Bosco di Rhems, Forragelida.
- NPCs: Loren (Leon) Artois (young general, Armsmaster), Renoir (his wounded mage advisor), Iulius (soldier-messenger), Leonard (the enemy "Caeldirite" commander, a vampire — "V").

**Torre di Caccia.txt — "La Torre di Caccia (personalità)"**
GM cast list for the **Torre di Caccia**, stronghold of the **Frecce d'Argento** rangers in the Bosco di Rhems. The Bosco has been invaded by goblins/orcs out of the Wald, cutting the fortress off from Armora for a month. Establishes the ranger command and the stranded dwarf embassy from the Wald that came to beg aid against the orcs.
- NPCs: **Sir Maegor** (Captain-Regent of the Frecce d'Argento, later revealed traitor); **Tyrion** (High Custodian/advisor); **Ulwor** (Ur-Tanoth ranger); **Aldric** (innkeeper of the Orso Ubriaco) with wife Martha, daughter Erinne, stable-hand Othor, maids Berta/Anya/Sansa; **Dwalor** (dwarf ambassador of the Wald) with **Nori** and **Tuldir**; **Ehldruss** (druid/animist who senses a dark influence in the forest); **Galdor** (elf fire-elementalist, cut off from the Academy via the "Polla della Veggenza"); **Drolam** (smith) and apprentice **Sedor**.

**Web of Goblins.txt**
A goblin-rescue adventure at the **Paiolo Volante** inn (a day north of Keswig, edge of Bosco di Rhems), run by the healer **Jarni** (friend of Telesius). A dying dwarf **Thulfren** reports a caravan carrying sacred **icone di Hokhmah** was ambushed by goblins; sheriff **Jarron** rallies a rescue. The goblins, led by orc-cleric **Magnar** and ice-corrupted orc-cleric **Lushgurth** (both of **Varshnak/Varshank**), are performing a ritual. One icon secretly hides the church-relic **L'Occhio di Luce** (Eye of Light), which recently cracked when consulted as an oracle at the **Caldera** temple of Hokhmah (founded centuries ago by seer **Norin il Venerabile**).
- Locations: Paiolo Volante, Keswig, Bosco di Rhems, Caldera.
- NPCs: Jarni, Thulfren, Jarron, Magnar, Lushgurth, Nusgam (dwarf cleric of Hokhmah), Sulfren (monastery head), Telesius (referenced).
- **Relic:** L'Occhio di Luce.

**La Caduta della Torre.txt**
The Torre di Caccia falls. **Sir Maegor** is exposed as a traitor in league with agents of **Lord Vorak** (himself a vassal of **Gwalgamaur**), promised lordship over the forest. Maegor had held the dwarf diplomats prisoner under false pretenses. The PCs return to find the tower flying gallows: **Tyrion**, smith **Drolam**, **Galdor**, and loyal rangers hanged; the dwarf envoys impaled — **Yonker** spots **Dwalor's** signet ring on the ground. **Lord Vorak** (a towering man in blood-colored **Demonic Steel** armor, red beard, wild eyes) frees captured PCs on horseback with the message: "the Wald has fallen, the Torre di Caccia is lost, and this land is Ours!" Also: **Drusilde's** side-mission to steal/copy the map of Armora's Academy magical defenses.
- NPCs: Maegor, Lord Vorak, Gwalgamaur (referenced), Ulwor, Drusilde.

**Vacations.txt — per-PC interludes (strong PC-identifying material)**
Recap: after freeing captured companions from the goblins and returning to the Torre di Caccia, the party found it taken by orcs, Maegor a traitor, the rangers hanged and the Wald dwarf delegation impaled. Lord **Vorak** — bestial (yellow eyes, fangs, claws), on a saber-tooth tiger — arrived leading an orc army; **Surya** challenged him to single combat with his sword **Masamune** and was beaten with inhuman ease. Vorak released the party to carry news of the fall of the Torre and the Wald to Armora. Individual scenes:

Varn Dalen suspects Surya's sword is the lost **Spada di Luce** from the legend of the **Stregoni Neri** (Black Sorcerers who tried to build an empire centuries ago and were defeated by a warrior wielding a Sword of Light). **Qi-long** agents are hunting Surya for the sword. (Attendant: **Kaios**.)
- **Isidoro** passes his **Iniziato** trial at the Academy; assigned a mentor, **Maestro Gaemon**, by **madama Alyss** (High Member of Light) at the **Sala dei Cristalli** (sanctuary of **Dignir**).
- **Yonker** broods in his lab; **Gotrek** (dwarf ghetto militia chief) tells him Granduca **Leonius** is sending a contingent to Bosco di Rhems and then the Wald; Gotrek departs with dwarf fighters and asks Yonker to keep spying inside human society.
- **Baudolino** flees after killing a mysterious hooded assailant (hands bandaged); still investigating the mechanical warships sunk off Vallanya (only Armora can build such ships).
- **Drusilde**: imprisoned at the Academy by **Valentine** on **Belengard's** orders; a shadow-being (**the Ombra / Stregoni Neri wraith**) takes the stolen defense-map plans and leaves an iron ring with the note **"SE CERCHI RISPOSTE CERCHI GLI STREGONI NERI"** ("If you seek answers, seek the Black Sorcerers").

**Prologo 01 - il Complotto dei 5.txt — THE COMPLOTTO DEI 5 (canon backstory, 2990 d.M.)**
Set **2990 d.M. (35 years before the campaign present)**. **Leonius VonGryffen I** has just succeeded his father **Iulius VonGryffen IV "il Pacificatore."** Armora has just ended wars against **Caeldir** and **Vallanya** and allied with the dwarf realm of the **Wald**. **Vhorinne VonGryffen** (Leonius's sister) founds the **Arcano Ordine degli Artefici**, beginning Armora's modernization (backed by **Echtelius**, guild-head of the Elemental Academy). Fearing loss of power, **five conspirators** — High Members **Sigurd VonGraven** (Light) and **Adrith Imrazir** (Ice), **Sirius Edergarth** (Echtelius's other pupil), **Losseren Zwalich** (a Custode del Verbo), and the outlaw necromancer **Samoth** — plot to overthrow the government and install a **magiocrazia**. On **15 Viverna** they strike; a brief civil war is put down: Sirius kills Echtelius but is slain by **Belengard**; Adrith and Losseren mortally wound Vhorinne but she traps them; Samoth raises undead; Sigurd attacks Leonius but is stopped by **Branden** (of **La Fratellanza**) and vanishes forever. Samoth is later tracked, killed, burned, and sealed in his tomb under a **triple seal**.
- Also names the Academy's High Members at the time: **Roderick Eldar** (Air; future master of Telesius), **Sigurd VonGraven** (Light; master of Isidoro), **Thorak Forgiarune** (Earth), **Balthazor Rogald** (Water), **Adrith Imrazir** (Ice), **Melania Ierendel** (Fire). Notes **Belengard** was then one of Echtelius's two pupils (not yet Academy head) and **Telesius** was an 8-year-old apprentice.

**Omicidio.txt — "OMICIDIO" (murder investigation)**
The PCs (steered by **Gotrek**) investigate the murder of the dwarf artificer **Nairin**, found stabbed in his lab by apprentice **Fori**. Nairin had recently grown rich, left the ghetto often, and consulted forbidden books (incl. *Anime Inquiete*) at the **Gilda degli Artefici** library (librarian **Elendiel**, an elf alchemist). His missing project — a machine designed to **siphon the blood and souls of many people into a dead body** — was commissioned by a human. The trail runs through fixer **Rast** (Tal-Tanoth), taverns (the Boccale di Pietra kept by dwarf **Oin**; the 4 Ubriaconi kept by **Wacho** with waitress **Odessa**) to smuggler-boss **Sharky**. At the docks the PCs find **Telesius** himself loading crates with Sharky; Telesius orders an attack and flees by boat.
- NPCs: Nairin (murdered artificer), Fori, Gotrek, Elendiel, Rast, Oin, Wacho, Odessa, Sharky, Telesius.
- **Canon:** ties Telesius and Sharky to Nairin's soul-siphon machine.

**Rescue.txt — "SALVARE TELESIUS"**
The PCs have until **30 del Falco** to find and rescue **Telesius**. Captain **Artonius** delivers dwarf-language plans of **Nairin's machine** (the soul/blood-siphon), and names another man present that first night: the gentleman **Leonard**. A meeting at the ruined temple of **Mithra** goes bad: **Leonard** takes Nairin's plans and teleports away; **Sharky** and **Wulfgar** (wolf-master archer) fight. Telesius is held near the fishing village **Keswig** (~100 km from Armora on the **Telendrium**, in a swamp) at the ruined **Tomba di Samoth**. History checks reveal **Samoth** was a necromancer who took part in the **Complotto dei 5, 35 years ago**. At the tomb, **Leonard** is trying to restore Samoth to unlife using the machine but needs a powerful mage like Telesius. Guarded by the risen skeleton **Mekrull** (Samoth's apprentice). Village survivor **Marcus** reveals Leonard took the villagers into the swamp to Samoth's tomb.
- Dates/locations: 30 del Falco; Keswig, Telendrium, Tomba di Samoth.
- NPCs: Telesius, Artonius, Leonard (vampire), Sharky, Wulfgar, Mekrull, Marcus, Samoth (referenced).

### "A Black Sun Rising" campaign

**riassunto.txt — "A BLACK SUN RISING: Riassunto delle puntate precedenti"**
A full recap of a separate campaign set in the **Principato di Vallanya**. **PROLOGO:** shipwreck survivors — mage **Alfonso** (a **Manto Azzurro**) and dwarves **Don Pablo** and **Raphael** — are entrusted a gold fragment inscribed in **Ragashano** (the Adepts' tongue) for a demon-summoning ritual; a murder marked by a **black sun** dagger points to the crime-cult **Alba Nera** (offshoot of the **Incappucciati**). They gain ally **Kiki** (a **Tal-Tanoth**) and, with Sergeant **Oleandro**, foil a demon-summoning cult. **CH. I–III:** hired by **Derreck "lo svelto"** (Tal-Tanoth), they uncover the Alba Nera's iron-smuggling and Adept-relic hunt; betrayed by Admiral **Ramirez deStuba**; rescued by pirate **Inigo "lo sbarbato"** (half-elf) and taken to the pirate free-port **Ombra del Mar**; they beat the Alba Nera to lost **Cumbre de Oro** — the golden, cursed city of **Taremorn** (king **Lorith**), **cursed millennia ago by one of the Principi Demoni**. There the Alba Nera leader is unmasked as the beloved mage **Tobias** (Alfonso's old tutor and a Manto Azzurro), who seizes the **Scettro di Lorith** and flees; **Raphael** loses a hand fighting Lorith's spectre to cover the escape. They gain **Ruben "Morte in Vita"** (a scarred **Xion**, follower of **Heis**, mad god of Chance and Wind) and con-man **Valerio Luiz** (a **Sidhe**). Alfonso quits adventuring; the party reaches **El Paso**, found ruled by the villainous **Tobias**, who enslaves people to mine iron and build **demon-powered constructs**. Later chapters (IV–VI) are unwritten.
- **Demon census:** an unnamed **Principe Demone** cursed Taremorn; the Alba Nera attempts demon summonings.

### "Evil Never Dies" campaign (sci-fantasy planet)

**New1.txt — "EVIL NEVER DIES"**
PCs are shipwrecked on a mysterious planet: the old **Valesian Empire**, invaded and half-destroyed by the technologically superior alien **Xatos**. Surviving city-states (**Ranghnak**, **Valezak**) resist. Races: **Valinar** (reptilian automata-makers), **Falanar** (leonine warriors), **Tulgar** (wolf-like nobles/Inquisitors), and Humans ("**George**", construct-makers). Magic ("the Ancient Art") and psionics ("ESPer") are hunted. The party crosses a toxic jungle created by **Karagrorn "il Caduto"** (a Tulgar druid corrupted by magic), meets evil dwarf **Fylmor**, braves the "**Caverne del Labirinto Infernale della Morte**," and confronts the final boss **Ilidan**, the **Signore del Crepuscolo** (Lord of Twilight), empowered by a crown.
- Distinct setting; likely a separate/other-world arc.

## Events for the timeline

1. **2990 d.M.** — **Iulius VonGryffen IV "il Pacificatore"** dies; his son **Leonius VonGryffen I** becomes Granduca of Armora, ending the recent wars with Caeldir and Vallanya and cementing alliance with the **Wald**.
2. **~2990 d.M.** — **Vhorinne VonGryffen** founds the **Arcano Ordine degli Artefici**, launching Armora's "modernization" (backed by Echtelius).
3. **15 Viverna 2990 d.M.** (text also mis-writes "3290") — **Il Complotto dei 5**: Sigurd VonGraven, Adrith Imrazir, Sirius Edergarth, Losseren Zwalich, and the necromancer Samoth attempt a coup to found a magiocracy. It fails: Echtelius is killed (by Sirius, who is killed by Belengard); Vhorinne is mortally wounded but traps Adrith & Losseren; Sigurd is stopped by Branden and vanishes; Samoth is later killed, burned, and sealed under a triple seal.
4. **~3025–3026 d.M. (campaign present)** — Armora at war on multiple fronts (Vallanya, Caeldir, orcs) while allied with **Zaalesha** and the **Wald**.
5. **3 Grifone 3026 d.M.** — **La Festa Danzante** at the Padiglione delle Scienze di Armora; a discovered **xebechani tower** with mechanical ghouls on the NW border draws the PCs in (Gen. Loren Artois).
6. **~3026 d.M.** — Goblin/orc invasion of the **Bosco di Rhems** and collapse of the **Wald** under **Gwalgamaur's** armies (Lords Vorak/Maara/Durga/Shura).
7. **~3026 d.M.** — Betrayal of **Sir Maegor**; fall of the **Torre di Caccia** and the Frecce d'Argento; **Lord Vorak** proclaims the Wald and the Torre lost; Wald dwarf delegation (Dwalor, Nori, Tuldir) massacred.
8. **~3026 d.M.** — Murder of the dwarf artificer **Nairin** and discovery of his **soul/blood-siphon machine**, tying **Telesius**, **Sharky**, and **Leonard** to a plot to resurrect **Samoth**.
9. **30 del Falco** — deadline for/rescue of **Telesius**, held at the **Tomba di Samoth** near **Keswig** on the **Telendrium**.
10. **29 del Falco** — **Vallanya** declares Armora attacked it after two Armoran mechanical warships are sunk off its coast (casus belli).
11. **2 del Ragno** — **Caeldir** offers neutral mediation between Armora and Vallanya.
12. **7 Ragno** — A **Mithra** mission with the **Sacra Catena** reaches Armora (at Prince Elgard Yust II's appeal); a **Zaalesha** galley arrives with ambassador **Vessh'Kse**.
13. **~3026 d.M.** — **Logarth** unmasks the invader as the golden dragon **Gwalgamaur**; the PCs seek the **Corno di Hurin** in **Kelak-Varn** to break **Lord Shura's** undead siege of **Tor Varn** (King Fundin).
14. **~3026 d.M.** — At the **Santuario delle Braci**, records reveal **Gwalgamaur** is the awakened xebechani **"Custode del Quadrante 74."**
15. **~3026 d.M.** — Party recovers the Adept-era **Amuleto degli Antichi** (Numidium), the relic **Ignus**, the **Occhio di Luce**, and the **Corno di Hurin**; Surya's **Masamune** is suspected to be the lost **Spada di Luce** of the Stregoni Neri legend.
16. **~3026 d.M.** — Demonic assault on Armora led by **Valark** (Flagello Demoniaco). Separately, the **Abigor** cult (Mano del Destino) works to spread the war (Torralta).
17. **~3026 d.M.** — **Leonard** is destroyed by the PCs, then **reborn** as a vampire via the **Macchina della Resurrezione**; final confrontation in the **Tomba di Nemelatekh** with **il Tecnocrate's** forces.
18. **(Undated, Black Sun Rising, Vallanya)** — The **Alba Nera** cult and the corrupt mage **Tobias** pursue Adept relics; discovery that **Taremorn/Cumbre de Oro** was cursed **millennia ago by one of the Principi Demoni**; Tobias seizes the **Scettro di Lorith** and enslaves **El Paso** to build demon-powered constructs.

## Conflict flags

- **HIS-5 / HIS-9 (Era della Marea; relic-bearers vs the Ulan-Tang party; "two unnamed adventurers"):** **No direct evidence.** None of these files mention "Era della Marea," "Ulan-Tang," or "two unnamed adventurers." What IS present is heavy **relic-bearer lore** that may be adjacent: multiple named relics (**Spada di Luce/Masamune**, **Occhio di Luce**, **Ignus lo Scettro delle Braci**, **Scettro di Lorith**, **Corno di Hurin**, **Amuleto degli Antichi** of Numidium), and an ancient legend that the **Stregoni Neri** once tried to build an empire and were **defeated by a warrior wielding a Sword of Light**. These are candidate hooks but do not resolve HIS-5/HIS-9.
- **Present ≈ 3518 d.M.:** These sessions do NOT support 3518. Explicit dates are **3026 d.M.** (3 Grifone) and **2990 d.M.** (Complotto dei 5, "35 years ago"), plus month/day dates in the "Falco/Ragno/Viverna/Grifone" calendar (29 Falco, 30 Falco, 2 Ragno, 7 Ragno, 15 Viverna, 3 Grifone). The Armora campaign sits ~490 years **before** the assumed present. (Flag a possible typo: "3290" appears once where 2990 is meant.)
- **Sovereign Demons / demon census:** **Abigor** is explicitly a **"Demone Sovrano" (Sovereign Demon), lord of battlefields**, actively worshipped and fought (Mano del Destino, Toyman/Una Tenebrosa Adunanza). Other demon incursions: **Valark** (Flagello Demoniaco commander) + **Shurgol** attacking Armora; an unnamed **Principe Demone** who cursed **Taremorn**; the **Alba Nera** attempting demon summonings; a **Demone della V Cerchia**; lesser demons (Hothrog, Imp, Demone Spinoso).
- **Armora's rulers (Leonius vs Vespertina VonGriffen):** Ruler here is unambiguously **Granduca Leonius VonGryffen I** (spelled **VonGryffen**), son of **Iulius VonGryffen IV "il Pacificatore,"** reigning ~2990–3026+. His sister is **Vhorinne VonGryffen**. **No "Vespertina" appears** — consistent with Vespertina being a much later successor near 3518, not a same-era rival. Note the spelling variance VonGryffen vs VonGriffen.
- **Complotto dei 5 / Cinque:** Fully detailed in `Prologo 01 - il Complotto dei 5.txt` and referenced in `Rescue.txt`. The **five**: **Sigurd VonGraven** (Light), **Adrith Imrazir** (Ice), **Sirius Edergarth** (Echtelius's pupil), **Losseren Zwalich** (Custode del Verbo), and the necromancer **Samoth**. Dated **2990 d.M.**, aiming at a **magiocrazia**; foiled by Belengard, Vhorinne, and Branden of La Fratellanza. Samoth's sealed tomb near Keswig becomes a live plot point (Leonard's resurrection scheme).
