# Campaigns-A Digest (Ymir actual-play session logs)

Source: `/home/user/ymir-world/sources/extracted/batch2/campaigns-a/` (26 `.txt` files, Italian prose with English chapter titles; these are GM prep + write-ups for tabletop RPG sessions, Rolemaster-style stat blocks).

The 26 files resolve into **three distinct campaign arcs** plus one possibly-standalone session. Proper nouns are preserved as spelled in the sources (spelling is inconsistent in the originals — e.g. *DelNorte/delNorte*, *Aqua Muerta/Aque Muerte/Aqua Linda*, *Glissa/Alissa*, *Edored/Eodred*).

> **NOTE ON DATING:** None of these files carries an explicit absolute date (no "d.M." year, no "3518"). All dating is relative — see the anchors flagged in "Events for the Timeline." Nothing here fixes the present at ≈3518 d.M.; treat these as undated relative to the calendar.

## ARC A — "La Cabala Purpurea" (The Purple Cabal) — a Gaerwath demonology/necromancy campaign

A villain-protagonist campaign set inside the **Gaerwath**, an evil magocracy of Fazioni (Factions: Demonologists, Necromancers, Biomancers, Mechanists). The PCs are junior members of these factions dragged into a conspiracy around **three books encoding the True Names of the Lords of Demons.**

### 001Back.txt — Campaign backgrounds + first session
**Synopsis:** Character backgrounds and Session 1. A Mechanist-apprentice/Sword Dancer PC (baab's, unnamed) is sent to collect a package; a Necromancer PC, **Rag'shagan** (Simo's, an ex-Biomante who wears the flayed face of his old body), is sent to deliver a sealed letter and casket. Both their masters are found murdered — one necrotized at his desk, the others brutally hacked apart alongside a **Demonologo** who is the Mentor of the third PC. The casket holds a large book written in demonic script; the three PCs are now bound by a conspiracy that keeps expanding.
**Canon established:** The Gaerwath's factional society (Fonderie/foundries, ritual body-transfer rite of adulthood among Biomanti, Vat-grown corrupted bodies, no one officially kills in the Gaerwath but "disappearances" are common). Books of demonic script are lethally guarded knowledge. A murdered Demonologo Mentor connects the party.
**Locations:** The Gaerwath (unnamed city).
**Named characters:** **Rag'shagan** (young Necromancer, ex-Biomante, PC — Simo's); **Yich'an Khril'lyr "Il Debole"** ("The Weak," young Demonologo / Evil Light Elementalist / Summoner-Alchemist, PC — narrator's; scion of a leading Demonologist family); baab's PC (Mechanist apprentice-smith / Sword Dancer, unnamed). NPCs: Yich'an's unnamed **Mentore** (Demonologo, murdered); the murdered Necromancer master; the murdered Mechanist Capo Incantatore.

### 01Inizio.txt — "I Tre Libri" (The Three Books)
**Synopsis:** Lore dump on the campaign's central MacGuffins. Three books in a mysterious alphabet each hold the **Vero Nome (True Name) of one of the three true Lords of Demons — Asmodeus, Xardas, and Masak.** The names were kept by **Alastor il Sapiente** (Alastor the Sage), a mortal mage imprisoned in a planar prison for having learned them. A cabal of mages, **La Cabala Purpurea**, worked to extract the Names; they reached Alastor's prison (which led them to **T'nyss**) only to find the Lords had cut out his tongue, sewn his mouth, and driven him mad before killing him. They obtained the Names anyway via a dream-scribe.
**Canon established (demon lore — census-relevant):** The three "veri Signori dei Demoni" are **Asmodeus, Xardas, Masak.** True Names grant power over them. **Alastor il Sapiente** was jailed in a planar prison by the Lords for knowing the Names.
**Named characters:** **Alastor il Sapiente** (mortal mage, keeper of the demon-lords' True Names, imprisoned & killed); **Morgalad il libraio** (a librarian who transcribes people's dreams onto paper via a ritual and marks each book with a rune "M"; unwittingly scribed the Three Books from Alastor's dreams — the "M" rune is the clue that leads to him); **La Cabala Purpurea** (the group name the murdered masters used).

### 03Santuario.txt — "Santuario della Cabala" (The Cabal's Sanctuary)
**Synopsis:** The Cabala Purpurea held its meetings in a grove just **north of T'nyss**, chosen because it hides insignificant-looking ruins that conceal the **portal to Alastor's prison**. With the Names already obtained, the Sanctuary's defenses are mostly gone — only giant spiders (placed by the Biomante) and traps remain. A face-shaped iron door guards the room holding the portal: a black stone arch inscribed with runes like those in the Books, activated by touch.
**Canon established:** The gateway to Alastor's planar prison sits in ruins north of T'nyss, behind the Cabal's Sanctuary. Recovered loot includes "uno strano amuleto metallico rappresentante una faccia lunare" (a metallic lunar-face amulet — recurs as a motif).
**Locations:** Grove north of **T'nyss**.
**Named characters:** none new (references the Biomante of the Cabala).

### 04Oltre il Portale.txt — "Oltre il Portale" (Beyond the Portal)
**Synopsis:** Through the portal the PCs enter the **Primo Pale** (First Pale), a grey twilight demiplane with special rules (non-magical fire won't light, Essence-casting is taxed, PP drains over time). **Alastor's Prison** is a hexagonal cell-complex; at its center a metal throne holds Alastor's chained, mouth-sewn corpse on a pedestal reading "Alastor il Sapiente." Before him someone drew four elaborate circles to siphon his memories — three empty, one holding a full, glowing dream-gem vial. Escaping leads out into the **Primo Confine** (First Confine), an unnatural white savanna roamed by First-Pale demons.
**Canon established (planar geography):** The **Primo Pale** and the **Primo Confine** are named demonic outer regions; native creatures include **Tlaque** (winged whitish bovine-headed demons that scream Fear) and **Demoni del Primo Pale.** Alastor's memories were extracted into a dream-gem via ritual circles. "La Spirale del Destino" (the Spiral of Destiny) is a fixed encounter with a giant Tentacle.
**Locations:** **Primo Pale**, **Primo Confine**, Alastor's Prison.
**Named characters:** **Alastor** (his prison/corpse/throne). Creature-types: Tlaque, Demone Primo Pale, Guardia.

## ARC B — "Il Tesoro di Roberto Rodrigo l'Armorita" (Chapters I–X) — a Cartosa pirate campaign

A treasure-hunt campaign in the tropical/piratical south. Backdrop: the **Principato di Cartosa** at war with the **Repubblica di Meridian.** A party of adventurers hunts the legendary lost treasure of the pirate **Roberto Rodrigo "l'Armorita,"** shadowed by the vengeful undead sorcerer **Al Assur, il Sadhirita.** Chapters are explicitly numbered (Capitolo I–X) with English titles.

> **ARMORA TIE-IN:** Roberto Rodrigo is nicknamed **"l'Armorita" "a causa della sua provenienza"** — i.e., he hails from **Armora.** He is described (Ch. IV/VIII) as a noble/warlock who fled the **Regni Giovani** (Young Kingdoms) for practicing dark arts and turned pirate at Cartosa. This links the pirate arc, loosely, to Armora.

### 01 Intro+scarecrowman.txt — Capitolo I: "Scarecrow Man"
**Synopsis:** Intro that assembles the party and sets the treasure hook: Rodrigo hid a fabulous treasure and drew a map, hidden in his old cell at **Forte Leòn**; the PCs mean to steal it and lay low at **La Roca.** In La Roca (a neutral free-port between Cartosa and Meridian), a string of murders is really the work of **Felipe Buscadero**, the governor's gardener, who animated a straw golem ("Scarecrow Man") from a forbidden book, *Guida alla Vita Artificiale*, to kill his tormentors and rivals for **Giustina.**
**Canon established:** La Roca is an autonomous smugglers' free-port between Cartosa and Meridian. The legend of Roberto Rodrigo l'Armorita (pirate of Cartosa, ship *Implacabile*, captured at Forte Leòn, cursed his mutinous crew from the gallows). Forbidden animation magic exists in cheap grimoire form.
**Locations:** **La Roca**, **Forte Leòn**, Cartosa, Meridian.
**Named characters:** **Roberto Rodrigo "l'Armorita"** (legendary dead pirate — the whole arc's quarry); **Don Armando de la Corona** (governor of La Roca); **Giustina de la Corona** (his daughter); **Sandro** (old de la Corona servant); **Felipe Buscadero** (gardener, the golem-murderer); **Pablo** (innkeeper of "All'Ancora Arenata"); **Carlos** (local smith); victims Fernando, Paraiba, Luis, Miguel (son of merchant Luigi Manchez), Francisco.

### 02 TheLivingDead.txt — Capitolo II: "The Living Dead"
**Synopsis:** A fishing town (used as a lab, "Cay") recently occupied by Meridian's forces under **Generale Romero DelNorte** and his feared squad the **Sciacalli Purpurei** (Purple Jackals). A "plague" is actually DelNorte's alchemical experiment: the sick vanish into the governor's-palace-turned-prison and return as zombies — DelNorte is prototyping a reagent to raise a zombie army for Meridian.
**Canon established:** Meridian's army fields a **Grey Noble** general, **Romero DelNorte** (hides his nature behind a house-mask, magic scimitar "**Forgiavedove**"), pursuing alchemical zombie-army research. The inquisitorial list "**Gloria**" tracks miscredenti (DelNorte is on it as "Dottor Romero DelNorte").
**Locations:** an occupied coastal fishing town (Cay); the governor's palace/prison.
**Named characters:** **Generale Romero DelNorte** (Grey Noble, Meridian general, alchemist-necromancer; also called Santiago in a leftover draft note).

*(Note: a same-numbered fragment "I Tre Libri" appears elsewhere; the Living Dead file itself is Chapter II of the pirate arc.)*

### 03 Riding the Storm.txt — Capitolo III: "Riding the Storm"
**Synopsis:** At sea the PCs are wrecked by a giant octopus then a supernatural storm, and are taken aboard the ghost-frigate **Implacabile**, captained by the spectre **Fernando Lopez.** The vessel is Roberto Rodrigo's own cursed ship, doomed to sail an eternal tempest as punishment for the mutiny that abandoned Rodrigo to the gallows. The ghost of the loyal bosun **Guillermo l'Orbo** (murdered for opposing the mutiny) guides the PCs to Rodrigo's Jolly Roger, which lets them escape.
**Canon established:** The *Implacabile* is a real cursed ghost-ship trapped in a perpetual storm; Rodrigo's dying curse (heard by the gods, who granted vengeance) is canon. The mutiny led by second-in-command **Fernando Lopez** against loyalists like Guillermo l'Orbo. Rodrigo's cabin holds his log (the *diario di bordo dell'Implacabile*) and Jolly Roger.
**Locations:** open sea; the *Implacabile*.
**Named characters:** **Fernando Lopez** (the *Implacabile*'s captain / mutineer, now a Spettro); **Guillermo l'Orbo** (loyal one-eyed bosun, murdered by mutineers, now a helpful ghost); **Roberto Rodrigo** (the ship's original captain).

### 04 Evil Dreams.txt — Capitolo IV: "Evil Dreams"
**Synopsis:** At war-ruined **Punta del Toro** (occupied by Meridian's **Leoni Grigi**), townsfolk are tormented by nightmares seeded by a dark-skinned **Sadhirita** merchant. The PCs are pulled into a shared **Incubo** (dream-realm of non-euclidean **Grotak**-metal ruins) where a **Marchiata** girl, **Alissa**, guides them to destroy the **Tessitore di Sogni** (Dream Weaver). On waking, the hooded Sadhirita (**Al Assur**) reveals he knows they seek Rodrigo's treasure. Rodrigo's log reveals a duplicate treasure-map was left with his dwarf friend **Thorondir** at **Guaporé.**
**Canon established:** The **Sadhirita** are a dark-skinned foreign people/tradition of sorcerers. **Marchiati** (Marked) can walk the dream-world. **Grotak** is a metal from a demonic dimension. Rodrigo was learned in Ymir's history and old Xebechani legends, a noble fled from the **Regni Giovani**; he made two copies of the treasure map, one entrusted to the dwarf **Thorondir** at Guaporé with "a strange object from the treasure."
**Locations:** **Punta del Toro** (ruined; Accademia and Ordine degli Scudi in ruins); the dream-ruins; **Guaporé** (referenced).
**Named characters:** **Al Assur, il Sadhirita** (dark sorcerer stalking the PCs — arc antagonist); **Alissa** (12-yr-old Marchiata dream-walker; also spelled Glissa); **Elena** (Alissa's mother); **Estebàn** (innkeeper of "L'Oro del Bucaniere"); **Thorondir** (dwarf, Rodrigo's friend, holds a copy of the map at Guaporé).

### 05 Fast as a Shark.txt — Capitolo V: "Fast as a Shark"
**Synopsis:** A **Maelstrom**-born magical storm hits, then the PCs are captured by orc pirates of **Bagrat il Mezz'orco** (a were-shark, Cartosa's most-wanted pirate, ship *Sanguinaria*), and imprisoned on his island base **Aqua Muerta** (formerly **Aqua Linda**, a Meridian base during the **Guerra delle Onde** / War of the Waves). Escaping with triton help, they must slay the silver-vulnerable Bagrat and pass the lair of **Kaelodhros**, an ancient Dragon-Turtle.
**Canon established (census-relevant):** The **Maelstrom** is a named source of magical storms/mutations (later: swamp-trolls and unique troll races are "mutated by the Maelstrom's flux"). The **Guerra delle Onde** (War of the Waves) is a past war between Meridian-era powers; **Bulusture** (metal corrupted by elemental Water) armors/weapons date from it, wielded by great warriors. Silver harms lycanthropes/Bagrat.
**Locations:** the sea/Maelstrom; **Aqua Muerta** island (old Meridian fort); Kaelodhros's lair.
**Named characters:** **Bagrat il Mezz'orco** (were-shark pirate captain of the *Sanguinaria*, bounty 750mo); **Kaelodhros** (ancient Dragon-Turtle guard-beast). Orc jailers **Gofnur, Lagash, Urknar**; other orc names listed: **Gorak, Vaklak, Yiirkmaal.** Loot from the Guerra delle Onde: **Frangionde** (bastard sword, twin of the lost **Tessionde**), Armatura delle Profondità.

### 06 After Dark.txt — Capitolo VI: "After Dark"
**Synopsis:** At xenophobic **Guaporé** (garrisoned by the **Falchi Dorati** under Comandante **Juan Miranda**), the PCs seek the dwarf Thorondir at the **monastero di San Felipe** — a monastery of **Hokhmah** (goddess of good knowledge) secretly corrupted. Two vampires vie there: the abbot **Zenon** and the "helpful" sacerdotessa **Clarissa**, each scheming to seize the vampiric **Coppa del Nosferatu.** Signs of **Netunshiel, il Signore delle Cripte** (jackal iconography) pervade the place.
**Canon established (census-relevant):** **Netunshiel** is a demon/dark power titled "**il Signore delle Cripte**" (Lord of the Crypts), symbolized by dogs/jackals. Vampires and their powers are detailed. **Hokhmah** = goddess of knowledge turned to good. Al Assur awaits the PCs at Meridian (foreshadow).
**Locations:** **Guaporé**; village & **monastero di San Felipe** (~20–25 km off).
**Named characters:** **Juan Miranda** (Comandante of the Falchi Dorati at Guaporé); **Zenon** (abbot of San Felipe, powerful vampire, ex-alchemist); **Clarissa** (vampire posing as a priestess); **Fernando** & wife **Amanda** (San Felipe innkeepers).

### 07 The Boogeyman.txt — Capitolo VII: "The Boogeyman"
**Synopsis:** En route to **Porteno** then **Meridian**, the PCs capture **Kvetch**, a demon sent by the Sadhirita to steal Rodrigo's keys; he reveals **Al Assur** is an undead awaiting them at Meridian with a trap, believing Rodrigo's last home (pre-arrest) is there. At Meridian, the Senate is led by **Carlos Benicio Valdantini** (general of the **Idra d'Argento**). Al Assur's shadow-assassins (**Babau**) attack.
**Canon established (politics):** Meridian's **Senato della Repubblica** is currently led by **Carlos Benicio Valdantini**, of an old Meridian noble family and general of the army's chief squad the **Idra d'Argento.** Meridian, desperate to win the war, has recruited **Gratar** and similar creatures; after expelling the **Manti Azzurri** (Blue Cloaks), rival mage-guilds arose — the **Accademia delle Arti Arcane di Meridian** and the **Università degli Studi Ermetici.** Squads: **Cinghiali di Ferro** (Porteno, Comandante **Chago de Canar**). Guilds: **Gilda dei Cantori**, **Compagnia degli Scudi.**
**Locations:** **Porteno**, **Meridian** (Republic capital, on a river).
**Named characters:** **Kvetch** (captured demonietto informant); **Carlos Benicio Valdantini** (head of Meridian's Senate, general of the Idra d'Argento); **Chago de Canar** (Comandante at Porteno); **Juana/Juanna Evena** ("Gloria," a Monaca — see Ch. VIII); "Stefano Vai" (unfinished note). Al Assur (offstage). Magic item **Giduzio** (intelligent Falchion, Legal).

### 08 Son of Evil.txt — Capitolo VIII: "Son of Evil"
**Synopsis:** The true backstory: Rodrigo was a powerful **warlock** who allied with the Sadhirita **Al Assur** to find an ancient artifact; at the artifact's tower Rodrigo treacherously killed Al Assur to take it, but Al Assur, dying, cursed him (he could not activate it without the Sadhirita) and vowed to return. At Meridian's **Biblioteca** the PCs can research this; a proclamation ceremony hides an assassination attempt, and Gratar sent by Al Assur ambush the PCs in the marshes.
**Canon established (relic lore — potentially HIS-relevant):** The treasure's core is a **pre-Cataclysm artifact "forse addirittura più antico degli Adepti"** (perhaps older than the Adepti), known by many names: **Vajira, Arca della Legge (Ark of the Law), la Nave degli Dei (Ship of the Gods), l'Apparato di Q-Lahb (Apparatus of Q-Lahb), il Vascello del Caos (Vessel of Chaos)** — its bearer wields power "superior to any other in all Ymir." **Al Assur** is now a **Wraith.** Ymir troll races are "mutati dal flusso del Maelstrom."
**Locations:** Meridian (library, marshes); the artifact's hidden jungle tower.
**Named characters:** **Roberto Rodrigo** (revealed as warlock); **Al Assur, il Sadhirita** (now a Wraith, arc BBEG).

### 09 Bonus 1 - Bigger than the Devil.txt — Bonus Chapter: "Bigger than the Devil"
**Synopsis:** The PCs accidentally awaken **Moghakh-Ghob, signore del Kur**, a mighty lieutenant of the armies of Chaos sealed at the dawn of creation, when the caotic energy of a spell by **Aiolos** and a planar convergence weakens his prison. To re-seal the ever-growing demon they must seek the ancient magic-expert swamp-dragon **Damamargath il Vile** and use a fragment of the crystal sword that first bound him — one PC entering the demon's dimension to sever his tie while others kill the weaker sealed avatar.
**Canon established (census — MAJOR):** **Moghakh-Ghob, signore del Kur** — a **possente luogotenente delle armate del Caos**, sealed "all'inizio della creazione" by the forces of Law; a crystal sword was used to seal him. Statted at 40° (full) / 20° (sealed), "SL@" tag (Sovereign-class). **Damamargath il Vile / "il Fetido" / "il Magnifico"** — a very ancient magic-wise **Drago delle Paludi** (element: Air), present when the demon was first sealed (30°, SL). **Aiolos** = an in-world spellcaster whose magic triggered the release.
**Named characters:** **Moghakh-Ghob** (Sovereign demon, lord of the Kur); **Damamargath il Vile** (ancient swamp-dragon sage); **Aiolos** (caster whose spell freed the demon).

### 09 Bonus 2 - Burn in Hell.txt — Bonus Chapter II: "Burn in Hell"
**Synopsis:** A short **Abisso** (Abyss) excursion: the PCs deal with the mad Abyssal sorcerer **Zahr il Folle**, who lives in a barrier-warded tower amid unknown-tongue tomes and needs the roots of the **Zapbirch** ("Strangolatore Abissale") to finish a research. Features a **Cacodemone** and demonic-steel gear (Lama Infernale).
**Canon established:** The **Abisso** as a demonic plane; **Zahr il Folle** an Abyssal-dwelling sorcerer. Sovereign-class **Cacodemone** (20°, SL).
**Named characters:** **Zahr il Folle** (mad Abyssal sorcerer).

### 10 Forbidden Zone.txt — Capitolo X: "Forbidden Zone"
**Synopsis:** Deep in the jungle past the **Roccia del Teschio** (Skull Rock) and the hunting-grounds of the **Nkishi** tribe (led by the giant **Makni'wa**), the PCs reach the treasure's true site: a **Zona Proibita** — ruins of a very ancient, advanced/"SF" civilization, with an all-gold tower (Rodrigo's map's "tower"). A last **Custode Meccanico** (Mechanical Guardian, beast-form construct) bars the golden tower.
**Canon established:** The lost treasure sits in a **Zona Proibita**, remains of an ultra-ancient hi-tech ("science-fantasy") civilization guarded by ancient constructs — consistent with the artifact being older than the Adepti. Jungle tribe **Nkishi** (weapons: **Makreesha di Shald**).
**Locations:** the jungle; **Roccia del Teschio**; the **Zona Proibita** / golden tower.
**Named characters:** **Makni'wa** (Nkishi war-leader). Custode Meccanico (guardian construct).

## ARC C — "La Guerra del Wald / Armora" — a dwarven-war + Armora-politics epic

A large campaign spanning (1) the fall of the dwarven mountain realm **il Wald** to the dragon **Gwalgamaur** and orc warlords, (2) the human city-state **Armora** with its mage **Fratellanza** and Duke, and (3) an emerging **Tecnocrate** ("Technocrat") threat with a **Legione Meccanica** of constructs. Central plot objects: the **Rune Magiche / Runa del Potere** (Magic Runes / Rune of Power) and the **reliquie del potere** (relics of power), sought by the ancient sorcerer **Sigurd** and by dark powers. PC party (named in "Guerra e Incanti") includes **Isi/Isidoro, Drusi (Drusilla), Yonk**, and **Surya**; some PCs are dwarves and, in one sub-arc, command a band of wild orcs. File ordering within the arc is uncertain (labels mix "Atto 2/3" with standalone episode titles).

### Accampamento Orchetti.txt — "Accampamento degli Orchetti" (The Orc Encampment)
**Synopsis:** An orc/goblin war-camp of five **Lurg** (war-bands, ~150 orcs/goblins + 60 wolves) drawn from the nearby mountains, each under a commander; the supreme general "**Lord Mara**" is away gathering another contingent. The perimeter is patrolled; a controlling **Ombra degli Stregoni Neri** (Black Sorcerers' Shadow, a remote-controlled Wraith) lurks.
**Canon established:** Orc host organization (Lurg + wolf-riders + Troll di Montagna); the **Stregoni Neri** (Black Sorcerers) project remote-controlled Wraith-shadows to command orc forces.
**Named characters:** **Lord Mara** (supreme general of the camp, absent); Lurg commanders **Uglaark** (Teste di Lupo), **Rufnaag** (Assalitori Sanguinari), **Gaamash** (Teschi Spezzati), **Nuzghush** (Zanne di Serpe), **Ufthak** (Schiantanani).

### Fuga nella foresta.txt — "Fuga nella foresta" (Escape into the Forest)
**Synopsis:** Emerging from the goblin caves, the PCs meet a war-scouting party under the orc captain **Uglaark** (riding a Barghest) and must flee through the forest; a supernatural snowstorm nearly freezes them until silver arrows drive off the goblins and rescuers escort them to the **Torre di Caccia** (Hunting Tower).
**Canon established:** Continues the Wald war; unnatural snowstorms; silver-arrow allies operating from a Hunting Tower.
**Named characters:** **Uglaark** (orc captain on a Barghest).

### Attacco del Drago.txt — "L'Attacco del Drago" (The Dragon's Attack)
**Synopsis:** Introduces the arc's great antagonist, the dragon **Gwalgamaur** (66°, a Fire-element sovereign wyrm and mage). Also **Lo Scettro di Lord Durga** (a demon-mastery scepter) and **Rovine Sotterranee** — an ancient underground city with a lightning-trap entry and the riddle-guarded **Sale del Cieco** (Halls of the Blind) haunted by **Unseen** (spirits of those who hid their true identity in life).
**Canon established (census):** **Gwalgamaur** — an enormous Fire dragon-mage (66°, SL), the power conquering the Wald. **Lord Durga**'s scepter grants command over minor demons (x3 for evil casters, "Demon Communication," pact bonuses). **Unseen** as an undead type; the **Sale del Cieco** as a warded labyrinth-prison.
**Named characters:** **Gwalgamaur** (dragon overlord of the mountains); **Lord Durga** (referenced via his scepter).

### Acciaio e Oscurità.txt — "Acciaio e Oscurità" (Steel and Darkness)
**Synopsis:** Two set-pieces: the **Attacco di Bralg** (waves of bats, wolf-riders, hobgoblins, then the warlord brothers **Bralg** and **Zarg**) — winning gains the PCs 100 hobgoblins + 100 wolf-riders — and the assault on **Tor-Kragdin**, defended by **Lord Durga** and lieutenants including the sovereign-class **Gogonaur** and undead dwarven **Dreugh.**
**Canon established (census):** The renegade-dwarf warlord **Lord Durga** holds **Tor-Kragdin** with **DreadWing**, war-ogres, **Dreugh** (undead dwarves — slain dwarves rise as Dreugh), a **Gigante di Montagna**, and **Gogonaur** (25°, SL). Warlord brothers **Bralg** (sword-and-shield warrior, 18°) and **Zarg** (twin-blade mad assassin, 18°).
**Named characters:** **Bralg** & **Zarg** (orc/hobgoblin warlord brothers); **Lord Durga** (renegade dwarf lord of Tor-Kragdin); **Gogonaur** (sovereign lieutenant).

### Down down....txt — "Down Down to Goblin Town"
**Synopsis:** The PCs have settled the old dwarven road of **Karak-Fel** (their orc lieutenants: **Gork** and **Ugruk**) when **Lord Durga**'s forces — now entrenched at abandoned **Tor-Kragdin** since the Dragon went dormant — raid them via tunnels dug by the **Profondi** (Deep Ones). To get help the PCs may sway the neutral Deep-One chiefs.
**Canon established:** The PCs command wild orcs based at **Karak-Fel.** The **Profondi** (Deep Ones) are subterranean orc/goblin clans preferring neutrality; **Urborg** is the city of the orcs. With the Dragon less active, **Lord Durga** has seized Tor-Kragdin and dominates the region.
**Named characters:** PC lieutenants **Gork** & **Ugruk**; Deep-One chiefs **Zarbag** (greatest underground orc chief), **Bralg** (mad hobgoblin warband leader), **Vorg** (blind orc shaman, rumored undead), **Hurog** (great Ogre chief); **Lord Durga**.

### Atto 3 - TorVarn.txt — "Atto III — Tor Varn"
**Synopsis:** Beset by disease-bearing **Falchi Sanguinari** (carriers of the **Peste Rossa** / Red Plague — the dead rise undead), the PCs head to **Tor Varn.** Survivors **Brolin, Brannon, Nìm, Glìm** (fellow dwarves **Giuliano, Dìm, Tìm** are dead) recount the war's start: shortly after **re Threlin**'s death "a few months ago," the clans quarreled over succession at **Waldenheim**; only **Logarth il saggio**'s warning was heeded by the clan of **Dwalor** before orcs erupted everywhere. The mountains are now largely **Gwalgamaur**'s; near Tor Varn the forces of **Lord Shura** hold sway.
**Canon established (Wald history & geography):** **Re Threlin** (dwarven king) died a few months before the war began; a succession crisis at **Waldenheim** (dwarven capital). **Logarth il saggio** foresaw the danger. Only **Waldenheim** and a few fortresses still resist. Geography: **Tor Varn** (lord **Fundin**), **Torn Nifel** (ruined fortress of **Dwalor**) to the south, **Tor Lossar** to the north. **Lord Shura** fights with magic; his **Urtak** (orcs ritually made near-undead), **Hothrog** (jackal-headed demons he summons in packs), and orc **Stregoni** serve him.
