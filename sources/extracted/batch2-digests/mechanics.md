## 1. ESERCITI — Armies fielded in a great battle (`ESERCITI.txt`)

Two opposing orders of battle. These read like the muster for a single climactic
campaign (an "Army of Evil" vs. the "Army of Berthrant"). Useful raw material for
`factions.md`.

### ESERCITO DEL MALE — "Army of Evil" (Total 8,390 units)
A composite horde: dwarven traitors, goblin/orc masses, giants, trolls, and undead.
- 500 Dwarves, heavy infantry
- 500 Goblins, short bow
- 800 Goblin cavalry (mounted on **Wargs**, armed with lance)
- 1,700 Goblins, light infantry / **saboteurs**
- 3,300 Orcs (*Orchetti*), infantry — the bulk of the host
- 245 **Frost Giants** (*Giganti del Gelo*), Berserkers
- 995 Trolls
- 150 Ghosts (*Fantasmi*)
- 100 Zombies
- 100 Skeletons

Faction notes: an evil host mixing **orcs/goblins + Frost Giants + Trolls +
undead**, and notably including **Dwarf heavy infantry on the evil side** (a
renegade/enslaved dwarven contingent — worth flagging as a possible faction detail).
Warg-riding goblin cavalry and goblin saboteur units are signature elements.

### ESERCITO DI BERTHRANT — "Army of Berthrant" (Total 13,500 units)
A coalition of the free/good peoples. **Berthrant** appears to be its commander or
home realm.
- 1,500 Rangers
- 2,000 Dwarves, heavy infantry
- 3,000 Human infantry — **from Nortislong**
- 5,000 **Knights of the White Mountain** (*Cavalieri della Montagna Bianca*) —
  reinforcements arriving **from the Kelmark**
- 1,000 Elven Archers — **from the Caelebawn**
- 1,000 "Specials" — **Ents, Great Eagles**, etc.

Faction/geography notes (all promising canon hooks):
- **Berthrant** — a leader or realm heading the good coalition.
- **Nortislong** — a human realm/region supplying infantry.
- **Kelmark** — origin of the "Knights of the White Mountain" (a named knightly
  order — *Cavalieri della Montagna Bianca*).
- **Caelebawn** — an elven land supplying archers.
- Tolkienesque "special" allies: Ents and Great Eagles fight for the good side.

## 2. Re degli Orchi — Orc leadership & OORG (`Re degli Orchi.txt`)

Adventure titled **"La Tomba Sigillata" (The Sealed Tomb).** This is the single
richest piece of orc-hierarchy lore in the batch. Framed from an orc party's POV
(NPCs named **Vorg**, **Gork**, **Gurthak** — orc leaders/companions; an orc army is
camped in a city, digging in shifts to clear a blocked passage to the tomb).

### OORG — the Orc King / ancestral father of all orcs
- **Oorg** is the *legendary father of all orcs*, their **ancestral king**
  (*re ancestrale*), the mightiest of them all. A "know orc culture" check reveals him.
- **Creation myth:** the orcs were made by the gods **to fight** (against an unknown
  enemy); Oorg was their king.
- **The Rebellion:** Oorg rebelled against the very gods who made him. They defeated
  him and **buried his body forever in some remote region** — but legend holds he
  **never truly died**, only imprisoned. As punishment for his revolt, the gods
  **condemned all orcs to live forever underground and made sunlight hateful to
  them** (an in-world origin for orcish light-aversion).
- **His legendary war-gear (named relics):**
  - **La Schiantatrice** ("the Shatterer") — a weapon said to destroy any enemy.
  - An **impenetrable armor and shield**.
  - A **necklace of dragon eyes** rendering him immune to any spell.

### Languages tied to orc-kind
- **Black Speech** — the tongue of the tomb's prohibition inscriptions.
- **Ragashano** — a second, more arcane script paired with the Black Speech
  (a distinct language associated with the ancient orcish/imperial past).

### The Sealed Tomb (catalogue / crunch)
- The seal itself is a **complex magical lock disguised as an empty orc suit of
  armor** on a bronze pedestal, linked to the ceiling by strange tubes; breaking it
  requires *enormous destructive power*. Beyond it lies a second emergency seal.
- Guardians: **two Champions** — Lvl 20 Paladins (200 HP, Paladin Base spells).
- **Oorg** stat block (catalogue): Lvl 40, 525 HP, "Schiantatrice"-type weapon
  (x2 attacks), a **Fear** aura (RR vs Fear), and **Evil Essence** spellcasting
  (open + closed Essence lists). Effectively a boss-tier ancient orc god-king.

> Canon value: gives the orc hordes a mythic founder-king (**Oorg**), a named
> artifact-weapon (**Schiantatrice**), a cosmological reason for orcish
> subterranean/sun-hating nature, and a second orc-associated language (**Ragashano**).
> No explicit "Belligerent States" term appears in this file — the closest muster
> material is the "Army of Evil" in `ESERCITI.txt`.

## 3. Armora's magitech — War-mechs / animated constructs

Two construct "creation sheets" (point-buy build catalogues). These are the
war-mechs/steampunk automata associated with **Armora's magitech**. Both are built
by an in-world **construct-creation system** (Shape + Material + Size + Empowerment),
and crucially each is **animated by binding an elemental or demon** into the frame —
i.e. Ymir's mechs are **magitech golems powered by bound spirits**, not pure clockwork.

### EvilMecha — "Uccisore Meccanico" (Mechanical Slayer) (`EvilMechas.txt`)
- **Type:** insectoid — an oversized **scolopendra / centipede** shape.
- **Material:** Steel IV; **Size:** Large (x1.5).
- **Weapons/features:** **bladed tail**, **all-around vision**, **poison**;
  attacks by poisoned pincers and slashing.
- **Power source:** **Demonic Empowerment** — bound demonic force grants
  Lesser Attack (+10 BO) and Lesser Swiftness (+5 Init). This is the **"evil"
  variant** of Armora's constructs: **demon-driven war-mechs**.
- Catalogue stats: Creation Total 44; Lvl 14; 300 HP; high initiative (+21).

### YonkerMecha — humanoid bronze automaton (`YonkerMecha.txt`)
- **Type:** **humanoid** war-construct; **Bronze**; **Large (3 m tall)**.
- Medium speed; carry capacity ~250 kg; **rideable** (grants a Riding bonus — i.e.
  a piloted/mountable mech).
- **Power source:** **Lesser Earth Elemental** empowerment; power: Minor Attack.
- Catalogue stats: Creation Level 7; Lvl 4; 180 HP.
- The label **"Yonker"** appears to be a type/line name (possibly a place or maker);
  contrasts with the demon-powered EvilMecha as an **elemental-powered** model.

> Magitech notes for canon: Armora's constructs come in at least two lines —
> **elemental-bound bronze humanoids ("Yonker", rideable)** and **demon-bound steel
> beast-forms ("Uccisore Meccanico", insectoid)**. Mechs are graded by **material
> tier (Bronze → Steel IV)** and **empowerment tier (Lesser Elemental → Demonic)**.

## 5. Lance — "The Celestial Lance" & the Cult of the Blue Oyster (`Lance.txt`)

Adventure **"Alla Ricerca della Lancia Celeste" (In Search of the Celestial Lance).**
Mostly a dungeon crawl, but carries real theology/relic lore:

- **La Lancia Celeste** (Celestial Lance) — a holy relic of the god **Heis**, once
  wielded by the great priest **San Iacinto Estoleo, called "l'Impetuoso"**
  (the Impetuous). It vanished at his death **~1,500 years ago**.
- **In the year 2944 d.M.**, an oracle received a vision from Heis locating the lance
  on **Isla Ascondida** (the "Hidden Isle") — an island **submerged ~12 hours a day at
  high tide** (visible/reachable only at night/low tide). (Chronology anchor: 2944,
  vs. present ~3518 → prophecy ~574 years before "now".)
- The isle held a temple of the **Culto dell'Ostrica Blu** (Cult of the Blue Oyster),
  dedicated to one of the **Signori Demoniaci** (Demon Lords) and steeped in his
  corrupting influence.
  - Iconography names a **"Profeta dell'Ostrica Blu"** (Prophet of the Blue Oyster)
    and the **"Signore degli Abissi"** (Lord of the Abyss) — the demon-lord patron.
  - **Gemma di Gurklush** — a blood-red gem imprisoning a demon named **Gurklush**,
    of the **IV Confine** (the Fourth Boundary/Rim — a tier of the demonic planes).
- **The Celestial Lance** (catalogue): Magic Lance +5; lets the wielder **communicate
  with elementals** and cast **Charm Elemental I** once/day.

> Canon value: adds the god **Heis** and his saint-priest **San Iacinto Estoleo**;
> a demon-worshipping sea cult (**Cult of the Blue Oyster** / **Lord of the Abyss**);
> a named demon (**Gurklush**) and a cosmology term (**Confini**, the demonic
> "Boundaries/Rims", here the IV). Also introduces the **Ragashano**-less but
> demon-lord tier of the world's hostile powers.
