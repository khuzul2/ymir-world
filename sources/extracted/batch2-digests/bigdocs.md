## 1. WHAT EACH SERIES IS (identification)

Both series are **Game-Master adventure/campaign modules** — not lore treatises, gazetteers, or novellas. They are written as sequential adventure "chapters" with boxed read-aloud descriptions, keyed dungeon rooms, encounter tables, treasure lists, and stat-block tables (columns: `Lvl | Tag | PF | TA(BD) | BO | Iniz.` — i.e. level, hit points, armor/defense, offensive bonus, initiative). The rules system is unmistakably **Rolemaster/MERP** (critical hits graded A–E, spell "liste," PM = power points, PF = hit points, TR = saving throws, "manovre" by difficulty), **grafted onto a D&D-style "Planescape" cosmology** (Sigil, Baator, Limbo, Pandemonium, Gehenna, Slaad, Baatezu, Tanar'ri, factions like Harmonium/Anarchists). So they are **playable modules that embed large amounts of Ymir setting lore** as connective tissue.

They are **two distinct campaigns**, each in six installments:

- **Series A = "L'OCCHIO DI KZORR" ("The Eye of Kzorr")**, a campaign set in the **frozen NORTH of Ymir (Helgedad)**. Volume titles:
  - I — *L'Occhio di Kzorr* (The Eye of Kzorr)
  - II — *Venti di Guerra* (Winds of War)
  - III — *Tirannia e Mutamento* (Tyranny and Change)
  - IV — *Ritorno a Ymir* (Return to Ymir)
  - V — *No Rest for the Wicked*
  - VI — (untitled fragment; construct stat blocks only)
  - Party is a **villain/"evil" party** (the **"Lupi del Ragnarok"**, "Wolves of Ragnarok") that raises an orc-and-monster army to conquer the human realm of Nortislong, then is flung across the planes and returns to seek ancient evil cities. PC names referenced: **Varg** (a monk), **Ilidan**, **Amaru**, **Kamhal**, **Kronen**, **Shiranai**.

- **Series B = "IL SOLE NERO" ("The Black Sun")**, a campaign set in the **hot SOUTH of Ymir (Cartosa / the Principato / the Twin Coasts)**. Part titles:
  - PARTE I — *Il Sole Nero* (The Black Sun)
  - PARTE II — *Viaggio Contro il Tempo* (Race Against Time)
  - PARTE III — *Resa dei Conti a El Paso* (Showdown at El Paso)
  - PARTE IV — *Un Sapere Pericoloso* (A Dangerous Knowledge)
  - PARTE V — *Di Nuovo in Città* (Back in the City)
  - PARTE VI — *Un'Alba Color Sangue* (A Blood-Red Dawn; short construct-stat fragment)
  - Party is a **heroic/rogue party** opposing the **Alba Nera ("Black Dawn")** cult. PC names referenced: **Alfonso**, **Raphael/"Raff"**, **Syena / "Vale"**, **Pablo**, **Ruben** (party includes at least two dwarves).

**Bottom line:** two campaign scripts that between them touch nearly every major open question of the Ymir setting — the **Adepti**, the **Guerra Demoniaca (Demon War)**, the **demons and their Sovereigns**, the **Catastrophe / dark age**, the **ancient gods and lost pre-human civilization**, the **timeline**, and both the **northern and southern regions**.

## 2. COSMOLOGY & DEEP HISTORY (the load-bearing lore)

### 2a. The Catastrophe / dark age (frame of Series A)
Series A opens with an in-world epigraph attributed to *Cronache dell'Estremo Nord, vol. XVIII* (Chronicles of the Far North):
> "After centuries of prosperity and glory, the world fell into a dark age. An epoch without Law, nor Order…"

This frames the campaigns' "present" as a **post-Catastrophe dark age** following a lost golden age.

### 2b. The Xebechani creation myth and the primordial war (Series A, Vol V)
The single richest cosmological claim in either series is the **"Leggenda delle Città Scomparse"** (Legend of the Vanished Cities):

- **"Millions of years ago"**, the supreme god **Ech, creator of the cosmos, split into two distinct deities: Quetzacoatl and Azatzotech.** This schism triggered a **world-shaking war** ("la guerra fra Azatzotech e Quetzacoatl").
- The ancient civilization of the age was the **Xebechani** (Xebechano language; also an older tongue, **Tenocha/"antico Tenocha"**). Many Xebechani sided with the god of Life (Quetzacoatl); others were seduced by the dark gods and sided with **Azatzotech**.
- **Three Xebechani cities** dedicated themselves to dark gods and are central to Series A's endgame:
  - **Qalaktchla**, "the city that shines in the darkness," built in honor of the god **Yogh** (a Spider-God).
  - **Huitliya**, "where the dead reign," dedicated to **Azatzotech** himself.
  - **R'lyxqan**, "which silently waits," dedicated to the god **Xlucthulhu** (overtly Lovecraftian).
- At the war's end all three cities fell to the "hordes of good," were swallowed by cataclysms, and **vanished from the face of Ymir** — not destroyed, but lost, with their treasures and artifacts intact. A prophecy ("When the wolves of Hell walk the earth… / When a crowned king sits on the throne of the frozen abyss…") ties their reawakening to the campaign's events.
- The **"Vere Vie" ("True Ways")** are described as **ancient Xebechani portals** (one is found in the Rocca di Kellar). This gives the setting a named class of pre-human teleportation gates. (Note: "Xebechano" is also tied to a technological/artificer language elsewhere — see 3B, PARTE IV: warning signs at Tlantimextli's machine-halls are written "in xebechano.")

**Significance:** This is a coherent deep-time prehistory for Ymir — a **pre-human, meso-American-flavored civilization (Xebechani/Tenocha)** predating "the civilizations that currently dominate Ymir," worshipping a pantheon that split from one creator-god **Ech** into a Life/Anti-Life dualism (**Quetzacoatl vs Azatzotech**), with dark gods **Yogh** and **Xlucthulhu**. It sits *beneath* and *before* the Adepti layer.

### 2c. The Adepti / the Empire of the Adepts (frame of Series B)
Series B centers on the **Adepti (Adepts)** as a **lost golden-age empire** whose fall is an active mystery investigated in-world:
- **Cumbre de Oro / Taremorn** (Series B, PARTE II) was "once a flourishing city **within the empire of the Adepts**," built on a (now-corrupted) magical **Earthnode**. This establishes that the Adepts ruled an actual empire across Ymir.
- The scholars **Millennia Devantes** and **Tobias** "were studying the ancient secrets of the Adepts, to investigate the reason for their fall" (PARTE V). The forbidden knowledge is written in the **Ragashano** language — strongly associated with the Adepts.
- Three named forbidden Ragashano books (PARTE I): **"Litanie per il Grande Agothu"** (Litanies for the Great Agothu), **"Lo Specchio Nero"** (The Black Mirror), **"Osservazioni sui Corpi Confinati"** (Observations on the Confined Bodies). It is said these books contain "**what led the Adepts to ruin**" (Millennia). Other Ragashano tomes sought by the cult: *Note di Damarkan di Stalis*, *Compendio di Circoli e Pentacoli*, *Guida alla Vita Artificiale* (a construct-crafting manual).
- The villain **Andreas** (see 3B) is obsessed with the Adepts and dreams of restoring their **"Grande Pace" (Great Peace)** — "a new realm of peace like that of the Adepts." Her whole plot is an attempt to recreate the Adepts' order.

**Significance for the "Great Lie / Adepti / Ancient Gods" question:** These modules treat the Adepts as a historically real fallen empire whose downfall was caused by **forbidden demon-summoning magic and knowledge of "Confined Bodies."** They do **not** explicitly name or resolve a "Great Lie," but the phrase *"Corpi Confinati"* (Confined/Imprisoned Bodies) plus a "Great Agothu" plus the Xebechani theme of **gods split, imprisoned, and awaiting release** is highly suggestive of a "confined ancient gods" premise. Treat as a strong thematic anchor, not an explicit resolution.

### 2d. The Demon War(s) and the demons
Multiple layers of demonic history appear, and they may or may not be the same event:
- **"Le Guerre Demoniache" (The Demon Wars)** — the triad of weapons **The Cleaver / The Crusher / The Breaker** dates to these wars; each was wielded by "one of the **three lieutenants of the demonic forces**." (Series A, Vol II. Moga Khan's army-uniting axe is **The Breaker**; The Crusher is looted from Gormungand.)
- **"La Guerra Demoniaca" (The Demon War)** — Taremorn fell "during the **early phases of the Demon War**," when its king was tricked by **Ismael, a Demone Sovrano (Demon Sovereign)** (Series B, PARTE II).
- **Demon Sovereigns (Demoni Sovrani):** named examples are **Ismael** and **Asmodeus**. The Alba Nera's ultimate plan (PARTE IV) is to **imprison a Demon Sovereign — probably Asmodeus — inside a colossal war-construct** to animate it.
- **Demon taxonomy:** demons are ranked by **"Confine" (Confine/Circle), from ~1st up to at least the 6th**, plus **Demoni Sovrani** at the top. In-play they are mapped onto Planescape's Baatezu (Lawful, Baator) and Tanar'ri (Chaotic), with specific types (Abishai, Balrog, Goristroi, Cresh'urim, Narauk, Grumoz, Frzzzm, etc.).
- **Xardas "il Burattinaio" (the Puppeteer):** named as "one of the countless eyes of the **demon-lord** who is planning his revenge" — a demonic mastermind behind the dark-elf assassin Moredhel (Series A, Vol II). An unresolved larger antagonist.

### 2e. A planar seal over Ymir (Series A, Vol III)
A concrete, canon-relevant cosmological claim: **"The plane of Ymir has recently been subjected to numerous attacks by evil entities, so an alliance of Good deities placed a powerful seal preventing planar entities from easily entering the plane."** A single hidden portal to Ymir exists; opening it requires 4 keys, each held in a neutral deity's realm (see Vol IV). This explains *why demons must be summoned/smuggled in rather than invading freely* — a useful mechanic for the whole setting.

## 3. THE TWO CAMPAIGNS IN DETAIL

### 3A. SERIES A — "L'OCCHIO DI KZORR" (the North / Helgedad)

**Geography (Helgedad, the far north):** Kingdom/region of **Helgedad**; largest city **Kelmar**; second city **Tallvar** (walled port, ruled by house **Geidmund**, arms = the **Rampant Lion / Leone Rampante**); the **marca di Nortislong** (also Geidmund) and its capital **Nortislong**; the **Kelmark** snow-plains; villages **Hordenheim**, **Sljornburg**, **Invermore**, **Fireforge**; the dwarven fortress **Karak-Tor**; the **Monti Aestyr** (mountains) with the **Passo Skutnik**, the **Valle Sulfurea (Sulfur Valley)**, and the **Rocca di Kellar**; the **Foresta d'Inverno (Winter Forest)**; **Grudgemark / Grudgeburg / Harbor**; the dwarven necropolis **Sale Silenti (Silent Halls)** and the **Strada dei Re (Road of Kings)**; the **Accademia dei Criomanti (Academy of Cryomancers)**; the mage order the **Manti Azzurri (Blue Cloaks)** operate here too.

**Vol I — The Eye of Kzorr.** PCs pursue the sorcerer **Lord Vorgath** (a cryomancer of Nortislong nobility) who has stolen the artifact-medallion **the Eye of Kzorr**. The chase runs from Karak-Tor → Hordenheim → Tallvar → by ship (the *Tempesta*) toward Nortislong, shipwreck by a sea-monster, the ghoul-village of Sljornburg (whose ghouls sacrifice to the goddess **Thal'Khal**), and finally the cursed dream-realm of **Nortislong** ruled by **Zarak "La Bestia"** — a werewolf-like immortal tyrant (22nd lvl) trapped in a nightmare plane by the curse of a blind ghost-mage. A **second Eye of Kzorr** is recovered from the crypt of **Kzorr**; the Eye is the key to Zarak's prison. Key NPC: **Zia Istrid**, a witch with familiars Hugin and Mugin.

**Vol II — Winds of War.** After destroying the Eye at Nortislong, **Brennan** declares himself Legitimate King of the North. War rages between the marches of **Nortislong and Tallvar** (plus the **Caelebawn** elves to the south). Introduces the **Occhio di Kzorr** now held by **Sarina** (a Nortislong noblewoman/strategist, 5-year student of the Cryomancers' Academy, secretly manipulated by **Morannagul**). Major set-pieces: the mountain trek to the **Rocca di Kellar** (built by the legendary dwarf smith-alchemist **Kellar**), with its magical forge and a large treasury of named artifacts; recruiting an army from frost giants (king **Hildarf**), trolls, and **Moga Khan's Horde** (2,000 orcs united by the axe **The Breaker**); the deep **Tunnel** older than "the civilizations that currently dominate Ymir," home to the **Omungus** (worm-folk who worship "the Great Worm" and feed on psychic/magical energy); the dragon **Gormungand** (30th–32nd lvl) in the **Sale Silenti**; and the dark-elf assassin squad from the **Gaerwath** (Moredhel — a puppet of the demon-lord **Xardas**; Notorious; Celemor; Wakatta). Ends with the **Assedio di Un Giorno (Siege of One Day)** and the defeat of **Sarina**.

**Vol III — Tyranny and Change.** Sarina reveals the mastermind: **Morannagul, king of the dark elves (elfi oscuri)**, who devised the Eye-of-Kzorr scheme and instigated the Nortislong–Tallvar war "for his own unknown ends," using both Sarina and the PCs. He is at the conquered palace of Tallvar; via a magic portal the PCs confront him and he **banishes them to Hell (Baator)**. The volume then becomes a full **Planescape planar odyssey**: Avernus/Darkspire → the portal-city **Ribcage** → **Sigil** (factions: Deisti/Godsmen, Atei/Athar, Harmonium, Anarchists, Caoticisti; NPCs Yandor, Thomen, Deed, Zaymon) → **Limbo** (Slaad, the **Giardino di Eostra** and its **Alberi del Caos / Semi del Caos**) → **Pandemonium** (the fortress **Somberlain**, the ancient vampire **Faust**) → chasing the **"Tre Cacciatori" (Three Hunters — Legion, Abygor, Equimanthorn)**, pseudo-yugoloth mercenaries → **Gehenna** and the **Torre del Sapere Proibito** in the god **Gog's** Cittadella delle Mille Spire. This is where the **planar seal over Ymir** (see 2e) is explained.

**Vol IV — Return to Ymir.** A "keys" quest to reopen the sealed portal home. The portal lies in the realm of the goddess **Ishtar** (Elysium); four keys are held by four neutral deities:
- **Hudemia** — goddess of Obedience, Law, and Water (realm "La Fonte di Ogni Ordine," on Mechanus; her supplicants are the "Cloni"). *(Note: Hudemia also has convents in the southern Principato — see Series B, a cross-link.)*
- **Heis** — god of Wind and Chance (the wandering "Palazzo Incostante," Limbo).
- **Diis** — goddess of Passions (the "Giardino delle Fiamme," reached via the Efreeti City of Brass).
- **Therios** — god of Earth, the Hunt, and the Wild ("Il Groviglio Selvaggio," on Yggdrasil; intermediary **Koresh**, a were-lion).
This yields a small **neutral-deity pantheon**: Ishtar, Hudemia, Heis, Diis, Therios, plus Gog and Gehenna's gods.

**Vol V — No Rest for the Wicked.** PCs return to the Monti Aestyr. Recap of the interim: after the "**Siege of One Day**," the leaderless **Lupi del Ragnarok** army fell to infighting and was scattered; the ranger-captain **Berthrant Vaelaer** of the Grudgemark launched a crusade "to cleanse all Helgedad of the evil that had nested there" (the PCs, as villains, are now on the *receiving* end of a heroic crusade). The Rocca di Kellar is seized by the bandit **Ofthak "lo Sfregiato."** In the Rocca the PCs find a **Vera Via (True Way)** — one of the ancient **Xebechani portals** — leading to the lost cities. **Kellar's true secret** is revealed: he never wanted a fortress; he had found a map to the buried Xebechani city of **Huitliya** and dug into the mountain to reach it under cover of "building a keep." The volume then delivers the full **"Leggenda delle Città Scomparse"** (see 2b) and keys **Qalaktchla** (the living underground drow-like city of the **Figli di Yogh**, who worship the Spider-God) and the labyrinth-temple road to **Huitliya, the Dead City** of **Azatzotech** — ruled by the ageless undead queen **Tenochiletchla**, who can open the portal to **R'lyxqan** but requires the royal medallion held at Qalaktchla. Contains a very large dungeon (Aztec/meso-american-themed undead, cursed artifacts, king **Tezcatlipochitl III**) and the **Custode della Via / Custode della Via** guardian (85th lvl). Ends "GUERRA!" as the PCs' orc-horde ambushes 1,500 dwarves marching under Berthrant.

**Vol VI —** fragmentary: only stat blocks for **Costrutto Demoniaco Minore/Maggiore** (demon-powered war constructs dropped onto cities) — notably the **same construct concept as Series B's endgame** (see cross-links, §4).

**Notable named artifacts (Series A):** the two **Eyes of Kzorr**; **Scettro dei Sospiri**, **Morgenstern** (Kellar's cursed mithril morning-star), **Mornlosse "Neraneve"** (a corrupted elemental blade), **Forgia Speculare**, **Bracciali del Predatore**; from Gormungand's hoard **The Crusher**, **Mieticorpi**, **Elethar il Cacciatore**, **Corona del Sapere Proibito**, **Borsa delle Ossa**; and Huitliya's dark relics (**Pugnale del Rituale Blasfemo**, **Sudario della Pestilenza**, **Teschio di Cristallo**, **Staffa della Necromanzia**, etc.).

### 3B. SERIES B — "IL SOLE NERO" (the South / Cartosa & the Principato)

**Geography (the southern Principato / Coste Gemelle / Twin Coasts):** the capital-city **Cartosa** (seat of the **Principato**; the Marina's **Squadra Est**); **Nevarrez** (the **Coste Gemelle**, **Squadra Nord**); **Gargona** (Squadra Ovest); **Puerto Escudo** (Squadra Sud); **Armora**; the mining town **El Paso** (on the **Sierra Plata**, iron mines, Rio Plata nearby); the hidden pirate free-port **Ombra del Mar** (south of Nevarrez, ruled by renegade mage **Iago Ximen**); the lost jungle city **Cumbre de Oro / Taremorn**; the **Sierra Grande** (mage **Tlantimextli's** tower, the **Isola dei Relitti / Isla Ascondida**). Distant lands referenced: **Ophiur**, **Qi-Long**, **Khorish**. Organizations: the **Marina** with the **Ordine dei Dragoni del Mare** and the elite **Vele Rosse (Red Sails)**; the **Compagnia Commerciale di Cartosa**; the mage-order **Manti Azzurri (Blue Cloaks)** with academic ranks **Ricercatore / Ricercatore Anziano (Researcher / Senior Researcher)**; the thieves' guild the **Incappucciati (Hooded Ones)** and the rival **Società delle Ombre (Society of Shadows)**; the **Guardia Dorata / Guardia D'Oro (Golden Guard)**; a resistance/rebel movement (**la Resistenza**). A cult of ranks **Tal-Tanoth / Ur-Tanoth / Tanoth** (and warnings about "**Tanoth corrotti**" / corrupted Tanoth) — apparently a monastic or intelligence order to which the PCs' patron Derreck nominally belongs. Religion: temples to **Hudemia** (foundlings surnamed "de Nostra Madre"), **Mithra** (abandoned shrine at El Paso).

**Central plot — the Alba Nera ("Black Dawn") / Sole Nero ("Black Sun"):** A secret cell operating *within* the Incappucciati is revealed to be the **Alba Nera**, a cult seeking ancient artifacts "connected to the **Adepti** and forgotten magic," stockpiling iron, and forging weapons and **constructs**. Its aim, uncovered piece by piece, is to build a **colossal war-construct** and **animate it by imprisoning a Demon Sovereign (probably Asmodeus)** inside — a scheme lifted straight from the *Guida alla Vita Artificiale* and Ragashano tomes.

- **PARTE I — Il Sole Nero.** The Tal-Tanoth **Derreck lo Svelto** hires the PCs (100–150 gp) to investigate the cult. Investigation threads: murdered iron-merchant **Esteban Vidal**; the mad ex-Senior-Researcher **Millennia Devantes** (Ragashano expert who burned the forbidden books because they "brought the Adepts to ruin" and hears "Them" whispering); the goblin **Oloban**; iron being carted west out of Cartosa. Then a **naval mission**: the PCs are Navy plants aboard the merchant *Marianna*, meant to fail — the pirate **Bagrat il mezz'orco** (aboard the *Sanguinaria*) is *allowed* to seize the iron. They are "rescued" by the celebrated 18-year-old **Ammiraglio Ramirez deStuba** (Ordine dei Dragoni del Mare) — who is **secretly an Alba Nera agent** who staged the whole capture; the PCs overhear him conspiring with a hooded, pink-red-haired agent, are silenced, and thrown overboard.

- **PARTE II — Race Against Time.** Shipwrecked, the PCs are taken aboard the *Indomabile* of the half-elf pirate-rebel **Inigo lo Sbarbato** (part of the Resistance, enemies of the Marina and Vele Rosse) to **Ombra del Mar**. They track the Alba to **Cumbre de Oro / Taremorn** — the lost Adept-empire city, cursed with demon-gold by the Demon Sovereign **Ismael** for the treason of **King Lorith** (now a wraith). The Alba has sent the Blue Cloak **Tobias** (Ragashano scholar) to recover tomes and the **Scettro di Lorith** (a scepter granting power over lesser demons).

- **PARTE III — Showdown at El Paso.** Derreck (having foiled the assassination he set the PCs up for) re-hires them (500–700 gp) to learn what the Alba does with its iron: the mining town **El Paso**, now seized by the Alba with **Tobias installed as governor**, running a **fonderia (foundry)** that forges "**weapons and construct-parts**," its black smoke sickening the land. **Nevarrez's governor Juan Barreo** is a puppet of Ramirez/the Alba. The PCs kill Tobias and recover more Ragashano tomes (incl. *Guida alla Vita Artificiale*, teaching "Construct Mastery"). A recurring shadow-using enemy stalks them; a band of odd castaways (**il Biondo, il Muto, il Mago, il Grosso, il Talpa, il Manca**) joins them.

- **PARTE IV — A Dangerous Knowledge.** Via the **Isola dei Relitti** (frog-folk **Gratar** who wreck ships to sacrifice crews to **Torakosh l'Antico**) to the tower of the crippled artificer-mage **Tlantimextli** in the Sierra Grande. The PCs raid his "**Sale della Macchina (Halls of the Machine)**" — a construct-factory whose warning signs are written **in xebechano** — to recover the **Disco Matrice** from his treacherous ex-apprentice **Damarlor** (imprisoned inside an iron golem). **The reveal:** the recovered plans are for a **construct of colossal size, able to level any fortress by itself**, which the Alba intends to animate by **binding one of the Demon Sovereigns — probably Asmodeus — inside it.**

- **PARTE V — Back in the City.** Return to Cartosa; alliance with the **Società delle Ombre** (leader **Antonio Cyrano**) and the noble **don Andreas deEscolar** against the Incappucciati. **The master reveal — the identity of the Alba's leader:** **Andreas Carrillo di Cartosa** (registry-cancelled and re-surnamed "**de Nostra Madre**"), born **2981 d.M.**, sibling of the current heir **Alberto Carrillo di Cartosa V** — a **Marchiata (a "Marked" one)** foundling raised by Hudemia's nuns, then apprenticed among the Manti Azzurri under **Millennia Devantes**. As a child prodigy she became obsessed with the **Adepti** and their "**Grande Pace**," used Tobias to steal forbidden tomes, and performed a rite that **summoned a dark entity "dall'Altrove" (from the Elsewhere/Beyond)**; she then vanished, later returning immensely powerful, claiming her royal birthright, to seize the throne and found "a new realm of peace like the Adepts'." She now leads the Alba Nera from **Villa Corona**. Also: **Derreck is unmasked as "Dagralack," a shapeshifting demon** (Evil Mentalist, mist-form) working his own agenda; his patron's identity is left mysterious.

- **PARTE VI — A Blood-Red Dawn.** Fragment: stat blocks for the **Costrutto Demoniaco Minore/Maggiore** — the ~6-metre demon-powered war-constructs "launched or teleported over the city and dropped," each carrying smaller constructs — i.e., the payoff of the colossal-construct plot.

**Notable named artifacts (Series B):** the **Scettro di Lorith** (power over lesser demons), **"Piccolo Artiglio"** (a demon-detecting sword), the **Disco Matrice**, **Scudo di Arn**, the **Brootakeen** rapier of Dagralack (a metal "from a demonic dimension").

## 4. CROSS-LINKS BETWEEN THE TWO CAMPAIGNS (and to the wider setting)

- **Same world, opposite poles:** Series A = the frozen north (**Helgedad**); Series B = the tropical south (**Principato di Cartosa / Coste Gemelle**). Both name the mage-order **Manti Azzurri** and the goddess **Hudemia** — Hudemia is a *neutral-deity key-holder* in Series A/Vol IV **and** the convent-order that raises foundlings in Series B/PARTE V. Strong evidence the two campaigns share one canon.
- **The colossal demon-construct is the shared climactic device:** Series B builds toward it (Alba Nera + Asmodeus); Series A/Vol VI and B/PARTE VI both close on **"Costrutto Demoniaco Minore/Maggiore"** stat blocks with near-identical text. Suggests the author reused/linked the two endgames — the same "demon bound inside a giant construct" motif.
- **The Xebechani / "Vera Via" portals** anchor Series A's deep history; Series B's **"xebechano"** appears as the language on Tlantimextli's ancient machine-halls — implying the Xebechani were also the setting's lost *artificer* culture, tying construct-magic back to the pre-human age.
- **The Adepti thread** runs through both: Series B's plot IS the Adepti mystery; Series A's Cumbre-de-Oro parallel (an Adept-empire city fallen to a Demon Sovereign) reinforces it. *(Cumbre de Oro is actually detailed in Series B/PARTE II, but the "Adept city fallen to demon-gold" pattern recurs.)*

## 5. FLAGS ON THE WORLD'S BIG OPEN QUESTIONS

- **The Great Lie / Adepti / Ancient Gods:** These modules give the **Adepti** a concrete backstory — a real fallen golden-age *empire* whose ruin came from **forbidden demon-summoning and "Confined Bodies" (Corpi Confinati) magic** written in **Ragashano**. No explicit "Great Lie" is stated, but the recurring motifs — a creator-god (**Ech**) split into a Life/Anti-Life pair, dark gods (**Azatzotech, Yogh, Xlucthulhu**) *confined/vanished but "silently waiting"*, forbidden books on "**Confined Bodies**" and a "**Great Agothu**", and Andreas summoning an entity "**from the Beyond**" — form a plausible substrate for a "confined/hidden ancient gods" premise. **Flag as thematic support, not resolution.**
- **The Catastrophe:** Framed by Vol I's epigraph as a fall from "centuries of prosperity and glory" into a lawless dark age. Not dated or mechanistically explained here; consistent with the Demon War / Adept collapse being (a) cause(s).
- **The Demon War(s):** Attested as historical fact under two labels — **"le Guerre Demoniache"** (triad weapons, three demonic lieutenants) and **"la Guerra Demoniaca"** (Taremorn's fall, Demon Sovereign Ismael). Whether these are one event or several is left open. **Demon Sovereigns** named: **Ismael, Asmodeus**. Demon lord **Xardas "il Burattinaio"** is an unresolved revenge-seeking antagonist.
