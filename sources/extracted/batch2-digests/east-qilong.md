# East — The Qi-Long (Digest, batch2/east-qilong)

**Source files digested (all Italian, proper nouns preserved):**
1. `I regni del Qi Long e la resistenza ai draghi.txt` — original master text (author *Alessio*, 2008; revised by *Jarn83*, 2011).
2. `Qilongymir - revised.txt` — a later revision of the same master text, with renamed Dragon-Gods and one renamed province.
3. `Leggende del Qi-Long.txt` — short legend/rumor fragments (author *Petri Alessandro*, 2011).
4. `Cammino dell'Illuminato.txt` — an RPG dungeon/revelation scenario ("The Path of the Enlightened").
5. `Tempio Aria.txt` — an RPG combat/flashback scenario ("The Temple of Air").

Files 1–2 are the two versions of the same canonical document and are the core. Files 3–5 add legends and a metaplot that ties the Qi-Long into the wider Ymir story.

---

## 1. THE DEFINITIVE QI-LONG MODEL

**The Qi-Long is SIX independent, mutually hostile kingdoms — NOT one present-day empire.** Both versions of the master text open with the identical statement:

> *"Il Qi-Long è diviso in 6 regni, da sempre in guerra fra loro per il predominio. Ogni regno è governato con pugno di ferro da un Dio-Drago."*
> ("The Qi-Long is divided into 6 kingdoms, forever at war with one another for supremacy. Each kingdom is ruled with an iron fist by a Dragon-God.")

Key structural facts this material establishes unambiguously:

- Each of the six kingdoms has **its own Dragon-God tyrant.** The six are **RIVALS**, not colleagues — "each craves control of the whole territory and proclaims himself the strongest of the six." For **centuries** the war between them has been in a **stalemate** ("da secoli … in stallo"), none able to prevail.
- There is **no emperor, no capital called Tianqi, no Ouroboros, and no "loyal" Dragon-Gods** anywhere in these documents. The word "resistance" refers to mortals resisting the Dragon-Gods, not to any imperial hierarchy.
- The six named factions (Aironi Grigi, Cani Rossi, etc.) are **mortal RESISTANCE sects** — "triadi di resistenza" — one embedded in each kingdom, fighting to liberate/protect humanity from that kingdom's Dragon-God. **They are the resistance, not administrative "sect-provinces" of an empire.**

**Reconciling "six provinces" vs "six kingdoms":** the province/kingdom labels are just the internal terminology for the same six realms (one document even calls the fire realm both "Provincia di Cenere" and "pianura delle ceneri"). There are exactly six realms = six kingdoms = six Dragon-Gods = six resistance sects. The two versions disagree only on the *name* of the earth realm (see §2).

**The one hint of a former unity (the reconciling nuance):** two passages gesture at a single, older, supreme Dragon-God now gone:
- The Golden Hawks' entry says the **Capitale della Luce** (Capital of Light, in the North Kingdom) *"was founded by the **Vero Dio Drago** (True Dragon-God) and was already the capital in the time of his reign."* This implies one original Dragon-God empire, centered on the Capital of Light, in the deep past.
- The White Mongooses openly **serve an absent Dragon-God "not of this world — not yet,"** awaiting his **return** and guarding "his realm" with faith (see §5). The other sects mock this as superstition.

So the deep-time picture reconcilable from this material is: **one original "True Dragon-God" once ruled from the Capital of Light; the present age is a fractured, six-way stalemate among lesser/successor Dragon-Gods, opposed by six mortal resistance sects** — while a cult (Abigor, §7) exploits the chaos.

## 2. THE SIX DRAGON-GODS (DEI-DRAGO) — elements, realms, servants

**Nature of the Dei-Drago (both versions):** no one truly knows what they are. They normally wear human form but can turn into dragons at will (hence the name); they are formidable warriors, supreme mages, and masters of every martial art. They are *reputed* immortal — but "some say they are not truly immortal: when one dies, his successor takes his name, sustaining the illusion of immortality." All are rated **Level 80**. Whoever challenges their power is crushed.

The two versions give **two different name-sets** for the same six gods. Mapping by element/realm:

| Element | 2008 name (file 1) | Revised name (file 2) | Realm | Servants | Resistance sect |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fire** | Huolongnan ("Fire Dragon of the South") | Nanfang Huolong | **Provincia di Cenere** (Ash Province: sulfur flats, open mines, canyons, volcanoes) | **Oni** (orc-demon hybrids soaked in elemental fire) | **Cani Rossi** |
| **Water** | Seishima Tatsumoto ("Dragon of the Western Isles") | Tatsumoto Ryūdō | **Mare del Naufragio** (Sea of the Shipwreck: scattered islands; he controls the whole sea) | **Kappa** (armored amphibians) | **Tigri Celesti** |
| **Earth** | Weichong-Shanxuan ("Mighty Worm of the Black Mountain") | Qiángdà Shéshān ("Mighty Serpent of the Mountain") | **Altopiano delle Nuvole** *(2008)* / **Spire del Pianto** *(revised)* (mountain-desert; the true realm is underground — inhabitants are told the surface is lethal) | **Tengu** (cruel crow-humanoids) | **Scorpioni Neri** |
| **Ice** | Kuangshuang Long ("Dragon of the Frozen Expanse") | Mò-hán Lóng ("Dragon of the Frozen Desert") | **Pianura Gelata** (Frozen Plain: semi-polar desert, high mountains) | **Rakshasa Ur-Tanoth** crossed with **Ice Trolls** *(2008)* / with **devils** *(revised)* | **Manguste Bianche** |
| **Air / Wind** (UNDEAD) | Jiu-Yaofeng ("Black Eagle of the Malign Wind") | Hēi-yīng Fēngxié | **Foresta Morta** (Dead Forest: vast poisoned swamp) | **Yurei** (ghosts of powerful warriors who dared challenge him) | **Aironi Grigi** |
| **Light** (female; also called Thunder / "Light-and-Shadow") | Qiulei-Senbei ("Little Thunder Dragon of the Northern Forests") | Léilong Lìnbei | **Regno del Nord** (North Kingdom: almost entirely one immense city, "la Capitale" / **Chéngshì**, the Capital of Light) | **Kitsune** (fox-humanoid shapeshifters) | **Falchi Dorati** |

Notes worth flagging:
- The **Air/Wind Dragon is undead** ("non morto") — unique among the six.
- The **North Dragon is female** and is described inconsistently as a "Thunder Dragon," a "Light Dragon," and a "Dragon of Light and Shadow." She is the one tied to the *Vero Dio Drago*'s old capital.
- The **Ice Dragon is also female**, "savage / sadistic."
- Each god's entry lists an **Attitude** and **Nature** (personality): Fire = despotic/wrathful; Water = haughty/megalomaniac; Earth = dormant/unfathomable; Ice = savage/sadistic; Air = mellifluous/Machiavellian; Light = paranoid/schizophrenic.

**On MAG-11/12/14 ("when/how they appeared," Ouroboros):** this material gives **no appearance date and never mentions Ouroboros or "loyal" gods.** What it does give: the war has run "for centuries"; the Earth Dragon sowed "the seed of the lie centuries ago"; the gods may be a **succession-lineage** rather than truly eternal beings; and a possible mythic origin via the elemental-hubris confession in the *Cammino* (see §6). The timeline is relative ("da secoli"), never absolute.

## 3. THE SIX RESISTANCE SECTS ("TRIADI DI RESISTENZA")

Each sect is a clandestine martial-arts order fighting its local Dragon-God. All six share a base rank ladder (Novizio → Iniziato → Adepto → …) topped by a semi-legendary leader. They cooperate loosely against the gods but clash by temperament.

- **Aironi Grigi** (Grey Herons) — *Foresta Morta / Air*. Symbol: a heron's wing. Loose, non-hierarchical "triad"; gatherings ("nidiate") every ~5 years. Deliberately whimsical, wandering all Ymir doing odd deeds. Fight defensively, prioritizing civilian survival. Style: **"stile dei (quattro) venti divini"** (Style of the [Four] Divine Winds). Ranks: Novizio, Iniziato, Adepto, **Capostormo**, **Araldo dei venti** (elite couriers), **Maestro dei venti** (masters — only ones who may teach all the style's secrets and legitimize "cadet styles").
- **Cani Rossi** (Red Dogs) — *Provincia di Cenere / Fire*. Symbol: snarling jaws. Roam in "packs" (branchi) hunting the Oni; hardened, blunt, interventionist. Ranks: Novizio, Iniziato, Adepto, **Capobranco** (pack-leader), **Furia Rossa** (roving high-level coordinators/judges), **Mastino Scarlatto** (Scarlet Mastiff — the sect's head, a title passed down, wreathed in legend, keeps the rebellion united).
- **Falchi Dorati** (Golden Hawks) — *Regno del Nord / Light*. Symbol: a raptor's talon. The best-organized triad; urban cell-based espionage using the enemy's own deceit; refined scholar-poet warriors, heirs to the Capital's ancient tradition. Ranks: Novizio, Iniziato, Adepto, **Mastro Cantore** (inter-cell messengers using poetry codes), **Gran Falconiere** (territorial spymasters, never fight), **Fenice Rossa** (Red Phoenix — legendary, wholly secret supreme head).
- **Scorpioni Neri** (Black Scorpions) — *Earth realm*. Symbol: a scorpion's tail. The most secretive; solitary assassins hidden even from the people they protect (the populace, brainwashed to believe the surface is deadly, now distrusts them). Style: **"stile della cuspide vendicatrice"** (Style of the Avenging Cusp). Ranks: Novizio, Iniziato, Adepto, then **Maestro dei Veleni** (Master of Poisons, elite) and **Maestro dei Silenzi** (Masters of Silence — couriers who take a mysterious drug from the leader; skip a dose and die within hours, taking the sect's secrets to the grave); topped by the **Cuspide Nera** (Black Cusp — semi-legendary, reputedly immortal old master). *(The revised file relabels the mid-ranks as **Prima Cuspide / Maestro dei Veleni / Cuspide Nera**.)*
- **Tigri Celesti** (Celestial Tigers) — *Mare del Naufragio / Water*. Symbol: a tiger's claw. Ambush fighters who trap and harvest the great turtle-beasts and Kappa for their armor. Ranks: Novizio, Iniziato, Adepto, **Zanna delle Acque**, **Artiglio Celeste**, **Grande Tigre** (head).
- **Manguste Bianche** (White Mongooses) — *Pianura Gelata / Ice*. Symbol: a white lotus. A **faith-driven** order (see §5) enduring the cruel Rakshasa. Ranks: Novizio, Iniziato, Adepto, **Colonna dei Ghiacci**, **Kamaitachi**, **Maestro dei Ghiacci** (head).

**Inter-sect friction (from the "Dicono degli altri" gossip sections):** Aironi work poorly with Tigri, badly with Manguste, and grate on the austere Cani Rossi. Cani Rossi and Falchi Dorati distrust each other (brawn vs. scheming). The Manguste are widely derided for their "ridiculous superstition"; the Falchi even suspect they may be unwitting tools of the enemy. The Cani Rossi respect the Tigri as the only ones "who actually fight our enemies."

**Named servant characters:** **Sōjōbō**, a Tengu, "major supervisor and vicar-general of the seventh cohort" of the Earth Dragon-God, sent by high lieutenant **Daiyō** to inspect a border town — assassinated by a Black Scorpion.

## 4. LEGENDS OF THE QI-LONG (file 3)

- Beneath the Capital of Light lies **a vast parallel underground city, ruled by the TWIN of the Light Dragon-Goddess Quilei Senbei** — a striking new detail matching her "schizophrenic/paranoid" nature.
- Travelers crossing **from the Sadhir** into the Earth Dragon's mountainous realm report winged figures (Tengu) at dusk who abduct the careless. *(Geographic note: the **Sadhir borders the Qi-Long's Earth realm**.)*
- In the Foresta Morta, temples and inns seen at night are illusions conjured by the dead (Yurei) to devour travelers.
- The **Kamaitachi** are a nomadic people of humanoid weasels who love inflicting and receiving pain (torture/mutilation), tied to their shamans. *(Kamaitachi also appear as an elite rank among the Ice-realm White Mongooses.)*

## 5. THE WHITE MONGOOSE PARADOX (reconciles with the "True Dragon-God")

The White Mongooses' vignette is doctrinally distinct from the other five sects. A master tells a despairing brother, at a sacred ice altar assaulted by Rakshasa:

> *"We serve a Dragon-God who is not of this world — not yet. Awaiting his return, we must guard his realm with the greatest weapon he left us: our faith."*

So the Ice-realm resistance **resists the present Ice Dragon while serving a different, absent Dragon-God whose return they await.** Read together with the Golden Hawks' *Vero Dio Drago* who founded the Capital of Light, this points to a single original/true Dragon-God, now gone, whose return the Mongooses expect — the strongest in-text reconciliation of the "one supreme ruler (past)" vs "six warring kings (present)" tension.

## 6. THE PATH OF THE ENLIGHTENED — cosmological revelation (file 4)

`Cammino dell'Illuminato` is an RPG revelation-dungeon, but it carries major cosmology. Pilgrims pass "the Darkness of Nightmare" and "the Doubt of Illusion," tear "the last Veil," face a faceless judge-statue with a broken scale ("here you will be judged with unjust judgment"), then ascend an endless spiral of **six elemental sanctuaries — one per elemental divinity:**

| Element | Divinity | Sanctuary material | Inscription (confession) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Water | **Hudemia** | blue marble | "Sacrificing ourselves on our own altar / we bent the laws of the Cosmos to our will" |
| Wind | **Heis** | silvery stone | "With Fury we hurled ourselves / claiming Fate as our own" |
| Earth | **Therios** | obsidian-like | "Life itself we enslaved / and it bowed at our feet" |
| Ice | **Varshnak** | white marble | "Through the walls of the World / our scream silenced the fury of the elements" |
| Light | **Dingir** | solid gold | "The Three Realms together with the most Ancient / shine with our Wisdom" |
| Fire | **Diis** | ruby-like | "Drawing on the Astral fire / in our Forge we recreated the Essence" |

Rotating all altars toward a hidden reflection opens a **seventh sanctuary**, whose inscription is the group's full confession of hubris: *"We broke every Law, posing as Legislators … we chained the world to a grim Destiny … we created empty simulacra, pretending they were alive … cracking the walls of the World, we were possessed by the elements … Older than our madness, magic glows with its own enchantment … in our immense Arrogance we defiled the perfection of the Essence."* Beyond it lies the **Great Library** of forbidden Truth, guarded by an angel statue and two winged lions (**Lammasu** / an **Onirich [Dream] Champion**), whose voice warns mortals away ("Better to live in the darkness of ignorance than perish in the flame of a terrible knowledge"). Inside, a vision of a past **cataclysmic battle** — a fortress under demonic assault, a general shouting *"Resist! Resist! We must give them enough time to complete the ritual!"* — until a colossal **spider of fire** shatters the walls. The scenario ends at a great pendulum, with a keeper: *"You are not the first to reach here, nor the last … Are you ready to face the consequences of the answers?"*

**Significance for the Qi-Long conflicts:** the **six elemental divinities exactly match the six Dragon-Gods' elements** (Water/Wind/Earth/Ice/Light/Fire). Their confession — "empty simulacra pretending they were alive," "possessed by the elements," recreating "the Essence" in a "Forge" — reads as a candidate **origin myth for the Dragon-Gods** (beings born of an ancient magical over-reach), and links them to a broader Ymir cosmology of *Fato* (Fate), *Cosmo*, *Essenza*, the *Tre Reami* (Three Realms), *il più Antico* (the most Ancient), and Magic itself as the "seventh." This is interpretive (the text never says outright "these are the Dragon-Gods"), but the element-for-element correspondence is exact.

## 7. THE TEMPLE OF AIR — the Abigor metaplot & the Conspiracy of the Five (file 5)

`Tempio Aria` is a combat + flashback scenario that hooks the Qi-Long war into Ymir's wider intrigue.

- **The Abigor plot (present-day threat):** a dying mage, **Aghrast**, reveals the "mad plan of the **Cultists of Abigor**: exploiting the **mega-war between the Kingdoms**, they mean to open a **portal to the realm of Abigor**, through which his servants can invade Ymir." So the Qi-Long's endless six-way stalemate is being deliberately **exploited as cover** by a demon-cult. Antagonists include **Leonard** (a vampire wielding the Mithril sword "**Cerberus**"), **Rufia** (a sniper serving "the **tecnocrate**"/technocrat), **Aghrast** (mage), plus **Chaos Warriors of Abigor**, a **Mechanical Manticore**, and **Mechanical Ghouls** (constructs). PC allies: **Lucius Valor** (paladin), **Sanghar** (animist healer), **Rogar / Mhyr / Lorkan** (Ur-Tanoth warriors).
- **Flashback — "il Complotto dei Cinque" (the Conspiracy of the Five):** the PCs relive a past event at the **Accademia di Studi Elementali** (Academy of Elemental Studies), inside a **Ducato** (Duchy). A young **Belengard** drags them up the Academy tower to the guild-master's study, where **Sirius Edergarth** has just killed the master **Ecthelius**. Sirius insists "these **machines** will be our ruin," that a mysterious "**being** is using you all," and that he (not Sirius) is the true criminal; he reveals another master, **Iulius**, was murdered. As Belengard attacks, the **young Leonard** — wounded on the floor — stabs Sirius in the back. This ties the vampire Leonard, the "machines"/technocracy theme, and a hidden manipulating "being" to a founding catastrophe.
- **Artifact:** **NATHARIEN, il Diadema Celeste** (Natharien, the Celestial Diadem) — an intelligent, telepathic Air/Aether relic: x4 Arcane multiplier, flight, constant force-shields (+25 BD, +50 vs Air), "Shifting Law" spells, Aether Bolt, and a daily "Celestial Explosion."

These two adventure files clearly sit at the **seam between the Qi-Long and the rest of Ymir** (the Ducato, the Academy, the technocrat, Abigor). They are session/plot material, not encyclopedic lore, so treat their details as scenario-canon.

## 8. PER-CONFLICT RESOLUTIONS

**GEO-2 / GEO-6 (one empire vs six kingdoms):**
- **This material decisively backs SIX INDEPENDENT KINGDOMS, each with its own Dragon-God**, at war with one another in a centuries-old stalemate. Explicit, and identical in both versions.
- **No Tianqi, no hidden emperor, no six "loyal" sect-provinces appear anywhere.** The six named "sects" are the **mortal resistance**, one per kingdom — the opposite of ruling provinces.
- **Reconciliation of "six provinces vs six kingdoms":** identical set of six realms; "province" and "kingdom" are interchangeable labels for the same six Dragon-God domains. The only version-level disagreement is the earth realm's name (*Altopiano delle Nuvole* → *Spire del Pianto*).
- **The "resistance to the dragons" arc:** mortals, organized into six clandestine martial orders, fight to survive and to free humanity from the six tyrant-gods; one order (White Mongooses) frames its fight as service to an absent *True* Dragon-God awaiting return.
- **Former-unity thread (if canon needs a single ruler):** the *Vero Dio Drago* founded the Capital of Light and reigned there in the past; the present six may be successors/usurpers of that original throne. This is the only bridge this material offers toward a "one-ruler" reading, and it is firmly **past-tense**.

**MAG-11 / MAG-12 / MAG-14 (six Dragon-Gods, elements, Ouroboros, appearance):**
- **Six gods and their elements: definitively given** (§2 table) — Fire, Water, Earth, Ice, Air (undead), Light (female). Two full naming schemes recorded (2008 vs revised).
- **"Loyal":** contradicted — the gods are **rivals**, each claiming to be strongest; none serves another.
- **Ouroboros:** **not mentioned at all** in any of these files.
- **When/how they appeared:** **no absolute date.** Relative markers only ("for centuries," "the seed of the lie sown centuries ago"). Two origin hints: (a) they may be a **succession-lineage** faking immortality; (b) the *Cammino* confession of six elemental divinities ("possessed by the elements," "empty simulacra pretending they were alive," Essence recreated in a "Forge") supplies a possible mythic origin, element-for-element matched to the six gods.

## 9. KEY NEW LORE / CHARACTERS

- **Vero Dio Drago** — original "True Dragon-God," founder of the Capitale della Luce; implies a former single realm.
- **The Light Goddess's twin**, ruling a parallel underground city beneath the Capital of Light.
- **The six elemental divinities:** Hudemia (Water), Heis (Wind), Therios (Earth), Varshnak (Ice), Dingir (Light), Diis (Fire) — and a hubris/origin myth linking them to the Dragon-Gods; plus cosmology terms Fato, Essenza, Tre Reami, il più Antico, and Magic-as-seventh.
- **Cultists of Abigor** exploiting the Qi-Long war to open an invasion portal to Ymir; the vampire **Leonard** (sword "Cerberus"), the **tecnocrate**, and combat constructs.
- **Complotto dei Cinque** at the **Accademia di Studi Elementali** in a **Ducato**: Belengard, Sirius Edergarth, Ecthelius, Iulius, young Leonard, and "the machines"/a hidden manipulating "being."
- **Natharien, the Celestial Diadem** — intelligent Air/Aether artifact.
- **Sōjōbō** (Tengu vicar-general) and **Daiyō** (high lieutenant) — named Earth-realm officers.
- **Style names:** *venti divini* (Grey Herons), *cuspide vendicatrice* (Black Scorpions).
- **Geography:** the **Sadhir borders the Qi-Long** (specifically the Earth realm).

## 10. NEW CONFLICTS / INCONSISTENCIES TO FLAG

1. **Model clash with any "hidden emperor / six loyal Dragon-Gods / Tianqi / Ouroboros" canon:** this material presents six *rival* gods and *no* emperor, capital-Tianqi, loyalty structure, or Ouroboros. Direct tension with MAG-11/12/14's premise of "loyal" gods.
2. **Sects = resistance, not provinces:** if canon treats the six named sects as the ruling "sect-provinces" of an empire, this material contradicts it — here they are anti-Dragon-God rebels.
3. **Two incompatible Dragon-God name-sets** (2008 vs revised) for the same six beings — must pick a canonical set (e.g., Fire: *Huolongnan* vs *Nanfang Huolong*; Earth: *Weichong-Shanxuan* vs *Qiángdà Shéshān*; Air: *Jiu-Yaofeng* vs *Hēi-yīng Fēngxié*; Ice: *Kuangshuang Long* vs *Mò-hán Lóng*; Light: *Qiulei-Senbei* vs *Léilong Lìnbei*; Water: *Seishima Tatsumoto* vs *Tatsumoto Ryūdō*).
4. **Earth realm renamed** *Altopiano delle Nuvole* → *Spire del Pianto* between versions.
5. **Ice Dragon's servants** differ: Rakshasa Ur-Tanoth × Ice Trolls (2008) vs × devils (revised).
6. **North Dragon's element is ambiguous:** "Thunder Dragon" (god-list) vs "Light Dragon (female)" (region) vs "Dragon of Light and Shadow" (Falchi text).
7. **Immortality is questioned in-text** (successor-takes-the-name), which cuts against any "these specific gods have reigned since date X" claim.
