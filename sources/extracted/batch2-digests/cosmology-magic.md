# Batch 2 Digest — Cosmology & Magic

> English digest of 11 Italian source files from
> `sources/extracted/batch2/cosmology-magic/`. Markup stripped; proper nouns preserved.
> Mechanics (Rolemaster stat blocks, TR/TA/BO/BD/PM/PP, critical tables) are summarised
> into setting terms except where an exact value is load-bearing (element/critical types,
> realm multipliers, names, counts).
>
> **Files digested:** Demoni · Planes2 · Triade in dettaglio · Strumenti di Potere ·
> Le Reliquie del Potere · La Vera Via · Terre dei Sogni · PRECETTI DI HEIS ·
> IL SETTIMO CIRCOLO · Ragash · Incontri casuali Gaerwath.
>
> **Reading note:** several of these are GM adventure/bestiary scratch-files (Ragash, La
> Vera Via, Terre dei Sogni, Gaerwath) and one is a mechanics catalogue (Demoni). They
> carry a lot of *incidental* worldbuilding that firms up or complicates existing canon.

## 1. Demons & the Abyss / Chaos (`Demoni.txt`)

**Demonic materials & the corruption tax.** Demonic objects inflict **Corruzione
(Corruption)** on any mortal who uses them: ~1 point/day for full demonic armour, less
for lighter pieces, ~1 point/week for weapons.
- **Vetro Abissale (Abyssal Glass)** — a very tough grey-blue *magical resin* "from
  another plane of existence," used by demons and extradimensional creatures. Unlike
  Demon Steel it is light enough for mortals to wield; armour of it is impervious to all
  elemental attacks. (Confirms canon's "Vetro Abissale" material entry.)
- **Acciaio Demoniaco (Demon Steel)** — a blood-red metal ~250% the weight of steel,
  **forged directly with infernal fire**; grants big weapon bonuses plus random magical
  powers (extra crits, *Blasphemous* crits, double damage). Too heavy for mortals.
  Example: the **Falce Infernale (Infernal Scythe)**, which can "**Reclaim a Soul**"
  (soul-absorption, treated like a *Dark Absolution*). Confirms canon's "Acciaio
  Demoniaco (Ahriman-corrupting)."

**Demons are made of Essenza Oscura (Dark Essence).** The **Fusione Demoniaca (Demonic
Fusion)** rules state outright that demons are "composed of **Essenza Oscura**" and can
be used as reservoirs of *corrupted magical power*. A demon can fuse lesser demons into
itself/its gear (up to its own Type in number). Mortals can do it too — gaining the
demon's spell-lists but taking heavy Corruption and risking possession. This is direct
support for the **Corrupted-Essence** demon-origin frame (vs. the Plane-of-Chaos frame).

**Named Sovereign Demons / demon-lords appearing here:**
- **Xardas, il Signore dei Complotti (Lord of Plots/Schemes).** His servants are the
  **Burattini (Puppets)** — wax golems cloaked in illusion and animated by demonic
  intelligence, infiltrating societies to run his intricate plots. (New Sovereign name.)
- **Yogh** — called a "cruel god"; the **Tessiombra (Shadow-Weavers)**, giant demonic
  shadow-spiders that weave three-layered shadow-webs, are "children of the god Yogh."
  Tessiombra are **Type III demons**, often conjured by the sorcerers/summoners of
  **Ulan-Tang**. (Reinforces Yogh as a spider/shadow/poison power — relevant to COS-5.)

**The Natharl'Nacna — the eldest demons, dwellers in the Void.** A caste of demons
**exiled long ago from their plane of origin, who settled in *il Vuoto* (the Void)**.
"Said to be the most ancient of demons, and that from them **descend the Demoni
Sovrani**." Feared even by other demons; they count as **Type VI** but cannot be summoned
by ordinary spells — only via a dread tome, the **"Libro degli Estranei" (Book of the
Outsiders/Strangers).** Sub-types: **Carac'nin** (assassin-demons, bite acts as
*Absolution*), **Cresh'urim** (warrior-demons), **Dark Rythsykh** (thought to be
*projections* of some entity, not truly real in this multiverse), **Meleth'Scor**
(floating tentacled grey spheres; immune to *Aether* and *Nether* crits), **Pred Yrric**
(among the most powerful Void demons; reflect mental spells). → This places a whole
demon-race **in the Void**, as ancestors of the Sovereigns.

**Phlegetauro (Phlegethauri)** — fire-demons of "remote **infernal planes** full of lakes
of fire/magma"; three-headed, wreathed in flame; know *Fire Law*; explode into a Fireball
when slain. Establishes **infernal/fiery planes** as a demon habitat distinct from the Void.

**Amalgama Demoniaca (Demonic Amalgam)** — a magical *construct* (not a demon) of several
demons fused with metal into a tentacled horror; wields a *Void* critical gaze
("Sguardo del Nulla").

## 2. Planes & the "beyond" (`Planes2.txt`) — a full D&D-Planescape layer

This file is **explicitly Planescape** and overlays a *Great Wheel* multiverse on top of
Ymir's Player-Guide cosmology. It is a GM's crossover/adventure sketch, but it introduces
a lot of "beyond" geography that does **not** match the canonical planes:

- **Sigil**, the city of doors, with its Quarters (Quartiere della Signora, degli
  Impiegati, delle Gilde, del Mercato, Inferiore, Alveare/Hive). Landmarks: **Palazzo del
  Giullare (Palace of the Jester)**, **La Strada dei Templi** (from the *Gran Tempio
  Abissale* to the *Sacro Tempio dei Reami Celesti*), **la Forgia delle Rune di Ilymuth**
  (a blue-skinned **Urloc** mage who inscribes *Bladerunes* for pay).
- **La Caduta di Gog:** the god **Gog** (god of Treachery) is defeated for plotting with
  the **gods of Chaos against Law**, and banished from the **Gehenna** to **Cathrys, the
  second circle of the Plane of the Carceri (Prisons).** His former allies the **Baatezu**
  now resent his followers; wandering the Lower Quarter risks an **Abishai**.
- The **Guerra di Sangue (Blood War)** between **Baatezu** and **Tanar'ri**; **Marilith**
  are the Tanar'ri war-strategists. Rosters of **Fiends** (Sword Demon, Storm Demon, Demon
  Scourge, Succubus, Nightmare) and **Celestials** (Centaur/Winged/Noble/High Champion).
- Adventure hook **"Il Bambino Prodigio":** in **Arborea, 1st ring (Olimpo)**, at
  **Nascita di Afrodite**, a child **Sylvius** is born destined to be the greatest
  "playboy" ever, able to move even demons and celestials. The Succubus-Queen **Lilith**
  wants him; her agent **Eleshtar** hires the PCs to abduct him to Lilith's realm, the
  **Giardino dei Piaceri Proibiti (Garden of Forbidden Pleasures).**

**Cosmological weight:** this multiplies the "beyond" rather than clarifying it — see the
COS-19 note. Treat as an imported Planescape crossover unless deliberately canonised.

## 3. The "Triade" — what is it? (`Triade in dettaglio.txt`)

**Answer: not a cosmic triad of three gods.** In Ymir, "**la Triade**" is the collective
name for the network of **secret resistance societies** ("triadi di resistenza," in the
Chinese-triad / martial-sect sense) operating inside **Qi-Long** against the **Dei-Drago
(Dragon-Gods)**. There are **six** of them, each based in one Dragon-God's territory and
each built around a signature martial art. This is the *rebel* mirror of the six ruling
**Grandi Sette Imperiali** in `factions.md`.

The file details four fully and names two (still stubs):

| Resistance sect | Symbol / region | Fights the Dragon-God of… | The Dragon-God's servitors |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Aironi Grigi (Grey Herons)** | heron's wing · **Pianura Morta** (Dead Plain), poisoned swamps | **Air** (Dio Drago dell'Aria, "**Jiu-Yaofeng**") | — |
| **Cani Rossi (Red Dogs)** | snarling jaws · **Provincia delle Ceneri** (Province of Ashes) | **Fire** | **Oni** |
| **Falchi Dorati (Golden Hawks)** | raptor's talon · **Regno del Nord** / **Capitale della Luce** | **Light and Shadow** | **Kitsune** |
| **Scorpioni Neri (Black Scorpions)** | scorpion's tail · **Altopiano delle Nuvole** (Plateau of Clouds), tunnels | **Earth** | **Tengu** (e.g. **Sōjōbō**, under lieutenant **Daiyō**) |
| **Tigri Celesti (Celestial Tigers)** | *(stub)* — ranks incl. *Zanna delle Acque* (Water-Fang) → implies **Water** | | |
| **Manguste Bianche (White Mongooses)** | *(stub)* — ranks incl. *Colonna dei Ghiacci*, *Kamaitachi*, *Maestro dei Ghiacci* → implies **Ice** | | |

So the six sects map to **six Dragon-God elements: Air, Fire, Light-and-Shadow, Earth,
Water, Ice** — a near-mirror of the six public **Elementi** (with *Light-and-Shadow* in
place of Light, and *Air* present). Each sect has its own rank-ladder, mother-style,
training packages, talents/flaws, and mutually-jealous inter-sect banter ("Dicono degli
altri"). Highlights:
- **Aironi Grigi:** whimsical wind-stylists ("stile dei quattro venti divini"); loose,
  cell-based (a "**nidiata**" gathering every ~5 years); deliberately act like harmless
  drunks. Ranks: Novizio → Iniziato → Adepto → Capostormo → Araldo dei venti → Maestro
  dei venti. Roam all six kingdoms of Ymir.
- **Cani Rossi:** pack-fighters surviving among the Oni-ravaged Ash Province; ranks
  Novizio → Iniziato → Adepto → Capobranco → **Furia Rossa** → **Mastino Scarlatto** (the
  head of the sect, a legendary figure). A vivid opening vignette (the adept **Zhou**
  dying to cover his pack) shows the Oni are led by a **half-demonic human warrior**.
- **Falchi Dorati:** urban-espionage masters in the **Capitale della Luce** — said to have
  been **founded by the "Vero Dio Drago" (True Dragon-God)** and to have been capital in
  his reign. Cell structure, poetry-based recognition codes; ranks Novizio → Iniziato →
  Adepto → **Mastro Cantore** → **Gran Falconiere** → **Fenice Rossa** (the legendary,
  unknown head). Mother-style **Stile del rapace aureo**; special attack **Occhio di
  Garuda**; flaw: compulsive secrecy.
- **Scorpioni Neri:** lone assassin-ninja of the cloud plateau, **hidden even from the
  people they protect** (the Dragon-God's "Grande Bugia / Great Lie" has convinced the
  populace the Dragon *protects* them). Mother-style **Stile della cuspide vendicatrice**;
  ranks Novizio → Iniziato → Adepto → Prima Cuspide → Maestro dei Veleni → **Cuspide
  Nera**. All Scorpions must take demophobia or agoraphobia.

*Note the local echo of the Great Lie:* the Earth Dragon-God's subjects genuinely believe
they are privileged and protected — a regional instance of the "grande bugia" motif.

## 4. Reliquie / Rune / Strumenti di Potere — the sealing artifacts (`Strumenti di Potere.txt`, `Le Reliquie del Potere.txt`)

This is the **definitive** account of the six Reliquie and clears up what they seal.

**The frame (Strumenti di Potere, opening):** To counter the assault of the **Demoni
Sovrani** during the **Guerre Demoniache (Demon Wars)**, the **Adepti** created the mighty
**Rune Magiche (Magic Runes)** — instruments of immense power. In the last days of the
war, the great **fortress-city** that housed them was besieged so ferociously that the
Runes risked being *corrupted* by the dark power. So the Adepti **sealed them with six
objects, the six Reliquie del Potere**:

| Reliquia | Form | Realm ×4 multiplier | Element / domain |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ignus** | lo Scettro delle Braci (Sceptre of Embers) | **Essenza** | Fire (*Fire Law*; "Fiamma Purpurea") |
| **Saralis** *(also spelled **Sarnak**)* | l'Anello dell'Inverno (Ring of Winter) | **Flusso** | Ice/Cold; reflects a spell 1×/day; Co-drain aura |
| **Meletskar** | la Lama di Luce e Ombra (Blade of Light and Shadow) | *(none — physical weapon)* | Light **and** Shadow; +Electricity crit; Holy vs. undead/darkness |
| **Tassalung** | il Gioiello degli Abissi (Jewel of the Abyss) | **Mentalismo** | Water (*Liquid Manipulation*; underwater breathing; *Mind Shout*) |
| **Natharien** | il Diadema Celeste (Celestial Diadem) | **Arcano** | Air (Flight; force-shields; *Shifting Law*; *Aether Bolt/Ball*) |
| **Oedring** | il Martello del Mondo Sotterraneo (Hammer of the Underworld) | *(none — physical weapon)* | Earth (*Krush* crit; magnetism; smithing) |

- **"Only the possessor of all six Reliquie can fully activate the Rune Magiche"** and
  command their power. Each Reliquia is intelligent and communicates by telepathy.
- **The Rune Magiche are FOUR** ("Le Rune Magiche sono 4"). They are "an Adepti attempt
  to drive back the Demoni Sovrani"; **with the right means they can be used as power
  sources by anyone, but only by unlocking them with all six seals can their true
  potential be reached.**
- Two clean **artifact→Reliquia** links: the four *realm-multiplier* Reliquie map one-to-one
  to the **four magic realms** — Essenza (Ignus), Flusso (Saralis), Mentalismo (Tassalung),
  **Arcano** (Natharien) — while **Meletskar** and **Oedring** are the two physical
  weapons (Light/Shadow + Earth). Four realm-Reliquie ≈ four Rune. (See MAG-5 impact.)
- **Masamune → Meletskar:** the **Masamune** (an indestructible blade that strikes as a
  Magic +10 weapon — canon says borne by **Surya**) "**becomes** MELETSKAR." So the
  Masamune is Meletskar's base/dormant form.
- Each Reliquia entry in *Le Reliquie del Potere* also lists a **weaker "-->" version**
  (e.g. Ignus: full ×4 Essenza vs. reduced ×3 Essenza), implying **tiered/attunement
  grades** of each relic.
- **Name/power variance:** the Ring of Winter is spelled **Saralis** in one file and
  **Sarnak** in the other, with slightly different powers (cold aura + spell-reflection vs.
  touch life-drain). Canon uses *Saralis*.

**The Temple of the Custodi (adventure geography — firms up the physical setting):**
- A ruined **Xebechani (Xebechano)** temple; carvings depict the **Guerre Demoniache**.
- Its inner 7-ring floor: the first six rings are **each dedicated to one of the Antichi
  Dei (Ancient/Outer Gods)**; the seventh holds an elevator to the **Santuario di Saralis**.
- The steles are written in high **Xebechano** and tell of the **fortress-city of
  Xanadu**, which long withstood the **Demoni Sovrani** thanks to the **Rune Magiche** that
  protected it, and how the Runes were **sealed using the Six Reliquie**. → *This names the
  fortress that held the Runes as **Xanadu**, not Ragash.*
- The **Santuari (Sanctuaries):** the **Santuario dell'Inverno** (holding Saralis) is in
  the city of **Tzolkin**; the **Santuario delle Braci** (Ignus) is in the city of
  **Yaotl** (now lost). A **custode's key** grants limited access across sanctuaries and is
  itself "a small attempt to reproduce a Rune" — it can produce minor **temporal
  alterations** and grows stronger if attuned to a real Rune. The **locations of the other
  Sanctuaries can be researched in the great library of Xuthal.** NPCs: **Nanauatl** (Alto
  Teurgo, actually a hologram), the Technocrate's pupil **Calavark**, cultist **Aghrast**
  (of **Ahriman and Abigor**).

**The Dei Xebe (Xebechani "Dèi Concettuali / Esterni") — functional descriptions.** The
temple file catalogues six **Concept-Gods** that clearly correspond to the Xebechani
Outer-Gods in `secret-the-great-lie.md`:
- **sguardo-sul-nulla** — "the thought of those who look but do not comprehend"; all that is
  dark, alien, uncomprehended; worshipped as a god of **Wisdom, the Occult, Madness**.
  (= **Tuulhak-Xanar**, the Observer / the wandering **Blood Moon**.)
- **tricotomia-di-atti-e-pensieri** — the division of all action into **three Ways: Good,
  Evil, and Nothing**; least-understood and most-worshipped of the Outer Gods. (= the
  **Judge / Trichotomy**.)
- **parola-che-tace** — the **Cosmic Silence** that creates Music and Word; god of Arts,
  Magic, Oblivion, Destruction. (= the **Silent**.)
- **essenza-di-tutto-ciò-che-era-è-e-fu** — the **Primordial Essence** of Essence;
  worshipped by **Arcanisti**.
- **connubio-in-uno** — the all that merges into the one; god of Creation, Life, Death,
  Union, Spirits, Nature. (= the **Mother**.)
- **fiume-di-essere** — **Time** (past/present/future, transformation and decay); god of
  Time, Death, Decay, Birth, Destruction.

## 5. La Vera Via & portals (`La Vera Via.txt`)

An **airship adventure** whose title, "**La Vera Via (The True Way)**," ties into the
Xebechani **Vere Vie** portal network. The party flies from **Armora** ~3000 km on the
airship **"Folgore,"** captained by **Eric Legrasse** (a half-orc ex-war-criminal whose
face was surgically changed by **Setorn**), to the **Montagne degli Spiriti (Mountains of
Spirits).** En route they are attacked by **Viverne d'Acciaio Vallaniche (Vallanic Steel
Wyverns)** with riders ("what are they doing here?!") and chased by a rival airship.
- **The "Spirits of the Mountains" are actually Firbolg** — "a **corrupted lineage of
  fairies (fate), exiled long ago** to these mountains," ruled by the mage-king
  **Cedwyn-Naag.** Their forces include Firbolg sorcerers, elemental serpents, an earth
  giant, and Will-o'-Wisps; cornered, Cedwyn-Naag summons a powerful **nightmare
  (Spajlen-Tier).**
- Prop items include a **Sfera di Vera Luce (Sphere of True Light)** linked to the villain
  **Leonard**, a **Displacement** cloak, and **Aljira** (a drug/substance). New material
  detail: **Firbolg = exiled corrupted fairies**; the **Vere Vie** thread continues.

## 6. Terre dei Sogni — the Dreamlands (`Terre dei Sogni.txt`)

Bestiary/mechanics for the **Mondo dei Sogni / Terra dei Sogni** (Dreamlands):
- **Incubi (Nightmares), Types I–VI** — every one hideous and unique; they prefer to
  **terrorise** rather than kill. A victim killed *in the dream-world* by an Incubo risks
  losing **Sanity (Sanità)** points. Each can sense a mortal's greatest fear and weaponise
  it. (Same block reused in `Ragash.txt`.)
- **Entering someone's dreams requires a strong bond** with the sleeper. Named hooks: the
  **Tecnocrate** (via the remains of his ship), **Belengard** (via a device he built),
  **Sigurd** (via **Isidoro**). Each dream unfolds differently.
- **Esterni Evocabili (Summonable Outer entities)** — a Lovecraftian roster usable as
  summonables: **Divoracervelli (Mind-eaters/Flayers)**, **Byakhee** (immune to Cold,
  Nether, Void; fly), **Nof-Keh** (slow organic pillar-guardians), **Tresh** (flying
  five-tentacled "octopi"), **Moglath** (winged humanoids, evil-Flow lists), **Shoggoth**
  (corrosive amorphous blobs; vulnerable to Light). These are the practical "Outer" bestiary
  behind the Outer-Gods theme.

## 7. Ragash — the Dream-city (`Ragash.txt`)

Confirms **Ragash is reachable only as its Dreamlands mirror** (the physical city is gone
under the Maelstrom — supports HIS-15). This file is a Dream-Ragash adventure:
- Reuses the **Incubi** block, and adds the **Arhat** — a special **Dreamlands undead**:
  spirits who, via the **"Rito dell'Eternità Argentea" (Rite of the Silver Eternity),**
  withdrew into the Dream-World *just before death*. Serene, faintly gilded; know
  *Enlightening Ways*; can be turned/banished back to the Dream-World but not destroyed by
  undead-control. Races "tied to dreams" (like **Folletti**) become Arhat most often.
- **Surya** is sent into the Dream-World by **Logarth** with three silver coins to find the
  ferryman **Lo-Feng** (who *looks* like a **kitsune** but is really a dream).
- Dream-Ragash NPCs/locations: **Beniamino Gentile Fonda** ("Sognatore di Invenzioni,"
  dreamer-inventor and lens-maker who muses that some creatures refract mind and essence as
  he refracts light); a grim **Sanatorio** holding the **dreams/nightmares of a great
  doctor, Setorn**, watched by an **Entità Occhio (Eye Entity)** — Setorn's spy;
  **Vespertina VonGryffen** (canon Armora ruler) at the garden's centre, a "**mechanical
  woman**" **imprisoned in a dream of gears and steam** (i.e. **Arcadia**) from which
  something/someone stops her waking though she senses "something terrible happening" — her
  crystallised tear grants access to the **Closed list "Chaotic Senses."** A dandyish
  **Dante Gabriel Rossetti** guides them to the **Biblioteca (Library)**, guarded by a
  **Campione Onirico (Dream Champion)** and a **Lammasu.**

This firmly ties **Ragash → Dreamlands → Arcadia (steam-dream)** and links **Setorn** and
**Vespertina VonGryffen** to the dream-layer.

## 8. Heis precepts (`PRECETTI DI HEIS.txt`)

Clerical code for **Heis**, here explicitly the **god of Chaos** *and* of **Air/Wind** —
matching `magic.md`'s Air Elemental Lord (Heis). "A capricious, inconstant god who rarely
answers but asks little." His clerics track a stat called **Grazia (Grace)** measuring
fidelity to Heis and *dedication to the cause of Chaos*. The precepts:
- A cleric of Heis **must be Chaotic** (any morality — neutral is most common — since Heis
  is indifferent to good/evil, only to Chaos).
- **Wind and air are sacred**: never poison or foul them (no venom-gas/foul smoke); say a
  brief prayer before lighting a fire; try to stop others from poisoning the air.
- **Gambling is sacred**: a follower may **not cheat** and must trust wholly to **Chance
  (il Caso)**; must punish cheats.
- Heis likes his followers to **feel the "purezza della Vera Aria" on their bodies**: gain
  **1 Grace per 5 points of Air Elemental Corruption** suffered.
- **Battle Frenzy** pleases him (not required).

*Significance:* explicitly welds an **Elemental Lord (Air) to "Chaos"** and to a
gambling/chance/frenzy cult — a small but real bridge between the elemental pantheon and
the "Caos" theme (relevant to the Piano-del-Caos vs. Corrupted-Essence debate).

## 9. Il Settimo Circolo — Armora's magic college (`IL SETTIMO CIRCOLO.txt`)

Full **statute** of the **Collegio del Settimo Circolo (College of the Seventh Circle)** —
"Private College of Magical Wisdom for Gifted Youth," in **Armora**. Deepens the canon note
that the **Libro Rosso** was co-produced with this college.
- **Founded and directed by Ser Drusilde, Cavaliere di Armora, pupil of Xochiyayotl** (a
  Xebechani-named master). She is both sole **Founding Member** and current **Director**.
- **Mission:** the welfare/progress of the **Granducato di Armora**; oaths of fealty to the
  Crown; study of magic "in all its forms," **with particular focus on the arcane** and on
  **security, counter-enchantment, and resolving magical emergencies.**
- **Allied** with the **Accademia degli Studi Elementali** and the **Gilda degli Artefici**;
  and bound by a special friendship to the **Sezione di Controllo Magico (S.C.M.)** of the
  City Guard — to which senior students do integrative **fieldwork.**
- **Governance:** Director renewed every 5 years, chosen by the **Enclave dei Maestri** (all
  tenured masters) from a slate; **Founding Members hold absolute veto**; statute changes
  need the Crown's review and a new oath.
- **Enclave dei Maestri:** **Saverio Attanasius Grimbaldi** (Runemage, dead languages),
  **Lucretia Hesserman** (Arcanista, new-spell research), **Alfredo von Zimmer** (Summoner,
  planar lore), **Riccardo Varo Obertus** (Astrologo, advanced maths & **ritual geometry**).
- **Admission:** Armora residents **under 18**, no criminal record, who show **latent
  magical talent** or have basic training at the **Accademia di Studi Elementali** (rank
  *iniziato*) or the **Gilda degli Artefici** (rank *fabbro/smith*). Older applicants by
  the Director's licence. **50 gold/year**; texts extra. Ends in a theory+practical exam for
  a **diploma** valid in the Grand Duchy's civil/military rolls.

## 10. Magic mechanics & elements (cross-file)

- **Four magic realms as ×4 multiplier categories** (from the Reliquie): **Essenza,
  Flusso, Mentalismo, Arcano** — Arcano treated as a **peer realm** with its own artifact
  (Natharien), *not* as the mere union of the other three. See MAG-5 impact.
- **Element / critical types actually used across the batch:** **Void, Aether, Nether,
  Shock, Fire/Calore (Heat), Cold/Gelo (Ice), Elettricità (Electricity), Krush, Impact
  ("A Impact" = Air-Impact), Acido (Acid), Taglio (Slash), Poison, Light (Luce/Holy),
  Darkness/Umbra, Blasfemo (Blasphemous), Disrupt/Distrupt.** The six Reliquie give a clean
  **six-domain elemental backbone** (Fire, Ice, Water, Air, Earth, **Light-and-Shadow**),
  while the crit-roster shows the **derived/negative types** (Void, Aether, Nether, Shock,
  Krush, Acid, Umbra, Blasphemous) layered on top. See MAG-4 impact.
- **Corruption** is a **point-based contamination axis** (Corruzione / Corruzione Elementale
  da Aria), earned from demonic gear, demon-fusion, or elemental exposure — distinct from
  the four realms. Supports "3–4 realms + a corruption axis" reading.
- **Closed lists surfacing as rewards:** "**Chaotic Senses**" (Ragash) and "**Enlightening
  Ways**" (Arhat) — thematic dream/chaos spell-lists.
- **Custode's Key** = a miniature partial **Rune** producing **minor temporal
  alterations**, strengthening when attuned to a true Rune (time-magic hook).

## 11. Gaerwath — encounter/faction table (`Incontri casuali Gaerwath.txt`)

Random-encounter table for **Gaerwath** (dark-elf/demon-haunted realm) that incidentally
catalogues its powers and hazards:
- **Five sorcerous factions** hold fortresses: **Mecanisti (Mechanists/constructs),
  Demonologi (Demonologists), Biomanti (Biomancers), Sognatori (Dreamers), Necromanti
  (Necromancers).** Fortresses roll 1-3 Mecanisti / 4-6 Demonologi / 7-8 Biomanti /

# Conflict impact — RESOLVES / CHANGES / COMPLICATES

### COS-2 · Which Catastrophe account is true — **COMPLICATES (adds a frame)**
No source here narrates the Catastrophe directly, but *Strumenti di Potere* adds a **fourth

the fortress-city **Xanadu**. This dovetails with account (3) ("demons loose, being fought")
but frames the demons as a **war already underway**, not a single ritual mishap, and it
introduces **Xanadu** (distinct from Ragash). **Answer:** still unresolved among (1)/(2)/(3);
this material *thickens* the demon-war backdrop and argues that "Adepti vs. Sovereign
Demons" was a protracted conflict, consistent with the merge-recommendation (C) but not
selecting it.

### COS-11 · What the Maelstrom seals (demon-prison vs. Plane of Chaos; the isolation bell) — **COMPLICATES**
Nothing here touches the Maelstrom or the "campana di isolamento" directly. But *Demoni.txt*
places demons in **multiple homes** — the **Vuoto (Void)** (Natharl'Nacna, ancestors of the
Sovereigns), "remote **infernal planes**" (Phlegetauri), and "another plane" (Vetro
Abissale) — and confirms demons are **made of Essenza Oscura (Dark Essence).** **Answer:**
does not decide demon-prison vs. Plane-of-Chaos; it **supports the Corrupted/Dark-Essence
demon-origin** and shows the "beyond" holding several demon-realms at once, which fits the
"unify as one substance" recommendations (COS-3/MAG-8) but leaves the seal's target open.

### COS-19 · The overloaded "beyond" (Void / Spazio Esterno / Inferno / Piano del Caos / Dreamlands) — **COMPLICATES (worsens the overload)**
This batch **adds more layers, not fewer.** *Demoni* puts demons in the **Void** and
**infernal planes**; *Planes2* imports an entire **D&D Great Wheel** (Sigil, Gehenna,
Cathrys/Carceri, Arborea/Olimpo, the Baatezu-vs-Tanar'ri **Blood War**, the Abyss); *Terre
dei Sogni* + *Ragash* flesh out the **Dreamlands** (with Lovecraftian **Esterni**);
*Gaerwath* adds **Dream-Mushroom** gateways and dimensional portals. **Answer:** the "beyond"
is now Void + Outer Space + infernal planes + a Planescape wheel + Dreamlands. The clean
COS-19 recommendation (one Outer Space = the Void; Inferno = engineered prison = Piano del
Caos; Dreamlands adjacent) is still workable, **but the *Planes2* Great Wheel is a genuinely
new, hard-to-fold cosmological frame** — flag it (see New Conflicts).

### COS-18 / MAG-1 / MAG-15 · Dragon origins — **COMPLICATES (minor); does not resolve**
No origin statement. But the batch adds: a **"Vero Dio Drago" (True Dragon-God)** as

Draghi vs. 20 Adepti-made Veri Draghi) remain unresolved; the new "Vero Dio Drago" founder
fits the "True Dragon" tier and can slot under the existing 3-kinds recommendation.

### MAG-4 · The true set of Elements — **COMPLICATES toward the recommended model**
Strong support for "**six prime elements + a closed list of derived/negative types**." The
**six Reliquie** give a clean six-domain backbone — **Fire (Ignus), Ice (Saralis), Water
(Tassalung), Air (Natharien), Earth (Oedring), Light-and-Shadow (Meletskar)** — i.e. the
public **Elementi** with **Light doubling as Light-and-Shadow** and **Air explicitly
present.** Meanwhile the crit-roster confirms the **derived/negative types** MAG-4 worried

**Answer:** keeps six prime elements; **expands** the named derivation list (add Shock,
Krush, Impact, Blasphemous, Acid to the previously-noted Nether/Void/Umbra/Aether). Note
"Storm/Spectral" from the earlier audit do **not** appear here; **Air** and **Aether** do.

### MAG-5 · Magic realms vs. corruption axis — **CHANGES (important)**
The Reliquie are ×4 **multipliers for four *named* realms: Essenza, Flusso, Mentalismo,
Arcano** — one Reliquia per realm — and **"Usufruitori Corrotti" is NOT among them.**
Corruption instead appears as a **point-based axis** (Corruzione from demonic gear/fusion;
Air Elemental Corruption in the Heis cult). **Answer:** this is direct evidence for **four
realms = Essence / Flow / Mentalism / Arcane, with corruption as a separate axis** — which
**revises** MAG-5's "**3 realms + axis**" recommendation toward **4 realms + axis**, and
**revises MAG-3**: Arcano behaves as a **distinct fourth realm with its own artifact**, *not*
as "the union/high tier of the other three." (Four realm-Reliquie also neatly match the
**four Rune Magiche**.)

### MAG-9 · Six Reliquie vs. the Door-of-Despair set — **RESOLVES the Reliquie half; supports separation**
*Strumenti di Potere* + *Le Reliquie del Potere* **fully specify the six Reliquie** (names,

six unlocks the Runes' true power. The **Door of Despair / Chiave del Nulla / Sangue
Elementale / Pergamene** set is **not mentioned here at all.** **Answer:** the Reliquie are
**definitively the Rune-unseal set** (keyed to Sanctuaries at **Tzolkin/Yaotl**, catalogued

Door-of-Despair set, exactly as MAG-9 recommends. It also links **Masamune → Meletskar**,
tidying one loose blade.

# NEW conflicts / tensions this batch introduces

1. **What the Rune Magiche actually unseal — Xanadu weapon-vault vs. Maelstrom/demon-prison
   (HIGH, ties MAG-9 / COS-11 / History).** Canon (`history.md`, `magic.md`,
   `secret-the-great-lie.md`) says gathering the six Reliquie unseals the **Rune del Potere**,
   which **cracks the Maelstrom's isolation and the demons' prison**, triggering the Era della
   Marea. But *Strumenti di Potere* frames the Rune Magiche as **anti-demon superweapons made
   by the Adepti**, sealed for safety at the fortress-city **Xanadu**, usable as power sources
   — with **no** statement that unsealing them opens the Maelstrom. **Two readings of the same
   act:** apocalyptic seal-break vs. recovering a weapon-cache. Needs a decision.
2. **"Xanadu" vs. "Ragash" as the seat of the Rune/Adepti power (MED).** The Runes were
   housed and sealed at **Xanadu** (this batch), whereas the Adepti *capital* that became the
   Maelstrom is **Ragash** (canon). Are Xanadu and Ragash the same city under two names, two
   different Adepti/Xebechani cities, or is Xanadu a later Xebechani reconstruction? (The
   temple is Xebechani, suggesting Xanadu may be a **Xebechani** fortress, not the Adepti
   capital.)
3. **"Le Rune Magiche sono 4" — a count where canon gave none (LOW/MED).** This batch pins
   the Rune del Potere at **four** (matching the four realm-Reliquie); canon leaves them
   uncounted. Adopt "four Runes"?
4. **Planes2's D&D Great Wheel as a parallel cosmology (MED, feeds COS-19).** Sigil, the
   Blood War, Baatezu/Tanar'ri, Gehenna/Carceri/Arborea, Gog's fall — a whole Planescape
   multiverse that does not map onto the Player-Guide planes. Decide: canonise as the "true"
   structure of the beyond, treat as an in-world *foreign* cosmology, or shelve as a
   non-canon crossover.
5. **Saralis vs. Sarnak name variant (LOW).** The Ring of Winter is spelled both ways, with
   two different power write-ups. Canon uses **Saralis**; normalise and pick one power set.
6. **"Triade" terminology collision (LOW/MED).** "La Triade" here = the **six Qi-Long
   resistance societies**, but there are **six**, not three, and canon's `pantheon.md`
   already uses "triad" for a *feminine/maternal* three-goddess grouping. Also potential
   overlap of these six *rebel* sects with the six ruling **Grandi Sette Imperiali**
   (`factions.md`) — the manual needs to keep "ruling sects" vs. "resistance triads" distinct.
7. **New Sovereign/great-demon names to reconcile (LOW).** **Xardas** (Lord of Plots, master
   of the Burattini) and **Yogh** as a demon-"god" (father of the Tessiombra) should be
   checked against the Sovereign roster and against COS-5 (Yogh-in-two-pantheons).
