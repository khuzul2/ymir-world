# Ymir — Regions Digest (batch2)

English digest of the Italian RPG-worldbuilding files in
`sources/extracted/batch2/regions/`. Proper nouns preserved as written in the
sources. Present day ≈ 3518 d.M. Source files are a mix of GM adventure notes
(Wald, Xebech, Kyria, Erg, Ulan-Tang) and short in-world "legends" texts
(Sadhir, Alfheim, Helgedad, Ophiur), plus a gazetteer page for Meridian.

A per-conflict impact summary is at the end.

---

## Wald (a Young Kingdom)

**Location / terrain.** A cold, mountainous highland riddled with old dwarven
works. On the party's map the region lies ~70 km NW of **Tor-Varn** (~10 days on
foot, ~7 with "Yonker's machine") and ~40 km N of **Tor-Throst** (~5 days, ~3
with the machine). The **Santuario delle Braci** (Sanctuary of Embers) is marked
next to Tor-Varn. Identifying the region is "Conoscenza Regione (Wald) Molto
Difficile," i.e. it is an obscure, hard-to-place backwater. Terrain hazards
include rockfalls/landslides, crevasses, and scattered ruins — mostly the
disused remains of dwarven watchtowers.

**Peoples.** Native **Nani** (dwarves) built the "Tor-" strongholds (Tor-Fel,
Tor-Varn, Tor-Throst) but are now enslaved. The land is overrun by a "Chaos"
horde: **Goblin** (heavily penalised in full sunlight), **Orchetti** (orcs; hate
sunlight but no mechanical penalty) including **Orchetti Elite** and **Comandanti
Orcheschi**, **Troll di Montagna** (mountain trolls, immune to natural heat/cold),
**Bestie Alate** (winged beasts), **Cavalcalupi** (goblin wolf-riders), **Gratar**,
and summoned **Demoni del Caos**. There are also traces of past **Xebechani**
contact (a Vera Via / True Way node is present).

**Rulers / politics — "Gli Adoratori del Caos" (The Chaos Worshippers).** The
dwarven fortress **Tor-Fel** has been captured by goblins; its dwarves enslaved.
The warband is led by:
- **Glob-Riba-Noz** (15th lvl) — an ancient, insane **Gratar Rosso** (Red Gratar)
  who devoured most of his own clan in honor of the god **Heis**. He preaches that
  Heis will drive out the dwarves *and the dragon*, leaving all Wald to his chosen
  followers (a mixed-race mob). Wields a **Bacchetta del Caos** (Wand of Chaos).
- **Bish-Bosh** (8th lvl) — a jealous goblin sorcerer who believes the Gratar's
  power comes solely from the wand.
- **Umagaur** (20th lvl) — an old mountain troll jailer; wields **The Mace of
  Umagaur** (Clava +15, chance to break a limb on a severe critical).
- **Nìm** — "chief" of the imprisoned old dwarves.

**Note:** the horde speaks of driving out "the dwarves **and the dragon**," so
there is an (unnamed) dragon presence/power in Wald that the cult opposes.

**Key hazard / site.** **Fungo Rosso (Yanwer)** — a subterranean fungus whose
spores incite violent, psychotic behavior. The starting cavern is a
centuries-abandoned, ruined dwarven fortress.

**Present day (≈3518).** A frontier in collapse: an ancient dwarven realm largely
reduced to ruins, its surviving dwarves enslaved, its passes and holds infested
by a Chaos-worshipping monster horde under a mad Red Gratar. Travel requires an
Orientation roll every 4 hours and rolls on a wandering-monster table dominated by
orc/goblin/troll contingents.

## Xebech (Xebechani homeland)

**Location / terrain.** Reached via the **Vera Via** (True Way) network
(activation is an "Estremamente Difficile" Attunement maneuver). The homeland's
centerpiece is a floating city; a nearby menace is the **Isola della Morte**
(Island of Death), an undead-plagued place (Cadavere Putrescente, Magro Notturno,
Verme Putrido, Vile).

**Xuthal — the capital.** "Masterpiece of Xebechan engineering": an enormous
**flying city** held aloft by a huge reservoir of **Ixliss**, a stone with
antigravity properties when properly charged. Splendid but decaying — many
underground quarters have been abandoned to demographic decline, and "strange
shadows" now roam them. Whole sections (**Le Sale Dimenticate** / the Forgotten
Halls) are sealed as "unstable."

**Peoples (Xebechani castes/subraces named here).**
- **Xattva** — the arcanist elite (Arcanista/Wizard/Elementalista, 40th lvl);
  fatalistic, disengaged from "mundane" affairs.
- **Xion** — the priesthood that actually runs the state church.
- **Xidhe** — diplomats/scribes/mentalists (e.g., **Omoteuchtli**, a 15th-lvl
  Mentalist who greets visitors).
- **Xudra** — the laboring underclass; the only ones who deal daily with the
  sealed halls, which they simply avoid. (Xudra names: Xotl, Yuthl, Octlas, Tuchl,
  Seketh.)

**Rulers / politics.** Nominally ruled by a grand council of Xattva, the **Alti
Illuminati** (High Enlightened), which handles the gravest matters — chiefly the
state of the **Pozze/Fosse** (the Pools/Pits) and the threat of the Island. Three
council factions:
- **Xochiyaoyotl** (Arcanist 40) — non-interventionist/pacifist, near-fatalist.
- **Cuauhxicalli** (Wizard 40) — moderate; favors small containment actions on the
  Island and occasional small experiments on the Pozze. **Strongest** faction.
- **Tlazocamati** (Arcane Elementalist 40) — interventionist; wants a heavy assault
  on the Island and major changes to the Fosse. **Weakest** faction.

Real power, however, lies with the **Culto delle Stelle** (Cult of the Stars), the
church run by the Xion, since the Xattva care little for worldly problems. Its
six **Alti Teofani** (all Xion Priests, 20th lvl): **Tezcatlipoca,
Mictlantechuli, Tamoanchan, Tlaxcala, Xinihuini, Naxmatoatl**. The **Biblioteca**
(Library) is run by the Xion (head librarian **Xlutalla**) with Xidhe scribes.

**Foreign residents in Xuthal.** **Elor Lethyliss** (Elf Mentalist, seeking
knowledge in the old Xebechan temple quarter); **Vincenzo Alvarez** (a Cartosan
thief/cartographer); **Barulf** (a Dwarf Priest of the **Monti Bianchi** / White
Mountains).

**The hidden threat.** Deep in the city dwells **Mologorth**, a **Terrorite**
demon (32nd lvl, tag "VI"), a lieutenant of the **Demoni Sovrani** (Sovereign
Demons). He appears as a black man with a long serpent's "thing" and great bat
wings; his bite drains ~1d0+20 HP, he radiates a Fear aura (30 m), is immune to
heat/cold, and knows many spell lists. He commands Shade Demons, lesser demons,
and reanimated Xebechani, and seeks to **corrupt the whole city to expand the
Isola della Morte** — without yet daring to attack the powerful Xattva directly.

**Religion — Dèi Xebe (the Concept-Gods / Outer Gods).** Six abstract "Concept
Gods" (Dèi Esterni), openly described, each mapped by less-sophisticated
worshippers onto conventional domains:
- **sguardo-sul-nulla** ("gaze-upon-the-void") — the alien/incomprehensible;
  worshipped as god of Wisdom, the Occult, Madness.
- **tricotomia-di-atti-e-pensieri** — the division of all acts into three Ways
  (Good / Evil / Nothing); the least understood and most worshipped Outer God.
- **parola-che-tace** ("word-that-is-silent") — Cosmic Silence that births Music
  and Word; Arts, Magic, Oblivion, Destruction.
- **essenza-di-tutto-ciò-che-era-è-e-fu** — the Primordial Essence; worshipped by
  Arcanists.
- **connubio-in-uno** ("union-in-one") — Creation, Life, Death, Union, Spirits,
  Nature.
- **fiume-di-essere** ("river-of-being") — Time; past/present/future, decay, birth.

**Present day (≈3518).** A magnificent but hollowing-out flying metropolis: a
declining population, sealed decaying quarters, a fatalist arcane elite ceding
day-to-day power to a star-cult priesthood, and a Sovereign-Demon lieutenant
quietly metastasizing in its depths while the Island of Death grows.

## Kyria

**Location / terrain.** An **island** reached only by airship (~3 days' flight from
the staging port). It is ringed by a dense fog bank in which all magical devices
switch off and airships lose altitude — forcing crash-landings. The island holds
mountains and a single city.

**The Dream-World veil.** Kyria was **partly hidden inside the Mondo dei Sogni**
(Dream World) centuries ago to conceal it from the **Demoni Sovrani**. Time passes
differently there, so the inhabitants seem to ignore the effects of time (a fact
of great interest to the character Samael's longevity research).

**Peoples.** **Tanoth** (including white Tanoth — the elder **Sanghar**), **Ur-
Tanoth** warriors and animists (the latter cast True Hold en masse), **Giganti
delle Nuvole** (Cloud Giants), **Ucorni** (tendril-lashing plant-beasts), men, and
"other creatures."

**Rulers / politics — the Legione Sognante (Dreaming Legion).** Kyria is governed
by the dragon **Gildiss** through a secret order, the "Dreaming Legion" (or simply
"the Legion"), which has trained for centuries for the last battle against the
**Demoni Sovrani**. Founded by Gildiss, it unites men, tanoth, giants and others.
- **Gildiss** (60th lvl) — a **Drago dei Sogni** (Dream Dragon), a follower of
  **Topramesk**. Notably, he can **remove the shadow of the Essenza Corrotta**
  (Corrupted Essence) from a person.
- **Sanghar** — wise Tanoth Animist, leads the escort to the city.
- **Lucius Valor** — a **Paladin of house/family Letia**; initial jailer of visitors.
- **Valhetchka** — a **Xattva** Summoner and ex-demonologist; teaches that entities
  *un*connected to the Corrupted Essence can be summoned, and warns of corruption.

**Key site.** **Thonis** — the only city on Kyria. The Legion also knows of a
**Reliquia** (Relic) hidden in an abandoned **Xebechan ruin** in the mountains
(part of a broader multi-relic quest; see Ulan-Tang below).

**Present day (≈3518).** A hidden dream-shrouded island fortress-culture led by an
immortal Dream Dragon, arming for an eschatological war against the Sovereign
Demons; outsiders who crash through the fog are treated as prisoners and
interrogated in Thonis.

## Grande Erg (the Erg desert)

**Location / terrain.** A vast **sand desert** ("GRANDE ERG"). It lies roughly a
**10-day camel journey from Ulan-Tang** (the location of Nemelatekh's tomb is
given relative to Ulan-Tang), tying the Erg to the Ulan-Tang desert belt. Travel:
one encounter roll per 8 hours (oases, sandstorms, ruins, nomads, caravans,
raiders).

**Peoples / creatures.** Human **Nomadi**, **Carovane** (caravans), and **Predoni**
(raiders). Beasts: **Serpente delle Sabbie** (Sand Serpent), **Basilisco**,
**Gatto del Deserto**, **Sciacalli** (jackals), **Scorpioni Giganti**, **Scarabei
Schienagiada** (Jadeback Beetles). The apex predator is the **Zaar'Qrak** ("Morte
Squartatrice" / roughly "Slaughtering Death" in the Erg tongue): a ~3.5 m,
six-limbed, black-furred, bone-plated nocturnal horror, a solitary ambush hunter
so feared that Erg-dwellers pale at its name.

**The Dream-Lands / Mubafer.** From the Erg (via the seer **Faazi-Ghaib** /
Faazhi-Gaib and a ritual/potion) one can enter the **Terre dei Sogni** (Dream
Lands), where the archmage **Mubafer** is imprisoned in a luxurious palace-bubble.
Mubafer (Wizard 50th lvl) wandered the planes with his lover **Azhaara**, betrayed
her with a being named **Mindelume**, and was sealed away until he forgets all
feeling for both women. He is the son of the thaumaturge **Nemelatekh**; he knows
the location of **Nemelatekh's tomb** (10 days by camel from Ulan-Tang), which is
split into a false sepulcher (right — holds the **Martello/Hammer**, an elemental
Relic) and the true sepulcher (left — the corpse and treasures, guarded by a
construct forged with the Hammer's power).

**Sub-plot.** "Il Dottore" (the Doctor) hijacked the airship **"Folgore,"** turning
commander **Eric Legrasse** and most of the crew into undead, and abducted
**Yonker** to study his symbiont.

**Present day (≈3518).** A deadly open sand-sea of nomads, raiders and monsters,
functioning as a threshold to the Dream Lands and to buried tombs of forgotten
mages/thaumaturges.

## Ulan-Tang

**Location / terrain.** A desert city reached via a *damaged* **Vera Via** (a
botched activation dumps travelers into **il Vuoto** / the Void first). It sits amid
sand-buried ruins in the same desert belt as the Grande Erg (identical
wandering-encounter table; the local apex predator is called **Razarac** in place
of Zaar'Qrak). A guide (e.g., the brigand **Hazif**) makes the crossing far easier.

**History.** Once a free trade city dedicated to **Ishtar**; conquered centuries ago
by sorcerers who worship **Yogh**. Now "a city of enchantments, assassinations and
intrigue" — but also of opportunity.

**Rulers / politics.** The **Gran Sacerdote di Yogh**, **Sazar-Gadash lo Stregone**
(the Sorcerer), is supreme authority, having assassinated his predecessor
**Azim-Mulath**. He is rumored to be a demon's son (some say of **Asmodeus**). There
are **no city guards** — everyone fends for themselves — and the great families run
the city through private guards, assassins, mages and healers.

**Noble families** (with signature garb):
- **Gadash** (head: Sazar-Gadash) — now run the Temple; violet tunics.
- **Ishra** (Bal-Ishra) — feared mages/summoners; red tunics.
- **Tyarreth** (Yugal-Tyarreth) — master alchemists and drug-sellers; olive-green robes.
- **Miraz** (Bozisha-Miraz) — Seers and spies; blue hooded cloaks. Little in the
  city escapes them; even the Gran Sacerdote takes their counsel.
- **Gaib** (Baras-Gaib) — shadow-and-illusion manipulators; black clothing; rumored
  ties to the **Azar-Kalish / Azhar-Kalish**.
- Lesser houses: **Azhir** (Muhab-Azhir; sages/scribes — Alak-Azhir was of this
  house), **Mulath** (Nemer-Mulath; necromancers, fallen from grace; white-and-gold),
  **Mek** (Jerrek-Mek; scribes, Azhir's rivals — engineered Alak-Azhir's murder).

**Commerce.** Everything is for sale; the city specializes in **Magic, Alchemy and
Pleasure**. Scrolls/potions/wands are sold up to 10th-level spells only. A **Mercato
Notturno** (Night Market) run by freaks, half-demons, succubi, and vampires trades
in things too obscene even for daytime Ulan-Tang.

**Key NPCs / sites.** **Alnac-Creb** (thieves'-guild boss); **Raafa as-Keeme**
(Bard/Houri, runs the brothel **Il Giardino dei Piaceri**; secretly an agent of the
**Azhar-Kalish**; knew **Sigurd**); **Jasshar-Ishra** (half-demon summoner, runs the
opium den **Il Cuscino di Seta**); **Faazhi-Gaib** (evil Seer of house Gaib, holds
the "cubo del sangue di Hudemia," knows Mubafer's whereabouts); **Califa la Strega**
(a Succubus at the Night Market with a unicorn's horn and an Incubo-binding ritual);
**Adhal-Kibreeshi** ("the Whispering Lady" / "Creeping Death"), a giant spider (35th
lvl) revered by many as an **avatar of Yogh**.

**The Relics quest (cross-region geography).** Ulan-Tang notes list the locations of
several elemental **Reliquie**:
- **Terra (Earth / the Hammer):** unearthed from a **Xebechan ruin**, later sold to
  **Nemelatekh, a thaumaturge of the Regno di Korish** (Kingdom of Korish/Khorish),
  who forged many magic items with it; buried with him in his secret tomb.
- **Acqua (Water):** reputedly on an island near the **Principato di Cartosa** — but
  the island is said to have sunk centuries ago.
- **Luce (Light):** long ago recovered in the **Impero Letio** (Letian Empire); a
  fanatical order of **Mithra** priests (**S. Septimum**) ritually split its Light
  and Shadow essences. The luminous blade was lost during the **Empire's dissolution**
  and last placed in far-off **Qi-Long**; the shadow-seed is kept in a monastery not
  far from **Armora**.

**Present day (≈3518).** A corrupt, Yogh-ruled sorcerer-city of feuding magical
houses, drug and pleasure trades, and a demon-blooded high priest — a hub for
intrigue, forbidden lore, and Relic-hunting leads. (The file ends with an
incomplete "GALADAN" heading.)

## Meridian

**Location / terrain.** Capital of the **Repubblica di Meridian**. It began as an
outpost at the **edge of the jungle** and was built for defense **on a system of
natural and artificial islands in the middle of one of Ymir's largest rivers**.
Eternal rival of neighboring **Cartosa**. A major commercial/cultural center famed
for its buildings on the water, its artists, and — above all — its production of
**Urakron**, a precious enchanted wood abundant in the adjacent jungle and the basis
of the Republic's wealth.

**Rulers / politics.** Governed by the **Pubblica Assemblea** (the noble houses),
which holds both legislative and executive power. It is headed by a **President**
(currently **Carlos Benicio Valdantini**), elected every 5 years from among the
members; the office's real weight has ranged historically from figurehead to
near-dictator. The Assembly's guards are the **Custodi**, in parade armor and
house-masked helms.

**Key sites — the 7 Quartieri (districts), one per major house.**
- **Porto Vecchio** (Old Port) — partly destroyed by Cartosa's fleet in the **Guerra
  delle Onde** (War of the Waves); still used by small/discreet craft; explorers'
  jumping-off point for the interior.
- **Le Gilde** (the Guilds) — seats of the **Compagnia degli Scudi**, **Accademia dei
  Cantori**, **Società Mercantile**; the bourgeois quarter.
- **Giardini** (Gardens) — the founding houses each planted a tree here to symbolize
  their alliance; now competing luxury gardens, theaters, and courtesans' houses.
- **Mercato** (Market) — the busiest quarter; goods from all Ymir by day, shadier
  trades by night.
- **Il Meandro** — the oldest, poorest quarter; a labyrinth of filthy alleys and
  canals.
- **Porto Nuovo** (New Port) — built over the razed quarters of **Pescheria** and
  **Faro**; the latter belonged to house **Vallerini**, who **betrayed the other
  seven houses** by secretly allying with Cartosa during the war.
- **Uffizi** — seat of the Assembly and government; includes the **Santuario**, which
  hosts foreign dignitaries and envoys (including the **Xebechan mission**).

**Present day (≈3518).** A wealthy, arts-loving island-republic on a great river at
the jungle's edge, structurally defined by its rivalry with Cartosa (and the scars
of the War of the Waves) and by the Urakron trade; it maintains active diplomatic
ties, including with the Xebechani.

## Sadhir (from "Leggende del Sadhir")

**Location / terrain.** The **Deserto Scintillante** (Glittering/Shimmering Desert) —
a **salt desert**. Few travel there and fewer return alive or sane. Beneath the sands
run galleries hundreds of kilometers long, carved through rock and salt, linking the
cities of **Al-Sidath** and **Al-Jabur**; these tunnels were once the refuge of some
of the mightiest mages of the war that led to **Ymir's destruction and the
Maelstrom**. Some scholars link the desert's hallucinations to the **Mondo dei Sogni**.

**Peoples / rulers.** Human and Arabesque in every particular: the **Sadhiriti** (the
Sadhirites) are a masked desert people (they wear the region's typical protective
masks); the **sultan of Al-Sidath** owns a collection of ancient artifacts each
guarded by a mighty **Marid** (djinn). **No lizardfolk of any kind appear** — no
Sarradhi, no reptilian sultanate; the ruler is a (human) sultan and the cities bear
human/Semitic names.

**Legends / present-day situation.**
- The **cities of Al-Sidath and Al-Jabur are mysteriously untouched by the Salt**;
  something guarded within them may be the true cause of the Salt-storms.
- Mirages sometimes take the form of a fortress or city that lures travelers inside to
  imprison them forever — said to be the abode of the mysterious **Signore delle
  Illusioni** (Lord of Illusions).
- A colossal creature is said to **swim through the desert sands like a fish through
  water**, raising storms; caravans that vanish are blamed on it.
- **Gash-Azr** ("Ombre del Sale" / Shadows of Salt): wraiths in ragged cloaks and Sadhir
  masks, beneath which is only darkness and salt; they drain bodily fluids, leaving
  desiccated corpses, and haunt remote ruins and the cities' streets on the darkest nights.
- The **Sadhiriti await a savior** — a stranger who will come to the desert and **free
  them from the Salt**.

**Present day (≈3518).** A cursed, salt-choked human desert of two intact
oasis-cities under a sultan, haunted by salt-wraiths and a sand-swimming leviathan,
its people awaiting a foretold deliverer to lift the curse of the Salt.

## Alfheim (from "Leggende dell'Alfheim")

**Location / terrain.** **Not given.** The text is pure lore and provides **no
geographic anchor whatsoever** — no direction, neighbor, or coordinate.

**Peoples / rulers.** The **elf kingdom**, ruled by a **queen** (a powerful
mage-queen). The elves of Alfheim can **speak with the trees** of their realm, using
them to learn in real time everything happening within their borders. Most of the
population is said to be lost in a **collective trance** that projects the enchanted
energies making the realm **inaccessible** from outside.

**Legends / present-day situation.** Recently the realm is said to be **besieged by an
unstoppable enemy against which even the queen's magic is powerless**. Those who pass
near its borders speak with horror of the **driadi** (dryads), lovely beings that drag
the unwary to a horrible death.

**Present day (≈3518).** A sealed, tree-linked, trance-shrouded elven queendom under
existential siege by an unnamed unstoppable foe — but still with no stated location.

## Helgedad (from "Leggende dell'Helgedad")

**Location / terrain.** The northern land of the **Vorskgen**; its **northern borders**
hold **eternal ice** beneath which lie **ancient buried cities** — said by Vorskgen
shamans to be **older than the human race**. (This "far-north / eternal ice" framing
supports a northern placement for Helgedad.)

**Peoples / rulers.** The **Vorskgen** (a warrior people). No ruler is named; the piece
is legend-focused.

**Legends.** Even the bravest Vorskgen warriors shudder at **Varg-Korg**, "the perfect
warrior" — once perhaps human, now little more than a **machine**, cursed to wander
battlefields forever seeking new fights. No one has survived a duel with him, and
merely seeing or naming him is thought to bring misfortune.

**Present day (≈3518).** An icy Vorskgen north of warring clans, haunted by an
unkillable cursed champion and by prehuman cities frozen under its glaciers.

## Ophiur (from "Leggende dell'Ophiur")

**Location / terrain.** A **jungle** land ("the jungle of the Ophiur") dotted with
ancient ruins. No coordinates are given, but it is explicitly a jungle region.

**Peoples / rulers.** Home to **creatures of the most ancient races**, said to have
lived on Ymir **since the world's origins** and to have **survived the Wars of the
Mages** — i.e., Primeval/ancient peoples. Also **tribes** who, for protection, make
regular sacrifices to the demons and vampires infesting the old ruins.

**Legends.** The **Giardino di Xiombarg** (Garden of Xiombarg), hidden in the heart of
the Ophiur, guarded by ancient guardians, holding **miraculous plants that cure any
illness or affliction**.

**Present day (≈3518).** A primeval jungle nation of very ancient races and tribal
peoples, its old ruins infested by demons and vampires (appeased by tribal sacrifice),
concealing a legendary healing-garden.

# Conflict-impact summary

### GEO-1 / GEO-5 — Sadhir: human city-states vs. Sarradhi lizardfolk — **RESOLVES toward HUMAN**
"Leggende del Sadhir" (the file the brief calls decisive) portrays a **thoroughly
human/Arabesque Sadhir**: human-named cities **Al-Sidath** and **Al-Jabur**, a
**(human) sultan of Al-Sidath**, the **Sadhiriti** people, protective masks, **Marid**
djinn, and a salt-desert curse. **There is not a single mention of the Sarradhi or of
lizardfolk anywhere.** This strongly backs GEO-1 Option B / the GEO-5 tiebreaker
(human Sadhir) and undercuts the gazetteer's Sarradhi-sultanate picture. It is
*compatible with* the "layered" reading (GEO-1 Option C / GEO-5 Rec) only in that the
Salt/drying is clearly the region's defining catastrophe — but the file gives **no
positive evidence for any Sarradhi successor state**; the sultanate it depicts is human.
Net: this material tips the Sadhir decisively human and provides no support for lizardfolk.

### GEO-8 — Solanthya = Letia's capital-region vs. separate realm — **NOT ADDRESSED (unchanged)**
No file mentions **Solanthya** (or Solantia). The material only confirms adjacent facts:
Letia was an **Empire** ("**Impero Letio**") that later underwent a "**disfacimento**"
(dissolution), and a noble **house/family "Letia"** survives (the paladin Lucius Valor on
Kyria). Useful context for Letia, but no bearing on the Solanthya question.

### GEO-10 — three undisambiguated deserts — **SUPPORTS separation / refines**
The sources distinguish **two different deserts**: the **Sadhir = a salt desert**
("Deserto Scintillante," salt-storms, Al-Sidath/Al-Jabur) versus the **Grande Erg =
a sand desert** (Zaar'Qrak/Razarac, nomads/caravans, ~10 days from Ulan-Tang). Ulan-Tang
sits in the **Erg/sand belt**, and this belt is tied to the **Regno di Korish/Khorish**
(Nemelatekh's kingdom). This backs the GEO-10 Rec to keep the **Sadhir (salt)** and the
**central Erg+Khorish (sand)** as separate deserts. (No data on any far-southern
desert-continent, so that leg stays open.)

### GEO-11 — La Catastrofe: one event or several — **PARTIAL / CONTEXT ONLY**
The Sadhir legend ties the desert's mages'-refuge tunnels to "**the war that led to
Ymir's destruction and the Maelstrom**," and the Sadhiriti await deliverance "**from the
Salt**" — thematically linking the Sadhir's salting to the great Wars-of-the-Mages/
Maelstrom cataclysm. This is *consistent with* the single-upheaval Rec but does **not
explicitly** unify the Sadhir drying with the NW Gaerwath/Isole sea-raise, and gives **no
date**. Treat as supporting context, not a resolution.

### GEO-12 — Alfheim's location — **NOT RESOLVED (still blind)**
"Leggende dell'Alfheim" adds rich lore (mage-queen, tree-speaking, collective trance,
inaccessible realm, dryads, an unstoppable besieging enemy) but **still gives no
