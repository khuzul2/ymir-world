# FRAGMENTS — transcription digest & merge proposal

> Source: `sources/raw/Fragments.pdf` (6 handwritten Italian pages, scanned; no text layer — read
> from page images). **Nothing has been merged.** This document (1) digests what the source says and
> (2) proposes what to merge, with alternatives, conflicts, and the exact files that would change, for
> you to choose. Decisions to make are in **§3** and the checklist in **§6**.

The file is four in-world documents — hence its name. All four circle one subject the manual currently
treats only from a distance: **the Adepti as named individuals**, and the demons they fought.

---

## 1 · What the source says (digest)

### Fragment A (pp. 1–2) — "The Fifth Adept, and the Gem of Ört"
A first-person treatise by an old, unnamed scholar; this is **section XIII** of a numbered catalogue
of the Adepti (so the catalogue runs long — this instalment covers *the Fifth Adept*).
- **The Fifth Adept was Ört di Xand, called "the Shadowed" (l'Adombrato)** — touchy of temper; he
  **ruled the south-east of Mandolia, in the regions of the Sadhir and Ophith (Ophiur)**. His pupil
  was **Valamor, of the Aleth-thaar dynasty**, who vanished in the misty far North "in the 624th year
  of the Sun of the Golden Dragon."
- The historian **Tarogald of Mileus**, in his *De Daemonomachia* (a work of 26 books, most lost),
  tells how the Adepti used **a Gem made by Ört** to defeat demon-armies pouring from the
  interdimensional **Void**. Its later fate is unknown; **Zarog lo Stifeo** claims it went to the
  royal treasury of **Hasl'Af**, but none was ever found there. **King Teran X of Cartosa**, "tyrant
  of the southern seas," sailed south on an old map to find it and vanished (freeing Cartosa of his
  tyranny). Many sought it; none succeeded.
- **The Gem's power:** its bearer can summon and command **the Seven Stars of the Sky of Destiny** —
  the Stars of **Enchantment, Gifts, Life, Death, the Divine World, Mind, and Dominion**.
- The scholar's conclusion: **the Gem is not an object but a ritual** — a working that gathers the
  energy to evoke the Stars into a gem-like focus. The ritual is carved on **a round stone tablet
  Ört broke into four fragments**: two he gave his pupil Valamor to hide, two he hid himself. **Ört
  "ascended into the sky,"** but first left **a manuscript recording where all four fragments lie**,
  entrusted to a **monastic order of the far Sadhir — the temple of the forgotten god Jerbal, in the
  middle of the Shimmering Desert (south-east Sadhir).**

### Fragment B (p. 3) — "Ea, Lord of the Abysses"
The deep-past backstory of the demon **Ea** (whom canon already records as slain in 1570):
- Before the Demon Wars, before the Letian Empire, **Ea — a mighty vassal of the demon-lord Sith** —
  coveted Ymir, meaning to drain its power, rise to **Sovereign-Demon** rank, and dethrone Sith.
- **Sith saw through it, feigned being duped, and lent Ea legions — then abandoned him at the crucial
  hour.** Ea was defeated; the forces of good **killed him and sealed his spirit in a magic stone,
  buried deep**, with a **warning stele** foretelling doom if the tomb were disturbed.
- Ea's spirit sent **a call across space and time**. Ages later **a mage-antiquarian, Cresius**,
  answered it, found the stone, and **Ea possessed his body and trapped Cresius's spirit in the
  stone.** Weakened, Ea sought to return to Sith's service, hoping to be forgiven… *(the page ends;
  this evidently leads into the canon 1570 Blue-Oyster resurgence and Ea's final destruction).*

### Fragment C (p. 4) — "The Diary of Loregar: the Halls of Taros"
A transcribed treasure-route to **the Halls of Taros** by a dying writer:
- From the **south slope of the Peak of Ulmar**, ~half a day north-east, to the **Plain of the
  Stelae**; find **the Stele of the Martyr, which bears the Runes of Power that also adorned the
  Crown of the Kings of ancient times** (a runic inscription is drawn). At its base are **the symbols
  of the Four Kings**; set an **amulet** in the circle, turn it a half-turn right — a passage opens
  down into **the Crypts of Audin, beneath the Halls of Taros**. Follow the stones marked with **the
  Sign of Vor** and press the seventh…
- The writer left the amulet with **Belthan, an old friend, at the Temple of Ishtar**, to be given to
  whoever presents the document. **Taros was the pupil of Svelos — the great Adept famous for helping
  Topramesk drive back the demon Gol Sauthaur in the Demon Wars, and for writing the Iron Tome (Tomo
  di Ferro).** The writer warns of a particular trap he read of in **the Imperial Library of Valentia**.

### Fragment D (pp. 5–6) — "The Temple of the Blue Oyster (typical)"
A floor-plan and priesthood note for the **Cult of the Blue Oyster** (Ea/Sith's cult):
- **1** a purification basin; **2** a "Sacred Mosaic" of a blue oyster that is secretly a **life-
  draining enchantment**, siphoning worshippers' vitality into a hidden vessel; **3** the **Tower**,
  atop which sits **the great Blue Oyster**; **4** a **priests' meditation room** from which hidden
  "priests" work illusions during the rites.
- **The Priests of the Blue Oyster** are mostly evil mages and illusionists, with mystics and some
  mentalists, **led by true clerics of Sith.** *(The mosaic's numbers are Rolemaster mechanics — to
  be stripped for the system-agnostic manual; the flavour is kept.)*

### New proper nouns introduced
**Adepti & pupils:** Ört di Xand (5th Adept), Valamor (Aleth-thaar dynasty), Svelos (great Adept),
Taros (Svelos's pupil). · **Demons:** Sith (demon-lord, Ea's master, patron of the Blue Oyster),
Gol Sauthaur (Demon-Wars demon). · **Mortals/scholars:** Tarogald of Mileus, Zarog lo Stifeo,
Loregar, Cresius (mage possessed by Ea), Belthan, King Teran X of Cartosa. · **Places:** Mandolia,
Xand, Hasl'Af, Mileus, Valentia, Peak of Ulmar, Plain of the Stelae, Crypts of Audin / Halls of Taros,
temple of Jerbal. · **Artifacts:** the Gem of Ört / the Seven Stars of the Sky of Destiny (+ its four
tablet-fragments & locating-manuscript), the Iron Tome, the Crown of the Four Kings, the Halls-of-Taros
amulet, Ea's warning stele. · **Deity:** Jerbal (a forgotten god). · **Calendar:** "the Sun of the
Golden Dragon" (an archaic reckoning). · **Books:** *De Daemonomachia* (Tarogald), the *Diary of
Loregar*, the *Iron Tome* (Svelos).

## 3 · Conflicts & decisions (choose)

**D1 · Where does *Sith* sit?** The source makes Sith a demon-lord above Ea. Canon's demon roster
(`bestiary.md`): 17 living Sovereign Demons + Ea & Saxul slain; only Asmodeus, Xardas, Masak reach
Ymir after the Tide. Options:
- **(a) ★ Sith = one of the great Sovereign Demons** (a ruling one, of whom Ea was a vassal). Cleanest;
  slots into the existing roster.
- (b) Sith = a **higher tier** — a Demon-Prince *above* the Sovereigns (adds a new rank).
- (c) Sith = **a title/aspect of Ahriman** himself (the unmaking-force as lord of all demons).
- (d) Sith = an **alias** of an existing named Sovereign (e.g. quietly fold into Asmodeus/Xardas/Masak).
- *Side choice:* the name "Sith" reads as a Star-Wars borrow — **keep it** (the setting borrows freely)
  or **rename** (e.g. Xith, Seth, Sithuul).

**D2 · The named Adepti vs. the "seven-Adept ritual."** Canon (`secret-the-great-lie.md`) has
Topramesk's **ritual of the seven Adepts** cause the Catastrophe. The source numbers Adepti (Ört = the
Fifth). Options:
- **(a) ★ The catalogue names the seven great Adept-god-kings** — Topramesk among them, Ört the Fifth;
  they are the usurper-"gods" of the Great Lie. (Reconcile "Ört ascended into the sky" = he *became*
  one of the false sky-gods; the ones who worked the *final* ritual died, others "ascended" earlier.)
- (b) The Adepti were **many**; "the Fifth" is just a ranking, unrelated to the seven ritual-casters.
- (c) Keep the two sets **separate and unexplained** (some Adepti named, the seven a distinct cabal).

**D3 · The "Sun of the Golden Dragon" calendar.** Options:
- **(a) ★ Adopt as the archaic pre-Maelstrom / Adepti-era reckoning** (add to Appendix A & `calendar.md`;
  dates like "624 of the Sun of the Golden Dragon" are deep-past). Leave its link to the Dragon-Gods
  deliberately vague.
- (b) Tie it explicitly to the **Dragon-Gods / a golden dragon** (Gwalgamaur?) — richer but commits lore.
- (c) Treat it as one people's local reckoning, not adopted book-wide.

**D4 · The Crown of the Four Kings & the Runes of Power.** Canon: the **Runes of Power** are the four
sealed Adepti Runes (unsealed by the six Relics at the Tide). The source says the Runes "adorned the
Crown of the Kings of ancient times," with "the Four Kings." Options:
- **(a) ★ Soft tie:** the four Runes were once set in an ancient **Crown of the Four Kings** — enriches
  the Runes' backstory, no contradiction.
- (b) Keep the "Four Kings" as a **separate** old-regalia motif, unrelated to the Runes.

**D5 · Where are the Halls of Taros / Peak of Ulmar?** The source doesn't geolocate them. Options:
- **(a) ★ Place in the southern lands (Sadhir/Ophiur/Mandolia)**, near the Adepti's old southern seat
  and Jerbal's temple — keeps the Adepti material clustered in the south.
- (b) Place in the **north/east** (the "misty far North" where Valamor vanished; Valentia's empire).
- (c) Leave **unplaced** (a "lost site" the map does not mark — fits the book's frontier tone).

**D6 · King Teran X of Cartosa.** Canon Cartosa is a Letian **colony → Principality**, ruled by
**governors then the Carrillo princes** (from 1906) — **no kings**. Options:
- **(a) ★ Legendary/apocryphal early tyrant** whose historicity the Cartosan chroniclers dispute (fits
  ch. X's love of tall tales); not slotted into the Carrillo regnal list.
- (b) **Relocate him** — make him a king of another southern realm, not Cartosa.
- (c) Fold him into the **Carrillo backstory** as a disowned tyrant-ancestor.

**D7 · Minor normalizations (low stakes; I'll default unless you object):**
- **Hasl'Af** → unify with canon **Hasl'Aret** (one royal city with a treasury). · **Ophith** → **Ophiur**.
- **Mandolia** → treat as the **archaic name of the southern landmass** (Sadhir + Ophiur), used in old
  texts — no map redraw. · **Gol Sauthaur** spelling kept as the source gives it.

## 5 · Two bigger design choices (optional, high-flavour)

- **The scholars as Postil hands or as a quoted leaf.** The four fragments are *exactly* the kind of
  in-world document the book now collects. Two ways to use their authors:
  - **(a) ★ As new Postil hands** — Tarogald of Mileus (the dead historian, cited across the Adepti
    material), the dying diarist Loregar, and the old catalogue-writer become recurring marginal
    voices, tightening the new marginalia layer around the deep past.
  - **(b) As a new quoted leaf/chapter** — bind "the Adept-Fragments" as an inserted document (like the
    censored leaf or the sounding-report), the mortal-scholar's catalogue of the Adepti, with GM
    margins revealing the Great Lie underneath. Richer, larger.
  - (c) Both — the leaf, with the scholars also annotating elsewhere.
- **How much of the dungeon to canonize.** The Halls of Taros come with a working *mechanism* (amulet,
  runes, the seventh stone, a trap). Keep it **as a GM seed only** (15b), or also **write the room-by-
  room** as an appendix map? Recommend seed-only unless you want a playable set-piece.
