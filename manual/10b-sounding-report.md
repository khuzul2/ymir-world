# X·B · THE SOUNDING-REPORT OF SSAS'KHER
## *an inserted document, not a chapter: the deep kingdom, rendered in numbers*

**Being the report of soundings delivered to the Council of Peoples at the Crystal Seat on the fourth of Tortoise, 3517, by a legation of the Sohleugir of Ssas'Kher; rendered out of the trade-pidgin of the reef-marks by Sirvaneth, Grey-elf broker of the pearl-trade at Elendor, sworn to render and not to mend.**

> *Curator's note.* The Sohleugir surface to trade and seldom for anything else; in the two and a
> half centuries of the Equilibrium the Council's minutes record exactly two legations from the
> deep, and this is the second. It did not come to converse. Four came up at the Elendor
> pearl-mark, showed the wall-sign of Ssas'Kher, delivered this report cut into shell tablets,
> heard Sirvaneth's rendering read back, corrected one figure — she had made their rope ten metres
> — and went under again on the same tide. I asked leave to copy the report into this book. The
> legation asked what the book weighed. Scales were brought; the number satisfied them; consent
> was given, and no further word. I bind the leaf here, beside the navigator's home waters, where
> their trade-marks are. The reader who finds it dry is advised to read the last entry twice. —
> *the Curator*

---

[*The broker's note on the rendering. The trade-pidgin of the reef-marks has numbers, nouns, and
verbs of motion, and does not keep adjectives; where an adjective stands below it is mine, and I
have used as few as I could bear. Their rope I render at nine metres and one hand; their weight at
a third of a hundredweight, rounded in the Council's favour at their own instruction. One word I
could not render, and have glossed where it falls. I was sworn to render and not to mend, and I
have mended nothing — not even where I wished to. — S.*]

**REPORT OF THE KINGDOM OF SSAS'KHER TO THE COUNCIL OF THE PEOPLES OF THE AIR.** The kingdom
reports in seven soundings. The kingdom does not interpret.

**Sounding the first — the trade.** Pearls delivered at the surface-marks in the last sixty
cycles: 4,140 strings. Coral: 610 weights. Received against them: worked steel, 388 weights; cast
glass, 512 weights. Short-weight found in the kingdom's deliveries: none. Short-weight found in
the surface deliveries: 17 instances; corrected, 17. The kingdom gives notice: from this cycle its
need of worked steel doubles. Notice is given so that the weights will be ready.

**Sounding the second — the walls.** The crest of the walls of **Ssas'Kher** stands at 61 ropes
below the surface. The foot of the walls stands on the reef-floor at 179 ropes. Height of the
walls, foot to crest: 118 ropes. Courses of coral added since the last report: three. Entries of
assault in the wall-tally since the founding: 14. Breaches: none. The newest entry of assault is
912 cycles old.

**Sounding the third — the watch.** Stations of watch kept by the kingdom: 17. Three concern this
Council. Item, the ground your sailors call the Oyster's water: watch of 40, relieved at the sixth
cycle; watchers entered in the death-tally of that station since it was set: 118. What is under it
has not moved. Pearls of that ground brought up: none. Offers from surface merchants for such
pearls, these sixty cycles: 6. Refused: 6. Item, a trench in which a door of the ancients stands
on the sea-floor. The door stood shut from the year 0 of your count. Since 3025 it stands open.
Things that have come through it: 5. Stopped: 5. The kingdom withholds the bearing. Item, the
shelf of the land that rose. The kingdom set that station in the cycle of the rising. Fish are
absent within 40 leagues of that shore. Things that have come down that shore into the water: 10.
Stopped: 9. Not stopped: 1. That entry stands open.

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *Read the third sounding rightly and you have the Sohleugir entire: the walls that were never
> breached belong to a people who have never besieged anything. Their art is not the keeping-out
> but the keeping-in. They do not cleanse the sea's wrongs; they **contain** them — ring them with
> watchers, sink lattices of worked steel and cast glass about them (now the reader knows what the
> fairest merchants buy metal* for*), and pay the fee in the one coin the report does not gloss:
> the hundred and eighteen of the Oyster's station did not drown in weather. They died holding a
> door shut from the outside, against a drowned congregation that is still holding its service on
> the inside. And mark the arithmetic left standing open: down the risen continent's shore, ten;
> stopped, nine. The Ophiur leaf's margin records a thing that came out of the prison-crack at the
> Tide and walked south looking for a throne no one would contest. Two leaves, one remainder. The
> Sohleugir know it is loose. They logged it. The log is now yours.*

**Sounding the fourth — the cost.** Sohleugir entered in the death-tallies of all stations since
the founding: 4,966. Sohleugir standing watch at this delivery: 712. Cycles of service owed to the
stations by each of the kingdom's living: 30. The kingdom does not ask for help. The kingdom
reports the numbers.

**Sounding the fifth — the sound.** A sound stood in the deep water from before the kingdom's
founding. Its bearing was the centre of the world. It did not change, and it was entered nowhere,
as the sea is entered nowhere. In the cycle 3025 of your count the sound stopped. The kingdom's
tally of that cycle holds this entry: the sea has lost a sound.

**Sounding the sixth — the count of the beat.** When the sound stopped, a beat could be counted
where the sound had been. [*Their word for the beat is the word for a heart heard through a hull.
The comparison is theirs, and it is the only one in the report. — S.*] The kingdom set a
counting-station in 3027. Interval of the beat, 3027 to 3478: 12 days. Beats counted in those
cycles: 13,560. Beats missed: none.

**Sounding the seventh — the present count.** In the cycle 3479 the interval lengthened: 12 days
and one quarter. In 3499: 12 days and one half. In this cycle: 13 days. Beats missed since 3506:
4. The newest missed beat stands 41 days before this delivery. Entry: **the rhythm of the
Maelstrom is faltering.** The kingdom does not interpret. The kingdom has counted. The report is
ended.

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *The sound that stood in the deep until 3025 was the isolation bell — the Adepts' quarantine
> over the whole world, which every people above the water mistook for silence. The Sohleugir
> alone lived inside the bell, and heard it, and when it ceased they did the one sane thing done
> anywhere that year: they began to count. The Maelstrom is scar and seal; the beat is the seal's
> pulse; and the pulse now skips, on a curve that lengthens. This is the Equilibrium's countdown,
> delivered to the age's highest table as a trade-document — and the Council thanked the legation,
> sanded the ink, and filed it under trade. No one at the Seat asked the one question the report
> was built to raise: what a people whose whole craft is containment intends to do about the thing
> at the centre of the world, now that it needs containing and cannot be ringed. They have doubled
> their order of steel. Weigh that.*

> *Curator's note.* Asked by the Council what the numbers portended, the legation answered: "The
> numbers." The shell tablets are lodged in the Seat's archive. Their weight is eleven of their
> weights, exactly; I have come to believe that nothing they make weighs an uneven amount. — *the
> Curator*
