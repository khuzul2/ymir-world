# X · CARTOSA, MERIDIAN, AND THE SOUTHERN SEAS
## *The home waters: the Principality, the Republic, and everything a master must know south of the middle sea*

**From the home-water books of the sea-diary of Ramiro Estioca, licensed navigator of the Hermetic Order of the Blue Mantles (Navigators), master of the brigantine *La Bendata*; with excerpts from the ledgers of Anselmo Duarte, factor of the Order at Puerto Nuevo. Gathered posthumously at the Crystal Seat.**

> *Curator's note.* The circumnavigation (Chapter V) made Ramiro's name; these entries, kept
> between commissions in his last years, make his portrait. I have interleaved them with his
> factor's ledgers, which arrived in the same tin — a second voice, drier even than the first,
> that prices what the diary loves. Nothing corrects a romantic like his accountant. — *the Curator*

---

**Fourth of Boar, 3517. Home water; wind steady off the Twin Coasts; the Bendata knows the way without me.** Two islands the world calls by one name. **Cartosa**, under the **Sierra Plata**; and the sister isle under the **Sierra Grande**, where **Meridian** rides its river. Between and about them run the **Twin Coasts**, and a scatter of roads and anchorages I could con blindfolded, like my ship's namesake: Garzona, Portejo, Santa Hermana, Puerto Nuevo, La Roca, Punta del Toro where it all began. Jungle inland, dry plains south of the ranges, and weather that gives honest warning, which is more than can be said of the politics.

> *From the factor's ledger (Puerto Nuevo).* Item, refit of the *Bendata*: cordage, pitch,
> Meridian oak, 212 doubloons. Item, harbour dues, 9. Item, tariff to the Order on the ship's
> weather-work, 31 — entered under *weather*, the Order having taxed the wind these thousand years.

**Eleventh of Boar.** Landsmen think the **Principality** old as its rocks. It is a colony grown fat, and I say that with love. Letian keels under **Marco Cartaurelius** made Punta del Toro; a fort followed, then a province — **Cartoris** — renamed **Cartosa** when the captain died aboard; a governor was jumped up to **Prince** nineteen centuries after the Maelstrom, and the **Carrillo** have kept the palace since — through plague, three wars with Meridian, and once, the old men say, through a year when the sun itself turned traitor. Say what you like of the Carrillo (quietly): they have never taxed the sea more than it would bear, or not for long.

**Twentieth of Salamander. Rain; the Old Lighthouse tavern at Garzona.** Old Jacinto — eighty years old for the thirty I have known him — did his winter piece tonight: the **Year of the Black Sun**. Five hundred years gone now, and every teller's grandfather's grandfather manned a boom-chain in it. *The sun came up black as a hanged man's tongue* — Jacinto does the voice — *and hooded men came out of the cellars with the Black Sun on their breasts: the* ***Black Dawn***. Fires in the old city; chains across the harbour mouth; and young **Alberto Carrillo V**, that was after called the Steady, holding the palace stair until the Navy came up from Santa Hermana with the true dawn behind them. The coup failed; the cult burned; the boys bought Jacinto rum and slept badly. So did I, and I know it for history. Some tales keep their teeth.

> ✒ **Fernando Carrillo, Prince of Cartosa** · *3518* — From the chair, since the chronicles will not: it was Alberto V, "the Steady," who held the palace stair in the Black Sun year, not Rodriguez XIII, who reigned two centuries after and never once drew in anger — three annals confuse the pair, and all three are wrong. Alberto was the fifth of his name; Rodriguez the thirteenth Carrillo to sit the palace; they share neither a century nor a temper. I keep both their swords, and only one has ever been blooded.

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *The teeth are real. The Black Dawn was no faction of ambitious lords: it was — is — a cult of
> **Ahriman and the Sovereign Demons**, its seal the **Black Sun**, its captain in that year the
> mage **Tobias**, its true business the relics of the Adepts (a sceptre; certain maps to a golden
> city — see below). It burned the way embers burn. A Black Sun, note, is only a sun you cannot
> see shining; the cult keeps its own calendar, and five hundred years is not long on it.*

**Second of Stag. Across the strait to Meridian; wind fair, papers in order, flag polite.** Say "the enemy" in a Cartosan tavern and you mean the **Republic of Meridian**; say it aboard and you mean the weather; the weather is more sincere. An **island-republic**: the city rides natural and made islands in a great river's arms at the jungle's edge, built on water like a wager that paid. The **Public Assembly** of its noble houses governs, under a President elected each five years — figurehead or tyrant according to the man — and the **Wardens** guard the Assembly in parade armour and house-masked helms. Its wealth is **Urakron**, the enchanted wood of the adjacent jungle (my own ship's knees are Urakron); its pride is its artists; its memory is the **Wave Wars**. The **Old Port** keeps the scars of our fleet's work; the **New Port** stands on two razed quarters, one of them the Lighthouse of the **Vallerini** — the house that sold its own city to Cartosa in the second war and was paid with a governorship. Three wars in all: Meridian won the first, lost the second, and half-won the third, which leaves the score at the most dangerous number a rivalry can stand at. I love the place the way you love a rival: exactly, and armed.

> *Curator's note.* The reader will find charts, and one persistent gazetteer, placing Meridian
> inland. It is an island-republic, as the fathoms and the customs-receipts agree. — *the Curator*

> *Factor's ledger.* Item, Urakron, quarter-load, against a letter of credit on the Mercantile
> Society of Meridian, 480 doubloons; discounted 8 in the hundred at Cartosa, *the ink being
> Meridianese*.

**Ninth of Wyvern.** For the young master keeping his first log, the order of things ashore. The **Navy** is the Principality's pride and its true crown; the **Admiral** outweighs most ministers, and within the Navy ride the **Sea Dragons** — four squads of fifty out of Santa Hermana, under their Grand Dragons and the **Elder Dragon**, who is by tradition the Admiral himself. Sea-saints with sabres; salute them. The **Gold Guard** is the land army and the peacetime watch, thirteen ranks of gold braid the nobles sneer at — and some of that braid answers to the Shadows, mind. The **Shield Company** are the mercenaries the Guard hires when the work turns real. My own paymasters, the **Blue Mantles**: the mages' guild of the realm, wed to the Navy by old treaty, three factions under one blue cloak — the **Navigators**, who move ships, weather, money, and me; the **Cerulean Eye**, diviners and letter-readers, the Prince's secret police in all but invoice; and the **Drowners**, battle-mages whose name is the only honest advertising in the Principality. Every essence-worker in the realm registers with the Order and pays dues, or is outlaw — priests and nobles exempt, naturally; the Order's genius was making wizardry a tariff. I pay mine each Griffon, and each Griffon I say the same word over the receipt. Below all this, the **Shadow Society** runs the under-trade — "never met them," says every man who has — and at Meridian the **Guild of Singers** licenses even the singing of spells; so between the Mantles and the Singers, the only free magic in the south is a mother's blessing, and I expect a stamp for that any year now.

> ✒ **a soldato of the Gold Guard, seventh rank** · *3522* — He calls our braid the sort the nobles sneer at, and he is not wrong; I have stood a hundred night-watches on that same palace stair and never met a noble on it after dark. Somebody keeps the peace while the Sea Dragons collect the songs, on thin pay and a cold stone stair, and it is us. Salute the Dragons; but nod to the Guard, for we are the ones awake.

> stock its alchemists breed by a rite they do not publish — the Order's own licensed man writes
> nothing, and the huntsman of Chapter XIV, who is nobody's man, writes all a reader needs.

**Sixteenth of Wyvern.** The **nine houses**, rated as a pilot rates anchorages. **Carrillo** — the palace; deep water, mind the guns. **Contreras** — the Admiralty; a Navy house, promptest pay in the realm. **Vidal** — Navy also, smaller, fair. **Escorsa** — heads the Blue Mantles always; my owners' owners; polite, and reads your mail. **Cadenas** — rich as a reef is sharp; they keep an Archmage in the family the way others keep hounds. **Vallerini** — turncoats ennobled; Merchant Guild; the Meridian governorship, such as remains of it; charming; count your fingers after. **Escolar** — merchant-risen, feuding with Contreras, and their manifests and their cargoes are cousins at best; the Shadows have hooks in that house. Sail for them once, cash, no signature. **Stuba** — disgraced in a coup plot; bad credit, worse friends. And **Gibraleon** — I will write it where their proctors cannot reach: rumour gives them necromancy and demon-traffic, and rumour has been right about smaller things. **No Gibraleon charter.** I have refused three. The *Bendata* floats on my superstitions.

**Fifth of Serpent. Watering up the Rio Tapajero; boats out, muskets dry.** The interior is not empty; it is patient. The **Nkishi** — the wild orcs — let beasts bite them from boyhood and wear the scars as we wear medals; brave past sense; their **Bokkor** shamans keep the ancestor-spirits and the dark forest gods, the **Orisha**, of whom the **Mayombe** are the ones you pray not to interest. Trade steel and rum at the bank; keep the boats afloat. The **M'Bantha** goblins stole my second-best kettle at the third bend, as they have stolen something on every watering these thirty years: they hold that our power lives *in our things* — "**Cargo**," the traders call the creed — and go decked in buttons, spoons, and one bosun's whistle I still miss. Cunning as bilge-rats, and not stupid; ask the punitive expeditions that never came back. In the highlands sit the **Arawak**: dwarves who work stone and bone and will not touch metal, gems braided into their hair to count their deeds, and death preferred to capture — remember that before you play at press-gangs. Their mountains hold **Shald**, the magic glass worth more than emerald, and the uninvited expeditions that go up after it feed the mountains. Last, the **Ur-Tanoth**, shy as smoke: the finest woodwork, herbs, and furs on Ymir, but they sicken and die if caged, so the trade runs through the **Tal-Tanoth** — the little cat-folk, who take a broker's tenth and your heart. Sell to all; cheat none; anchor mid-stream.

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *Deep in that same jungle stands the tale the whole south tells and no one prices honestly:
> **Cumbre de Oro**, the golden city — truly **Taremorn**, a rich city of the Adepts' empire,
> raised on an Earthnode now curdled. In the Demon War's first days its king was tricked by
> **Ismael**, a Sovereign Demon, and a Demon Prince cursed the city for its greed: the gold is
> real, and whoever takes it joins the garrison — a soulless skeleton bound inside those sad
> walls. The Black Dawn went looking for it in the Black Sun year. That is the measure of them:
> they knew what the gold costs, and went anyway.*

**Eleventh of Hawk. The pearl-mark off Costa Sileña; sea like poured glass.** Laid the *Bendata* at the reef-mark, hoisted the shell-sign, and by the tide's turn they came up: the **Sohleugir**, the true lords of the southern seas, older than every flag in this book. Their kingdom is below — **Ssas'Kher**, whose coral walls have never been breached, not in millennia, not by anything. They surface to trade **pearls and coral** for worked steel and glass: few words, exact weights, and no haggling, because the sea does not haggle. I have dealt with them man and boy and been cheated never, which I can say of no port with a customs-house. Take off your hat to them. It costs nothing, and they notice.

> ✒ **Ximena, pearl-diver of Costa Sileña** · *3523* — I have gone down at this mark since I was nine, and the master speaks true: take off your hat. Do more — never bring up a lightless pearl from the south ground, whatever a factor bids for it, for the deep lords count those, and a diver who lands one comes up wrong or does not come up. My aunt went down for one; we do not say her name at the reef.

> *Factor's ledger.* Item, pearls of Ssas'Kher, two strings, 620 doubloons. Item, rose-branch
> coral, 75. Nota: the master again refused to anchor over the drowned ground south of the mark,
> which the men call the Oyster's water; the delay is charged to piety.

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *Under the Oyster's water lies what is left of the **Cult of the Blue Oyster**: the drowned
> shrine where the Sovereign Demon **Ea**, Lord of the Abysses, was called up — and unmade, the
> first of the Sovereigns ever slain. What the cult left below: a shell-temple cracked open like
> its namesake, and the **Blue Oyster Shadows** — the cultists' shades, still keeping a service
> their god cannot hear. The pearls of that ground grow large and lightless, and should not be
> brought up. The Sohleugir do not breach such places; they **contain** them. Their patience with
> Cartosan anchors ends at that mark, and the master's piety kept a peace he never knew was in
> question.*

**Third of Spider.** The ship's chest, southern issue, for masters new to these waters. **Utombe**, a jungle-leaf distillate: kills pain outright — a man will fight on past a wound that should have him screaming, and not know he is dying; issue it, and count the vials. **Qarando** root: fire and gladness in the blood; boarding-courage in a chew — and your best hand diving over the rail after a fleeing prize like a fool. I ration it to fights I mean to win anyway. **Subotan**, the violet poppy: bliss, torpor, visions, then need; illegal in the Principality this long age and smuggled every year of it; the margins are handsome and the Navy hangs you handsomely — choose. **Rud-Tekma** berries the wizards chew: spells fly truer, they say, while the chewer sits entranced like a saint on a shelf — which is why my weather-worker chews nothing during weather. As for beasts: the **Daretas**, six-legged hill-oxen in armour plate, tender eating and hide for shields; the **Reptoids**, horse-lizards that furnish mounts and most of the realm's leather, my sea-boots included; and the **Yarhim**, the great blue crabs, platter-sized to nine metres across — Maelstrom-marked beasts, whose shell makes armour kings feud over, and whose taking makes widows.

**Nineteenth of Spider.** Last, the blood of it all: **money**. The south runs on the **doubloon** and the **piece-of-eight**, and above a chest's weight, on **letters of credit** of the merchant guilds, good at a shave from Armora's counting-houses to the eastern banners. Prize-money for lawful war; tariffs on everything, including, as noted, the wind. Beyond home waters every land coins its own — church coin in Letia, marks and scrip in Armora, strung cash and jade in the east, hacksilver north, salt and coral in the Sadhir; and in the Gaerwath they pay in **Arvo**, powdered Essence, and in bound devils, and a master who accepts either deserves the change. Between realms the trade rides on metal by weight, on Shald and mana-salt, and on paper and trust — and paper is only trust with a seal on it. So is a ship's log. So, I suppose, is this.

> ✒ **The Armora Crier** *(pasted clipping)* · *3531* — SOUTHERN EXCHANGE: the Cartosans still reckon in doubloons and pieces-of-eight, bless their romance, but the smart money crosses the middle sea in honest Armorite marks — and the fish-lords of the deep, we are told, have just doubled their standing order for our steel. Even the sea-bottom banks with Armora. Terms at par, whatever they tell you in the Merchant District.
