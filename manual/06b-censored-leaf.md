# A LEAF PASSED BY THE CENSORS OF SOLANTHYA
## *an unnumbered facsimile, bound between the sixth and seventh chapters*

**An extract of the rolls of the Clericate of Mithra, fair-copied at the archive-towers of
Solanthya, examined and passed by the Office of the Censors of the holy city, and bound here in
facsimile — the censors' strokes preserved as they fell.**

> *Curator's note.* When I pressed the Annalist on his chapter, he replied — with a pride I found
> touching — that the rolls of Solanthya stand open to any honest scholar. I applied; the Clericate
> obliged; and in due season came this extract, sealed, and certified *complete* by its covering
> letter. The reader may judge. I have bound it in facsimile rather than in copy, for the strokes
> are the most instructive writing on it; I did not try to read beneath them — discourteous, and,
> as the reader will see, unnecessary. The censors of Solanthya are thorough. Their jurisdiction,
> like all jurisdiction, ends at the edge of the text. — *the Curator*

Brother Ansever of the Lower March, inquisitor: commissioned, served, laid down his office with
honour. His name endures on the roll.

Brother Coltrame of the northern march, inquisitor: commissioned, served, died in the faith at his
post. His name endures on the roll.

Brother ~~████████ ██████~~, inquisitor: commissioned; ~~twice commended by the synod for zeal in
examination~~; ~~posted to the eastern marches, where the roads of the old world run out~~.
~~His name endures on the roll.~~

**§ From the acts of a synod at Solanthya, held in the year ~~████~~.**

In the matter of the man ~~████████ ██████~~, sometime brother of this Church and inquisitor of
the Holy Office: the synod, having heard what may not be repeated and read what may not be copied,
adjudges as follows. ~~That he fell from the faith he was sworn to defend into the practice of the
arts he was sworn to burn.~~ ~~That, sought by the correction of his brothers, he fled beyond the
Empire's gates and beyond its mercy.~~ ~~That of what he wrought thereafter, and of the gate in
the east, and of the sign in the sky, this synod, having heard testimony, resolves to hear no
more.~~ Wherefore the name ~~████████ ██████~~ is stricken from the rolls of the Clericate, his
commissions annulled, his examinations void, and his memory placed beneath the stroke; and where
chronicle or charter cannot go forward without him, let it write *a nameless heresiarch*, and go
forward.

**§ The censors' warrant.**

Examined against the rolls and passed for release. What stands may be copied. What is stricken is
stricken: let the copyist not enquire after the strokes, for such enquiry is itself matter for
examination. Given under the seal of the Office of the Censors of Solanthya.

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *The name beneath the strokes is **Heinrich Kramer** — the same name all three times, and the
> censors of Solanthya are diligent, which is a kind of confession: one stroke says* error,
> *three identical strokes say* memory. *They struck even the synod's year, so no reader may set
> it beside 3026 and count. For the man himself — inquisitor, twice commended; the fall, the
> flight, the lich at the ancient gate of Ulan-Tang, the red moon his own Church read for an omen
> — see the margin of the sixth chapter; the dead do not improve with repetition. Attend to what
> the leaf shows. To strike a man from a roll they must first find him on it. To decree him
> nameless they must name him in the decree — then strike the decree's own sentence, a Church
> biting its tongue in the middle of the word. And nothing was burned: Solanthya burns nothing;
> ask where the unstruck copies sleep. Now hold the page at arm's length. Every bright leaf in
> this book has passed some office of censors — a church's, a crown's, a narrator's own careful
> heart. This is only the one where the strokes were left showing. They struck the name from the
> rolls, from the acts, from the friar's whole loving chapter. They could not strike it from the
> margin. Nothing strikes the margin. Now you know what the margins are for.*

*Colophon.* Unnumbered facsimile leaf, bound between Chapters VI and VII. Drawn from
`canon/characters.md` (Heinrich Kramer — Mithran inquisitor turned lich; the Ulan-Tang portal,
3026 d.M.) and `canon/factions.md` (the Clericate of Mithra and its command of Letia; Kramer a
*former* inquisitor of the Church). **The erasure itself is canon**: the name struck from the
rolls of the Clericate (the Chapter VI margin) and the chronicles' "nameless heresiarch" formula
(Chapter IV). **[INVENTED]**: the facsimile conceit entire — the Office of the Censors, the
released extract, the roll's furniture and its two intact brothers, the synod-act's wording
(including the struck clause on the gate and the sign in the sky, consistent with the Chapter VI
margin's judgment that the Church's ignorance is feigned), and the censors' warrant. No new lore:
the margin restores only what Chapters IV, VI, and XV already carry.
