# XV·B · THE VEILED HAND'S WORKBOOK
## *seeds from the margins, cut for planting*

> ⟡ **GM ONLY. As the chapter before it, and more so: that one told the truth, this one puts it
> to work. If you are a player and you are reading this line, you are stealing tools. Put them
> down.** ⟡

**Set down by the hand that wrote the margins, for use; sewn in behind the fifteenth chapter,
where the Curator will not look. He unsewed one quire of mine, found the paper watching him, and
resolved to be brave about it exactly once.**

---

You are back, and this time you want tools. Good. The fifteenth chapter told you what the world
is; it did not tell you what your table does with it on the next long evening. This is that book:
my workbook, the margins of fifteen chapters shaken out, sorted by ground, and sharpened.

I wrote *I do not tie ends. I point at them*, and I stand by it. But a pointed end no table ever
pulls is mystery wasted, and waste offends me more than blasphemy does. So each seed below is four
motions and no more: **situation** — what is true when the players arrive; **faces** — throats to
speak with; **stakes** — what the pull is worth; **complication** — the tooth in the fruit. Where
the chronicles keep no name I have supplied one. Do not mistake my naming for canon; the colophon
confesses everything, in one place, where confession belongs.

The year, unless a seed says otherwise, is **~3518** — the Equilibrium, the eye of the storm you
were warned about. But the whole line is playable, and there are five doors into it: **the Burning
Days** (the Horde in the east, the secessions, Saxul over the broken land); **the eve of the Tide,
3015–25** (wolves, waves, machines — the cascade); **the Tide itself, 3025–27** (the key turning);
**the New Era of Chaos, 3026–3260** (red moon, open gates, walking dead); and **the New
Equilibrium, ~3518**. Seeds tagged for another frame play best there. The untagged assume the
calm — and you remember what the calm is made of.

---

### 1 · Letia & Solanthya

The most watchful state on Ymir, blind in exactly the places it kneels.

**The sealed galleries.** *Situation:* since the Chaos, the dwarves of Naughrith keep their
deepest galleries sealed and say nothing — not to Solanthya, not to their own young — while their
kin in the white peaks pray upward with unusual fervour; and this year the mountain's grain-orders
have quietly doubled. Fed garrisons grow. So do fed prisoners. *Faces:* a Naughrith door-warden
who hires outsiders because outsiders can be made to forget; the Solanthya quartermaster-priest
who noticed the invoices; whatever answers the prayers, or does not. *Stakes:* what the mountain
always held, or what slipped the cracked prison — no one who went down has come up to say.
*Complication:* the seals are dwarven and recent. The locks beneath them are neither, and were
built to open from the other side.

**The crypt of heresies.** *Situation:* the high Clericate does not burn forbidden books; it
curates them, in crypts under Solanthya. One folio has left the crypt — the **Vlatch** dossier,
the one on the art of time — and the archive wants it back without admitting the archive exists.
*Faces:* the crypt-archivist, who has read everything and believes none of it, which is how the
Church keeps him sane; the Inclusivist synodal who authorised the "loan"; the buyer, whose receipt
is signed in a script that troubles the eye. *Stakes:* Chronomancy is the one art I decline to
explain even here. *Complication:* the Vlatch are said to notice their readers — later, and out
of order.

**The struck name.** *Situation:* Kramer's name was struck from the rolls of the Clericate, but
strike-marks are ink too, and a notary of the rolls has found the counter-file: the Church knew
what its runaway hound was building at Ulan-Tang before the red moon rose. *Faces:* the notary,
honest and therefore in danger; the Annalist of Solanthya, who calls Kramer "a nameless
heresiarch" and may or may not know he is lying; the censors' office, which works nights.
*Stakes:* whether the world's most sincere theocracy survives its own filing system.
*Complication:* someone above the censors wants the file found. Ask why before you deliver it.

**The empty throne** *(era: the Burning Days' last hour, 2563–2610)*. *Situation:* Saxul is dead
over the broken land and Valdis Nemertine dead with him; the throne of Letia stands empty;
forty-seven years of regents and marcher-lords bleed the provinces while the Clericate learns what
a vacuum is for. *Faces:* a regent's tax-captain; a martyr-cult of Valdis growing faster than the
Church likes; a young cleric drafting, in perfect sincerity, the theology of the coming seizure.
*Stakes:* who holds Letia — 2610 answered it one way; your table may answer otherwise.
*Complication:* Helgedad is halfway out the door, and the Horde can smell blood from the east.

### 2 · The Young Kingdoms — Armora and her belt

Better-lit than anywhere on the map, which is how you hide things in plain dark.

**The engine never found.** *Situation:* scrap-miners under the Frozen Gorge have raised a machine
the Guild never licensed, and a collector is paying over the odds, in clean scrip, no questions
returned. *Faces:* a Guild assayer who wants it melted before it is understood; an S.C.M. captain
whose orders did not come through the S.C.M.; the collector. *Stakes:* the Resurrection Engine
drank the blood and souls of the living to wake the dead, and it woke the vampire Leonard after
his destruction. It was never found. It was never destroyed. *Complication:* consider on whose
behalf a collector buys a machine like that — and whether the machine, which has waited five
centuries to be found, is being bought or is doing the buying.

**The annual denial.** *Situation:* the Palace's yearly denial that a Rune of Power sleeps beneath
Armora was issued two weeks early, because the lamps of the Industrial District have begun to
flicker in unison. Something else is drawing on the sleeping fire. *Faces:* the Grand Duchess's
permit-mistress, who signs the denial and keeps the real ledger; a College measurer whose
Essence-meter rings at nothing visible; whatever is on the far end of the draw. *Stakes:* a city
built over one of the four anti-demon engines learns what else is plugged in. *Complication:* the
draw traces to dead Waldenheim — the other buried Rune — which means someone has reached it
through a kingdom that no longer exists to object.

**The politest neighbours.** *Situation:* the changed dwarves of the Wald — the folk who went
below when the dragon broke their halls, and came back up seeing in the dark — have asked, for the
first time in five centuries, for outside help: an escort *down*. *Faces:* a Wald door-opener
whose eyes take lamplight the way a cat's do; a mountain-kin envoy who calls the Wald-folk nothing
at all, which is dwarvish for grief; what they left below. *Stakes:* whatever the generations of
dark made of them, they made terms with it too. *Complication:* the escort is not protection. The
terms require witnesses, and the Wald has run out of its own.

**The watchdog's war** *(era: the Tide, 3025–27)*. *Situation:* the War of the Machines, played
straight: Gwalgamaur the Golden — Warden of Quadrant 74 in his makers' ledger — wakes to a duty
five ages stale and comes to secure what the city denies possessing. *Faces:* the YonkerMecha line
before the gates; the Silver Arrows, who will not come back from the Rhems Wood; the hidden
Technocrat in his workshops, binding demons into steel. *Stakes:* the two buried Runes, which the
dragon knows are there and the defenders do not. *Complication:* your players fight the watchdog
of the house they are standing in — while Meridian and Helgedad burn in the same years, and no one
connects the fires.

### 3 · Helgedad & the north

Cold keeps things. That is what cold is for.

**The coils.** *Situation:* a miner has come up out of the Aestyr diggings knowing no name but
one, and it is not his. The Marcher houses want the shaft collapsed; the Order of the White
Mountain wants to go down. *Faces:* the returned miner; a White Mountain knight who has read the
old charters and is right about all of it; five half-forgotten generals, still in the maze, still
obedient, still searching. *Stakes:* the labyrinth holds the Lord of Oblivion by forgetting — a
prison whose walls are your memory of the way out. *Complication:* every accurate map of the coils
thins them. The party's map is the breach.

**The thaw.** *Situation:* the Frozen Waste's edge has retreated a league in ten years, and green
things grow among the black-stone ruins of the watch-city. Helgedad celebrates the pasture. Do not
let them. *Faces:* a Vorskgen guide who will take you to the glass streets and no farther; an
Ognir factor selling reclaimed grazing; Nihoggr, the Ice Dragon, whose sleeping breath the cold
is. *Stakes:* the Frozen Hell is literal, and warmth is the gauge on the dragon's sleep.
*Complication:* the prisoner in the Aestyr sought to wake this thing once, and his will still
seeps out of the maze in dreams. The thaw has an author.

**The wolf-winter** *(era: the eve of the Tide, 3015–25)*. *Situation:* Nortislong falls in the
Siege of One Day, and the war is not a conquest — it is an excavation with a terror for a spade,
its trenches all in the wrong places for a war. *Faces:* the five dark generals, still fleshed;
Marcher levies who know the ground; whoever first notices what the digging is toward. *Stakes:*
the deep roads to the maze's heart, or the dragon's grave. *Complication:* the employer forgets
its own instruments. Victory in this campaign looks like vanishing, and the generals already have.

### 4 · Qi-Long

Six certainties, none sufficient. Work the gaps between them.

**The courier-meter.** *Situation:* the ninth chapter of this very Compendium scans, maxim for
maxim, in the courier-meter of the Master-Singers — the verse-code of the Golden Falcons. In six
kingdoms the book you are holding is a resistance dispatch in Council binding. Decide what it
says. *Faces:* a Master-Singer who will neither confirm nor teach; a Golden Pavilion fence selling
"annotated" copies; the Pilgrim himself, still walking, still remembering everything. *Stakes:* a
muster, a map, or one name. *Complication:* six Dragon-God courts keep codebreakers too, and
possession of the book is confession enough.

**The mongooses' guest.** *Situation:* the White Mongooses, who serve a Dragon-God "not yet of
this world," have stopped waiting: they are paying, quietly and in relics, for passage-rights on
the True Ways. Fetching has begun. *Faces:* a Mongoose abbess of perfect and terrifying serenity;
a Xebechani way-keeper who took the relics and has begun to regret the arithmetic; a Golden Falcon
with the sanest idea in the east — *stop them*. *Stakes:* which arrival — the absent warden,
Ouroboros, or two names for one appetite. *Complication:* the last time something of that order
moved, Alfheim burned in a season.

**The waking of the six** *(era: the Burning Days, ~1900–1920)*. *Situation:* Mah-Dhol comes
down, and six wardens rise from posts five ages old to take its provinces. Play the generation
that watched gods replace an emperor. *Faces:* the last imperial chancellery, filing the end of
the world in triplicate; the first resistance cells choosing their animal names; six new courts
learning to be worshipped. *Stakes:* everything the six kingdoms are was settled in twenty years.
*Complication:* the wardens did not conquer — they *resumed*. Ask what each throne stands guard
over. The sects never have.

### 5 · Cartosa & the Twin Coasts

Money remembers what churches forget. Read the ledgers.

**Lightless pearls.** *Situation:* a string of pearls too large and too dark has surfaced in a
Cartosa counting-house, which means someone anchored over the Oyster's water and dived. *Faces:*
the diver, drowning on dry land by inches; a Sohleugir factor whose famous courtesy has stopped;
the Blue Oyster Shadow that came up with the string. *Stakes:* the Sohleugir do not breach such
places — they contain them — and their patience with Cartosan anchors ends at that mark.
*Complication:* putting the pearls back means diving the drowned shrine of the first slain
Sovereign, where the congregation still keeps its service — and has lately noticed that something
answers.

**The golden garrison.** *Situation:* an expedition bankrolled by house Gibraleon is recruiting
for the jungle — and they know exactly what the gold of Taremorn costs, which is the point. They
are not buying gold. They are buying the garrison. *Faces:* the Gibraleon patron; a Black Dawn
quartermaster entered in the books as a chaplain; the cursed king Ismael tricked, still keeping
accounts inside those sad walls. *Stakes:* an army that cannot desert, march-ready since the Demon
War. *Complication:* the curse enlists recruiters too. Every soldier of that garrison signed
someone else before the walls took him.

**The Armorite's case.** *Situation:* a Yarhim-crabber's nets have brought up the sealed chart-case
of Roberto Rodrigo, "the Armorite" — the pirate the ballads refuse to let drown. *Faces:* the seer
Obnubia, who priced the case, declined to open it, and looked away first; three noble houses'
agents at one quiet auction; a ballad-seller who knows the verse everyone else sings wrong.
*Stakes:* whatever a legend thought worth a false grave. I do not know what is in the case.
Neither does Obnubia. Price accordingly. *Complication:* a man like that seals nothing without
deciding who should open it. The auction is the will being read.

**The Black Sun** *(era: the eve of the Tide, 3017)*. *Situation:* the coup year. The Black Dawn
moves through the nine houses while the Principality thinks it is fighting politics, and the
cult's true errand is the relics of the Adepts — a sceptre; certain maps to a golden city.
*Faces:* the mage Tobias, captain of the cult; a Carrillo loyalist captain; a Shadow Society
broker selling both sides the same names. *Stakes:* Cartosa — and the errand the coup was cover
for. *Complication:* the cult keeps its own calendar. The coup can fail and the errand still
succeed. History says both happened.

### 6 · The Gaerwath & the elf lands

One people, three creeds, and a door that has begun to work both ways.

**Hunt or homecoming.** *Situation:* the rumour-name **Shulma** is real and growing, and a gate
dead since the Tide now warms at the same hour of every long-year. The road to Tchyo is open, and
something on the far side is using it. *Faces:* a Light-Elf gate-warden who has waited her whole
life to say *the Messenger*; a Dark-Elf observer under orders to let anything through — either
outcome vindicates the creed; a Renouncer who came to watch her kin be wrong, whichever way it
falls. *Stakes:* the direction of travel. The elves were *hunted* off Tchyo, and a Messenger is
not the only thing that follows a fleeing people's trail once the road reopens. *Complication:*
all three creeds will act before anyone knows. Faith is faster than evidence, and better armed.

**The Eternal's arithmetic.** *Situation:* Morannagul has not aged since 2550; every Gaerwath
child pays a trial that takes from the failed a terrible cost; and no record names where the
subtraction goes. A Nemyel escape-line wants its ledger carried out — years of names, ages, and
what each failure lost. *Faces:* a Nemyel Arvo-scraper with the ledger sewn into her coat; Pips
Quondam, who has run this cargo before and charged nothing, once; a Nath auditor whose office
exists so that no such ledger does. *Stakes:* the mechanism of a throne's eternity. *Complication:*
publishing the arithmetic does not free the Nemyel. It prices them — and the Eternal has never
once been asked to his face. Ask.

**The scaffolds of Alfheim.** *Situation:* Ouroboros unmade Ost-en-Galad because something there
satisfied a condition, and no one on Ymir has answered what. Five centuries on, the Light Elves
rebuild under a queen of Inanna's line — and the works have reached the height at which the old
vigil, the beacon that called *Home? Home?* down the reopened ways, resumes. *Faces:* the queen,
replanting a splinter and calling it the promise; a Grey-elf surveyor who has done the terrible
sum; the warden overhead, who does not rage, only responds. *Stakes:* whether the condition is
about to be satisfied twice. *Complication:* to stop the beacon is to break the creed of everyone
building it. To let it stand is to learn the trigger by demonstration.

### 7 · The Sadhir deserts

The desert does not bargain. It files.

**The oracle that asks for you.** *Situation:* the Oracle of the Brine Veil has always taken
questions. Lately it gives answers unasked, addressed by name to travellers three dunes away. The
Pearl's congregation is recruiting on a schedule. *Faces:* a Narazeen wheel-warden who burns what
surfaces; a Nashifet relic-hunter who has sold the Oracle's location twice, to two buyers, both
alive; the Oracle, mouthpiece of a shape slowly filling. *Stakes:* the Deep Pearl is gathering
what the Abyss remembers of its Prince, and the Salt-Drowned are enrolled, not born.
*Complication:* the Sarradhi cure is patience — give it nothing. Your players are, professionally,
the something.

**The arrears of Khorish.** *Situation:* the Eternal Pharaohs bought their eternity as a tenancy
from Netunshiel, Lord of Crypts, with the corvée for rent; the creditor died at the Tide; the
contract, demon-drafted and therefore flawless, does not lapse. Now a claimant has presented
itself at court with the deed. *Faces:* Athmokethepek I, who declined to die and is learning what
he consented to instead; Nefthi, who read the contract; the claimant — heir, forger, or collector
for something worse than either. *Stakes:* every soul that labours dead in Khorish is an asset in
the transfer. *Complication:* disproving the claim requires the original, which is filed in the
estate of a dead Sovereign, in the Abyss.

**The two doors.** *Situation:* the salt will not release Al-Sidath and Al-Jabur; the desert is
holding its two oldest doors shut. Old sultans' coin — and older oaths — under the counting-tables
of Tuleph are financing a dig anyway. *Faces:* a hermit-crab guide who remembers which kingdom was
hers first; a Zalim'un coral-steward refusing every permit; whoever underwrote the older oath.
*Stakes:* what the drowned island-kingdoms were sealed against. Or sealed *with*. *Complication:*
everything the desert takes, it keeps, and offers back at a price, in the voice of something you
loved. The lore-keeper wrote that in the twelfth chapter. She was not being lyrical.

### THE DOUBLE LEAF · ULAN-TANG

The Curator's reviewers refused this town a chapter, and they were right: readers do not need it.
You do. So it gets my two pages instead.

The facts, dry. A city-state on the river **Tang**, which was sacred to **Ishtar** — honest
ledgers still carry contracts sworn in her name, older than the spider and quietly kept. The
sorcerers of **Yogh** took the city; the spider-god is no local upstart but an old, real,
cross-cultural power — the Xebechani feed the same god under the same name — and his High Priest,
**Sazar-Gadash**, demon-son of Asmodeus, rules by right of having murdered his predecessor
Azim-Mulath. There are no city guards. There are the **five houses** and their private knives:
**Gadash** in violet, the priest's own; **Ishra** in red; **Tyarreth** in olive; **Miraz** in
blue; **Gaib** in black, who keep the seers. There is the **Black Lotus**, which pays for
everything; the **Night Market**, where everything else is paid for; and behind it all the
**Azar-Kalish**, the Conclave of Shadows, whose sign is the spider and whose invoices are settled
in lives. The great spider **Adhal-Kibreeshi**, the Whispering Lady, is nearer the god's true
avatar than his priest will ever be. And the reason any of this matters: Ulan-Tang stands on an
ancient portal, and in 3026 the lich that had been the inquisitor **Kramer** opened it, and the
red moon rose. Yogh keeps his web on a door.

**Kramer's legacy.** *Situation:* the lich's workings around the gate were never dismantled —
house Gadash wards them, taxes them, and understands none of them; and lately every instrument in
the scaffold has turned, all together, toward the quarter of sky where the red moon *receded*.
*Faces:* Sazar-Gadash, who will sell access twice; a Cult of the Stars observer who came to kneel
and stayed to measure; a Brotherhood mage-hunter very far off her map. *Stakes:* a gate that has
admitted a god's attention once. The working is not a summons; it is an appointment.
*Complication:* tearing it down may be worse. Kramer built *around* something already there — the
door is the ancients', not the lich's, and doors do not mourn their locksmiths.

**The night market.** *Situation:* what cannot be bought anywhere is priced here nightly — and
when your table loses track of anything this folio trades in, a lightless pearl, a crystal tear,
a scroll that should be under Xanadu, this is where it resurfaces, under lamps, with a lot-number.
*Faces:* a Miraz auctioneer masked in blue; an Azar-Kalish under-broker who settles disputes
permanently; the consignor, never present, always punctual. *Stakes:* whatever you let leave the
tent. *Complication:* all sales are final in the fullest sense. The Market's guarantee is the
spider's, and refunds are collected in lives.

**Five houses, one web.** *Situation:* Sazar-Gadash's title rests on the murder of his
predecessor, and that right is transferable; one of the four other houses intends to transfer it.
*Faces:* an Ishra knife-mistress with the votes of three kitchens; **Faazi-Ghaib**, house Gaib's
seer, selling every faction the same true dream; the Whispering Lady, who does not care who is
priest. *Stakes:* the Lotus trade, the Night Market, and custody of the god-touched gate.
*Complication:* Yogh has presided over both sides of every succession the city remembers. The
winner inherits the web with the spider still in it.

**Under the red moon** *(era: the New Era of Chaos, 3026–3260)*. *Situation:* Ulan-Tang in the
years the Gaze hung overhead — the gate still warm, the Xebechani arriving to kneel, doom-cults
and pilgrims and profiteers, a city learning to tax an apocalypse. *Faces:* the first Gadash
gate-wardens, writing the tariff schedule for the end of the world; a star-priest who has waited
its whole long life to be right; Kramer's leavings, still cooling. *Stakes:* the city that owned
the crack in the sky. *Complication:* for two centuries everything sold in the Night Market
passed under the Eye. Decide what looked back — and what it marked for later.

### 8 · Ophiur

The morning country. The oldest bones are the least buried here.

**The honest map.** *Situation:* no honest map of Gazal's undercity exists — until now. A survivor
has come up with one that is *accurate*, which is worse: it marks the tribute-roads, and the
throne at the bottom that something out of the prison-crack sat itself on after the Tide. *Faces:*
the mapmaker, who will sell the going but not go; a Zabhula factor pricing vaults by the fathom;
the taker of tribute below, which has begun issuing receipts. *Stakes:* the free city is free
because the masters below are paid. *Complication:* publish the map and the tribute-peace ends;
suppress it and you have decided, alone, that the arrangement should stand.

**The kept garden.** *Situation:* someone at your table's edge is dying of something nothing
cures — a Marked child, a Pool-sick Xebechani envoy, your campaign's own casualty — and the Garden
of Xiombarg cures *anything*. The songs are specific. *Faces:* a map-seller preparing a
comfortable retirement; an Ur-Tanoth guide who will walk to the tree-line and not one step past
it; the guardians, older than the walls they ward. *Stakes:* the cure is real. *Complication:* a
garden is where you keep what you mean to plant again. The guardians do not stop thieves so much
as select stock — and the price of leaving may be to carry a seed out, and plant it, and not ask
what grows.

**The Storm turns.** *Situation:* the Ur of Tarkur follow the Storm across the savanna, and their
Seekers scent the Essence raw — the one faith on Ymir that was never lied to. The Storm has
stopped migrating. It is circling a point in the deep interior, and the kingdom is turning with
it. *Faces:* a Seeker who will not name what she scents; Nyema of Ga'Namesh, writing down the
songs as they change; the conclave of Horatli, which bought up every old migration-chart this
year and sold nothing back. *Stakes:* the currents shifted when the seals broke, and the honest
compass is pointing. *Complication:* the dragon-wardens circle the same interior and do not land.
Two watches over one point. Decide what has earned both.

### 9 · The seas & the isles

Water is the world's honest margin. Read what washes up.

**The failing rhythm.** *Situation:* the Sohleugir sound the deep water and do not exaggerate,
and their soundings say the Maelstrom's rhythm is faltering. This year's report never reached
Elendor. *Faces:* a veiled Sohleugir courier-factor whose patience is thinning; the Crystal Seat
clerk who noticed the gap in the series; three buyers who each want the number to prove a
different comfort. *Stakes:* the storm is scar and seal both — the world's doomsday gauge.
*Complication:* the report was not stolen for the number. It was stolen for the trend-line.
Someone wants to know *when*.

**The veil of Kyria.** *Situation:* the mist reads sleepers, and those it wants, it keeps; the
refugees in Thonis did not come by ship. A family with money wants a kept one back. *Faces:* the
Thonis harbourmaster, who keeps a list of arrivals and no ledger of departures; an Ur-Tanoth
islander who pities the mainland on principle; the kept one — safe, dreaming, and not of the
opinion that she is kept. *Stakes:* whatever the island is being kept asleep *for*, untouched by
the Catastrophe and intended to stay so. *Complication:* the only way in is to be wanted. Become
the kind of sleeper the veil keeps — then, being kept, leave.

**The unmapped continent.** *Situation:* the Isle of Demons has stood five hundred years without
a map and without growing, which is the worst news patience can send. A chartered expedition —
Council-flagged, or privately mad — means to fix a coastline at last. *Faces:* an Armorite
aeronaut with a barometric theory about the bruised sky; a Cartosan master who will sail to the
horizon of it and not one league closer; whichever of tyranny, plots, and illusions notices first.
*Stakes:* the first survey of a patient neighbour. *Complication:* it is not a gap in the book.
It is a door — and a survey party is how a door learns who knocks.

**The sewn chapter.** *Situation:* the Curator wrote that he chose not to pursue the matter of the
fifteenth chapter's binding. He has pursued it since, through three respectable intermediaries,
and the third is hiring. *Faces:* the Curator, wry, exact, and frightened in a scholarly way; a
Tylweth Teg paper-wright who found the watermark and washed her hands twice — her people hate
everything the Maelstrom has touched, and she cannot say why this is worse; me. *Stakes:* you have
read the chapter; you know. *Complication:* I will grade the attempt.

### 10 · The Dreamlands, & elsewhere

Nothing in the calm forbids dreaming. It is the one border the Equilibrium never closed.

**The permanent situation.** *Situation:* sleepers from Armora's licensed parlours report a
courteous gentleman in yellow who keeps a gate and offers permanent employment. The offers are
sincere. One husband has said yes. *Faces:* Topramesk the Yellow, Lord of the Void, three and a
half thousand years into his watch on the dream-road through Ragash-below toward steam-wreathed
Arcadia; the new employee, promoted quickly; the licensed dream-pilot hired to fetch him home.
*Stakes:* the gatekeeper is staffing up a watch. Decide whether it is *against* something or *for*
something, because he already has. *Complication:* the wages are real, the work is real, the door
does need watching — and the employee does not want rescuing.

**The palace that unlocks.** *Situation:* the archmage Mubafer sits in a prison built as a palace,
sealed until he forgets the two loves he betrayed, and he has been forgetting for a very long
time. A client of the Ulan-Tang seers wants a thing only Mubafer still remembers. *Faces:*
Faazi-Ghaib, selling the crossing — this folio's roads keep meeting, and that is the roads'
doing, not mine; Mubafer, courteous and diminishing; the two loves, whose names are the lock.
*Stakes:* the memory your client is buying may be one of the two the seal is counting.
*Complication:* remind him, and the sentence resets with witnesses inside. Let him forget, and the
palace unlocks around something that no longer knows why it should not leave.

**The weeping engine.** *Situation:* on the deeper shores stands a garden with a mechanical woman
at its centre who weeps tears of crystal and calls herself Vespertina — and her tears have begun
appearing in the waking market, lot-numbered, under lamps. *Faces:* the weeping one — echo,
archive, or the Peacemaker's actual sleep; an Armora palace under-secretary paying handsomely for
the subject to be changed; the harvester. *Stakes:* what a peacemaker's dream is maintaining. Her
peace, perhaps. Literally. *Complication:* every tear taken wakes her a little. Decide what the
peace does when its maintainer stops dreaming it.

**The axe on the mountain.** *Situation:* Tofango's body was found; **Oblivion** was not. On some
peak — I do not know which, and neither does anyone, so choose the one your map can spare — a
demon of rank sits imbued in a blade, patient, five centuries into practising how to sound
reasonable. *Faces:* a Brotherhood mage-hunter carrying Tears of Davedars and orders to burn any
hand that lifts it; a shepherd village that stopped going up the mountain a generation ago and no
longer remembers deciding; the demon, which remembers everything. *Stakes:* the artefact that ate
the mind of the age's greatest axeman. *Complication:* the axe does not need to be swung. It needs
to be *carried down*. It will pay in victories, and the first several are genuine.

**The wrong door.** *Situation:* the True Ways go only where the ancients wanted to go — until a
revived way misdelivers, and travellers step out beneath a sky no map of Ymir owns: the quarantine
line of **Zend**, or the turning dark of **X-01**, the empire's switchboard, still working.
*Faces:* a Xebechani way-keeper performing maintenance liturgy for machinery whose manual is lost;
the maintainer of a quarantine held for fifty centuries, which has procedures for visitors;
whatever the delivery was *for*. *Stakes:* the network remembers every station, including the ones
the empire closed from the outside. *Complication:* switchboards do not err. They connect calls.
Someone was called, and your players answered.

Go. The calm is counting down, and the patience is not yours either.
