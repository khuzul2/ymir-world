# XV·B · THE VEILED HAND'S WORKBOOK
## *seeds from the margins, cut for planting*

> ⟡ **GM ONLY. As the chapter before it, and more so: that one told the truth, this one puts it
> to work. If you are a player and you are reading this line, you are stealing tools. Put them
> down.** ⟡

**Set down by the hand that wrote the margins, for use; sewn in behind the fifteenth chapter,
where the Curator will not look. He unsewed one quire of mine, found the paper watching him, and
resolved to be brave about it exactly once.**

---

You are back, and this time you want tools. Good. The fifteenth chapter told you what the world
is; it did not tell you what your table does with it on the next long evening. This is that book:
my workbook, the margins of fifteen chapters shaken out, sorted by ground, and sharpened.

I wrote *I do not tie ends. I point at them*, and I stand by it. But a pointed end no table ever
pulls is mystery wasted, and waste offends me more than blasphemy. Each seed is four motions:
**situation**, **faces**, **stakes**, **complication** — what is true when the players arrive,
throats to speak with, what the pull is worth, the tooth in the fruit. Where the chronicles keep
no name I have supplied one; do not mistake my naming for canon. The colophon confesses
everything, in one place, where confession belongs.

The year, unless a seed says otherwise, is **~3518** — the Equilibrium. But the whole line is
playable, and there are five doors into it: **the Burning Days** (the Horde, the secessions, Saxul
over the broken land); **the eve of the Tide, 3015–25** (wolves, waves, machines — the cascade);
**the Tide itself, 3025–27** (the key turning); **the New Era of Chaos, 3026–3260** (red moon,
open gates, walking dead); **the New Equilibrium, ~3518**. The untagged seeds assume the calm —
and you remember what the calm is made of.

---

### 1 · Letia & Solanthya

The most watchful state on Ymir, blind exactly where it kneels.

**The sealed galleries.** *Situation:* since the Chaos, Naughrith's dwarves keep their deepest
galleries sealed; their white-peak kin pray upward with unusual fervour; this year the mountain's
grain-orders doubled. Fed garrisons grow. So do fed prisoners. *Faces:* a door-warden who hires
outsiders, because outsiders can be made to forget; the quartermaster-priest who noticed; whatever
answers the prayers. *Stakes:* what the mountain always held — or what slipped the cracked prison.
*Complication:* the seals are dwarven and recent. The locks beneath are neither, and open from the
other side.

**The crypt of heresies.** *Situation:* the high Clericate does not burn forbidden books; it
curates them, in crypts under Solanthya. One folio is missing — the **Vlatch** dossier, the one on
the art of time — and the archive wants it back without admitting it exists. *Faces:* the
crypt-archivist, who believes nothing he guards; the synodal who authorised the "loan"; the buyer,
whose receipt is signed in a script that troubles the eye. *Stakes:* Chronomancy. The one art I
decline to explain even here. *Complication:* the Vlatch notice their readers — later, and out of
order.

**The struck name.** *Situation:* Kramer's name was struck from the Clericate's rolls, but
strike-marks are ink too, and a notary has found the counter-file: the Church knew what its hound
was building at Ulan-Tang before the red moon rose. *Faces:* the notary, honest and therefore in
danger; the Annalist, who may know he is lying when he writes "a nameless heresiarch"; the
censors' office, which works nights. *Stakes:* whether the most sincere theocracy on Ymir survives
its own filing system. *Complication:* someone above the censors wants the file found. Ask why.

### 2 · The Young Kingdoms — Armora and her belt

Better-lit than anywhere on the map, which is how you hide things in plain dark. And when your
campaign needs a dead thing walking: the Resurrection Engine was never found, and never destroyed.

**The annual denial.** *Situation:* the Palace's yearly denial that a Rune of Power sleeps beneath
Armora came two weeks early, because the lamps of the Industrial District have begun to flicker in
unison. Something else is drawing on the sleeping fire. *Faces:* the permit-mistress who signs the
denial and keeps the real ledger; a College measurer whose Essence-meter rings at nothing visible;
whatever is on the far end of the draw. *Stakes:* a city over one of the four anti-demon engines
learns what else is plugged in. *Complication:* the draw traces to dead Waldenheim — the other
buried Rune — reached through a kingdom no longer alive to object.

**The politest neighbours.** *Situation:* the changed dwarves of the Wald — who went below when
the dragon broke their halls, and came back up seeing in the dark — have asked, for the first time
in five centuries, for outside help: an escort *down*. *Faces:* a door-opener whose eyes take
lamplight the way a cat's do; a mountain-kin envoy who calls the Wald-folk nothing at all, which
is dwarvish for grief; what they left below. *Stakes:* whatever the dark made of them, they made
terms with it too. *Complication:* the escort is not protection. The terms require witnesses, and
the Wald has run out of its own.

**The watchdog's war** *(era: the Tide, 3025–27)*. *Situation:* the War of the Machines, played
straight: Gwalgamaur the Golden — Warden of Quadrant 74 in his makers' ledger — wakes to a duty
five ages stale and comes to secure what the city denies possessing. *Faces:* the YonkerMecha line
before the gates; the Silver Arrows, who will not come back from the Rhems Wood; the hidden
Technocrat, binding demons into steel. *Stakes:* the two buried Runes, which the dragon knows are
there and the defenders do not. *Complication:* your players fight the watchdog of the house they
are standing in.

### 3 · Helgedad & the north

Cold keeps things. That is what cold is for.

**The coils.** *Situation:* a miner has come up out of the Aestyr diggings knowing no name but
one, and it is not his. The Marcher houses want the shaft collapsed; the Order of the White
Mountain wants to go down. *Faces:* the returned miner; a White Mountain knight who has read the
old charters and is right about all of it; five half-forgotten generals, still in the maze, still
searching. *Stakes:* the labyrinth holds the Lord of Oblivion by forgetting — a prison whose walls
are your memory of the way out. *Complication:* every accurate map of the coils thins them. The
party's map is the breach.

**The thaw.** *Situation:* the Frozen Waste's edge has retreated a league in ten years, and green
things grow among the black-stone ruins of the watch-city. Helgedad celebrates the pasture. Do not
let them. *Faces:* a Vorskgen guide who walks to the glass streets and no farther; an Ognir factor
selling reclaimed grazing; Nihoggr, the Ice Dragon, whose sleeping breath the cold is. *Stakes:*
the Frozen Hell is literal, and warmth is the gauge on the dragon's sleep. *Complication:* the
prisoner in the Aestyr sought to wake this thing once, and his will still seeps from the maze in
dreams. The thaw has an author.

**The wolf-winter** *(era: the eve of the Tide, 3015–25)*. *Situation:* Nortislong falls in the
Siege of One Day, and the war is not a conquest — it is an excavation with a terror for a spade,
its trenches all in the wrong places for a war. *Faces:* the five dark generals, still fleshed;
Marcher levies who know the ground; whoever first notices what the digging is toward. *Stakes:*
the deep roads to the maze's heart, or the dragon's grave. *Complication:* the employer forgets
its own instruments. Victory looks like vanishing, and the generals already have.

### 4 · Qi-Long

Six certainties, none sufficient. Work the gaps between them.

**The courier-meter.** *Situation:* the ninth chapter of this Compendium scans, maxim for maxim,
in the courier-meter of the Master-Singers — the verse-code of the Golden Falcons. In six kingdoms
this book is a resistance dispatch in Council binding. Decide what it says. *Faces:* a
Master-Singer who will neither confirm nor teach; a Golden Pavilion fence selling "annotated"
copies; the Pilgrim, still walking, still remembering everything. *Stakes:* a muster, a map, or
one name. *Complication:* six Dragon-God courts keep codebreakers too, and possession of the book
is confession enough.

**The mongooses' guest.** *Situation:* the White Mongooses, who serve a Dragon-God "not yet of
this world," have stopped waiting: they are paying, quietly and in relics, for passage-rights on
the True Ways. Fetching has begun. *Faces:* a Mongoose abbess of terrifying serenity; a Xebechani
way-keeper who took the relics and regrets the arithmetic; a Golden Falcon with the sanest idea in
the east — *stop them*. *Stakes:* which arrival — the absent warden, Ouroboros, or two names for
one appetite. *Complication:* the last time something of that order moved, Alfheim burned in a
season.

**The waking of the six** *(era: the Burning Days, ~1900–1920)*. *Situation:* Mah-Dhol comes
down, and six wardens rise from posts five ages old to take its provinces. Play the generation
that watched gods replace an emperor. *Faces:* the last imperial chancellery, filing the end of
the world in triplicate; the first resistance cells choosing their animal names; six new courts
learning to be worshipped. *Stakes:* everything the six kingdoms are was settled in twenty years.
*Complication:* the wardens did not conquer — they *resumed*. Ask what each throne stands guard
over. The sects never have.

### 5 · Cartosa & the Twin Coasts

Money remembers what churches forget. Read the ledgers.

**Lightless pearls.** *Situation:* a string of pearls too large and too dark has surfaced in a
Cartosa counting-house — someone anchored over the Oyster's water and dived. *Faces:* the diver,
drowning on dry land by inches; a Sohleugir factor whose famous courtesy has stopped; the Blue
Oyster Shadow that came up with the string. *Stakes:* the Sohleugir do not breach such places —
they contain them — and their patience with Cartosan anchors ends at that mark. *Complication:*
putting the pearls back means diving the drowned shrine of the first slain Sovereign, where the
congregation still keeps its service — and has noticed that, lately, something answers.

**The golden garrison.** *Situation:* an expedition bankrolled by house Gibraleon is recruiting
for the jungle, and they know exactly what the gold of Taremorn costs. That is the point: they are
not buying gold, they are buying the garrison. *Faces:* the Gibraleon patron; a Black Dawn
quartermaster entered in the books as a chaplain; the cursed king Ismael tricked, still keeping
accounts inside those sad walls. *Stakes:* an army that cannot desert, march-ready since the Demon
War. *Complication:* the curse enlists recruiters too. Every soldier of that garrison signed
someone else before the walls took him.

**The Armorite's case.** *Situation:* a Yarhim-crabber's nets have brought up the sealed
chart-case of Roberto Rodrigo, "the Armorite" — the pirate the ballads refuse to let drown.
*Faces:* the seer Obnubia, who priced the case, declined to open it, and looked away first; three
noble houses' agents at one quiet auction; a ballad-seller who knows the verse everyone else sings
wrong. *Stakes:* whatever a legend thought worth a false grave. I do not know what is in the case.
Neither does Obnubia. Price accordingly. *Complication:* a man like that seals nothing without
choosing who should open it. The auction is the will being read.

### 6 · The Gaerwath & the elf lands

One people, three creeds, and a door that has begun to work both ways.

**Hunt or homecoming.** *Situation:* the rumour-name **Shulma** is real and growing, and a gate
dead since the Tide now warms at the same hour of every long-year. The road to Tchyo is open, and
something on the far side is using it. *Faces:* a Light-Elf gate-warden who has waited her whole
life to say *the Messenger*; a Dark-Elf observer under orders to let anything through; a Renouncer
who came to watch her kin be wrong, whichever way it falls. *Stakes:* the direction of travel —
the elves were *hunted* off Tchyo, and a Messenger is not the only thing that follows a fleeing
people's trail. *Complication:* all three creeds will act before anyone knows. Faith is faster
than evidence, and better armed.

**The Eternal's arithmetic.** *Situation:* Morannagul has not aged since 2550; every Gaerwath
child pays a trial that takes from the failed a terrible cost; no record names where the
subtraction goes. A Nemyel escape-line wants its ledger carried out — names, ages, what each
failure lost. *Faces:* a Nemyel Arvo-scraper with the ledger sewn into her coat; Pips Quondam, who
has run this cargo before and charged nothing, once; a Nath auditor whose office exists so that no
such ledger does. *Stakes:* the mechanism of a throne's eternity. *Complication:* publishing the
arithmetic does not free the Nemyel; it prices them. And the Eternal has never once been asked to
his face. Ask.

**The scaffolds of Alfheim.** *Situation:* Ouroboros unmade Ost-en-Galad because something there
satisfied a condition, and no one has answered what. Five centuries on, the Light Elves rebuild —
and the works have reached the height at which the old vigil, the beacon that called *Home? Home?*
down the reopened ways, resumes. *Faces:* the queen of Inanna's line, replanting a splinter and
calling it the promise; a Grey-elf surveyor who has done the terrible sum; the warden overhead,
who does not rage, only responds. *Stakes:* whether the condition is about to be satisfied twice.
*Complication:* stop the beacon and you break the creed of everyone building it. Let it stand and
you learn the trigger by demonstration.

### 7 · The Sadhir deserts

The desert does not bargain. It files.

**The oracle that asks for you.** *Situation:* the Oracle of the Brine Veil has always taken
questions. Lately it gives answers unasked, addressed by name to travellers three dunes away. The
Pearl's congregation is recruiting on a schedule. *Faces:* a Narazeen wheel-warden who burns what
surfaces; a Nashifet relic-hunter who has sold the Oracle's location twice, both buyers still
alive; the Oracle, mouthpiece of a shape slowly filling. *Stakes:* the Deep Pearl is gathering
what the Abyss remembers of its Prince; the Salt-Drowned are enrolled, not born. *Complication:*
the Sarradhi cure is patience — give it nothing. Your players are, professionally, the something.

**The arrears of Khorish.** *Situation:* the Eternal Pharaohs bought eternity as a tenancy from
Netunshiel, Lord of Crypts, the corvée for rent; the creditor died at the Tide; the contract,
demon-drafted and therefore flawless, does not lapse. A claimant has presented itself at court
with the deed. *Faces:* Athmokethepek I, learning what he consented to instead of death; Nefthi,
who read the contract; the claimant — heir, forger, or collector for something worse. *Stakes:*
every soul that labours dead in Khorish is an asset in the transfer. *Complication:* disproving
the claim requires the original, which is filed in the estate of a dead Sovereign, in the Abyss.

**The two doors.** *Situation:* the salt will not release Al-Sidath and Al-Jabur; the desert is
holding its two oldest doors shut. Old sultans' coin — and older oaths — under the counting-tables
of Tuleph are financing a dig anyway. *Faces:* a hermit-crab guide who remembers which kingdom was
hers first; a Zalim'un coral-steward refusing every permit; whoever underwrote the older oath.
*Stakes:* what the drowned kingdoms were sealed against. Or sealed *with*. *Complication:*
everything the desert takes, it keeps, and offers back at a price, in the voice of something you
loved. The lore-keeper wrote that. She was not being lyrical.

### THE DOUBLE LEAF · ULAN-TANG

The Curator's reviewers refused this town a chapter, and they were right: readers do not need it.
You do. It gets my two pages instead.

The facts, dry. A city-state on the river **Tang**, once sacred to **Ishtar** — honest ledgers
still carry contracts sworn in her name. The sorcerers of **Yogh** took it; the spider-god is old,
real, and cross-cultural — the Xebechani feed the same god under the same name — and his High
Priest **Sazar-Gadash**, demon-son of Asmodeus, rules by right of having murdered his predecessor
Azim-Mulath. There are no city guards. There are the **five houses** and their private knives:
**Gadash** in violet, the priest's own; **Ishra** in red; **Tyarreth** in olive; **Miraz** in
blue; **Gaib** in black, who keep the seers. There is the **Black Lotus**, which pays for
everything; the **Night Market**, where everything else is paid for; and behind it all the
**Azar-Kalish**, the Conclave of Shadows, whose sign is the spider and whose invoices are settled
in lives — the great spider **Adhal-Kibreeshi**, the Whispering Lady, being nearer the god's true
avatar than his priest will ever be. And the reason any of it matters: the city stands on an
ancient portal, and in 3026 the lich that had been the inquisitor **Kramer** opened it, and the
red moon rose. Yogh keeps his web on a door.

**Kramer's legacy.** *Situation:* the lich's workings around the gate were never dismantled —
house Gadash wards them, taxes them, and understands none of them; and lately every instrument in
the scaffold has turned, all together, toward the quarter of sky where the red moon *receded*.
*Faces:* Sazar-Gadash, who will sell access twice; a Cult of the Stars observer who came to kneel
and stayed to measure; a Brotherhood mage-hunter very far off her map. *Stakes:* a gate that has
admitted a god's attention once. The working is not a summons; it is an appointment.
*Complication:* tearing it down may be worse. Kramer built *around* something already there — the
door is the ancients', not the lich's.

**The night market.** *Situation:* what cannot be bought anywhere is priced here nightly — and
when your table loses track of anything this folio trades in, a lightless pearl, a scroll that
should be under Xanadu, it resurfaces here, under lamps, with a lot-number. *Faces:* a Miraz
auctioneer masked in blue; an Azar-Kalish under-broker who settles disputes permanently; the
consignor, never present, always punctual. *Stakes:* whatever you let leave the tent.
*Complication:* all sales are final in the fullest sense. The guarantee is the spider's, and
refunds are collected in lives.

**Five houses, one web.** *Situation:* Sazar-Gadash's title rests on the murder of his
predecessor, and that right is transferable; one of the four other houses intends to transfer it.
*Faces:* an Ishra knife-mistress with the votes of three kitchens; **Faazi-Ghaib**, house Gaib's
seer, selling every faction the same true dream; the Whispering Lady, who does not care who is
priest. *Stakes:* the Lotus trade, the Night Market, and custody of the god-touched gate.
*Complication:* Yogh has presided over both sides of every succession the city remembers. The
winner inherits the web with the spider still in it.

**Under the red moon** *(era: the New Era of Chaos, 3026–3260)*. *Situation:* Ulan-Tang in the
years the Gaze hung overhead — the gate still warm, the Xebechani arriving to kneel, doom-cults
and pilgrims and profiteers, a city learning to tax an apocalypse. *Faces:* the first Gadash
gate-wardens, writing the tariff schedule for the end of the world; a star-priest who has waited
its whole long life to be right; Kramer's leavings, still cooling. *Stakes:* the city that owned
the crack in the sky. *Complication:* for two centuries everything sold in the Night Market passed
under the Eye. Decide what looked back — and what it marked for later.

### 8 · Ophiur

The morning country. The oldest bones are the least buried here.

**The honest map.** *Situation:* no honest map of Gazal's undercity exists — until now. A survivor
has come up with one that is *accurate*, which is worse: it marks the tribute-roads, and the
throne at the bottom that something out of the prison-crack claimed after the Tide. *Faces:* the
mapmaker, who will sell the going but not go; a Zabhula factor pricing vaults by the fathom; the
taker of tribute below, which has begun issuing receipts. *Stakes:* the free city is free because
the masters below are paid. *Complication:* publish the map and the tribute-peace ends; suppress
it and you have decided, alone, that the arrangement should stand.

**The kept garden.** *Situation:* someone at your table's edge is dying of what nothing cures — a
Marked child, a Pool-sick Xebechani envoy, your campaign's own casualty — and the Garden of
Xiombarg cures *anything*. The songs are specific. *Faces:* a map-seller preparing a comfortable
retirement; an Ur-Tanoth guide who walks to the tree-line and not one step past it; the guardians,
older than the walls they ward. *Stakes:* the cure is real. *Complication:* a garden is where you
keep what you mean to plant again. The guardians select stock rather than stop thieves — and the
price of leaving may be to carry a seed out, plant it, and not ask what grows.

**The Storm turns.** *Situation:* the Ur of Tarkur follow the Storm across the savanna, and their
Seekers scent the Essence raw — the one faith on Ymir that was never lied to. The Storm has
stopped migrating. It is circling a point in the deep interior, and the kingdom turns with it.
*Faces:* a Seeker who will not name what she scents; Nyema of Ga'Namesh, writing down the songs as
they change; the conclave of Horatli, which bought every old migration-chart this year and sold
nothing back. *Stakes:* the currents shifted when the seals broke, and the honest compass is
pointing. *Complication:* the dragon-wardens circle the same interior and do not land. Two watches
over one point. Decide what has earned both.

### 9 · The seas & the isles

Water is the world's honest margin. Read what washes up.

**The failing rhythm.** *Situation:* the Sohleugir sound the deep water and do not exaggerate, and
their soundings say the Maelstrom's rhythm is faltering. This year's report never reached Elendor.
*Faces:* a veiled Sohleugir courier-factor whose patience is thinning; the Crystal Seat clerk who
noticed the gap in the series; three buyers who each want the number to prove a different comfort.
*Stakes:* the storm is scar and seal both — the world's doomsday gauge. *Complication:* the report
was not stolen for the number but for the trend-line. Someone wants to know *when*.

**The unmapped continent.** *Situation:* the Isle of Demons has stood five hundred years without a
map and without growing, which is the worst news patience can send. A chartered expedition —
Council-flagged, or privately mad — means to fix a coastline at last. *Faces:* an Armorite
aeronaut with a theory about the bruised sky; a Cartosan master who will sail to the horizon of it
and no closer; whichever of tyranny, plots, and illusions notices first. *Stakes:* the first
survey of a patient neighbour. *Complication:* it is not a gap in the book. It is a door — and a
survey party is how a door learns who knocks.

**The sewn chapter.** *Situation:* the Curator wrote that he chose not to pursue the matter of the
fifteenth chapter's binding. He has pursued it since, through three respectable intermediaries,
and the third is hiring. *Faces:* the Curator, wry, exact, and frightened in a scholarly way; a
Tylweth Teg paper-wright who found the watermark and washed her hands twice — her people hate
everything the Maelstrom has touched, and she cannot say why this is worse; me. *Stakes:* you have
read the chapter; you know. *Complication:* I will grade the attempt.

### 10 · The Dreamlands, & elsewhere

Nothing in the calm forbids dreaming; it is the one border the Equilibrium never closed. And when
sleep palls, *elsewhere* remains: the True Ways go only where the ancients wanted to go — and the
ancients wanted **X-01**, still turning in the dark, and **Zend**, whose quarantine someone has
maintained for fifty centuries. Ask against what. The Tangle, too, still keeps the Heart of Chaos,
and no narrator of this book ever dared the subject.

**The permanent situation.** *Situation:* sleepers from Armora's licensed parlours report a
courteous gentleman in yellow who keeps a gate and offers permanent employment. The offers are
sincere. One husband has said yes. *Faces:* Topramesk the Yellow, Lord of the Void, deep in his
long watch on the dream-road through Ragash-below toward steam-wreathed Arcadia; the new employee,
promoted quickly; the licensed dream-pilot hired to fetch him home. *Stakes:* the gatekeeper is
staffing up a watch. Decide whether it is *against* something or *for* something; he already has.
*Complication:* the wages are real, the work is real, the door does need watching — and the
employee does not want rescuing.

**The palace that unlocks.** *Situation:* the archmage Mubafer sits in a prison built as a palace,
sealed until he forgets the two loves he betrayed, and he has been forgetting a very long time. A
client of the Ulan-Tang seers wants a thing only Mubafer still remembers. *Faces:* Faazi-Ghaib,
selling the crossing — this folio's roads keep meeting, and that is the roads' doing, not mine;
Mubafer, courteous and diminishing; the two loves, whose names are the lock. *Stakes:* the memory
your client is buying may be one of the two the seal is counting. *Complication:* remind him, and
the sentence resets with witnesses inside. Let him forget, and the palace unlocks around something
that no longer knows why it should not leave.

**The axe on the mountain.** *Situation:* Tofango's body was found; **Oblivion** was not. On some
peak — I do not know which, and neither does anyone, so choose the one your map can spare — a
demon of rank sits imbued in a blade, five centuries into practising how to sound reasonable.
*Faces:* a Brotherhood mage-hunter carrying Tears of Davedars and orders to burn any hand that
lifts it; a shepherd village that stopped going up the mountain and no longer remembers deciding;
the demon, which remembers everything. *Stakes:* the artefact that ate the mind of the age's
greatest axeman. *Complication:* the axe does not need to be swung. It needs to be *carried
down* — and it will pay in victories, and the first several are genuine.

---

That is the workbook: the margins' ends, pointed and now sharpened. Pull the ones your table
deserves; there is always one more door than the folio. Do not tell the Curator this quire exists.
He has enough to be brave about.

Go. The calm is counting down, and the patience is not yours either.
