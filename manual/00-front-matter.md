# THE YMIR COMPENDIUM
### being a true gathering of the tales, histories, and doctrines of the world of Ymir, as its own peoples tell them
#### assembled at the Crystal Seat of Elendor, in the years of the New Equilibrium

---

## Preface of the Curator

I am the Magister-Compiler of the Crystal Seat, and this book is not mine. Not a word of it,
save these. What follows was written by many hands across many centuries and many tongues — by a
priest of Xebech who has outlived empires, by a friar of Solanthya who believes his Church could do
no wrong, by a sea-navigator who trusted his ledger more than his gods, by a Dark-Elf loremaster who
holds the rest of us in gentle contempt. I have only gathered them, set them in order, and here and
there added a line where two of them call the same thing by different names, or swear to events that
cannot both be true.

Do not mistake this for confusion. It is the truest map of Ymir I could make. A world is not what a
single scholar declares it to be; it is the quarrel of everyone who has ever tried to declare it. I
have let them quarrel.

We stand now near **three thousand five hundred and eighteen years after the Maelstrom** — deep in
the calm the histories call the **New Equilibrium**, the long exhale after the age of storms. From
this quiet shore the narrators look back, and so shall you.

### How to read this book
- **The body of each chapter is its author's own account** — their beliefs, the knowledge of their
  age, and their prejudices, left intact. Trust it as far as you would trust the one who wrote it.
- Where I have something to add, you will find it marked *Curator's note*. I try to be brief.
- And — a warning I give once — there are **margins** in this book that I did not write, and cannot
  account for. They appear in a hand that the copyists could not reproduce and the eye does not like
  to rest upon. They contradict the text they sit beside. I have left them, because I have come to
  fear they are the only wholly honest thing between these covers.

### The Order of This Book

*Set down at the binders' insistence, and against my own preference for letting a reader be
ambushed. The attributions are the narrators' own; the small judgements are mine. — the Curator*

- **I · Cosmogony** — *Xochiyayotl, Xattva Loremaster of Xuthal, Star-Priest of the Cult of the
  Stars.* The making of all things, from the one tongue old enough to have been told it.
- **II · Of the Gods** — *Fra' Teodabir, torch-priest of Hokhmah, of the Brotherhood of the
  Torch.* Every creed on Ymir, reconciled. Very nearly.
- **III · Of Magic** — *Sir Drusilde, Archmage of the Seventh Circle of Armora; dated 3027.* The
  Essence weighed, measured, and — her word, never mine — governed.
- **IV · Of the Ages** — *the Annalist of Solanthya, chronicler-friar of the Clericate of
  Mithra.* Every year in its order. Trust his dates; audit his judgements.
- **V · The Lay of the Land** — *the sea-diary of Ramiro Estioca of Cartosa.* The world from a
  masthead, which is how it actually looks.
- **VI · Letia** — *the Annalist, returning.* His mother's house. He counts the windows.
- *(Here follows an unnumbered leaf: **A Leaf Passed by the Censors of Solanthya** — a facsimile,
  struck through where the censors earned their bread. I bind what I am given.)*
- **VII · The Young Kingdoms** — *"The Armora Crier," a broadsheet-man.* Progress, at volume,
  with advertisements.
- **VIII · Helgedad** — *Hrafnkel Ognirsson, skald of the Nortmaar.* Cold country, long memory,
  no apologies.
- **IX · Qi-Long** — *the Pilgrim of the Thundering Gate.* Six kingdoms, six dragons, one road
  walked slowly.
- **IX·B · A Letter to a Dead Pupil — Xebech** — *Xochiyayotl, returning: a letter addressed to
  Sir Drusilde, five centuries dead, deposited unasked at the Crystal Seat.* I did not solicit
  it, and I could not have refused it.
- **X · Cartosa** — *Ramiro Estioca, returning, with a Blue Mantles factor's ledgers.* Home
  waters. The diary ends where the diarist did.
- **X·B · The Sounding-Report of Ssas'Kher** — *delivered to the Council of Peoples; rendered
  from the trade-pidgin by a Grey-elf broker.* Numbered soundings, no adjectives, and one
  measurement the whole world turns on.
- **X·C · The Silver Isles & the Council** — *the Curator, in body-text at last.* My own ground.
  The reader will find that the margins have opinions about me. That seems only fair.
- **XI · Gaerwath & the Elves** — *Aelryn Vhaeloth i-Celeblyar, Dark-Elf Loremaster of
  Morannagul the Eternal.* The rest of us, ranked from above.
- **XII · The Sadhir Deserts** — *Adhima the Rakhama'i, Sarradhi lore-keeper; with a human
  caravan-scribe's rebuttals.* The Wheel, and one man who declines to turn with it.
- **XII·B · Ophiur** — *Nyema of Ga'Namesh, Keeper of the Thousand Songs of the Ivory
  Coalition.* She crossed three seas to correct my blank map. The map deserved it.
- **XII·C · The Book of the Sleeping Shore — the Dreamlands** — *a retired dream-pilot of a
  licensed Armorite parlour, thirty years in the trade.* Where the sleeper goes; what the deeper
  shores cost; who keeps the doors.
- **XII·D · The Speaker of the Tally — the Belligerent Kingdoms** — *an envoy of a Khan's
  tribute-court, her words taken down at the Crystal Seat through two interpreters, who do not
  agree.* The captors' ledger, in the captors' words. It was owed a hearing.
- **XIII · Of the Peoples** — *Pips Quondam, a Tal-Tanoth traveller.* Everyone, observed from
  knee height, which misses nothing.
- **XIV · Of Beasts** — *a huntsman's field-book; a ranger of Therìos.* Nineteen names in the
  old lists, their tracks, and their prices.
- **XIV·B · Figures of Renown — an Inquisition record** — *a dossier of the Clericate of Mithra,
  obtained rather than gifted.* The heroes and horrors of the late ages, as the Church files
  them: alphabetically, and under suspicion.
- **XV · Behind the Veil (GM)** — *set down by the hand that wrote the margins.* I did not
  commission this chapter and cannot account for it. Players: your book ends at XIV·B.
- **XV·B · The Veiled Hand's Workbook (GM)** — *the same hand, in a working temper.* For the one
  who runs the world; unfinished business, sorted by region.
- *(Here follows an unnumbered closing leaf: **A Note Left Unanswered.** It is mine, and I will
  say nothing else about it.)*
- **Appendices A–K** — *the Curator's own.* Of the years (A); of the calendar (B); of monies
  (C); a register of persons (D); a glossary (E); of the tongues (F); of herbs, smokes &
  draughts (G); a register of instruments & materials (H); doors into Ymir, for the one who runs
  the world (I — GM); of the plates, and an index of places (J); and of the making of this book
  (K).

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM) — the Veiled Hand** ⟡
> *You who read the margins: read them alone. Everything the bright text tells is believed by those
> who tell it, and almost all of it is a lie told to the tellers. The gods are real. So are the
> things that wear the gods. Turn the page, Curator's guest, and I will show you the seam.*

*Herewith, then, the Compendium — beginning where all things begin, with the making of the world.*
