# THE REGISTER OF HANDS — the Postils of the Ymir Compendium

> Master reference + writers' brief for the **Postils**: the layer of in-world reader-annotations
> accreted across time. This file is the single source of truth. Writers: obey the **markup spec**,
> honour each hand's **voice/ink/reliability**, hit the **density targets**, and place the
> **marquee postils verbatim** where anchored (they carry threads across chapters and must match).

---

## The frame (in-world)

This edition is the **merged exemplars**. When the Compendium was bound at the Crystal Seat (~3518),
fair copies went out — to Solanthya's censors, the College of the Seventh Circle, the Armora Crier's
morgue, a Xebech reckoning-house, an Ophiur song-hall, a Cartosan ship, a frontier chapel, a prison
library. They were read, corrected, defaced, carried through wars, and some came home. **The last of
the Curators** — the final holder of an office that ends with him — gathered every surviving marked
copy into one, "so the book might be read as its readers left it." His preface is `00b-the-last-curator.md`.
His own hand (⌘) appears rarely, only to reconcile the copies or to grieve.

Because the merge happens *after* 3518, hands may reach into the book's future — **but every
age-scale future claim is disputed in the margin** (one hand asserts, another doubts); nothing hard.

## Markup spec (obey exactly)

A **postil** is a blockquote line led by the nib glyph `✒`, then the hand's name, a `·`, an italic
place/date, an em-dash, and the note:

```
> ✒ **Xochiyayotl** · *the Cycle you number 3480, night of Tortoise* — Imperfectly is kind.
```

A **reply** (a later hand answering an earlier one, in the same margin) uses a nested `>>`:

```
>> ✒ **a cadet of the Seventh Circle** · *3506* — who keeps writing in the Directress's own copy?!
```

Rules:
- One postil per line. Keep each to 1–3 sentences.
- **Date** every postil in the book's calendar where the hand can be dated (e.g. *3491*; *ninth of
  Spider, 3488*; *reign-year 947*; *the Cycle you number 3480*). The **undatable** hands (the Night)
  and abrupt breaks (Drusilde's death) are the deliberate exceptions.
- Place a postil-block **immediately after the paragraph it comments on** (like the GM margins do).
- **Never** put a decision-ID (COS-, HIS-…) in a postil. Postils are diegetic; they cite nothing.
- Postils are **player-facing** (unlike the `⟡ GM ⟡` margins). Do not reveal GM-only secrets in a
  postil; a postil may *guess* at a truth the GM margin states (irony), never confirm it.
- English only (per `TRANSLATION-MAP.md`). A hand may quote one old-tongue word if a voice would.

The build script renders `✒` postils as tinted handwritten marginal blocks, per-hand ink, threaded.

## THE HANDS

Each entry: **sigil · ink · era · voice · reliability · haunts**, then any **marquee postils**
(place these verbatim at the anchor; they thread across chapters).

### Band I — the deathless threaders

**✧ Xochiyayotl** — pale grey-blue, the faintest ink in the book · dates "by the Cycle" · the eldest
reckoner of Xebech, narrator of I & IX·B, Drusilde's teacher five centuries back · *glacial,
crystalline, deep truth; reliable but cold* · **haunts:** 01, 03, 09b, 15.
- Anchor 01 (the Essence as "remembering"): `> ✒ **Xochiyayotl** · *the Cycle you number 3480, night of Tortoise* — Imperfectly is kind. The world does not misremember; it is *made* to. I taught this child of mine the difference, and see he left it out.`
- Anchor 03 (Drusilde's definition of the realms / the Essence): `> ✒ **Xochiyayotl** · *the Cycle you number 3502* — She was my finest pupil and she still simplifies for the warm and quick. Forgive her. I have four hundred years yet to correct it, and she has none.`
- Anchor 15 (the Great Lie / the Adepts sealed the Old Gods): `> ✒ **Xochiyayotl** · *undated* — You have written the truth and dressed it as a secret. We of Xebech kept it the other way: in plain sight, where no warm eye lingers long enough to read.`

**❈ Morannagul the Eternal** — deep unfading black · dates by *his own reign-year* (reign began 2550;
so reign-year N = 2549 + N) · deathless dark-elf emperor of the Gaerwath · *cold ornate hauteur,
deep-time menace; true but self-serving* · **haunts:** 01, 11, and one cameo in 04.
- Anchor 01 (the Adepts "broke" the world / the Catastrophe): `> ✒ **Morannagul** · *the nine-hundred-and-sixty-second year of my reign* — "Broke." I walked it before and after; it was not a breaking but a *theft*, and I am among the few left who remember being robbed. The child of Xebech flatters the thieves because it studied under them.`
- Anchor 11 (the elves' exile / the Curator's "fair voice" counter-note): `> ✒ **Morannagul** · *the nine-hundred-and-eleventh year of my reign* — The Curator gives "both sides" as though time had two. There is my account, and there is the account of those who did not last to give one. I have read every anathema written against me by men now four centuries in the ground. I keep them. They are the only letters that still arrive.`

**◐ the Yellow Postillant** — amber/ochre, written *on the reverse of the leaf, showing through* ·
undated, or dated to no calendar · *(the reader suspects a dreamer at a gate; the GM knows Topramesk)*
· courteous, patient, offers "employment" · **haunts:** 01, 12c, 15 — rare, unsettling.
- Anchor 01 or 15 (Ragash / the Maelstrom / the Adepts persist): `> ✒ **a hand in yellow** · *(no date; the ink shows through from the far side)* — The city you mourn is not gone. It is only asleep, and I keep the gate, and the post is always open. Turn the page over. I wrote you a door.`
- Anchor 12c (the Dreamlands / a gate): `> ✒ **a hand in yellow** · *(reads through from behind)* — You describe my country as a traveller describes a house he has burgled. Come properly next time. Ask for the gentleman in yellow. I will have found you work by morning.`

**✝̶ the struck hand** *(a clean young inquisitor → the lich M'Raker; the SAME hand, corrupting across
900 years — write the dated stage that fits each anchor)* — iron-gall brown, darkening to near-black
by the late notes · **reliability: begins true, ends malign** · **haunts (in order):** 06 (2563,
clean) → 06b censored leaf (post-3026, cold) → 14b entry XI (later, inhuman) → a last one, 03 or 15.
- STAGE 1 — Anchor 06-letia (near Valdis / the Broken Land / a young inquisitor's piety): `> ✒ **Fr. H. Kramer, of Solanthya** · *2563* — I was there when the Fever fell. I set this down so my own doubt would have a witness: Heaven used us, and I was clean, and I mean to stay so. Whoever reads this in a better year — pray I did.`
- STAGE 2 — Anchor 06b-censored-leaf (near the strike / the erased name): `> ✒ **the same hand, altered** · *~3040* — They struck my name and thought that ended the argument. A name is a handle. I have put mine down. What holds the pen now needs no handle, and does not tire, and has read every book in the house — including the one that absolved me.`
- STAGE 3 — Anchor 14b entry XI (his own struck entry): `> ✒ **[a hand that is barely a hand]** · *undated, but the ink is younger than it should be* — Falx wrote me well. He filed a heresiarch and missed a *tenant*. I am still in the house, examiners. I read the register each generation to see whether you have learned to spell my new name yet. You have not. Try M——.`

**▬ the Night** *(the Notturni — writes as "we")* — a note that reads like a hole in the page,
near-black · **undatable** · alien, plural, cryptic · **haunts:** any 2–3 places, rare.
- Sample (place once, e.g. 14 beasts or 15): `> ✒ **the Night** · *(no date; the letters seem cut from the page rather than inked)* — You write "creature." We are not one and not many. We came for the Work. Your book is a small part of the Work. We are reading it. We have read the part where you notice us. We are still reading.`

**☥ the Eternal Pharaohs** *(Nefthi & Athmokethepek of Khorish, deathless)* — dry gold · dates by
dynastic year · imperious, bored, very old · **haunts:** 12 sadhir, one cameo in 04.
- Anchor 12 (Khorish / the Eternal Pharaohs / the corvée of the dead): `> ✒ **Nefthi, by grace undying** · *the eight-hundredth year of Our first reign* — The scribe writes "necromancy" as a southern man writes "weather" — a thing that happens to other people. Come to Khorish and see whose hands built the road you complain of. Then complain to them. They are still working.`

**▽ Leonard** *(the vampire, "file chained open" — reappears after each reported death)* — dried-red ·
grim comedy · **haunts:** 07, 14b.
- Anchor 14b entry XVI (his own "destroyed / pending" entry): `> ✒ **L.** · *filed the year they burned me the third time* — The Office keeps my file open. Considerate. I keep the Office's the same way. We are the only two institutions in this book that understand that "destroyed" is a *process*, not an event.`

### Band II — the keepers, copyists & censors

**✠ the Solanthya censor** *(Frater Falx & his successors)* — **red correction; black strikes**
(`~~struck~~`) · doctrinaire, defaces, lies by omission · **haunts:** 02, 04, 06, 15, 14b — several.
- Style: strikes a heretical clause and writes the "corrected" version in red; or appends a curt
  ruling. e.g. `> ✒ **the Censor of Solanthya** · *3521* — ~~the gods are the Adepts wearing masks~~ *the gods are the gods.* Let the copyist not enquire after the stroke. The enquiry is itself matter for examination.`

**⌘ the last Curator** — neutral iron ink · the assembler of this edition; rare, only to reconcile
copies or to grieve · honest, melancholy · **haunts:** front matter, and 1–2 seams where copies
disagree.
- Style (reconciling two copies): `> ✒ **the last Curator** · *(no year; I have stopped keeping them)* — Two copies quarrel here, and both hands are dead, and I have set them side by side rather than choose. Choosing is what the first Curator did. I only gather now. There is no one left to send the fair copy to.`

**✎ a cadet of the Seventh Circle** — graphite/pencil · exam-cramming, doodles, one genuinely useful
mnemonic, a rude limerick, awe at defacing a master's book · human, funny, mostly harmless · **haunts:**
03, the appendices.
- Style samples: `> ✒ **a cadet of the Seventh Circle** · *3506* — MEMORISE: three realms, Arcane is the union, corruption is the axis. This is on the examination EVERY year. The Directress set it herself. (She is dead. She still sets it.)` / a reply to Drusilde's stopping pen (see thread below).

**▤ the Armora Crier's press** — printer's black + a red stamp · pasted clippings, "STOP PRESS",
ad-copy · boosterish · **haunts:** 07, 10.
- Style: `> ✒ **The Armora Crier** *(pasted clipping)* · *3529* — STOP PRESS: the "peaceful runes" the Palace denies for the ninth year running have, this morning, denied the Palace a whole district. See our evening edition. Widows' discount as always.`

**§ Messer Crovos Bravaso**, President of Magical Control *(canon)* — formal brown-black · pompous,
official · **haunts:** 03 only, once.
- Anchor 03 (the S.C.M. / permits / the Red Book): `> ✒ **Messer Crovos Bravaso, Pres. S.C.M.** · *3029* — Supervisor's Note: this chapter is the Directress's *preface* to my Red Book, and is approved for lay reading. The Book itself is not. Applicants for Permit B should not cite this margin to the Board. Several have. It did not go as they hoped.`

### Band III — the self-annotators

**‡ Yusuf al-Marrah**, caravan-scribe of Tuleph — brown, small, precise, furious, **dated** · the
precedent-in-chief (already in Ch. XII); his fury now reaches beyond his own leaf · reliable on
fact, cranky on tone · **haunts:** 12 (home), 13 peoples, the calendar appendix.
- Anchor 13 (any sweeping claim about desert peoples): `> ✒ **Yusuf al-Marrah, of Tuleph** · *ninth of Chimera, 3499* — I have now corrected this book in two chapters and I am told there are sixteen. My family has kept ledgers a thousand years to be summarised, wrongly, by a Tal-Tanoth who footnotes everyone and verifies no one. I will be in the third chapter I find. Watch for me.`

**⟐ Drusilde** — a distinctive viridian/teal ink, an elegant fast hand · self-annotates her own Ch.
III and her Figures-of-Renown entry · **THE STOPPING-PEN THREAD (place verbatim, it is the layer's
signature mystery):**
- Anchor 03, early (she confirms a point): `> ✒ **Drusilde, Directress of the Seventh Circle** · *3029* — I stand by every word of this. I wrote it twice — once alive, and once after, when I found I had opinions the grave had not settled.`
- Anchor 03, at a LATER passage (the pen breaks): `> ✒ **Drusilde** · *undated; the hand stops mid-word* — No — that is not what my teacher mea—`
  - reply: `>> ✒ **a cadet of the Seventh Circle** · *3506* — the Directress's note ENDS HERE. mid-word. every copy I have found does the same, in the same place, and no one at the College will say why. some say she died writing it. she died four hundred years ago. the ink is not four hundred years old.`
  - reply: `>>> ✒ **the last Curator** · *(no year)* — I have compared nine copies. The cadet is right, and braver than his elders: the break is identical in every hand, and the ink of the reply beneath it is older than the ink above. I do not explain it. I only report that the Directress is down as author of a whole chapter, and as five centuries dead, and that both her copies keep writing.`
- Anchor 14b (her Steamclad-Sky company / the resurrection-pact): `> ✒ **Drusilde** · *undated* — The Crier says I came back "on a pact I will not name." I will not name it because I have not finished paying it, and a debt read aloud comes due faster. Ask me again in a chapter where I am still writing.`

## Discipline & tagging
- Postils are diegetic; they may be biased, wrong, or lying. The chapter **colophons** gain one line
  noting the postil layer and flagging that its content is in-character (add: *"Postils: the marginal
  hands are [INVENTED] reader-voices per `HANDS-REGISTER.md`; their claims are in-character and not
  canon unless a fact is independently attested."*).
- Any postil carrying a **true useful fact** may be trusted; age-scale **future** claims are disputed
  by design and never canon.
- Keep the `⟡ GM ⟡` layer separate and player-hidden; postils never confirm a GM secret.
