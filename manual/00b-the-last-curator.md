# A WORD FROM THE LAST CURATOR
## *set before the book by the one who gathered its copies*

I am the last of the Curators, and this is not the book the first of us made.

The first Curator made a clean thing: one fair copy, bound at the Crystal Seat in the year of the
Maelstrom 3518, every hand accounted for, every quarrel set in its proper note. Then he did the
sensible thing a maker does — he had copies made, and sent them out, so the knowledge would not die
with the one volume. A copy went to the censors of Solanthya, who improved it. A copy went to the
College of the Seventh Circle, where the cadets defaced it and a dead Directress kept writing in it.
A copy went aboard a Cartosan ship, and came back salt-stained and short three chapters and richer by
a marginal life. Copies went to Xebech, to Ophiur, to a chapel on the burnt marches, to a prison whose
name I will not give. They were read. That is the worst thing you can do to a book, and the only thing
worth doing.

What you hold is not the fair copy. It is all of them at once.

I have set the copies side by side and reproduced, as faithfully as an old man's eyes allow, the hands
that gathered in their margins across the years since — the corrections and the curses, the useful
directions and the useless griefs, the arguments that ran on for a century because one party was
deathless and would not let it drop. Where two copies disagree, I have let them disagree; choosing was
the first Curator's office, and I do not hold it. I only gather. **Learn the hands as you go — the
book keeps a table of them at the end** — for they are as much the Compendium now as the chapters they
crawl upon; a knowledge is only alive while people are still arguing with it, and here, pressed into
the paper, is the arguing.

You will ask why the copies all came home to me, and why there was no one to whom I might send a fresh
one out. That is the one question in this book I have left unanswered, and I have earned the right to
leave one. Read the margins. Some of the later hands answer it, and I would rather you heard it from
them than from me.

> ✒ **the last Curator** · *(no year; I have stopped keeping them)* — One instruction, then I will
> be quiet and let the dead talk. When a note frightens you, look at its date. The frightening ones
> are always dated *after* this book was finished. Someone kept reading. Someone is reading still.
