# THE HANDS IN THE MARGINS — a proposal for a marginalia layer

> **What this is.** A design proposal (nothing built yet) for adding a fourth apparatus layer to
> *The Ymir Compendium*: **in-world annotations left by many readers across time** — the notable
> figures themselves and the ordinary people of Ymir who owned, copied, argued with, and defaced the
> book as it passed through their hands. The aim, in the author's words: *add flavor, add useful bits
> of knowledge, and make the manual — and Ymir, and its people — feel old, handled, and alive.*
>
> Decide the items in **§9 (Decisions needed)** and I will build it.

---

## 1 · Why this works for *this* book

The Compendium is already, by its own declaration, "written by many hands across many centuries and
many tongues." It already runs a disciplined apparatus of layered voices. And it **already contains
in-world marginalia** in two places we can generalise:

- **Yusuf al-Marrah's rebuttals** in Ch. XII — a caravan-scribe's objections, "small, precise,
  furious, and **dated**," crowding the margins of Adhima's account "on top of his house." This is
  the exact device, already canon. It is our **precedent-in-chief**.
- **The *Red Book of Magical Control*** (Drusilde's own in-world book, in the sources) carries a
  signed **Supervisor's Note** by *Messer Crovos Bravaso*, a **"How to use this book,"** chapter
  epigraphs, disputed-reading formulas ("*some erroneously hold that…*"), and — the in-world word we
  should adopt — **the *Postilla*, a marginal addendum.** Ymir's scholars already annotate in the
  margin and have a name for it.

So the layer isn't a new conceit bolted on; it is the book **living out a promise it already made.**
The working name for the layer, borrowing the canon term: **the Postils** (a postil = a marginal
note; a *postillant* = one who writes them).

---

## 2 · The hard constraint that shapes everything: mortality

The research turned up a load-bearing fact. **Most peoples of Ymir are short-lived** — even elves
live only ~200 years, Fae ~150, Xebechani castes ~250–300. So the book *cannot* mostly be annotated
by "the notable figures across the ages": Aelryn, Nyema, the Carrillos, most narrators are
**present-day** hands who can only speak within one lifetime.

Centuries of accretion therefore come from **two engines**, and the whole design follows from keeping
them distinct:

1. **Many mortals in sequence** — a different owner in each generation, each leaving a note and dying,
   so the *same page* gathers hands dated decades or centuries apart. This is where the "handled by
   all of Ymir" texture lives, and where the **humble many** belong.
2. **A few deathless threads** — a short, special list of beings who genuinely persist, whose *single
   recognisable hand* recurs across the whole timeline. This is where the *awe* lives: a note dated
   to reign-year 40 and another to reign-year 960, unmistakably the same pen, is proof of a deathless
   thing turning the pages. (This list is short by canon: Morannagul the Eternal, the ancient
   Xochiyayotl, dream-bound Topramesk, the lich Kramer/M'Raker, the vampire Leonard, the Eternal
   Pharaohs, the alien Notturni, and the Veiled Hand.)

## 3 · The in-world conceit (how one book bears notes across centuries)

**Recommended: THE MERGED EXEMPLARS.** When the Compendium was bound at the Crystal Seat (~3518), the
Council had fair copies made and sent out — to Solanthya's censors, the College of the Seventh Circle,
the Armora Crier's morgue, a Xebech reckoning-house, an Ophiur song-hall, a Cartosan ship, a frontier
chapel, a prison library. Those copies were read, corrected, defaced, carried through wars, stolen,
and some came home. What **this edition reproduces is a composite of the surviving marked copies**,
gathered by a later **Keeper of the Crystal Seat** "so the book might be read as its readers left it."

This single conceit buys everything:

- notes from **~3518 onward**, across the New Equilibrium's decades — the many mortal hands;
- **deep-time notes** from the deathless, who wrote at or after 3518 with older memory;
- a clean reason the **same passage** carries hands dated a century apart (different copies, merged);
- controlled, clearly-flagged **post-3518 "future" notes** (the exemplars kept travelling) for gentle
  foreshadowing — the same discipline the M'Raker coda already uses;
- named **hand-clusters by provenance** (the censor's copy, the soldier's copy, the College teaching-
  copy, the copy an immortal kept 200 years, the prison copy) so notes cluster believably instead of
  scattering at random.

*(Alternatives, if you prefer: **one travelled copy** — simpler, but strains credulity that a single
volume reached every hand; or **pure deathless annotators only** — elegant but loses the humble many,
which are the heart of the "real lives" feeling. I recommend the merged exemplars.)*

## 4 · The Register of Hands (the cast — curated recommended set)

The layer's backbone is a **fixed register of recurring hands**, each with a consistent voice, a
"handwriting" (ink + a sigil the reader learns to recognise), a reliability, and a set of chapters it
haunts. Below is a recommended **starting register** — a strong spread across four bands. (The full
candidate list from the source analysis is larger; this is the curated core.)

### Band I — the deathless threaders *(one hand, many centuries)*
| Sigil | Hand | Ink / signature | Voice & reliability | Haunts |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ✧ | **Xochiyayotl**, eldest reckoner of Xebech | the palest ink in the book, dated "by the Cycle" | glacial, crystalline, deep truth; **reliable** | I (cosmogony), III (his pupil Drusilde), IX·B, XV |
| ❈ | **Morannagul the Eternal** | black, unfading; dated by *his own reign-year* | cold hauteur, deep-time menace; **true but self-serving** | XI (elves), II, IV, the Prophecy |
| ◐ | **the Yellow Postillant** *(Topramesk, dreaming — reader suspects, GM knows)* | writes "on the reverse of the leaf," yellowish | courteous void-lord, offers "employment"; **unsettling** | I, XV, XII·C, the Adepts |
| ✝̶ | **the struck hand** *(Kramer → M'Raker)* | iron-gall going black, then worse; spans 2563→post-3026 | **its tone visibly corrupts across the centuries** — a clean young inquisitor's note, then a heretic's, then something inhuman | IV, VI, the censored leaf, XIV·B, III |
| ▬ | **the Night** *(the Notturni)* | a note that reads like a hole in the page; **undatable** | writes as "we / the Night," alien, cryptic | anywhere, rare, eerie |

| Sigil | Hand | Note |
|---|---|---|

### Band IV — the humble many *(a rotating chorus of mostly-anonymous, one-note hands — one per region)*
A tax-clerk of the Letian marches; a Case di Guarigione midwife; a Seventh-Circle permit-clerk; a
Nortmaar oar-man; a **Nemyel slave-child** of the Gaerwath (the note the Curator is angriest to

Sohleugir pearl-diver; a pilgrim to Valdis's three rival sword-shrines; an **orc hanged at the closed
gate**; a **child who later owned a copy** (a name written proudly, a pressed flower, a sum done wrong).
These do not recur — they are the crowd, and they carry the "real lives" of the whole map.

## 5 · What the notes *do* (annotation types)

1. **Correction / dispute** — "Wrong. I was there." (rival accounts, added lore)
2. **Confirmation / testimony** — "True; I saw it."
3. **Useful knowledge** — a route, a price, a spell caveat, a herb dose, a safe inn, a password — the
   author's "useful bits of knowledge," delivered as a reader's practical scribble
4. **Personal memory / elegy** — "My brother fell at this ford." (the feeling)
5. **Warning / hook** — "Do not seek this door." (adventure seeds in a fresh, personal register)
6. **A thread between hands** — two annotators answering each other **across decades** in one margin
   (the most alive device; supported by nested replies)
7. **Joke / doodle / graffiti** — a caricature, a rude couplet, a bored cadet
8. **Later-events update** — a note added years on, reporting what happened after (the book aging)
9. **Censor's strike / cipher** — defacement and hidden notes (the whole book inherits the censored
   leaf's trick)
10. **Self-annotation** — a subject reacting to being written about

## 6 · How it looks (markup & HTML)

The layer must be **instantly distinct** from the Curator's notes and, especially, from the GM margin
(which already owns the word "margin"). Reader-postils are **player-facing** — everyone reads them.

**Markdown source** — a new line-form the build script recognises, led by a nib glyph `✒` (vs. the GM
margin's `⟡`):

```
> ✒ **Morannagul** · *reign-year 947* — Deathless is a courtesy. I have simply not finished.
>> ✒ **a cadet of the Seventh Circle** · *3502* — who keeps writing in the Directress's own copy?!
```

- Nested `>>` = a **reply**, dated later — a margin conversation across time.
- Each hand's **sigil + name + place/date** heads the note; the date uses the book's own calendar
  (e.g. *"ninth of Spider, 3488"*).

**HTML rendering** (self-contained, no web fonts): the build script styles postils as **handwritten
marginal notes** — a tinted ink keyed per hand (sepia / iron-gall / a per-sigil accent), an italic
system-cursive stack, slightly smaller, a short left rule and offset; replies nested; multiple notes
on one passage stack as a little marginal conversation. Where the outer margin has room, short postils
**float into it**; otherwise they fall back to an inline styled block. The censor's strikes render as
`~~struck-through~~` red.

**A Legend of Hands** — a one-page table (front matter, or a short appendix "Of the Hands in the
Margins") mapping **sigil → hand → era → ink → reliability**, so the reader can identify the
handwriting. The legend is itself a flavour piece and a reader aid.

## 7 · Worked example *(mock-up, illustrative — on a cosmogony passage)*

> …and so the Essence, which the profane call magic, is not a power but a **remembering**: the world
> recalling, imperfectly, the shape it wore before the Adepts broke it.

> ✒ **Xochiyayotl** · *the Cycle you number 3480, night of Tortoise* — Imperfectly is kind. The world
> does not misremember. It is *made to*. I taught this child the difference and see he left it out.
>> ✒ **Drusilde** · *(undated; the hand stops here)* — Teacher, I did not leave it ou—
>>> ✒ **a cadet of the Seventh Circle** · *3506* — the Directress's note ENDS mid-word. nobody at the
>>> College will tell me why. third copy I've found that does this.

> ✒ **Morannagul** · *the nine-hundred-and-twelfth year of my reign* — "Before the Adepts broke it."
> I walked it before and after. The child of Xebech flatters the break; it was not a breaking, it was
> a *theft*, and I am among the very few who remember being robbed.

> ✒ **a pilgrim of the Wyvern road** · *3491* — copied the star-chant off this page for my mother's
> passing. it eased her. whoever you all are, quarrelling up here — thank you for the chant, and be
> quiet.

*(The mock-up shows, in twelve lines: the oldest hand; a self-annotator whose pen stops at her death;
a later reader noticing the mystery; a deathless menace correcting the record; and a humble stranger
using the book to bury his mother — four centuries, four stations of life, one page.)*

## 8 · Discipline (so it enriches and never muddies)

- **Postils are diegetic voices** — they may be biased, wrong, or lying, exactly like the narrators.
  Colophons tag which notes carry genuine lore versus in-character noise.
- **Some postils carry real [CANON] knowledge** (a true useful fact); flagged as such.
- **Post-3518 notes are explicitly apocryphal** — never hard canon (same rule as the M'Raker coda).
- **Density is capped** — a rhythm, not a flood: ~2–5 postils per chapter, on contested claims, famous
  figures, useful-knowledge spots, and emotional beats. **Strata by age of leaf:** the oldest chapters
  (cosmogony, magic, the Tide) carry the deepest layering; the late-bound leaves (Ophiur, the
  Companies) carry few — "the ink still wet" — which itself dramatises the book's history.
- **A master `manual/HANDS-REGISTER.md`** (like `NARRATORS.md` / `TRANSLATION-MAP.md`) tracks every
  hand's identity, dates, voice, ink, sigil, reliability, and their notes — so a fan-out of writers
  stays consistent.
- **No collision with the GM layer** — reader-postils are player-facing and use `✒`; the Veiled
  Hand keeps `⟡ GM ⟡`. Where they sit near each other, they make **irony** (a reader guessing at a
  truth the GM margin states), never redundancy. A few hands flirt with the hidden layer (the Yellow
  Postillant, the struck hand) — those stay player-facing-but-ambiguous, with the GM margin confirming.

## 9 · Decisions needed (pick, and I build)

1. **Conceit** — the **merged exemplars** (recommended), one travelled copy, or deathless-only?
2. **Scope** —
   - **S · a taste:** 3–4 chapters, ~6 hands, plain styled blockquotes, no build-script change. Proof
     of concept.
   - **M · the spine (recommended):** all chapters, the ~14-hand register above, new HTML postil style
     + Legend page.
   - **L · the living book:** all chapters *and* the Figures of Renown, the full register + humble-many
     chorus + cross-decade threads + a few flagged post-3518 future-notes + the Legend as a full
     appendix.
3. **The register** — greenlight the Band I–IV cast above, or add/cut specific hands? (Especially: do
   you want the **struck hand / M'Raker** as a corrupting-across-centuries thread, and **Drusilde's
   pen that stops at her death** — both are strong but touch the darker/GM material.)
4. **Future-notes** — include gently-flagged **post-3518** foreshadowing postils, or keep every hand at
   or before the 3518 present?
5. **Legend placement** — a front-matter page, or a short appendix?
6. **Density cap** — the ~2–5 per chapter rhythm, or lighter/heavier?

**Production, once decided:** Phase 1 — write `HANDS-REGISTER.md` + the markup spec + build-script
postil rendering + the Legend. Phase 2 — fan out writers (per-chapter authoring against the shared
register, then a per-marquee-hand consistency pass). Phase 3 — integrate, tag colophons, regenerate
the HTML.
