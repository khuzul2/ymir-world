# VI · LETIA, BULWARK OF THE FAITH
## *Of the Holy Empire of the North, its City, its Mountains, its Government, and its Long Watch*

**As set down by the Annalist of Solanthya, chronicler-friar of the Clericato di Mithra, in the
archive-towers of the holy city, in the year of the Maelstrom 3518.**

> *Curator's note.* The Annalist returns, and this time I have asked him to write of home. I knew
> what I would receive: a psalm. It is, in fairness, a very well-documented psalm — no one alive
> knows Letia's charters, censuses, and synods as he does — and the reader will learn more of the
> great theocracy here than from any ten hostile pamphlets. But a man describing his mother's house
> counts the windows and not the cracks. I have counted a few of the cracks. — *the Curator*

---

Of all the lands beneath the sun, one was set apart to be the sun's own demesne. I write of
**Letia**, the holy Empire of the north — and I confess at the outset what my brethren will forgive
and my critics will not: I love her. Let the reader weigh my testimony accordingly, and then let
him find better.

**Of the land and its people.** Letia is the great temperate north of the world: some **three
hundred million souls**, the most of them of humankind, dwelling in a patchwork of **marches and
baronies** that quilts the land from the western sea to the contested east. It is a country of
winter wheat and bell-towers; of pilgrim roads walked smooth by a hundred generations; of border
keeps whose stones remember the Horde. The dwarves of the mountains are our oldest friends; elves,
folletti, and travellers of every honest people walk our roads in peace. Three only are turned
away at the gates, and I shall defend the turning in its place.

**Of Solanthya the holy city.** At the heart of the Empire stands **Solanthya**, seat of the
**sommo sacerdote di Mithra** — the **Alto Teocrate**, whom the world calls simply the Teocrate of
Letia. Here are the great sun-temple, the synod-halls, and the **archive-towers** in which I have
spent my life, wherein is kept every charter, chronicle, and confession of the faith since the
Grande Paura. Foreign map-makers, in an error I have ceased correcting, often write *Solanthya*
across the whole north — and I have come to think the error a compliment: the city is the Empire,
as the heart is the man.

> *Curator's note.* The map-makers' habit is canon on the world map itself, where the label
> "Solanthya" stands for greater Letia entire. The Annalist's "archive-towers" I cannot document in
> any charter he cites — I suspect he is describing his own desk with love — but I have let the
> furniture stand. — *the Curator*

**Of the White Mountains and Naughrith.** Westward rise the **Monti Bianchi**, the White Mountains,
Letia's wall and treasury both; and in their roots lies **Naughrith**, one of the greatest
dwarfholds of all Ymir. The friendship of Naughrith and Solanthya is old past flattery. When the
goblin filth took the deep halls, dwarf and Letian bled together to cleanse them, **in the Goblin
Wars of 2735 to 2740 d.M.**, and the retaking of Naughrith is kept as a feast in both nations to
this day. The dwarves guard the deep places; we guard the light ones; the mountain stands between
and over us all.

> *Curator's note.* Letia's own charters spell it **Naughrit**; the chronicle-hand writes
> **Naughrith**. I have let the Annalist keep his aspirate, and flag the doublet so that no reader
> imagines two mountains. — *the Curator*

**Of the government of the Church.** Since **the year of the Maelstrom 2610**, when the crozier
took up what the crown had laid down, Letia has been governed by the **Clericato di Mithra**: the
Teocrate above, the prelates of the marches beneath, and the barons — good swords, most of them —
holding their lands in trust of the faith. I have heard this called tyranny by men who tax their
peasants blinder than any tithe. I answer only: come and walk it. The roads are safe, the granaries
are full against famine, the courts hear the poor. No government of the fallen world is clean; ours
is at least *aimed*.

And now the laws that the south loves to recite against us. It is true: **no orc may pass our
gates, nor any dark elf, nor any who serves the Tenebre; and none may openly profess a cult that is
not Mithra's.** I defend it without joy. The orc I need not argue — the Regni Belligeranti are
argument enough, written in burnt marches. The dark elf comes from an empire that keeps our kind as
slaves and binds demons for statecraft; charity does not require us to import the knife. And the
law of the open cult is a law of the *open* only — no man's hearth is searched — for a bulwark
cannot be built of two stones that refuse to sit together. Within the household of the faith we
quarrel freely enough, God knows: the **Inclusivists** would honour the neutral gods — Ishtar,
Thurms, and their kin — within Mithra's own liturgy, and the **Popolari** would keep the rite pure
and the folk-shrines folk. It is a quarrel of scholars, and it will pass, as such quarrels do.

> *Curator's note.* Three corrections, gently. The excluded peoples note that a law which bars a
> race is felt by shepherds and midwives who never bound a demon in their lives. "No hearth is
> searched" is the law's letter; its practice has varied with the zeal of the sitting inquisitors —
> a profession of which this Church has had some notable alumni, on which see the margin below. And
> the Inclusivist–Popolare "quarrel of scholars" is the sharpest division in the Church since
> Armora walked out; synods have been suspended over it. It may pass. It has not passed yet. —
> *the Curator*

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *What the theocracy's founding covered: 2610 was not a coronation but a filling of a vacuum —
> the throne empty since Valdis Nemertine fell in 2563, the regents failed, Helgedad already gone
> out the door. The Clericato took what none else could hold and then wrote the taking as
> providence (the Annalist's own order held the pen). And the deeper seam: the god in whose name
> the Teocrate governs three hundred million souls is, in part, a mask that other hands have worn —
> see the first chapter's margins. The most sincere theocracy on Ymir is built over the world's
> oldest forgery. None of its priests know. That is what makes it dangerous, and pitiable, and —
> should the truth ever surface — combustible.*

**Of the glories that were.** I will not write *losses*; the word concedes too much. The eastern
provinces, taken by the Horde in the years 2015 to 2030, are the **Regni Belligeranti** — not lost,
but *captive*, and remembered at every evening office. The southern realms that broke away in the
War of the Princes, 2200 to 2230, are the **Regni Giovani** — not enemies, but heirs who took their
inheritance early and coldly; the Empire that schooled them keeps their room ready. And **Helgedad**,
which withdrew in 2600 — the north is cold, and cold hearts follow cold weather; we did not compel
them, and I account the not-compelling among our honours.

> *Curator's note.* Set beside these three graceful sentences the plainer sequence: within six
> centuries the Empire lost its east to orcs, its south to its own princes, and its
> northmost kingdom to a secession it could not prevent — and ten years after that, its crown. The
> Annalist's chapter on the Ages tells the same facts with the same gilding; I have scraped there,
> and refer the reader rather than repeat myself. — *the Curator*

**Of Armora, the wayward daughter.** One wound I cannot write around, for it was dealt to the
Church herself. In the years of the Machines' war, the Granduke of Armora — **Leonius VonGryffen**,
whom courtesy compels me to name and nothing compels me to praise — declared against the Alto
Teocrate words I will not copy into a clean page, raised up his own **Chiesa Armorita di Mithra**
with himself for its patron, and took his engines to war against the Empire of the faith. That a
prince should covet provinces, I understand; princes are weather. That he should tear the seamless
garment and call the tearing reform — this I have not forgiven, and I am commanded to, and I am
still at my prayers about it. Yet mark how God writes: generations later it was a VonGryffen —
the Granduchess **Vespertina** — who brought Armora back to Letia's side, and the two churches'
banners went against the orc Khans together. The garment is rent still; but the hands hold.

> *Curator's note.* The words the Annalist will not copy: Leonius declared Solanthya's Alto
> Teocrate *corrupt*, and the Letio Church secretly given to Ahriman-worship. One may reject the
> charge — I do — and still notice that the Annalist quotes every slander ever uttered against
> Armora and not the one uttered against Solanthya. As for Vespertina: a treaty of equals against a
> common enemy, proposed from her side; the Chiesa Armorita stands unrepented and unreconciled in
> doctrine to this day. The hands hold. The garment is not mended. — *the Curator*

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *The alumnus the Curator alluded to: **Heinrich Kramer**, inquisitor of this very Church, who
> fell, fled, and in 3026 d.M. — as a lich — opened the ancient portal at Ulan-Tang and drew
> Sguardo-sul-Nulla, the Observer, into the world's sky. The red moon that the Teocrate read as an
> omen was lit by the Church's own runaway hound. His name was struck from the rolls of the
> Clericato; the Annalist, who has read every charter in Solanthya, calls him only "a nameless
> heresiarch." Decide for yourself whether that is ignorance. I do not think it is.*

**Of Letia in this present year.** I write in **3518 d.M.**, deep in the peace of the Nuovo
Equilibrio, and I will end as a witness, not an advocate. The marches sow and reap; the pilgrim
roads are full; the bells of Solanthya keep the hours as they kept them before the Tide. But do
not mistake the quiet of Letia for sleep. The border keeps are manned; the east is watched; the
Khans are counted; the synods, whatever their quarrels, agree upon the watch. We are old, and we
have buried provinces, kings, and one entire age of the world — and we are still here. Let the
last line of the chapter be the one carved over the sun-gate of the holy city: *The light is not
ours; the keeping of it is.*

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *One thing the watchful Empire does not watch, because it does not know to. Since the years of
> the Chaos, the dwarves of Naughrith have quietly sealed their deepest galleries beneath the Monti
> Bianchi and do not say why — not to Solanthya, not to their own young. Something below the White
> Mountains answered the Tide the way deep water answers the moon, and it has not entirely stilled
> since. A work of the Adepti; a thing that slipped the cracked prison; something the mountain
> always held — I have not gone down to see. Neither, yet, has anyone who came back up.*
