# VII · THE YOUNG KINGDOMS, AND ARMORA THE MAGNIFICENT
## *Being a Special Commemorative Edition of the city's most-read broadsheet, printed for the learned Compilers of the Crystal Seat*

**As set down by the Editor-in-Chief of *The Armora Crier* (*Lo Strillone*, as the street still calls it), who signs — as every Editor-in-Chief has signed since the founding, by long tradition and on advice of counsel — only: THE CRIER.**

> *Curator's note.* Every other chapter of this book was written by a person. This one was written
> by an institution. The *Crier* has been printed in Armora's Industrial District for very nearly
> five hundred years; it has survived a schism, a war, a dragon, and — by its own count — eleven
> libel actions, chiefly by remaining cheerful about all of them. I asked its editor for a sober
> history of the Young Kingdoms. I received instead the following, which is not sober, and which
> taught me more about Armora than any sober document could have. Read it as you would read any
> newspaper: for what it says, and for what it takes very great care not to say. — *the Curator*

**THE ARMORA CRIER**
*Special Commemorative Edition · in this 3518th year of the common reckoning, deep in the blessed Equilibrium · price two marks, guild scrip accepted at par (whatever they tell you in the Merchant District) · ALL THE NEWS THE STEAM CAN CARRY*

---

### A GOLDEN AGE, AND WE CAN PROVE IT

Citizens! Compilers! Honoured foreign readers who have never smelled proper coal-fog in your lives! You hold the finest gazette of the finest league of nations upon Ymir: the **Young Kingdoms** — eighty-five million souls, human and dwarf and Sohleugir together, freer, richer, and better-lit than anywhere else on the map. The wars are long over. The trams run to the minute. The Equilibrium, whatever the priests meant by it, has meant for us **two and a half centuries of uninterrupted commerce**, and if that is not a golden age the Crier should like to know what colour it is.

### HOW WE GOT FREE — A REFRESHER FOR THE YOUNG

Once upon a time (2200–2230, in the **War of the Princes**) our great-grandsires looked at the tired old Letian Empire, with its incense, its Inquisitors, and its tax-collectors in vestments, and said: *no, thank you.* Thus the Young Kingdoms — young then, magnificent now. Ever since, every dreamer, tinkerer, heretic, and honest fortune-hunter the Empire squeezes out has come south to us, and we have put them all to work. The scholars call what followed a **Magical Renaissance**. We call it Tuesday: constructs hauling freight, magic rediscovered, licensed, and *billed by the hour*.

### YOUR NEIGHBOURS, BRIEFLY AND FAIRLY

- **Grand Duchy of Armora** — see the remainder of this publication. You're welcome.
- **Kingdom of the Wald** — our stout neighbours under the mountains; the finest smiths and the truest allies Armora ever had. Much changed since the late unpleasantness with the dragon (see the Remembrance page); the halls are quieter now, and the folk who dwell below are — different. We wish them well.
- **Republic of Caeldir** — inventors of the steam-lance and of professional neutrality. Will mediate any dispute, at standard rates.
- **Principality of Vallanya** — proud princes, excellent wine, long memories. Still writes us letters about certain sunken warships of five centuries ago. We print the best ones.
- **Kingdom of Tydriell** — the stubborn north-west; hard riders, harder winters, the front porch of every orc war.
- **Zaalesha** — galleys, sea-silver, and the politest ambassadors afloat. Stood with Armora when it counted; the Crier does not forget.
- **Kingdom of Nessa** — queens, orchards, and the cleverest toymakers outside our own Guild; half the automata in your nursery are Nessene.
- **Galenia** — quiet, tidy, clock-making Galenia, which has not made news in two hundred years and considers this its chief export.

> *Curator's note.* "The late unpleasantness with the dragon" is the *Crier*'s entire account of
> the fall of the Wald — a kingdom overrun in a season by the hosts of **Gwalgamaur** at the opening
> of the War of the Machines (3025), its embassies massacred, its succession extinguished. The
> dwarves beneath the old halls are indeed "different"; the honest word is *changed*, and it is
> treated in its proper chapter. Newspapers bury what churches burn. — *the Curator*

### FIVE CENTURIES OF VONGRYFFEN — STEAM IN THE BLOOD

What is Armora? A **Grand Duchy**, and the happiest argument for hereditary rule ever printed. Consider the line:

**Iulius VonGryffen IV, "the Pacificator,"** who ended the old border wars and taught the city to bank. **Vhorinne the Foundress**, who raised the Arcane Order of Artificers and lit the first Leyden lamp. **Leonius I, "the Schismatic"** — the Technocrat, as he was styled — who looked at the corruption of Solanthya's High Theocrat, said *no, thank you* (it runs in the family), founded our own honest **Armorite Church of Mithra**, and defended the city through the whole of the Great War. **Vespertina, "the Peacemaker,"** who ended that war, reconciled us with Letia against the common orc, and gave the age its calm. And so, gloriously uninterrupted, down to Her present Radiance the **Grand Duchess Serafina VonGryffen II, "the Magnificent,"** under whose portrait this edition is respectfully printed and whose Permit Office is a model of efficiency.

> *Curator's note.* The reader should supply what five centuries of loyal typesetting omit: the
> "Schism" was a mutual excommunication and a shooting war, in which the Grand Duke's party and
> Solanthya's each accused the other of serving Ahriman; and "the Peacemaker" made her peace over
> the ashes that war left. The present Grand Duchess is, by every account I can obtain, exactly as
> efficient as her Permit Office. — *the Curator*

### THE SEVEN DISTRICTS, IN ONE TRAM RIDE

Board at the palace, alight at the future. **The High City**, where the Ducal Palace keeps the fog respectfully below its balconies. **The Military District**, barracks and parade-brass, home of our incorruptible S.C.M. **The New City**, where the Academy of Elemental Studies teaches lightning its manners. **The Merchant District** — banks, exchanges, the Casino (the Crier neither confirms nor denies its debts there). **The Low City**, our district of — hospitality. **The Dwarven Ghetto**, home of the House of the Council, where the best beer and the straightest talk in Armora are poured in the same rooms. And the **Industrial District**, seat of the Guild of Artificers, where the sky glows all night and your correspondent keeps his presses. Seven districts, one city.

### PROGRESS MARCHES ON EIGHT LEGS!

The Guild's new cargo-walker took its first stroll down the Promenade this month — eight legs, forty tons, and not a single crushed spectator, which is the kind of statistic that built this city. And it walks in famous company:

**STEAM-TRAMS**, running since before your grandmother complained about them. The **AIRSHIP SERVICE**, our aerial packets — because long-range teleportation remains (as the College politely puts it) *statistically discouraging*, and the reopened gates of the ancients go only where the ancients wanted to go, the future of honest freight is the airship, and the future is punctual. The **NAUTILUS**, pride of the fleet, which visits the fishes on the city's behalf. **ORNITHOPTERS** for the sporting gentleman (see advertisement). **LEYDEN-JAR AUTOMATA**, tireless in the mills. The **ESSENCE-METER** in every prudent counting-house, ringing its bell the instant anyone works magic near the strongbox. And the great **war-mechs** — the beloved bronze **YonkerMecha**, named for the celebrated dwarf artificer, which stood between this city and the horde and now stands, garlanded, in the Square of the Sciences.

As for the persistent rumour of steel walkers of quite another temper — the many-legged **"Mechanical Slayers"** your uncle swears he saw in the war — the Crier reminds its readers that the Guild has *never* licensed such a pattern, and that unlicensed things do not, officially, exist.

*— ADVERTISEMENT — **VONHALLER & SONS, ORNITHOPTER DEALERS**, Industrial District, third gasometer on the left. Rise above the fog and your neighbours! New and reconditioned wings, easy terms, tram-tokens taken in part exchange. No permit required below steeple height. Ask about our widows' discount.*

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *"Unlicensed things do not, officially, exist." Two men wore the name **the Technocrat**: the
> Grand Duke who took it as a style, and the one who earned it in the dark — the hidden engineer of
> the war, whose workshops bound demons into steel centipedes and raised **mechanical ghouls** from
> honest corpses, and whose masterwork, the **Resurrection Engine**, drank the blood and
> souls of the living to wake the dead. The vampire Leonard was destroyed and walked again by that
> machine's grace. It was never found. It was never destroyed.*

### DREAM RESPONSIBLY, CITIZENS

The **Oneiroscope** — the College's celebrated dream-engine — remains the marvel of the age and the ruin of the careless. Licensed dream-parlours in the New City will show you the Dream World for a fistful of marks: gardens no gardener planted, cities of brass, skies you will not describe at breakfast. The Palace asks us to remind readers: *patronise licensed parlours only*, observe the posted waking-times, and report any establishment promising "the deeper shores." Eleven tourists last season did not observe the waking-times. The Crier wishes their families every comfort.

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *The Oneiroscope is not a projector; it is a door. What the parlours sell as scenery is **the
> Dreamlands**, the plane where the souls of all dreamers walk — and it is not empty and it is not
> ours. The Outer Gods rule there, patient as tide; the yellow Adept **Topramesk** keeps the dream-gate
> of drowned Ragash; and dreamers who miss the waking-bell have not "failed to return" — they have
> been kept. One more thing the city does not print: travellers on the deeper shores report a garden
> with a mechanical woman at its centre who weeps tears of crystal and calls herself **Vespertina**.
> The Peacemaker's sleep is not restful.*

### MAGIC, LICENSED AND LOVELY

Other lands fear their wizards or worship them. Armora *files* them. Behold the four Permits, the alphabet of civilisation: **Permit A**, honest practice without guild dues; **Permit B**, temporary leave for the restricted arts — or for walking your summoned entity through the city gates like a respectable pet; **Permit C**, the same, for longer and broader; **Permit D**, the near-total licence, granted only by a reigning prince and about as common as a modest wizard. Over it all watch the **College of the Seventh Circle**, whose archmages wrote the book — literally: the famous *Red Book* — and the **Section for Magical Control**, plain soldiers trained to look a sorcerer in the eye and ask for his paperwork. Order, dear reader, is the true magic; the rest is merely lightning.

*— ADVERTISEMENT — **DEXTRIUS & QUILL, ADVOCATES BEFORE THE PERMIT BOARD.** Permit B refused? Familiar impounded at the gate? Essence-meter evidence "ringing" against you? We have argued before the S.C.M. for four generations. Discretion, dispatch, dignity. First consultation free; exorcisms extra. Offices: Merchant District, above the Casino (fittingly).*

> *Curator's note.* The permit alphabet is real, and the pride is earned; but the reader of the
> southern chapters will smile — Cartosa's Blue Mantles run the same licence under a different
> flag, and everywhere on Ymir the priests are exempt, "because no one dares ask the priests to
> register." Order is the true magic; collection is its ritual. — *the Curator*

### THE LATE UNPLEASANTNESS — A CIVIC REMEMBRANCE

Five centuries past (3025, say the plaques), the Young Kingdoms fell out among themselves — Armora, the Wald, and Zaalesha on the one part; Vallanya, Caeldir, and Tydriell on the other — and while the brothers quarrelled, a third party ancient and golden invited itself to the estate. The histories call it the **War of the Machines**. The Crier's founders called it circulation. We prefer to remember the heroism: the YonkerMecha line unbroken before the gates; the airship pickets over the Frozen Gorge; the Silver Arrows, who did not come back from the Rhems Wood. The dragon is gone. The city is not. Print that on the monument.

**"MERE SUPERSTITION," SAYS PALACE.** — And while we are remembering: to the perennial whisper that the ancients buried a **Magic Rune** — a "rune of power," no less — beneath this very city, and another under lost Waldenheim, and that this is what the dragon truly came for, the Office of the Grand Duchess replies, as it has replied for five hundred years: *mere superstition, unworthy of a modern people.* The Crier concurs, and reminds treasure-diggers that the sewers are patrolled.

> *Curator's note.* Set beside the annals, the Remembrance page is a marvel of subtraction: three
> armies became "a quarrel among brothers," and the war that opened the Age of the Tide became an
> anniversary. The wars of those years — Meridian's, Helgedad's, and this one — fell like dominoes,
> each feeding the next; the Crier's ancestors covered all three and connected none of them.
> As for the Palace's denial, I note only that it has been issued, unprompted, annually, for five
> hundred years. — *the Curator*

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *The Palace lies, and knows it lies. The Runes are real: two of the Adepts' four buried
> anti-demon engines sleep beneath **Armora** and dead **Waldenheim** — power-sources the city's
> founders built over, knowingly or not, and the true prize of the War of the Machines. Every
> lamp in the Industrial District burns a little of that sleeping fire. The annual denial is not
> reassurance; it is maintenance.*

> ⟡ **IN THE MARGIN (GM)** ⟡
> *And the dragon was no invader. **Gwalgamaur** was a custodian — one of the twenty True Dragons
> the Adepts engineered to guard their buried works, logged in the old sanctuaries as **Warden of
> Quadrant 74** — a warden waking to a duty five ages stale, come to secure what the city denies
> possessing. The heroes of the Remembrance page fought the watchdog of the house they were
> standing in.*

### LETTERS FROM OUR READERS

*Sir — You style the destruction of the Wald "the late unpleasantness." I stood the winter picket at the Frozen Gorge, and I have another word for it, which your compositor may not set. — AN OLD SOLDIER, Military District.*
**(The Crier thanks the correspondent for his service and for his restraint.)**

*Sir — Since visiting a licensed dream-parlour my husband speaks nightly of a courteous gentleman in yellow who "keeps the gate" and has offered him a permanent situation. Is this covered by our subscription? — PRUDENZIA W., High City.*
**(Madam: decline the situation. Our physician recommends warm milk and, firmly, no further sittings.)**

*Sir — Your money-page persists in quoting guild scrip at par with the industrial mark. My till persists in disagreeing. — A PUBLICAN, Dwarven Ghetto.*
**(The Crier quotes the official rate. The Crier's own printers are paid, we confess, in whichever is heavier that week.)**

**— THE CRIER: founded in smoke, printed in hope, sold for two marks. LONG LIVE THE GRAND DUCHESS! LONG LIVE THE FOG!**
