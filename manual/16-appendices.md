## Appendix A · The Reckoning of Years

All civilised dating counts from the birth of the Maelstrom: **d.M.**, *dopo il Maelstrom* —
"after the Maelstrom." Year 0 is the **Great Fear** (*Grande Paura*, in the old styling). (You
will hear provincials expand d.M. as *dopo Mithra* — "after Mithra"; they are wrong, and the
Church has given up correcting them.)

The ages, as the historians now divide them, with the old styling of each in parentheses:

| Age | Years (d.M.) | In brief |
|-----|--------------|----------|
| The Forgotten Times (*I Tempi Dimenticati*) | before reckoning | The making; the Great Worms; the world before peoples' memory |
| The Ancient Days (*I Giorni Antichi*) | before 0 | The reign of the Adepts; the Demon Wars; the Catastrophe |
| The Dark Days (*I Giorni Oscuri*) | 0 – ~1900 | Recovery; the rise of the Letian Empire; the Councils of the Peoples |
| The Burning Days (*I Giorni Infuocati*) | ~1900 – ~2590 | The Horde; the Young Kingdoms' secession; Saxul's fall |
| The New Days (*I Giorni Nuovi*) | ~2590 – ~3025 | The Second Council; the cascade of wars |
| **The Age of the Tide** (*L'Era della Marea*) | ~3025 – present | The Tide (~3025–27) · the New Era of Chaos (3026–3260) · **the New Equilibrium (3260 – now)** |

**The present of this Compendium is ~3518 d.M.** — two and a half centuries into the Equilibrium's
calm. Distinguish three things the careless conflate: **the Maelstrom** (the storm itself, standing
since year 0), **the isolation** (the seal upon the world that broke at the Tide), and **the Age
of the Tide** (the age we live in, named for the Bewitched Tide that opened it). *(The one who runs
the world, and would set a table in some other year than mine, is directed to Appendix I.)*

## Appendix C · Monies and Trade

Ymir has never kept one coin, and the reader should distrust any book that prices the world in a
single currency. What the trader must know:

- **Cartosa & Meridian** — struck **doubloons** and pieces-of-eight; letters of credit honoured in
  every honest port and several dishonest ones; the Blue Mantles tax magic like a cargo.
- **Armora & the Young Kingdoms** — industrial **marks** and guild scrip; permits and licences are
  themselves a currency; never buy a Leyden jar second-hand.
- **Letia** — church-minted coin under the Clericate; the tithe is not optional.
- **Helgedad** — hacksilver, arm-rings, and the memory of who owes whom; coin is for foreigners.
- **Qi-Long** — strung cash-coins, jade and silk for great sums; a sect's favour outbuys them all.
- **The Sadhir** — salt tokens and the Sultanate's coral-standard; mana-salt for the discerning.
- **The Gaerwath** — the elves trade in **Arvo** (powdered Essence) and, it is said, in bound
  Forces; do not trade there.
- **Between realms** — weight of metal, Shald, and letters of credit; the portals move goods for
  those who can pay the tolls of the True Ways.

## Appendix F · Of the Tongues of Ymir

*The narrators of this book wrote in nine languages and assumed the reader kept all of them. The
reader does not, and neither, in three cases, do I; what follows is what a compiler at Elendor can
honestly certify, which is less than a grammar and more than nothing.*

| The tongue | Where it is spoken | Its writing | A note the reader may trust |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Letian**, and the **Old Imperial** | Letia and every land Letia has held; the Clericate and the chanceries keep the elder register | the imperial round-hand | The "old styling" scattered through these appendices — *dopo il Maelstrom*, *Grande Paura*, the month-names — is the Old Imperial, as the Empire's charters have always written it. The daughter-speeches disagree with their mother chiefly about money. |
| **Cartosan** | Cartosa, Meridian, and every port that owes them | the same letters, in a saltier hand | A Letian daughter fattened on sea-terms; the toponyms of the south — Punta del Toro, Santa Hermana — keep its music. |
| **The Nortmaal speech**, and **Norsi** | Helgedad's marches; the Winter Forest keeps Norsi, which is older | runes, cut rather than written | A tongue of kennings: nothing is named that can be described sideways. Bargains sworn in it stay sworn, the grammar being witnessed. |
| **The Qi-Long tongues** | the six kingdoms of the east, one speech apiece, mutually hostile | one brush-script shared by all six | The script is shared so that edicts may be read by the enemies they concern. Tonal; a bow is part of the grammar. |
| **Xebechani** | Xebech, Xuthal, and the toll-houses of the True Ways | glyph-columns older than any warm alphabet | Unchanged for millennia — a speech with no baby-talk, for a people who are grown, not raised. |
| **The Elar tongue** | the Gaerwath | a script of ranks | The caste-words are its skeleton — *Elar*, *Nemyel*, *Kelvar*; *Lyar*, *Nath*, *Gash* — and there is no way to say *you* without declaring what the speaker may lawfully do to you. |
| **Sarradhi** | the Sultanate of the Sadhir | a wheel-script, written in rings from the rim inward | The trade-register is spoken through masks and admits no haggling; the desert does not bargain, and its language has caught the habit. |
| **Ophirita** | Ophiur; the Ivory Coalition coast | little; the Thousand Songs are the archive | Tonal praise-registers; a genealogy is a melody, and a library is a person who has learned it. |
| **The Sohleugir trade-pidgin** | the wet markets of the southern seas | tide-marks, weights, and numerals | No adjective survives translation into it, which its speakers account a feature. See the sounding-report at X·B. |
| **The banner-speech of the Khanates** | the Belligerent Kingdoms | Letian letters, cut to orcish teeth | A tongue of tallies and banners; the chronicler Mugraash wrote a whole history in it, in a spelling that is somehow both wrong and vigorous. |
| **The speech of the Little Folk** | the Silver Isles | they write everyone's scripts and lend their own to no one | Elendor's harbour speaks nine tongues before noon, and cheats politely in all of them. |
| **The Adepts' tongue** | nowhere living | inscriptions on instruments of the elder age | Scholarship reads perhaps forty words of it. Every one of them is a boast or a warning. |

**Of loan-words.** Certain words travel with no passport, and the narrators use them unglossed:
*mana*, the vulgar word for Essence, which every tongue owns and no tongue admits borrowing;
*Arvo*, from the Elar; *doubloon* and *pieces-of-eight*, from the Cartosan counting-houses;
*drakkar*, *knarr*, and *dromen*, from the Nortmaal shipwrights; *Shald*, which came out of the
Arawak mountains with the glass it names; and **d.M.** itself, the Old Imperial abbreviation the
whole world dates by, including the peoples who burned the Empire that coined it.

**A short key to pronunciation, offered without warranty.**
- The Xebechani initial **X** is a sound the warm mouth approximates — something between *sh* and
  a held hiss; the Xebechani do not mind, which is worse. Say *Xuthal* "SHOO-thal" and
  *Xochiyayotl* "sho-chee-YAH-yotl" and you will be understood; say "Zanadu" for Xanadu, as the
  Letians do, and you will also be understood, which annoys no one but me.
- **Ssas'Kher**: the doubled *s* is held; the mark is a swallowed breath. "SAHSS — KAIR," with a
  gulp between.
- The eastern **Qi-** is "chee." The small marks over Qi-Long names are tones, not accents; do not
  attempt them in port.
- Elar names: *Lyar* is "LEE-ar"; *Celeblyar* "keh-LEB-lee-ar"; *Aelryn* runs "ay-el" into one
  sound. **Gaerwath** is "GAIR-wath."
- Sarradhi **dh** is a breathed *d*, as in the Letian *the*; **Sadhir** is "sa-DHEER."
- In **Nkishi**, the *N* is its own syllable: "n-KEE-shee."
- **Hrafnkel**: the *H* is not silent, and neither is anything else.
- **Tylweth Teg**: "TUL-weth TEG," and smile while saying it; they will know why.

## Appendix G · Of Herbs, Draughts, and Dusts

The narrators mention the road-pharmacy of Ymir in passing; the traveller deserves the list plain.
Effects are given as the wise use them — the doses, and the prices of misuse, I leave to apothecaries
and to conscience. *(Bought honestly, half of these are medicine. Bought in an alley, all of them
are stories that end badly.)*

- **Utombe Distillate** — distilled from a jungle leaf; erases the *feeling* of wounds for an hour
  (the wounds, regrettably, remain). In heavy doses a paralysing and sometimes killing poison.
- **Qarando Root** — black-leaved; the Nkishi war-draught: euphoric fearlessness, at the
  cost of all caution. Warriors under it do not parry and do not chase; they only go *forward*.
- **Subotan** — the violet poppy: bliss, torpor, waking dreams. Illegal in the Principality and
  available within sight of every courthouse in it.
- **Elin** — a red alga; for a few racing moments the body outruns itself. Sailors chew it in
  storms and age accordingly.
- **Amatuba** — spores of the deep fungi; brief, prodigious strength. Nkishi and Arawak warriors
  swear by it, and their healers swear at it.
- **Kirsemal** — dried yellow flowers, inhaled: a profound stillness and clarity, prized by the
  Qi-adepts of the east; the body answers slowly while the spirit burns bright.
- **Xu Ran** — a long dark leaf whose smoke steadies the mind for improvised sorcery.
- **Rud-Tekma** — berries that sharpen a caster's art and sink the caster into trance; the
  entranced have been known to bless — or blast — whatever stands nearest.
- **Aljira** — bluish liquor of Black Lotus nectar: restores a mage's spent power at once, and
  each dose is a wager with the body; lose, and it takes instead of gives.
- **Black Lotus** — powdered petals; sorcery strengthened, every spell's grip
  lengthened. The vice of Ulan-Tang, where it is called an economy.
- **Green Lotus** — of the Great Erg: a two-hour visionary coma, past and future
  shown to the prepared and riddles to everyone else — and each dose risks the sleeper's soul
  slipping its mooring into the **Dream World**, from which some do not come back for the body.

## Appendix I · Doors into Ymir *(GM only)*

> ⟡ **GM ONLY. This appendix, like the fifteenth chapter, is for the one who runs the world, and
> for no other eyes. Players: Appendix H was your last stop; Appendix J is your next.** ⟡

*In the same hand as the margins, found — the Curator attests — already bound.*

The Curator fixed this book's camera at 3518 and nowhere says the tripod moves. It moves. The
line from the Great Fear to this binding is three and a half thousand years deep, and the deep is
usable. Here are five doors on that line. For each I give you the four things a table actually
needs: who reigns; what exists yet; what is commonly known against what is not yet true; and
which leaves of this book exist — because a book is also a thing in the world, and most of this
one has not been written yet, wherever you choose to stand.

**The first door · The Burning Days (~1900–2590).** Set your year anywhere in the long scourge;
if you want a single hinge, take 2563, the year of the Broken Land. *Who reigns:* an Empire with
a living crown — Letia has an heir until the day Valdis Nemertine spends herself on Saxul; the
Khans squabble on the bones of the captive east after 2030; Cartosa is a young Principality
(princes from 1906); the Qi-Long's six kingdoms are raw and their Dragon-Gods newly in residence;
from 2550 the Gaerwath has one master, and he is not going anywhere. *What exists:* the Coalition
of Peoples, as parchment (1735); the Blue Mantles' Academy only after 2508; no Crystal Seat
(2590), no Church-rule in Letia (2610), no Armorite schism, no Red Book. The world is sealed:
portals are dead stone, teleportation is a scholar's embarrassment, and everything travels by
sail. *Known, and not yet true:* everyone knows demons are real, historical, and beaten — two
Sovereigns slain is the age's pride. No one living has seen a working gate. "Xanadu" is an
eastern rumour; the six Relics are six unrelated treasures in six unrelated stories; the word
*tide* means water. *Which leaves exist:* none. Chapter I's account was old before any date on
this page, but the book is thirty lifetimes off; the friar's chronicle, the sailor's diary, the
arcanist's treatise are unwritten; their sources are alive, and quarrelling. Play here for war
with banners on it — horde, crusade, and a crown worth dying for, in a world where the doors do
not open.

**The second door · The eve of the Tide (3015–3025).** The very years the old campaigns were
played in; the storm is loaded and nobody has fired it. *Who reigns:* the High Theocrat at
Solanthya; Leonius VonGryffen in Armora, calling the Theocrat corrupt a little louder each year;
in Cartosa, Rodriguez XIII until the Black Sun year, then Alberto V (3017); Morannagul; the
Eternal Pharaoh; six Dragon-Gods. *What exists:* everything of the New Days — Crystal Seat, Blue
Mantles, Sea Dragons, church-rule — and none of the age to come: gates still dead, red moon
unrisen, the S.C.M. unfounded, the *Red Book* unwritten. The wars cascade: Wolves from 3015,
Waves 3018–23, Machines from 3025, and Berthrant Vaelaer is in Helgedad with thirteen thousand
five hundred, scouring. *Known, and not yet true:* everyone knows the wars; no one knows they
are a fuse. A respected inquisitor of Mithra is quietly curdling toward Ulan-Tang, and his name
is still on the rolls. The six Relics are antiquarians' gossip. *Which leaves exist:* still
none. Drusilde is alive, mid-career, two years from her treatise; the Crier's presses print his
grandfather's numbers; the skald's Wolves-lay is being lived, not sung; Ramiro is five centuries
unborn. Play here to stand in the last quiet and know, as your players will, exactly what the
quiet is for.

**The third door · The Tide itself (3025–3027).** Two years in which everything true becomes
false in order. The Relics are gathered; the Runes unsealed at Xanadu; the bell does not crack,
it *ceases*; every gate on Ymir lights in the same hour; the Bewitched Tide rolls out of the
Maelstrom; the Isle-of-Death barrier falls; the prison cracks, the continent rises, and three
Sovereigns walk ashore. In 3026 the struck-off inquisitor opens Ulan-Tang and the red moon rises;
Ouroboros wakes and Alfheim burns in a season; Leonius's schism goes from pulpit to powder. *Who
reigns:* whoever can hold a balcony. *What exists:* by 3027, the *Red Book* — dated Era of the
Tide 2 — and Armora's S.C.M., invented in a hurry, for reasons. *Known, and not yet true:* here
the ground of the appendix inverts — in these years almost nothing is commonly known and
everything is true. The world at large knows quakes, strange tides, a wrong moon, and dead men
walking; the causal chain — Relics, Runes, bell — is known to perhaps a dozen souls on Ymir.
Your players may be among them, which is the whole offer of this door. *Which leaves exist:* one.
Chapter III is wet ink in Armora, and its author is correcting proofs while the sky is wrong.

**The fourth door · The New Era of Chaos (3026–3260).** Two centuries and a third with the red
moon overhead, the doors open, and the dead impolite. *Who reigns:* the Church holds the north
like a dyke; Vespertina VonGryffen makes her peace with Letia against the Khans, and her line
keeps Armora; Ugrumuulsh One-Eye and his successors ride the east; the Tanoth take Hagelbarn
back; the deep dwarves seal themselves under the White Mountains and change; part of Xebech is
simply *elsewhere* for a while, and does not explain itself when it returns. *What exists:*
gates, working and feared; the demon-continent, on the charts and shunned; every institution of
the bright chapters, but younger, harder, and armed. *Known, and not yet true:* the moon is
ordinary — children are born and die under it; that it is an eye is known to the Xebechani and
believed by no one else. The Crier's golden age is being printed in these years too — as
propaganda about the future, numbers no living subscriber will see; the skald's matter is fresh
grief. Drusilde's treatise rides in soldiers' satchels. *Which leaves exist:* III, and the songs
that will become XII·B are already being kept. Play here for the world your Equilibrium-era
players' grandmothers survived: grimdark, open-doored, and lit from above by something that has
not blinked.

**The fifth door · The New Equilibrium (~3518).** The default; the year the Curator's camera
stands on. *Who reigns:* Serafina VonGryffen II, the Magnificent, in Armora; the High Theocrat
at Solanthya; the Carrillo still, in Cartosa; Morannagul, in his nine hundred and sixty-eighth
regnal year; the Council of Peoples at Elendor, which is the nearest thing the world has to a
roof. *What exists:* all of it; that is what a calm is. The red moon receded in 3260. *Known,
and not yet true:* the common knowledge is the bright text of Chapters I through XIV, entire —
that is what those chapters *are*. What is not common knowledge is every margin in this book, plus
one measurement: the Sohleugir report the Maelstrom's rhythm faltering, and the Sohleugir do not
exaggerate. *Which leaves exist:* the book in your hands, being copied at Elendor while you
read it. Play here for the long exhale — and remember, before you let the calm feel safe, whose
breath it is that is being held.

Five doors. The fifteenth chapter told you what the house is; the Workbook after it stocks the
rooms. This page is only the keyring. Do not lose it, Curator's guest — the Curator does not
know he is carrying it.

## Appendix J · Of the Plates, and an Index of Places

## Appendix K · Of This Book's Making *(out-of-world note)*

The Compendium is the player-facing world bible of Ymir. The body text of every chapter is an
in-world document with an in-world author's bias; the **⟡ margins ⟡ are for the Game Master
only** and carry the campaign's hidden truths. In its present binding the book comprises the
front matter (with the contents page, "The Order of This Book"), Chapters I–XV with their late
leaves (IX·B, X·B, X·C, XII·B, XII·C, XII·D, XIV·B, XV·B), two unnumbered leaves (the censored
facsimile after Chapter VI, and the closing note), the last Curator's prefatory leaf and its
**Postils** (the reader-hands gathered in the margins across the years since the binding), and
**twelve appendices, A through L** — of which Appendix I, like Chapter XV and its Workbook, is
GM-only, and Appendix L is the table of the marginal hands. The narrators are inventions; the
world they describe is drawn from the canon files in `canon/` (which cite the original source
documents), with connective inventions flagged chapter by chapter — and appendix by appendix — in
each colophon. The register of narrators lives in `NARRATORS.md`; the full lore-reference and its
sources live beside this manual in the repository. Contradictions between narrators are
deliberate where the sources themselves disagreed — the decisions that reconciled them are logged
in `conflicts.md`.

## Appendix L · Of the Hands in the Margins *(a table of the postils)*

The last Curator set this table before the book, that a reader might know a hand when he meets it.
The notes crawling these margins — the **postils** — were gathered from the many marked copies of the
Compendium as they came home over the years since the binding. Learn the ink and the sign, and you
will know who is speaking over your shoulder; but trust each hand only so far as you would trust its
owner, for a margin is where a book is *argued with*, and the arguing is not always right. A note's
**date** is its most important word: the ones dated *after* the book was finished are the ones to read
twice.

**The deathless hands** — one pen, many lifetimes; the ink does not change though the centuries do:
| Sign | Hand | Who, and how to read them |
|---|---|---|
| ✧ | **Xochiyayotl** of Xebech | the palest ink in the book; the eldest reckoner, who taught the Directress and outlived her; cold, exact, and to be believed |
| ❈ | **Morannagul the Eternal** | black and unfading; dates by his own reign; the deathless emperor of the Gaerwath — truthful, and never for your sake |
| ◐ | **a hand in yellow** | shows through from the wrong side of the leaf; courteous; offers you employment; do not accept |
| ✝ | **the struck hand** | begins as a young inquisitor's clean note and darkens, over four centuries, into something that no longer needs a name. Watch the ink age |
| ▬ | **the Night** | letters that seem cut from the page, never dated; writes as *we*; it is reading this table now |
| ☥ | **the Eternal Pharaohs** of Khorish | dry gold; bored; very old; measure their patience against your lifespan and lose |

**The keepers** — those who owned or copied or censored the book in their turn:
| Sign | Hand | Who |
|---|---|---|
| ✠ | **the Censor of Solanthya** | red corrections and black strikes; strikes the truth and writes the doctrine over it; the strike is the confession |
| ⌘ | **the last Curator** | the neutral hand that gathered these copies; speaks rarely, and only to reconcile the dead or to grieve |
| ✎ | **a cadet of the Seventh Circle** | pencil; crams for examinations, draws rude pictures, and now and then writes down something you can actually use |
| ▤ | **the Armora Crier** | print and paste-ins; sells you a clipping; discount for widows |
| § | **Messer Crovos Bravaso** | the pompous supervisor of the Red Book; official, and no help |

**The named who wrote upon their own portraits**, and the vast anonymous rest:
| Sign | Hand | Who |
|---|---|---|
| ‡ | **Yusuf al-Marrah** of Tuleph | small, furious, dated; corrects the desert chapters and threatens to correct more |
| ⟐ | **Drusilde**, Directress of the Seventh Circle | a fine viridian hand that annotates her own chapter — and, in one place, **stops mid-word**, and does not go on, and no one will say why |
| ✒ | **the many** | tax-clerks, midwives, oar-men, pilgrims, slave-children, a soldier before a battle, a child who later owned a copy — the commonplace ink, undyed and mostly nameless, in which the greater part of any real book is written |

*The living narrators of the chapters also reach back into their own leaves (Nyema, Aelryn, Marghuz,
Hesper, the reigning Prince of Cartosa); they write in their own known voices, and need no sign.*
