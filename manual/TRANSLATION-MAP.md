# Compendium Anglicization — term map

The manual's working convention kept Italian setting-terms; this map Anglicizes them. **Column 1 →
Column 2** everywhere in `manual/` (case- and article-aware). Proper names of people, gods, cities,
regions, races, and invented words are **kept** (list at the end). Items marked ⚑ are judgment
calls awaiting the author's confirmation.

## Editorial rules applied with the map
1. Where the text had "Italian (English gloss)", the gloss becomes the term and the doubling is
   collapsed; the Italian may survive **once per book** in italics at a natural first-mention.
2. Colophons keep source-file citations verbatim (they cite Italian filenames).
3. Narrators may still *quote* the old tongue deliberately (a line of a song, a slur being
   reported) — always with an immediate English rendering.
