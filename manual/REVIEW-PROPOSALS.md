# THE YMIR COMPENDIUM — Editorial Review & Proposals

> Reviewer's pass over the whole book (00–16, NARRATORS, OUTLINE), read as a reader, a GM,
> and an editor, against the full `canon/` set. **Nothing has been changed.** Every item below
> is a proposal for the author to accept, adapt, or strike.
>
> Format: **P-# · Title · [type] · [effort S/M/L] · [value high/med/low]** — ordered by value.

---

## State of the book (summary judgment)

The Compendium works. The anthology conceit is executed with unusual discipline: the
three-voice apparatus (narrator / Curator / Veiled Hand) never breaks, the two returning
narrators (the Annalist, Ramiro) are assets rather than repetitions, and the GM margins are
evenly distributed (2–4 per chapter; none starved, none flooded). Dates reconcile everywhere I
checked them — Morannagul's 968th regnal year lands exactly on 3518; Drusilde's "two years
since the Tide" matches her 3027 dateline; the huntsman's "nineteen names in the old lists"
matches the bestiary roster; the Wall of Aslund's 400 km survives translation into the skald's
"four hundred thousand strides." Chapter X's brevity is formally motivated (the diary ends
because the diarist does) and is not a defect.

The real gaps are **coverage** (four canon-rich subjects with no home: the Dreamlands, Xebech
the land, the Curator's own Isles, the orc lands), **GM tooling** (a deep 0–3518 timeline with
no guidance for playing anywhere but 3518; hooks scattered across fifteen chapters' margins),
and **reader navigation** (no in-book contents, no index of persons or places, a promised
"tongues" appendix that never materialised). Plus one true continuity error (P-7) and some
apparatus hygiene (P-14).

One craft caution for any new material: three chapters already run on the "second voice
undermines the first" duet (XII's rebuttals, XII·B's correction, XIII's Curator-vs-Pips).
It is the book's best trick and close to its quota. New chapters should find other shapes —
the proposals below do.

## Proposals — HIGH value

### P-1 · Kyria & the Dreamlands — "The Book of the Sleeping Shore" · [new-chapter] · [L] · [high]
**What.** A full chapter on the Dreamlands and Kyria: the geography of dream (the veiled
mist-isle and Thonis as the one waking anchorage; the parlours of the New City as the paying
door; Green Lotus as the desert door; the deep Erg's dream-gates and Mubafer's palace-prison as
cross-references), the Dreamers/Travellers, what the deeper shores cost — and, in the
margins, the whole hidden architecture: the Outer Gods as the Dreamlands' landlords, Ragash-
below and the gate to steam-wreathed **Arcadia** (Kraeva, Van Argan Stones — canon that
currently appears in *no* player-facing or GM chapter beyond two sentences of Ch.XV),
Topramesk as gatekeeper, the weeping mechanical "Vespertina" of Ch.VII's margin given its
payoff.
**Why.** This is the single largest canon-rich blank. The Dreamlands are the one other plane
players actually visit (the Oneiroscope, Green Lotus, Kyria's veil), the book *sells* them four
times (Ch.V, VII, XII, XV) and never delivers; Arcadia and the Kraeva are entirely unspent
canon. Also the strongest campaign-space the Equilibrium era offers (nothing in the calm
forbids dreaming). OUTLINE already lists it as planned.
**Where.** Bind after XII·B (as XII·C, another "late leaf") or between XIV and XV.
**Narrator & voice.** *The Hypnologist* — a retired dream-pilot of a licensed Armorite parlour,
thirty years of logged descents and one unlicensed one she is still paying for. Waking prose
that slides into present tense whenever the dream comes near; scrupulous about waking-times
the way Ramiro is about weather. She has "landed" on Kyria — in sleep, once, and was sent
gently away; the chapter's spine is her attempt to be kept. Fits the NARRATORS register
(professional, first-person, biased toward her own licence) and adds a register the book
lacks: hypnagogic.

### P-2 · Xebech, the Land — "A Letter to a Dead Pupil" · [new-chapter] · [L] · [high]
**What.** The missing regional chapter for the eldest people: Xuthal the flying city as a
*place* (streets, castes at home, the Xudra the world never sees), **Xanadu** the fortress of
the Runes (named in Ch.III and XV, never walked), the **True Ways** as Xebechani statecraft, the
75-year war for **Loquitchatzotech** (2020–2095) told from the side that lost it, the Cult of
the Stars' calendar and rites, the failing Pools as lived grief rather than gossip.
Margins: the portal-caste truth, X-01 and the partial evacuation ("the far sanctuary"), the
jailer's-servant patience of Ch.I's margin brought home.
**Why.** Ch.I gives Xebech's cosmogony, Ch.XIII its biology, Ch.V one dawn from a masthead —
the land itself, and Xanadu above all (where the age literally began), has no chapter. It is
the biggest asymmetry in the regional coverage: every other major civilisation got its own
chapter.
**Where.** After Ch.IX (the East cluster) or as IX·B.
**Narrator & voice.** **Xochiyayotl, returning** — but in a new form that doubles as the
book's promised narrator-to-narrator letter: *a letter to Sir Drusilde*, its pupil, five
centuries dead, deposited unasked at the Crystal Seat. The Ch.I voice (glacial, deep-time,
genderless) bent by the one thing that bends it: a student it outlived. "You asked me once
what Xuthal stands on. You were the only one who asked *on*, and not *how*." The Curator's
note can frame the impossibility of the address; the Veiled Hand can note who else reads
letters to the dead. Reuses a registered narrator (no register inflation) and cashes the
teacher–pupil thread Ch.III set up.

### P-3 · The Silver Isles & the Council — the Curator's own chapter · [new-chapter] · [M] · [high]
**What.** Elendor, the Little Folk at home (Tylweth Teg and Gnomes beyond Ch.XIII's thumbnail),
the Crystal Seat as a working institution — how the Council of Peoples actually sits,
votes, stalls; what the Coalition treaty still binds; how this very Compendium was
commissioned, gathered, and paid for (the goat of Ch.XIII settled from "the Compendium's
purse" implies an accounts-book — show it). The Catastrophe's breaking of the Isles from the
continent. Margins: why the Fae hate everything Maelstrom-touched "out of all proportion to
their size"; what the Council's founding owed Xebechani diplomacy (Ch.IV's margin, paid off);
optionally a flagged-INVENTED hook on what the Crystal Seat was built over.
**Why.** The frame of the entire book has no floor under it: the Curator's own ground is the
one region with no chapter, and the Council — "the nearest thing Ymir has to a united
government" (canon/factions.md) — is nowhere explained. And there is a joke the book has
already loaded: in Ch.VI the Curator scolds the Annalist because "a man describing his
mother's house counts the windows and not the cracks." Make him write his mother's house.
**Where.** Between Ch.X and XI (mid-ocean, where Ramiro anchors at Elendor) or as the last
regional chapter before XIII.
**Narrator & voice.** **The Curator, in body-text at last** — wry, exact, and audibly
uncomfortable at the loss of his footnote distance; and the apparatus inverts: *the Veiled
Hand annotates him*, correcting the Curator's own blind spots as he has corrected everyone
else's. (Ch.XV already inverted the frame once; this is the gentler rehearsal of it, and the
only chapter where the reader gets to watch the book's two editorial voices disagree about
one of them.)

### P-4 · "Doors into Ymir" — running a campaign in each era · [apparatus] · [M] · [high]
**What.** A GM appendix (suggested: Appendix G, or a Veiled-Hand postscript to Ch.XV) giving
five playable frames on the 0–3518 line: **(i)** the Burning Days (Horde, secessions, Saxul);
**(ii)** the eve of the Tide, 3015–3027 — the cascade of Wolves/Waves/Machines, i.e. the very
years the source campaigns were played in; **(iii)** the Tide itself, 3025–27; **(iv)** the
New Era of Chaos, 3026–3260 (red moon overhead, doors open, dead walking); **(v)** the
New Equilibrium default, ~3518. For each: who reigns, which institutions exist yet, what is
common knowledge vs. not yet true, and — crucially — *which chapters of this book lie in that
era* (in 3020 Drusilde's treatise is unwritten; in the Chaos the Crier's golden age is
propaganda about the future).
**Why.** The timeline is the setting's deepest asset and the book fixes the camera at 3518
without ever saying a table may move it. Ch.XV §6 explains the age; nothing helps a GM *use*
the other four. Cheap to write (all facts exist in Ch.IV and canon/history.md), very high
table value.
**Where.** With the appendices; cross-reference from Ch.XV §7 and Appendix A.

### P-5 · The Curator's Register of Persons (dramatis personae) · [apparatus] · [M] · [high]
**What.** An appendix listing every person named in the Compendium plus the canon figures the
book currently *never names*: entries of one dry line each — name · people/office · floruit ·
chapters where they appear. Curator-voiced; allow the Veiled Hand exactly one word on a few
entries (Topramesk — *"alive."* Morannagul — *"ask."* The two unnamed adventurers — an entry
with no name, which is itself the point). Sweep in the unspent riches of canon/characters.md:
Berthrant Vaelaer, Aslund, Inanna, Valdis, Tofango, Uglush *and* Ugrumuulsh (kept distinct per
HIS-16), the Carrillo line, Tobias, Leonard, Surya and the Masamune, Isidoro VonGraven, the
pirate legend **Roberto Rodrigo "the Armorite"** and the seer **Obnubia** (both currently absent
from the manual entirely).
**Why.** Seventeen chapters, ~120 proper names, no index. This is the single highest-leverage
reader aid, it doubles as the "Figures of Renown gallery" at a third of the cost, and it
quietly patches the characters.md coverage gap. (If the author later wants the full in-world
*gallery* chapter — a ballad-seller's broadsheet of heroes — this register is its skeleton;
propose register first, gallery as optional follow-on.)
**Where.** New appendix before the glossary.

### P-6 · The Veiled Hand's Workbook — adventure-seeds folio by region · [apparatus] · [M] · [high]
**What.** A GM-only folio (Ch.XV·B or Appendix H) that expands Ch.XV §7's pointers into
playable seeds, organised **by region**, 3–5 each, era-tagged (per P-4), each with situation /
faces / stakes / complication. Gather the hooks currently scattered through fifteen chapters'
margins so no GM has to re-mine the book: Naughrith's sealed galleries (VI), the Oyster's
water and Taremorn (X), the Deep Pearl and the Calling Depth (XII), the Netunshiel escrow
(XII), Shulma hunt-or-homecoming (XI), the Wald's changed dwarves (VII/XIII), Oblivion on its
mountain (III/XV), Gazal's undercity and the Garden of Xiombarg (XII·B), the labyrinth of
the Lord of Oblivion (VIII), the Pilgrim's cipher (IX). Give **Ulan-Tang a featured
double-spread** here — five houses, Night Market, Azar-Kalish, Kramer's legacy on the god-
touched gate — rather than a chapter of its own (see Rejected, below).
**Why.** Ch.XV §7 is excellent but deliberately terse ("I do not tie ends. I point at them");
the pointing is spread across 300 pages. A folio converts the book's greatest GM asset from
latent to usable. Keep Ch.XV §7 untouched as the in-fiction version.
**Where.** After Ch.XV, clearly GM-flagged.

### P-7 · Fix the Ch.V Ophiur interpolation (a true continuity slip) · [fix] · [S] · [high]
**What.** In Ch.V (entry "Twentieth of Griffon [3516], the Ophiur coast"), Ramiro's diary
says: *"— For the songs themselves, the Curator tells me a keeper of them has her own leaf in
this book; trust her over me. I only anchored there. She is *from* the morning country."*
This is voiced as Ramiro — but Ramiro was lost in 3518, his tin came home "two winters later,"
and per Ch.XII·B Nyema's leaf arrived *"after the book was sewn."* A dead man cannot have been
told about a leaf bound after his death; nor does anything suggest Ramiro and the Curator ever
corresponded. Recast as a marked Curator interpolation, matching the sketch convention already
used in that chapter — e.g. *"(For the songs themselves, a keeper of them now has her own leaf
in this book; trust her over the sailor. He only anchored there. She is from the morning
country. — the Curator)"*. While there: soften Ch.XII·B's framing note ("in the chapters
already bound, Ophiur is glimpsed only from the sea… my northern narrators pass over its
peoples with a phrase"), which overstates now that Ch.V's amended entry treats the Coalition
at respectful length — one clause ("glimpsed only from the sea, and even the fairest of them
only from an anchorage") keeps the apology honest.
**Why.** The book is otherwise watertight on chronology; this is the one place the retrofit
shows its seams, and it sits in the chapter most readers will check dates against.
**Where.** `manual/05-lay-of-the-land.md` (Ophiur entry); `manual/12b-ophiur.md` (Curator's
note).

## Proposals — MEDIUM value

### P-8 · A voice for the Belligerent Kingdoms — "The Speaker of the Tally" · [new-chapter] · [M] · [med]
**What.** A short late-bound leaf (XII·B length, not a full chapter) giving the orc lands a
voice at last: the Khanates a century after Ugrumuulsh, the hundred banners, the captive
Letian east *as the captors tell it* ("the burnt marches" from the other side of the smoke),
orc law, orc memory of Uglush. Margins: Gwart and the bio-soldier truth — with the RAC-1 rule
honoured (their myths, too, remember the maker in a crooked mirror).
**Why.** The book's stated method — "a world is the quarrel of everyone who has ever tried to
declare it" — has one loud silence: orcs appear only as scourge, exclusion-law, and Horde.
Every people speaks except the one three chapters legislate against. **Caveat, honestly
flagged:** canon here is thin (two warlords, the conquest dates, exclusion laws), so this leaf
is ~70% new invention — more than any other proposal. Hence medium, despite high thematic
value.
**Where.** After Ch.VI or with the late leaves.
**Narrator & voice.** Avoid a fourth rebuttal-duet. Suggest a **transcript**: *Marghuz,
speaker-of-the-tally, envoy of a Khan's tribute-court, her words taken down at the Crystal Seat
through two interpreters* — with the interpreters' bracketed disagreements about what she
actually said doing the double-voice work in a new shape. Terse, numerical, contemptuous of
adjectives; the Curator confines himself to procedure.

### P-9 · Map plates, gazetteer index & "names for which I hold no account" · [apparatus] · [M] · [med]
**What.** Three navigation pieces in one: **(a)** a plate-list page for the 3518 map the
Curator keeps citing ("the great geographical map of 3518 that hangs beside this Compendium")
— even as described plates pending the map redo; **(b)** a place-name index (toponym →
chapter); **(c)** the in-fiction jewel: the Curator's honest list of **map labels for which no
narrator supplied an account** — Itza, U-aj-tanis, Hibernia, Ljone del Saos, Luveburk, Cariba,
Tema-li, Ost-en-Mano, Dalkis, Thulos (all on the canon world map, none in the book) — offered
exactly as what they are: blank pages the reader's table may fill. Add an in-book "Order of
this Book" contents page to the front matter while at it (the book currently opens with no
TOC; OUTLINE.md is workshop-only).
**Why.** Seventeen chapters and a globe with no finding-aids; and the unaccounted-labels list
turns a production gap (map redo pending) into an invitation, which is very much this book's
manner.
**Where.** Front matter (TOC) + appendices (plates, index, unaccounted names).

### P-10 · Appendix of Tongues (with pronunciation) · [apparatus] · [S] · [med]
**What.** The missing languages appendix: the tongues of Ymir in one table (Letio and church-
Latin registers, Cartosan, the Nortmaal and Norsi speech, Qi-Long tongues, Ophirita, the Elar
tongue and its caste-words, Sarradhi, Xebechani), script notes, loan-words the narrators
assume, and a short pronunciation key — Curator-voiced, allowed to shrug in character ("the
Xebechani initial X is a sound the warm mouth approximates; the Xebechani do not mind, which
is worse"). **This also settles a promise the book breaks:** NARRATORS.md line 48 says
*"Appendices (calendar, tongues, currencies, the canon-vs-invented ledger) are the Curator's
own"* — the bound appendices (A–F) contain neither a tongues appendix nor the ledger (Appendix
F delegates the ledger to the colophons, fine — but then NARRATORS/OUTLINE should say so).
Amend both workshop files to match whichever way the author decides.
**Why.** Reader-experience: the book is full of four languages' worth of names with no guide;
and an explicit promised-vs-delivered mismatch should not survive an editorial pass.
**Where.** New Appendix between D and E; one-line amendments to `NARRATORS.md` and
`OUTLINE.md`.

### P-11 · Register of Instruments & Materials · [apparatus] · [S] · [med]
**What.** A Curator's register of named things, each with one line and a **"last credibly
seen"** column (which quietly makes it an adventure generator): the six Relics *and where
they now stand* — note that Ch.III's Curator promises "Where the four Runes stand now, and in
whose keeping, is matter for the historical chapters," **and no chapter ever delivers**; this
register should answer or explicitly refuse in character — the named blades the manual never
mentions (Hellion, the Dark Avenger, Mornlosse, Wave-Breaker, the Sword of Valdis), the Crown
of Sorcery, Oblivion, Soul-Reaper, the Masamune, the Door of Despair set (eight
Forgotten Scrolls, the Key of Nothing, Elemental Blood vessels), and the materials Ch.III
omits (Lagorak, Abyssal Glass, Bulusture, Galvorn, Adamantine, Eog, Yarhim-shell).
**Why.** canon/magic.md is measurably richer than Ch.III §VII–VIII; a register spends the rest
without bloating Drusilde's treatise, and closes Ch.III's dangling cross-reference.
**Where.** New appendix, cross-referenced from Ch.III and Ch.XV §7.

### P-12 · The Sohleugir Sounding-Report · [new-chapter (leaf)] · [S] · [med]
**What.** A one-to-two-page inserted document, not a chapter: *a sounding-report of Ssas'Kher,
delivered to the Council of Peoples and rendered from the trade-pidgin by a Grey-elf broker.*
Numbered soundings, no adjectives, exact weights — the driest voice in the book — ending on
the one line the whole setting turns on, stated as a measurement: the Maelstrom's rhythm is
faltering (canon: the Sohleugir report the weakening; currently this plot-critical beat lives
only in margins and Ch.IV's aside). Margins: what the Sohleugir *contain* rather than breach,
and what it costs them to keep containing it.
**Why.** A full undersea chapter would be mostly invention (canon is thin: Ssas'Kher, pearls,
veils, containment); this leaf spends exactly the canon that exists, gives the fairest
merchants their own words, and puts the setting's doomsday gauge in-world. Rejecting the full
chapter, proposing the leaf.
**Where.** Bound near Ch.X (their trade waters) or before Ch.XV (their report is the
Equilibrium's countdown).

### P-13 · The Censored Leaf of Solanthya · [apparatus/in-fiction] · [S] · [med]
**What.** A facsimile page: an extract of the Clericate's rolls — or a draft leaf of the
Annalist's own chapter — as passed by the Solanthya censors, strike-throughs visible, with the
Veiled Hand restoring the struck words in the margin. The natural subject is the one the book
already loads: the name **Kramer**, "struck from the rolls of the Clericate" (Ch.VI margin),
restored in a hand the censors could not strike. One page, no new lore, pure conceit-payoff.
**Why.** The anthology's unexploited trick #3 (after letters and the index): a censored page
makes the two reading-layers *visible as an artifact* — the book's whole thesis in one image.
**Where.** Between Ch.VI and VII.

### P-14 · Margin & colophon hygiene · [fix] · [S] · [med]
**What.** Four small consistencies:
1. **Ch.XI margin** ends *"See `secret-the-great-lie.md`, COS-17."* — bare file-references in
   margins are established GM convention (Ch.I, VIII, IX do it), but a **decision-ID** is
   workshop notation and appears in a margin nowhere else. Strip "COS-17" (it already lives in
   the colophon).
2. **Ch.VI margin** embeds *"[INVENTED — open GM hook: the sealed galleries and the stirring
   are new connective tissue, deliberately unresolved.]"* inside the Veiled Hand's voice — the
   colophon already carries this flag; delete the in-margin bracket (no other margin
   self-flags).
3. **Ch.VII**: the Crier's *"eighty-five million souls"* for the Young Kingdoms appears in
   no canon file and is not flagged in the chapter's otherwise scrupulous [INVENTED] list —
   add it (the colophons' reliability is a load-bearing feature of the whole project).
4. Decide the margin file-reference convention once (margins may cite canon files, never
   decision-IDs) and state it in NARRATORS.md's apparatus section.
**Why.** The apparatus discipline is the book's spine; these are the only four places it
slips.
**Where.** `11-gaerwath-elves.md`, `06-letia.md`, `07-young-kingdoms.md`, `NARRATORS.md`.

### P-15 · Matters Unbound — sweep of unused canon · [amendment] · [S] · [med]
**What.** Five canon items appear nowhere in the manual and deserve at least a sentence in an
existing chapter (not new chapters): **the Tangle and the Heart of Chaos** (1600 d.M. — a
named world-wonder wholly absent; one huntsman's entry in Ch.XIV, "I do not enter the Tangle,
and I have no bounty high enough to change that," would do it); **the Brotherhood** mage-hunters
of Tholaer the Valiant (a line in Ch.III §VI beside the S.C.M., or in the seeds folio);
**Hagelbarn** and the Tanoth's retaking of it in the Tide years (a Pips aside in Ch.XIII);
the **Conspiracy of the Five** (a Crier historical whisper, Ch.VII); the **Zortos** are in
Ch.XIV but their Blue Mantle makers get no Curator cross-note in Ch.X — add one.
**Why.** Cheap stitches; each is one narrator-voiced sentence, and together they close the
canon-coverage ledger.
**Where.** As listed per item.

## Proposals — LOW value

### P-16 · Ch.XIII readability & the "sixteen crossings" count · [fix] · [S] · [low]
**What.** (a) Pips's humans paragraph and Tanoth paragraph are single blocks of ~450 and ~600
words — the longest unbroken paragraphs in the book; break each at its natural seams (no
rewording needed; the table-talk conceit survives paragraphing). (b) *"the learned count
sixteen documented crossings"* — canon/races-index.md strikes Ugra-Tanoth from the sixteen
(now a full Tanoth branch), leaving fifteen. Either emend to "fifteen" or give Pips the save

since the Ugra got promoted, which the registers will admit any decade now."*
**Why.** Print-page readability; one arithmetic nit a careful reader will catch.
**Where.** `13-peoples.md`.

### P-17 · Coda: "A Note Left Unanswered" · [apparatus/in-fiction] · [S] · [low]
**What.** A closing half-page after Ch.XV: the Curator's letter *to* the Veiled Hand, left
between the last quire and the cover — the only letter in the book with no possible address
("If you read the margins as you made them, you will read this…"), asking the one question the
Curator never asks in his notes, and receiving no answer — unless the author wants the
watermark-eye motif to recur on this leaf, which is answer enough.
**Why.** Completes the letters conceit (P-2 opens it) and gives the book an emotional
close-of-frame after the Veiled Hand's cold one. **Risk:** one conceit past the quota;
propose it, but it is the first thing to cut.
**Where.** After Ch.XV, before the appendices.

## Candidates assessed and REJECTED (with disposition)

- **Ulan-Tang as its own chapter — rejected.** Ch.XII's Yusuf blocks plus two margins already
  give the reader everything a *reader* needs; the remaining riches (five houses, Night
  Market, Faazi-Ghaib, Kramer's legacy) are GM material → featured spread in the seeds folio
  (P-6). A third desert chapter would also unbalance the region against Letia's one.
- **Sohleugir/undersea full chapter — rejected as chapter,** downgraded to the sounding-report
  leaf (P-12): canon is one paragraph deep; a chapter would be near-total invention in a book
  whose colophons promise otherwise.
- **Magical materials & artifacts as a chapter — rejected as chapter.** Drusilde's treatise
  is the right length and voice; the surplus belongs in a register (P-11), not a second
  treatise.
- **"Figures of Renown" as a full in-world gallery chapter — deferred,** register first
  (P-5); the gallery can be built on it later if wanted.
- **Rebalancing chapter lengths — no action.** X is short by design (the cut-off diary); IX
  is long because six kingdoms are six; nothing reads bloated. The only pacing debt is
  paragraph-level (P-16), not chapter-level.
