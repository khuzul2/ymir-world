## Foundational questions (blocking, worth answering early)

- ❓ **Game system(s).** Which ruleset(s) did the Ymir campaigns use (D&D 5e,
  Pathfinder, a homebrew, something else)? This shapes magic, races, and what
  "house rules" means.
- ❓ **Meaning of "Ymir".** Is it the world's name, a primordial being/god, a
  place within the world, or a leftover working title? The Norse echo (Ymir the
  giant whose body becomes the world) may or may not be intentional.
- ❓ **Present day.** What era/year are campaigns set in — the "now" the manual
  is written from?
- ❓ **Tone.** High fantasy? Grimdark? Mythic/folkloric? Sword-and-sorcery? This
  steers the writing voice of the whole manual.
- ❓ **Number of campaigns & contributors.** How many campaigns fed into Ymir,
  and who are the friends contributing material? (Helps track source authority.)
